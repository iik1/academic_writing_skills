## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> All entries below are PRACTICE ONLY (inferred from 4 articles; guidelines inaccessible).

### Structural Deviations

**Delta 1 — Multi-study experimental design is the dominant structure for behavioural papers, not a variant.**

Field norm: FIELD_SKILL lists multi-study experimental design as one of several structural variants, treated as a minority pattern alongside single-study quantitative, qualitative, and modelling papers.

JSR practice: For behavioural service research (the largest article type in JSR), the multi-study experimental structure with 2–3 sequential independent studies is the default, not an exception. Both 2023 articles follow this structure; the 2018 experimental article follows it too. Single-study designs appear in quantitative panel or survey-based papers. A paper submitting a single behavioural experiment to JSR is likely to face revision pressure to add studies.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

---

**Delta 2 — "Overview of Studies" section appears as a bridge between theory and Study 1.**

Field norm: FIELD_SKILL describes multi-study papers as having Study 1 / Study 2 / Study 3 blocks, but does not specify any bridge section.

JSR practice: Multi-study experimental papers consistently include a short section labelled "Overview of Studies" positioned immediately after the hypothesis development and before Study 1. It describes the logic of the multi-study sequence, previews each study's design, and explains the cumulative evidential strategy. Absent from single-study papers.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

---

### Statistical Reporting

**Delta 3 — Bootstrapped confidence intervals for mediation are the standard reporting format.**

Field norm: FIELD_SKILL states that effect sizes and confidence intervals are expected, but does not specify the mediation reporting convention.

JSR practice: Mediation is reported via bootstrapped confidence intervals (95% CIs; occasionally 90% CIs) from Hayes's PROCESS macro or equivalent. The index of mediation (point estimate + SE + CI) is reported in-text alongside indirect effect decomposition. Confidence intervals of the form "CI₉₅ [.04; .28]" or "b = .X, SE = .XX, CI₉₅ [lower; upper]" are the standard format. Significance star tables alone are insufficient for mediation claims.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

---

**Delta 4 — Discussion subsections are always explicitly labelled as formal headings.**

Field norm: FIELD_SKILL describes Theoretical Implications, Practical/Managerial Implications, and Limitations as "moves" within the Discussion/Conclusion, more often integrated than presented separately.

JSR practice: Across all articles consulted, the Discussion section always splits into explicitly labelled subsections: (1) Theoretical Contributions / Theoretical Implications, (2) Managerial Implications, (3) Limitations and Future Research. These are presented as formal second-level headings, not as integrated prose paragraphs. The heading terminology varies slightly ("Theoretical Contributions" vs. "Theoretical Implications") but the tripartite structure with explicit labels is invariant.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

---

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Delta 5 — Theoretical background sections do not use "Literature Review" as a heading.**

Field norm: FIELD_SKILL lists "Literature Review / Theoretical Background / Conceptual Framework" as the label for this section.

JSR practice: No article observed uses the heading "Literature Review." The theoretical section is labelled "Conceptual Background," "Theoretical Development," "Theory and Hypotheses," or (in experimental papers) integrated into a combined "Theoretical Framework and Hypothesis Development" flow. Submitting with a section heading of "Literature Review" is likely to read as below the journal's expected register.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

---

**Delta 6 — Graphical abstract appears as a visual element alongside the text abstract.**

Field norm: Not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL.

JSR practice: At least one 2023 article (PMC10522451) includes a graphical abstract displayed alongside the textual abstract. Whether this is required or optional could not be verified from the inaccessible guidelines.

**Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting a single behavioural experiment.** JSR reviewers consistently request additional studies to demonstrate robustness, generalisability across populations/contexts, and underlying mechanisms. Plan for 2–3 studies from the outset.

2. **Reporting mediation with significance stars alone.** Bootstrapped confidence intervals with explicit indirect effect decomposition are required. Reporting b = .XX, p < .05 for an indirect path without a CI will be flagged in review.

3. **Heading the theory section "Literature Review."** JSR expects the theory section to be organised around constructs and hypotheses, not a literature survey. Use "Conceptual Background," "Theoretical Development," or a construct-centred heading.

4. **Integrating implications rather than labelling them.** JSR expects separate, explicitly labelled subsections for Theoretical Contributions, Managerial Implications, and Limitations — not prose paragraphs folded into a merged Discussion/Conclusion.
