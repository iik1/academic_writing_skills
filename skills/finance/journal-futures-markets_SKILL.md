# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Futures Markets

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell
- **Scope:** Monthly peer-reviewed journal covering derivatives, futures, options, hedging strategies, risk management, and financial engineering. Publishes empirical, theoretical, and structural papers in these domains.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/10969934/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Jiang, "Oil Prices, Inflation Expectations, and Bond Risk Premiums," JFM 2026, doi:10.1002/fut.70083; Fernandes, Kunda & Robe, "Commodity Futures Deliveries: Theory and Evidence From the US Corn Market," JFM 2025, doi:10.1002/fut.22585)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Free Format submission: no required section order, heading format, or manuscript structure is mandated at submission. Wiley reformats accepted manuscripts into journal style. Authors may structure the manuscript however they see fit for peer review. **ENFORCED**

### Length
No specific word or page limit stated in public guidelines. Free Format policy implies flexibility. **GUIDELINE ONLY** (no constraint at submission).

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No required subheadings. Observed length in published articles: approximately 130–190 words. No stated word limit in guidelines. JEL classification codes and keywords are required fields and appear below the abstract in published papers. **PRACTICE ONLY** (no explicit abstract word limit stated; JEL + keywords: ENFORCED by Wiley publication convention).

### Citations
Free Format: authors may submit in any citation style. The journal uses author-year parenthetical format in published articles. At submission, no specific format is required. **ENFORCED (post-acceptance reformatting).**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (Arabic numerals) in the majority of published papers. Free Format at submission means no heading style is mandated.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Manuscript should be submitted as an editable .doc/.docx file; figures in highest resolution possible, embedded or separate.

### Mandatory Disclosures
Three journal-specific requirements beyond FIELD_SKILL baseline:
1. **AI-generated content**: If any portion of the manuscript used AIGC tools (e.g., ChatGPT), their use must be described transparently in the Methods section. **ENFORCED**
2. **Data Availability Statement**: Required. Must be included in the manuscript, typically after the references or conclusion. **ENFORCED** (confirmed in both spot-checked articles)
3. **ORCID iD**: Required from the submitting author at submission. **ENFORCED** (administrative, not a writing constraint)

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated beyond standard Wiley policies.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Delta: Standalone Literature Review section is accepted.**

FIELD_SKILL norm: "A standalone Literature Review section is essentially never used" in mainstream empirical finance papers; related literature is integrated as narrative within the introduction.

JFM practice: At least one published article (Fernandes et al., 2025) uses a standalone "Literature Review" section as a major numbered section following the introduction. This is tolerated, consistent with the journal's ABS 3 position and its overlap with agricultural economics and derivatives-focused sub-disciplines where standalone literature sections are more common.

Classification: **RELAXATION** — ABS 3 tolerance; authors choosing the FIELD_SKILL integrated-introduction approach are equally acceptable; authors with a standalone literature section will not be penalized here in the way they would be at ABS 4*/4 journals.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Misreading Free Format as "no structure needed"**: Free Format applies to pre-submission formatting only. Reviewers still expect the standard introduction–methods–results–conclusion logic of rigorous finance research. Submit with a coherent structure; the journal simply will not desk-reject for citation or formatting style.

2. **Omitting the Data Availability Statement**: This is a hard Wiley requirement. A paragraph specifying data sources and access must appear in the manuscript, typically following the conclusion or references. It cannot be left to revision.

3. **Omitting AI disclosure**: If any text, analysis, or code generation used AIGC tools, a transparent description must appear in the Methods section. Omitting this is a policy violation, not a reviewer preference.

4. **Treating scope too broadly**: JFM publishes advances in derivatives, futures, options, and risk management specifically. Papers that use futures data incidentally but address a general asset pricing or corporate finance question are outside scope. The research question must center on futures/derivatives markets, hedging, or closely related instruments.
