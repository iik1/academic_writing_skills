# Journal Writing Skill: International Studies Quarterly

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (on behalf of the International Studies Association)
- **Scope:** Quantitative and qualitative empirical research in international relations and comparative politics; welcomes formal models and theory-driven work. The dominant methodological register is quantitative causal inference.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/isq/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1093/isq/sqag015; 10.1093/isq/sqag016)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandatory section sequence prescribed. Submission type determines broad scope:
- **Research Article:** Requires well-developed theoretical argument plus rigorous empirical analysis.
- **Research Note (4,000–8,000 words):** Novel, focused empirical contribution; new data permissible; quantitative or qualitative.
- **Theory Note (4,000–8,000 words):** Addresses theoretical or conceptual controversies; formal models permitted; no empirical component required.

### Length
- Research Articles: **12,000 words maximum**, counting tables, figures, footnotes, and bibliography. Abstract and up to 10 pages of appendices are excluded from this count. [ENFORCED]
- Research Notes and Theory Notes: **4,000–8,000 words** (online supplementary material excluded). [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured paragraph format; no labeled subsections required. Articles consistently carry the abstract in **three languages: English, Spanish, and French**. Authors must provide all three or the journal arranges translation—confirm current policy with the editorial office at submission. Observed length: approximately 150–250 words. No explicit word limit stated in guidelines. [PRACTICE ONLY for multilingual requirement — observed consistently in published articles; not spelled out as a submission requirement in guidelines]

### Citations
**Chicago Manual of Style, 17th edition, author-date format** (e.g., Smith 1994, 101). Consistent formatting acceptable at review stage; Chicago compliance required at acceptance. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
No journal-specific heading hierarchy prescribed. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
- Place inline, near first reference in the text. [ENFORCED]
- **Alt text required** for all images, figures, illustrations, and photographs. Alt text appears directly under the figure legend, preceded by "Alt text:". [ENFORCED]

### Notes
**Footnotes only; endnotes are not permitted.** [ENFORCED]

### Data and Replication
- Quantitative data used in accepted articles must be **successfully replicated by the editorial team** before publication.
- Authors must **deposit data to the ISQ Dataverse** upon acceptance.
- Pre-analysis plans (PAPs) are **strongly encouraged** for experimental designs; registration links should appear in the cover letter or first footnote. IRB documentation required for experimental research. [ENFORCED for Dataverse deposit; GUIDELINE ONLY for PAP encouragement]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Concurrent submission to other journals.
- Previously published work.
- Self-citations must be written in third person (do not use "my prior work" or similar first-person self-reference).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Abstract — Multilingual Publication
**Field norm:** Single-language (English) unstructured abstract.
**ISQ practice:** Abstracts are published in English, Spanish, and French. Both spot-checked articles carry all three versions. Authors submitting should be prepared to supply translations or budget for editorial translation. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Citations — Style Specified
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a citation style for the SOC SCI field.
**ISQ requirement:** Chicago 17th edition, author-date format. This is a binding constraint absent from the field baseline. [ENFORCED]

### Notes — Footnotes Only
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify note format.
**ISQ requirement:** Footnotes only; endnotes prohibited. In observed articles, footnotes are used extensively for methodological elaboration and clarifications—not merely incidental asides. [ENFORCED]

### Data Replication and Deposit
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes data availability statements are increasingly standard; pre-registration is "increasingly valued."
**ISQ requirement:** Replication by the editorial team is a **precondition for publication** of quantitative work. Dataverse deposit is mandatory at acceptance, not optional. This is stricter than the field baseline. [ENFORCED]

### Submission Types — Theory Notes
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not anticipate a dedicated non-empirical submission category.
**ISQ practice:** Theory Notes (4,000–8,000 words) explicitly permit formal models with no empirical component. This creates a distinct writing register—dense theoretical and/or formal argumentation without a Data/Results section. [ENFORCED]

### Self-Citation Voice
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address self-citation convention.
**ISQ requirement:** Self-citations must be written in third person. Authors should not write "as I argued in..." or "building on our earlier work..." but instead use their own names as they would for any other citation. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Exceeding the word limit by miscounting.** The 12,000-word cap includes tables, figures, footnotes, and bibliography. Authors often forget footnotes and reference lists count toward the limit. Only the abstract and the first 10 appendix pages are excluded.
2. **Endnotes instead of footnotes.** The journal prohibits endnotes. Submitting with endnotes requires reformatting before the manuscript can be processed.
3. **Omitting alt text for figures.** Alt text is a stated requirement. Missing alt text will need to be added; establishing the "Alt text:" convention below each figure legend from the start avoids rework.
4. **First-person self-citation.** Referring to your own prior work in the first person violates the journal's anonymization and self-citation policy. Use third-person name references consistently throughout.
5. **Quantitative work without a replication package plan.** ISQ requires editorial replication before publication. Authors should maintain a clean, reproducible dataset and analysis code from the outset; retrofitting replication materials after acceptance is time-consuming.
