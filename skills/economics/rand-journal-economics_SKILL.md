# Journal Writing Skill: RAND Journal of Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Wiley (on behalf of RAND Corporation)
- **Scope:** Industrial organization, regulation, applied microeconomics, and related fields. Papers must be of clear significance to the journal's readership; methodological generality alone is insufficient.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.rje.org/submissions.html and https://www.rje.org/styleguide.html
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (TSE Working Paper 1555, Bobtcheff, De Donder & Salanié, July 2025 — working paper version of a theory-regulation paper citing RAND Journal extensively; confirmed published RAND articles were inaccessible due to paywall. Style guide constraints are the primary source of hard constraints.)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section ordering. One journal-specific rule: **sections must be numbered consecutively; subsections must NOT be numbered.** Appendices are labeled with capital letters (Appendix A, Appendix B, etc.). In the working paper checked, subsections within Section 6 carried numbers (6.1, 6.2, 6.3); these would be stripped at production to comply with the style guide.

### Length
Hard page limits enforced at production:
- Main text: **maximum 40 pages** (double-spaced)
- Appendices + references combined: **maximum 10 pages**
- Total manuscript: **50 pages absolute maximum**

FIELD_SKILL does not specify page or word limits. These are binding journal-specific constraints. Label: ENFORCED.

### Abstract
- **Maximum 100 words**, unstructured (no labelled fields).
- Working paper versions routinely exceed this; conformity is enforced at submission and production.
- Label: ENFORCED.

### Citations
Author-year in-text citations follow FIELD_SKILL norm. Reference list format is journal-specific (Chicago Manual of Style):
- **Unnumbered, alphabetical order, placed at end of text** (before appendices).
- Journal article format: `Last Name, First Initial. 'Article Title.' _Journal Name_, Vol. No (Year), pp. range.`
- Book chapter format: include editor names, title in quotes, book in italics; page numbers omitted.
- Working papers: include working paper number, department, publisher, year.

Do **not** include page numbers in parenthetical in-text citations. Label: ENFORCED.

### Headings
- Main sections: numbered consecutively (1, 2, 3, …).
- Subsections: **not numbered** — titled only.
- Appendices: lettered (Appendix A, Appendix B, …).
- Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (working paper checked had numbered subsections, suggesting authors conform only at the production stage; reviewers may not enforce this prior to acceptance).

### Tables and Figures
- Embedded in text near first reference, full resolution.
- Numbered consecutively.
- Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Equations
Numbered consecutively in parentheses, **flush right**. Not specified in FIELD_SKILL. Label: ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions and Requirements
1. **Footnote length cap: maximum 50 words (3 typeset lines) per footnote.** FIELD_SKILL imposes no footnote length limit; this is a significant journal-specific constraint. Working paper versions routinely violate this; conformity is enforced at production. Label: ENFORCED.
2. **Supporting materials (online appendices) are explicitly discouraged.** When included, they are posted online without editing or typesetting and may be negatively assessed by reviewers. This directly conflicts with FIELD_SKILL, where a standalone appendix is described as "expected rather than optional." Authors should integrate essential material into the 10-page appendix+references allowance rather than relying on an online supplement. Label: ENFORCED.
3. **Explicit style mandate:** The guidelines require "a clear, concise and direct style" and reserve editorial rights to modify accepted manuscripts "to eliminate ambiguity and repetition and improve communication." This formalises what FIELD_SKILL implies but does not state as a journal policy. Label: ENFORCED.
4. **Conflict of interest disclosure** required at submission for financial relationships with companies whose products appear in the paper. Label: ENFORCED.
5. **Submission fee non-refundable** as of January 1, 2026. Not a writing convention, but a submission process constraint worth noting. Label: ENFORCED.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations
- **Appendix treatment:** FIELD_SKILL says a standalone appendix is "standard" and "expected." RAND Journal actively discourages supplementary online materials and will post them without editing if included; reviewers may penalise their presence. Authors must fit proofs and robustness material within the 10-page appendix+references budget or tighten the main text. PRACTICE ONLY confirmation: the working paper checked has four appendices (A–D) for proofs and simulations embedded in the manuscript; in the published version these would count against the 10-page allowance.

### Statistical Reporting
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No deviations observed.

### Voice and Register
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. First-person plural ("we"), active voice, high technical density confirmed in the working paper checked.

### Citation Density
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Dense in the introduction for gap and contribution framing; sparse in results.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
- **Theory paper subsection heads:** In the working paper checked, bold-font paragraph labels (e.g., **State of nature.**, **Supply.**, **Demand.**) function as un-numbered subsection markers within the main model section. This is a common practice in IO theory papers and consistent with the guideline that subsections are titled but not numbered.
- **Literature placement:** Related literature is surveyed at the end of the introduction, not in a standalone section. Consistent with FIELD_SKILL for shorter theory papers; no deviation.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Oversized appendix or online supplement.** FIELD_SKILL tells authors to expect an appendix. RAND Journal discourages online supplements and caps appendices+references at 10 pages combined. Material that would normally go in an online appendix must be cut or condensed.

2. **Long footnotes.** The 50-word (3-line) footnote cap is strict and enforced at production. Authors accustomed to discursive footnotes (common in theory papers) must move substantive qualifications into the main text, shorten them, or delete them. Working paper footnotes routinely exceed this limit by a factor of three or four.

3. **Numbered subsections.** Submitting a manuscript with subsections numbered (e.g., 3.1, 3.2) violates the style guide. Subsections should carry only a title.

4. **Abstract over 100 words.** Working paper abstracts are typically 150–250 words. The 100-word cap requires aggressive cutting before submission; the journal does not pad this limit.
