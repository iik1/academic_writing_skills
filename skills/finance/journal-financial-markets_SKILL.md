# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Markets

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Original research on securities trading, pricing mechanisms, order placement strategies, the role of information in securities markets, market microstructure, and price behavior. Balanced between theoretical and empirical work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-financial-markets/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1016/j.finmar.2025.101005; 10.1016/j.finmar.2025.101004)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Required elements: separate title page (double-blind), abstract, keywords, research highlights, main text, references, declarations (competing interests, funding, generative AI). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for body structure otherwise.

### Length
No word or page limit is stated in the accessible guidelines.

### Abstract
No explicit word limit stated in guidelines. In practice, observed abstracts run approximately 89–92 words — substantially shorter than the FIELD_SKILL norm of 150–250 words. Observed abstracts are single-paragraph and results-focused; they do not explicitly preview methodology, state the contribution, or position in the literature. **Label: PRACTICE ONLY, RELAXATION** (ABS 3).

### Highlights
Elsevier-mandated section. Required at final submission stage (not initial submission). Format: 3–5 bullet points, maximum 85 characters (including spaces) per bullet. Bullets should capture novel results in plain language, avoid jargon and acronyms, and target a general academic audience. Observed articles carried 4–5 highlights. **Label: ENFORCED.**

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical format confirmed in practice. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Figures generated by AI tools require disclosure in declarations.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Manuscripts must not be under simultaneous consideration elsewhere.
- Double-blind: the main manuscript must contain no author-identifying information. Author details go on a separate title page only.

### Submission Fee
**US$275 per new submission.** Payment is required before the submission is processed. 50% is refunded if the editor desk-rejects. Transferred papers (invited from another Elsevier journal) are exempt. **Label: ENFORCED.**

### Abstract Length and Content
**Field norm:** 150–250 words; explicitly previews method/data, key findings, and contribution.
**This journal (practice):** Observed abstracts ~89–92 words; results-focused; methodological preview and explicit contribution statement absent from abstract.
**Label: PRACTICE ONLY, RELAXATION** (ABS 3 — do not redefine as a new norm).

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing payment.** Submissions are not processed until the US$275 fee is paid. Failure to pay results in no editorial consideration regardless of manuscript quality.
2. **Including author information in the main manuscript.** This journal uses strict double-blind review. Any identifying information (names, institutional affiliations, acknowledgements, self-identifying citations like "in our prior paper [X]") in the main file triggers administrative rejection or deanonymization.
3. **Omitting or mis-formatting Highlights.** Highlights are required at final submission. Each bullet must be ≤85 characters including spaces; exceeding this limit or writing highlights as full sentences with jargon will fail the technical check.
4. **Writing a field-length abstract.** An abstract of 200+ words reads as overwritten relative to the journal's observed practice of ~90 words. Keep the abstract tight and results-focused; defer detailed methodology and contribution framing to the introduction.
