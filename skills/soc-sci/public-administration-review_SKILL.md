# Journal Writing Skill: Public Administration Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley / American Society for Public Administration (ASPA)
- **Scope:** Flagship practitioner-scholar journal of ASPA covering public administration research, theory, and practice. Unusually wide practitioner readership compared to most ABS 4 social science journals.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://publicadministrationreview.wordpress.com/submission-process/
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (all full-text articles paywalled; guideline items classified GUIDELINE ONLY unless requirement is unambiguous and editorial)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Required section order for research articles: Abstract → Evidence for Practice → Introduction → Theory → Methods → Results → Discussion → Conclusion. Section label "Theory" is prescribed (not "Literature Review"). Conclusion is a distinct required section, separate from Discussion. [GUIDELINE ONLY — confirmed from guidelines; article access unavailable for verification]

### Length
8,000 words maximum, including abstract, endnotes, and references. Tables, figures, charts, and appendices are excluded from the word count. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured. Maximum 150 words. Placed on a separate page preceding the manuscript body. [ENFORCED]

### Evidence for Practice
3–5 sentence-length takeaway points that distill practical implications for practitioners. Placed immediately after the Abstract, before the Introduction. No in-text citations permitted in this section. Encouraged at initial submission; required at revision stage. This section is entirely absent from FIELD_SKILL and has no analogue in any other ABS 4 SOC SCI journal surveyed. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style, 16th edition, Author-Date method. Distinctive requirement: all references must list authors' full first names (not initials). Do not use word-processor autoformat or footnote features; endnotes must be manually entered as unlinked text. Endnotes are listed as a block before the references section, not embedded as footnotes. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Article title and primary subheads: 14-point bold, title case. Secondary subheads: 12-point bold, title case. Sub-subheads (run-in style): 12-point bold italic, followed by a period. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Placed on separate pages at the end of the manuscript, with placement indicated in the text. No heavy borders or shading; use patterns instead of color in all tables and figures. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- First-person singular "I" in regular research submissions. Only permitted in commissioned editorials or commentary.
- Author names, institutional affiliations, and identifying web addresses in the manuscript body (blind review requirement).
- Automatically formatted or hyperlinked endnotes/references.
- Acronyms constructed from repeated phrases; acronyms must be used sparingly and spelled out at first use.
- Previously published manuscripts or manuscripts under simultaneous review elsewhere.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**Field norm:** First-person singular "I" is explicitly endorsed for single-authored work. Register permits high technical density.

**PAR delta 1 — First person singular prohibited:** PAR explicitly prohibits "I" in all regular research articles, including single-authored work. Single-authored papers must use passive constructions, third-person self-reference, or impersonal constructions ("this article argues," "the analysis shows") in place of "I." First-person plural "we" remains acceptable for multi-authored work. [ENFORCED — unambiguous guideline]

**PAR delta 2 — Reduced technical density:** Guidelines explicitly instruct authors to "avoid overly technical language" and to minimise jargon. This reflects PAR's hybrid practitioner-scholar audience and represents a meaningful constraint relative to the high technical density FIELD_SKILL endorses. Authors should write at a level accessible to a knowledgeable practitioner, not only to methodological specialists. [GUIDELINE ONLY — stated requirement; practice conformance unverified]

### Structural Deviations

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes flexible section conventions — "Literature Review" or "Theory/Literature Review" commonly used; Discussion and Conclusion may be merged.

**PAR delta:** The section labelled "Theory" (not "Literature Review") is the prescribed name; Discussion and Conclusion must be separate named sections. The placement of Evidence for Practice between Abstract and Introduction is a structural requirement with no parallel in FIELD_SKILL. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Self-Citation

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify how authors handle self-citations.

**PAR delta:** Authors must cite their own prior work in third person and use full citations in the references section. This is required for blind review purposes and is stated as an explicit requirement. [ENFORCED — blind review requirement]

### Reporting Standards and Transparency

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes pre-registration is "increasingly valued" and ethics/data availability statements are "increasingly standard."

**PAR delta:** PAR formally endorses and promotes four methodology-specific reporting standards: CONSORT (randomised trials), STROBE (quantitative observational studies), PRISMA (systematic reviews and meta-analyses), COREQ (qualitative studies). Data sharing via Dataverse or the Qualitative Data Repository is encouraged, with optional open-science badge recognition for pre-registration and open data. More formal and specific than FIELD_SKILL's general norms. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using "I" in single-authored work.** FIELD_SKILL endorses it; PAR prohibits it. Replace with passive voice, "this article," or impersonal constructions throughout.

2. **Omitting or misfiling Evidence for Practice.** This section must appear between the Abstract and the Introduction, must contain 3–5 sentences, and must carry no citations. Placing it at the end or embedding it in the conclusion is non-compliant.

3. **Using author initials in references.** PAR requires full first names in all reference entries. This diverges from standard Chicago and APA practice and is easy to miss.

4. **Exceeding the word limit on "included" content.** The 8,000-word ceiling covers abstract + endnotes + references. Authors accustomed to journals that exclude references from word counts will be caught short. Tables, figures, charts, and appendices are excluded.
