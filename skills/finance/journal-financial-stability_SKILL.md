# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Stability

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (in collaboration with Gabelli School of Business, Fordham University)
- **Scope:** International forum for theoretical and empirical macro- and micro-level research on the causes, management, and prevention of financial crises, with an explicit mandate for applied research relevant to public policy on financial stability.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-financial-stability/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (abstracts/metadata; full text paywalled — Chari et al. 2024, doi:10.1016/j.jfs.2024.101235; Hałaj et al. 2024, doi:10.1016/j.jfs.2024.101213)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section order beyond standard elements. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for overall structure. The following additional elements are required by Elsevier and must appear in the submission:
- **Highlights** (before abstract; see below)
- **CRediT author contribution statement** — each author's role stated using CRediT taxonomy (e.g., Conceptualization, Methodology, Writing – original draft). Required for all multi-author papers. [ENFORCED]
- **Data availability statement** — required; follows FIELD_SKILL norm.
- **Declaration of generative AI use** — required if any AI-assisted tool was used in manuscript preparation; must name the tool and describe its use. AI may not be listed as author or co-author. [ENFORCED]

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in the guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; hard limit of **250 words**. Must stand alone (avoid citations; if essential, include author and year only). Avoid non-standard abbreviations; define any abbreviations at first mention. [ENFORCED]

### Keywords
**1–7 keywords** required. Must be in English. Avoid multi-word phrases joined by "and" or "of." Use abbreviations only if firmly established in the field. [ENFORCED]

### Highlights
**Required by Elsevier; not present in FIELD_SKILL baseline.** Submit 3–5 bullet-point highlights capturing the core findings of the article. Each highlight must be ≤85 characters (including spaces). Highlights appear as a distinct submission element before the abstract and are displayed prominently in ScienceDirect listings. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical in-text format: (Author, Year) or Author (Year). Aligns with FIELD_SKILL. At submission, any consistent reference style is accepted; Elsevier reformats in production.

**Published reference list format (Elsevier author-year):**
- *Journal article:* Author(s), Year. Article title. *Journal Name* Volume, page range.
  - Example: Chari, A., Garcés, F., Martínez, J.F., Valenzuela, P., 2024. Sovereign credit spreads, banking fragility, and global factors. *Journal of Financial Stability* 72, 101235.
- *Book:* Author(s), Year. *Title*. Publisher, Location.
- Year follows author names directly (no parentheses in reference list); no issue number; DOIs strongly encouraged as hyperlinks.

This format differs from APA and from some other author-year styles: no parentheses around the year in the reference list. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (Arabic numerals; hierarchical subsections).

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific constraints observed beyond standard Elsevier production requirements.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Generative AI may not be used by reviewers or editors in peer review.
- Authorship changes not permitted after acceptance.
- Prior publication (other than preprint form) is not permitted.

### Language
**Inclusive language explicitly required.** Authors must use language that acknowledges diversity and conveys respect for all people; avoid descriptors implying superiority based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability. Gender-neutral plural constructions are recommended as defaults. [ENFORCED] This is not stated in FIELD_SKILL.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Policy Orientation in Structure (PRACTICE ONLY)
**Field norm:** Policy implications are typically addressed briefly in the conclusion under standard "implications" moves.

**This journal:** The journal's explicit mandate for "applied research useful in affecting public policy with respect to financial stability" drives a structural tendency to give policy implications more prominent treatment. In spotted abstracts, policy-facing conclusions appear routinely (e.g., "regulators must ensure the stability of the banking sector"). Authors targeting this journal should ensure policy implications are substantive and clearly articulated — either as a named subsection within the conclusion or as a dedicated "Policy implications" paragraph following main results, rather than limiting this to a single sentence. This pattern is consistent with journal scope rather than an explicit guideline requirement. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Reference List Format (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL specifies author-year in-text format but does not prescribe reference list layout.

**This journal:** Elsevier author-year format: year follows author names with a period (not in parentheses); no issue number in journal citations; page ranges given directly after volume. See Citations section above for full examples.

### Highlights Requirement (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** Not described in FIELD_SKILL.

**This journal:** 3–5 highlights of ≤85 characters each are a mandatory submission element. These are high-visibility items displayed in ScienceDirect search results and article pages; they must distil the most important empirical takeaways, not restate the abstract. Writing generic highlights ("we study X and find Y") is insufficient; reviewers and editors notice thin highlights.

### CRediT Author Contributions (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** Not described in FIELD_SKILL.

**This journal:** A formal CRediT taxonomy author contribution statement is required for all submissions (Conceptualization, Data curation, Formal analysis, Funding acquisition, Investigation, Methodology, Project administration, Resources, Software, Supervision, Validation, Visualization, Writing – original draft, Writing – review & editing). Each author's roles must be individually listed.

### Inclusive Language (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL.

**This journal:** Explicitly required by guidelines. Default to gender-neutral plural constructions and avoid language that implies hierarchy among demographic groups.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting highlights or writing them as abstract-restatements.** Highlights are a required, distinct submission element. They must be ≤85 characters each and capture specific empirical findings, not describe the paper's topic.

2. **Missing CRediT author contribution statement.** Elsevier requires individual-level role attribution. A common oversight on first Elsevier submission.

3. **Thin policy discussion.** This journal's explicit policy mandate means that results sections framed purely around academic contribution, with policy relevance relegated to a single concluding sentence, are a poor fit. Policy implications should be substantive.

4. **Wrong reference list format.** Authors accustomed to APA (year in parentheses in the reference list) or numbered styles must switch to Elsevier author-year format: year after author names, period-separated, no issue number, no "pp." before page range.
