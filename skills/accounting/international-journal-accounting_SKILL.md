# Journal Writing Skill: The International Journal of Accounting (TIJA)

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** World Scientific Publishing
- **Scope:** International and comparative accounting; cross-national financial reporting; accounting in emerging and developing economies; corporate governance, taxation, and auditing from an international perspective. Historically associated with the University of Illinois International Accounting Symposium.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.worldscientific.com/page/tija/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Lubberink 2025, "Bank Accounting, Bank Regulation, and Capital Requirements: A Review," DOI 10.1142/S1094406025300011; "Ethnic Diversity and Monitoring Effectiveness of the Board: Evidence from Banks," Vol. 60 Issue 3, 2025 — both accessed via IDEAS/RePEC)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No unique required section order mandated beyond the Synopsis requirement below. Standard accounting article structure otherwise applies.

### Length
No specific word or page limit identified in accessible guidelines.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED — Synopsis replaces abstract entirely.** TIJA does not use a conventional unstructured abstract. Instead, submissions require a structured **Synopsis** using a Level 1 heading "Synopsis" followed by labeled sub-sections. The confirmed sub-section labels are: **Research Problem**, **Motivation**, **Target Population**, **Adopted Methodology**, and **Findings** (or equivalent labels in the same sequence). Each sub-section carries a Level 2 heading. The synopsis is double-spaced with single-spaced content within each sub-section. No word limit identified in accessible guidelines, but published synopses are concise (each sub-section typically 1–3 sentences). JEL codes and keywords appear as separate fields alongside the synopsis.

### Citations
**ENFORCED — APA 7th edition (Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, 7th ed.).** In-text parenthetical citations use an ampersand between co-authors: (Author & Author, Year). Three or more authors: (Author et al., Year). Multiple citations in one parenthetical are separated by semicolons in alphabetical order. Reference list entries use initials only (no full first names), hanging-indent format, and include DOIs where available. The TIJA Style Guide specifies that TIJA deviates from APA on some points (particularly headings and appendices); the citation and reference rules themselves follow APA 7th.

### Headings
**GUIDELINE ONLY.** TIJA heading levels deviate from APA style. Appendix labels and titles are flush left and bold (not centered as in APA). Full heading specifications are in the TIJA Style Guide available from the journal.

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED.** Tables and figures are placed at the end of the manuscript (after references). Each must be called out in the text at the point of first reference. Figures and tables are not embedded in the body text in submissions.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No title page or author-identifying information in the submission document itself (blind review). Author details are entered separately in the submission system.
- No page charges.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where TIJA deviates from the account-field-writing-style baseline.

### Abstract / Synopsis Format
**FIELD NORM:** A single unstructured paragraph summarizing the research question, method, findings, and contribution. Keywords and JEL codes are separate fields.

**TIJA PRACTICE:** Abstract is replaced by a structured Synopsis with explicit labeled sub-headings (Research Problem / Motivation / Target Population / Adopted Methodology / Findings). The Synopsis functions as a form-driven structured abstract, not a prose paragraph. This pattern was confirmed in both spot-checked articles across different sub-fields (banking review article and corporate governance empirical article). **ENFORCED.**

### Citation Style
**FIELD NORM:** Author-date system; specific punctuation and reference-list format not specified at field level.

**TIJA PRACTICE:** Explicitly mandated APA 7th edition. Practical consequences: ampersand (&) between co-authors in parenthetical citations (not "and"); "et al." for three or more authors from first mention; reference list uses author initials only; DOIs formatted as hyperlinks per APA 7th. **ENFORCED.**

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting a conventional abstract.** TIJA requires a structured Synopsis with explicit labeled sub-headings. A single prose paragraph will not meet submission requirements, regardless of its quality.
2. **Using non-APA citation formatting.** Authors accustomed to Chicago, Harvard, or hybrid author-date styles must reformat. Specifically: use "&" not "and" in parenthetical co-author citations, use initials-only in the reference list, and include DOIs formatted as hyperlinks.
3. **Including identifying information in the manuscript file.** Blind review is enforced; author names, affiliations, and acknowledgements must be omitted from the submitted document and entered separately via the submission system.
4. **Embedding tables and figures in the text.** All tables and figures must be placed after the references, not in-line.
