# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Development Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Original research on all aspects of economic development, emphasising quantitative or analytical work that is novel and relevant. Papers using single-country data are welcome if findings speak to broader development questions; country-specific case studies without general-interest conclusions are explicitly excluded.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-development-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 full text (Bettin, Jallow, Zazzaro — "How Do Monthly Remittances Respond to Natural Disasters in Migrants' Home Countries?", CSEF WP 673, published JDE Vol. 174, May 2025); 2 abstracts/highlights panels (Byrne et al., Vol. 181; Dione et al., Vol. 181).

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override or supplement FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandatory section order is prescribed in the guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for standard articles. A **Short Papers** format exists as a separate submission track with its own constraints (see Length below).

### Length
Two tracks:

**Standard articles:** No maximum word limit stated in the accessible guidelines.

**Short Papers** [ENFORCED]: Maximum 6,000 words; maximum 5 exhibits (tables and figures combined); online appendix maximum 20 pages. Fast-track with 6–8 week decision target. These limits are stated explicitly and apply as hard caps.

### Abstract
Unstructured. Word limit not explicitly stated in accessible portions of the guide. Elsevier's general guidance for social science journals is 150 words; treat as a practical ceiling in absence of a stated limit. [GUIDELINE ONLY — exact limit unconfirmed from guidelines text]

### Citations
Author-year (Harvard) style, confirmed by article practice: "(Van Aalst, 2006; Coronese et al., 2019)". No deviation from FIELD_SKILL norm. [ENFORCED — Elsevier house style for this journal]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Arabic numerals observed in practice.

### Tables and Figures
For Short Papers: combined exhibit count (tables + figures together) may not exceed 5. For standard articles: no stated limit. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Explicit Prohibitions [ENFORCED]
- **No book reviews.** The journal does not publish them.
- **No reconsidering rejected papers.** Papers rejected by JDE will not be considered again under any circumstances, including new datasets or models. This is an unusually strict permanent bar.
- **No purely country-specific studies.** Papers that are "essentially in-depth studies of a specific country" are excluded unless findings carry broader interest for development economics readers.
- **Submission frequency cap.** An author (directly or through a co-author) may not submit more than three papers within any rolling 12-month period.
- **Submission fee.** USD 50 per new submission (USD 25 for student lead authors; waived for corresponding authors from low- and middle-income economies). Unpaid fee = non-submission.

### Other Required Declarations [ENFORCED]
- Competing interests declaration
- Funding disclosure
- Generative AI use statement (required if AI was used in manuscript preparation; exempted for basic grammar/spelling/reference tools)
- Replication materials (mandatory for Registered Reports)

### Registered Reports [ENFORCED]
A pre-results review format is available. Proposals are submitted as "Registered Report Stage I." Accepted proposals receive in-principle acceptance contingent on completing the pre-registered analysis. This format is not described in FIELD_SKILL and is a journal-specific submission pathway.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL canonical empirical order:** Introduction → Data → Empirical Strategy → Results → Conclusion.

**Observed in practice (one article):** Introduction → Related Literature → Empirical Strategy → Data → Results → Conclusions. Two deviations: (a) standalone Related Literature as section 2 in an empirical paper; (b) Empirical Strategy placed before Data.

- Standalone Related Literature section in empirical paper: FIELD_SKILL notes this is "often omitted in shorter empirical papers" and "more common in theory papers." The one full article checked used it. [PRACTICE ONLY — single observation; treat as plausible but unconfirmed JDE tendency]
- Empirical strategy before data: Reverses FIELD_SKILL's Data → Empirical Strategy order. [PRACTICE ONLY — single observation]

No other structural deviations observed.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **The permanent rejection bar.** Submitting a revised version of a previously rejected JDE paper under a new title or with new data violates an explicit editorial prohibition and will result in desk rejection. This applies even if co-authors change.

2. **Country-study framing.** Papers using a single country's data must make the broader development-economics contribution explicit from the abstract onward. Framing a paper primarily as a study of a specific country — even with rich identification — risks desk rejection on scope grounds.

3. **Short Papers exhibit cap.** On the Short Papers track, the 5-exhibit limit counts tables and figures together. Authors who draft a short paper with, say, 4 tables and 3 figures will need to cut or merge before submission.

4. **Submission frequency.** Co-authoring on multiple papers being submitted to JDE in the same period counts against each author's individual 3-per-12-months limit. Teams with multiple JDE-targeted papers should stagger submissions.
