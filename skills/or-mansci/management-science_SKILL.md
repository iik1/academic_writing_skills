# Journal Writing Skill: Management Science

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** INFORMS
- **Scope:** All aspects of management research across the full business school curriculum — operations, strategy, entrepreneurship, innovation, IT, organizations, accounting, finance, and marketing. Significantly broader than the OR/MS subfield label implies; empirical, behavioral, and theoretical work from any management discipline is in scope.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/mnsc/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Manshadi & Rodilitz 2022, DOI: 10.1287/mnsc.2021.4220 / arXiv:2002.08474)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section sequence is prescribed.

### Length
**Initial submission:** No page limit enforced, but Department Editors may desk-reject for excessive length. Succinctness is explicitly expected.

**Invited revisions:** Hard limit of **47 pages** at double spacing (25 lines/page) or **32 pages** at 1.5 spacing (33 lines/page). Electronic companions (online appendix) are excluded from this count.

**Fast Track submissions:** Separate submission pathway for short, high-impact papers. Total manuscript length ≤ **5,000 words** (including references and all content outside a separate supplementary appendix). Acceptance rate ≈ 7.7%; bar is substantially higher than regular track. Review is binary (accept with minor revisions or reject); limited feedback provided. [GUIDELINE ONLY — distinct pathway, not a writing style norm]

**Formatting (submission):** Minimum 1.5-spacing; 11-point font; 1-inch margins. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose. Maximum **250 words**. [ENFORCED]

Confirmed in spot-checked article: abstract is a single unstructured paragraph, approximately 200 words.

### Keywords
**3–5 keywords required**, listed immediately after the abstract under the label "Key words:". [ENFORCED]

This is a hard constraint not addressed in FIELD_SKILL. Keywords are separated by commas and set in lowercase (except proper nouns and abbreviations).

### Citations
INFORMS author-year style. In-text: `(Author Year)` or `Author (Year)` — **no comma** between name and year. Multiple citations separated by commas within parentheses: `(Ata et al. 2016, McElfresh et al. 2019)`. Reference list alphabetical by first author; format: `Author(s) (Year) Title. Journal Vol(Issue):Pages.` [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Numbered sections and subsections (1., 2., 3.; 4.1, 4.2). Additionally, **bold non-numbered paragraph headers** are used within the Introduction to organize motivational background, contributions summary, and model overview — a common structural device in published articles not addressed in FIELD_SKILL. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Data and Code Disclosure
All submissions must include a completed **Data and Code Disclosure form** explaining how the paper satisfies the journal's reproducibility policy. Required since June 1, 2019; applies to resubmissions as well. Sufficient detail for a peer to reproduce the essential content is required, not necessarily every element. [ENFORCED]

### Submission Fee
$79 fee for all original submissions effective August 1, 2025. Waived for INFORMS members, authors whose primary affiliation is in a low- or lower-middle-income country (World Bank designation), and authors requesting a hardship waiver (honor system, no documentation required). [ENFORCED — administrative, not a writing convention]

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated beyond standard simultaneous-submission and prior-publication policies. Prior rejected submissions that are resubmitted must be disclosed in the cover letter.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Introduction internal organization:** Published articles routinely use **bold non-numbered paragraph headers** within the Introduction to label distinct subsections (e.g., "Summary of Contributions:", "Modeling the Problem:", background case description headers). These are not numbered sub-sections — they function as visual organizers for longer introductions. FIELD_SKILL describes contribution enumeration and roadmap paragraphs but does not address this heading device.

Classification: **PRACTICE ONLY** — observed in article; not prescribed in guidelines.

### Scope Framing

FIELD_SKILL calibrates the field baseline to OR and management science methodology. Management Science explicitly publishes work from all functional management disciplines. Authors targeting this journal should frame motivation and contribution significance with cross-functional audiences in mind; narrowly technical framing appropriate for Operations Research or Mathematical Programming is insufficient here. Empirical, behavioral, and field-experiment papers appear alongside formal OR theory in the same issues.

Classification: **ENFORCED** (editorial statement) — not a deviation in writing style per se, but affects how motivation and contribution claims should be pitched.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing keywords.** FIELD_SKILL does not mention keywords; forgetting to include 3–5 keywords is a common omission in authors working from a field baseline alone.

2. **Exceeding the revision page cap.** The revision limit (47pp double-spaced) is a hard constraint. Papers that stay within the initial submission's informal length norm may still breach the revision limit if appendix material is not properly separated into an electronic companion.

3. **Submitting without the Data and Code Disclosure form.** Required for all submissions since 2019. Omission risks desk rejection.

4. **Framing contribution too narrowly as OR/MS.** Given the journal's cross-disciplinary scope, motivation that speaks only to a technical OR audience misses the readership and may read as under-motivated to area editors from other departments (Strategy, Finance, Behavioral).
