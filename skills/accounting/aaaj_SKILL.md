# Journal Writing Skill: Accounting, Auditing & Accountability Journal

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Emerald Publishing Limited
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary accounting journal publishing critical, qualitative, and interpretive investigations of accounting, auditing, and accountability and their socio-economic, institutional, and political environments. Explicitly welcomes heterodox theory, diverse methods, and social/political perspectives.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.emeraldgrouppublishing.com/journal/aaaj
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Argento et al. 2025, AAAJ 38(3):765–796, DOI 10.1108/AAAJ-01-2025-7670; Nikidehaghani 2024, AAAJ 37(9):128–159, DOI 10.1108/AAAJ-01-2023-6250)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
RELAXATION. No canonical section set is enforced. Sections are named thematically, not generically. Observed pattern across checked articles: Introduction → [Theoretical/conceptual section, named to reflect the theory used] → [Context/background section, thematically named] → Research method / Methodology → [Findings sections, thematically named] → Concluding comments → [Notes] → References. "Concluding comments" is the standard final heading, not "Discussion and Conclusion." No hypothesis development sections appear; theory sections replace them entirely.

### Length
Word limit not publicly confirmed in accessible guidelines. Each Emerald journal sets its own; the freshdesk support page confirms AAAJ has published guidelines but does not specify the limit. Articles checked ran approximately 30 pages in published form.

### Abstract
ENFORCED. Mandatory Emerald structured abstract with labeled bold headings. Required headings observed: **Purpose**, **Design/methodology/approach**, **Findings**, **Originality/value**. Optional additional headings: Research limitations/implications, Practical implications, Social implications. Maximum 250 words total. The Purpose section must open with the formula "This paper aims to…" — Emerald house style, confirmed across all published abstracts. Keywords listed separately immediately after the abstract. Paper type field required (e.g., "Research paper"). This entire abstract structure is absent from FIELD_SKILL and constitutes a hard override.

### Citations
ENFORCED. Harvard author-date style. In-text: (Author, Year) or Author (Year). Multiple citations in one parenthetical separated by semicolons. Emerald reference-list format: Author, I. (Year), "Article title in quotation marks", *Journal Name in Italics*, Vol. X No. X, pp. X–X, doi: 10.xxxx/xxxx. This specific format (Vol./No./pp./doi. order and punctuation) differs from the loose "author-date" described in FIELD_SKILL.

### Headings
PRACTICE ONLY. Both articles use Arabic numeral section numbering: bold title-case for Level 1 (e.g., **1. Introduction**, **2. Methodology**), italic for Level 2 with decimal notation (e.g., *3.1 Macro-cluster 1 – accounting*, *5.2 Future research directions*). Dashes (–) used in subheading labels. FIELD_SKILL describes Roman numerals as the AAA norm and Arabic numerals as common in other outlets; AAAJ uses Arabic numerals throughout.

### Tables and Figures
PRACTICE ONLY. Tables carry a "**Source(s):**" attribution line below the table body (e.g., "Source(s): Table created by authors"). Not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL.

### Explicit Prohibitions
ENFORCED. Generative AI tools are not permitted for content creation. Use for English language improvement only is permitted. Confirmed via Emerald editorial policy.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register
PRACTICE ONLY. Both spot-checked articles use "this paper" for self-reference throughout body and abstract ("This paper aims to…", "this paper contributes to…") rather than first-person "we." Passive voice is the dominant construction in method sections ("a systematic literature review was conducted," "A methodological approach…was used," "each cluster was independently reviewed by two authors"). FIELD_SKILL identifies "we" as the dominant field convention. The abstract's Purpose heading mandates "This paper aims to…" by Emerald house style (ENFORCED), and this third-person pattern extends into the body in observed articles. Authors submitting work that uses "we" throughout should expect the structured abstract to override this in the Purpose line.

### Statistical Reporting
RELAXATION. Not applicable as the primary register. Neither spot-checked article contains regression tables, coefficient reporting, or hypothesis-linked results. FIELD_SKILL's conventions on statistical narration and economic significance apply only to quantitative submissions; AAAJ publishes these rarely. Qualitative evidence is presented as extended narrative, analytical tables, and direct quotation from primary sources (field transcripts, documents, interviews), consistent with the AOS interpretive tradition described in FIELD_SKILL but here as the mainstream, not the minority.

### Structural Deviations
RELAXATION. The canonical FIELD_SKILL structure (Introduction > Hypothesis Development > Data and Research Design > Results > Discussion and Conclusion) does not appear. Confirmed across all three articles examined:
- Theory or Theoretical framework appears as a standalone named section positioned after the introduction, not embedded in a hypothesis development section.
- Sections are given thematic names reflecting their content ("The birthing of the NDIS," "Standardisation of financial carbon accounting," "Form: accounting for an individualising and totalising NDIS").
- The closing section is typically labelled "Concluding comments" or "Discussion and concluding comments," not "Conclusion" or "Discussion and Conclusion."
- A "Notes" section (endnotes) may appear between the concluding section and References.
- Supplementary material appendices are supported.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
PRACTICE ONLY. Research questions (RQ1, RQ2, RQ3…) formatted as a bulleted or indented list within the introduction, labelled with italicised RQ labels, replace the hypothesis enumeration convention in FIELD_SKILL. Observed in both checked articles.

PRACTICE ONLY. Articles draw extensively on social theory frameworks rarely cited in the FIELD_SKILL baseline (Foucault, Bourdieu, institutional logics, field theory, critical dialogic accountability, promissory narratives). The theoretical framing section must integrate the chosen theory with prior accounting literature rather than summarising prior empirical findings.

## Dimensions With No Delta
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT (high in introduction and theory, lower in results)
- Limitations acknowledgment in concluding comments: FIELD DEFAULT
- Paraphrase over quotation (except for primary field-data): FIELD DEFAULT
- Double-blind review: FIELD DEFAULT

---

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting an unstructured abstract.** AAAJ requires the Emerald labeled format. Submitting a plain paragraph abstract will be rejected at desk review.
2. **Opening the Purpose line with "We aim to…" instead of "This paper aims to…"** Emerald house style mandates the third-person formula in the structured abstract.
3. **Framing the paper around hypothesis testing.** AAAJ's dominant paradigm is interpretive and critical. A paper structured around H1/H2 formal hypotheses without qualitative or theoretical grounding will likely be desk-rejected as out of scope.
4. **Using AI tools for drafting or content generation.** Explicitly prohibited by Emerald editorial policy. Disclosing AI use in content creation may result in rejection.
