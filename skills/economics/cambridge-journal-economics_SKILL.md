# Journal Writing Skill: Cambridge Journal of Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (for Cambridge Political Economy Society)
- **Scope:** Heterodox economics in the traditions of Marx, Keynes, Kalecki, Robinson, and Kaldor. Welcomes theoretical, applied, interdisciplinary, history of thought, and methodological work; strong emphasis on realistic analysis, critical perspectives, and policy relevance. Not a mainstream-methods journal.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/cje/pages/general_instructions
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Tony Lawson, "Social relations, social positioning theory and Marx," CJE 49(6):1215–1254, 2025; DOI: 10.1093/cje/beaf001; accessed via Cambridge repository PDF)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandated section order. Theory and history-of-thought papers follow a discursive structure with numbered decimal sections and subsections. No required sections beyond abstract and references. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for empirical papers. [ENFORCED]

### Length
Submissions should not normally exceed **10,000 words including tables and references**. The qualifier "normally" makes this a soft ceiling, but the journal flags overlong submissions at desk review. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured, maximum **250 words**. Must be followed by up to five keywords, three JEL classifications, and a correspondence address. No structured fields (background/objectives/methods). [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical style throughout (e.g., Gould, 1980; Marx, 1973, p. 163). Reference list uses hanging-indent, alphabetical, author-year format. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Arabic-numeral decimal sections and subsections (1., 2.1, 2.2, 3.1.1, etc.), displayed in italic on a line above the paragraph. No convention mandated for the final section title; "Summary and final comments" observed in practice. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
All copies must be in Journal style (authors are directed to consult a recent issue). No additional mandated rules extracted from guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise. [ENFORCED by reference to house style]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Mathematics and econometrics:** Use mathematics only when it does not compromise realistic analysis. Use econometrics only when essential for drawing valid inferences. Major steps in the argument must be made intelligible to a non-mathematical/econometric reader. Mathematical and econometric derivations must go in an appendix. Papers that do not follow this guidance are **rejected at preliminary desk review before peer review**. [ENFORCED]
- **Footnotes:** Must not be created using the automatic footnote feature of the word processor. Indicated by superscript numerals in the text. [ENFORCED]
- **AI disclosure:** Use of AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) must be disclosed in the cover letter to editors AND in the Methods or Acknowledgments section of the manuscript. AI tools do not qualify as authors. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Person

**Field norm:** First-person plural "we" is near-universal at ABS 4*/4 level, including sole-authored papers.

**CJE practice:** The sole-authored article examined uses first-person singular "I" throughout ("I am suggesting," "I shall suggest," "I cannot find," "I believe"). "We" does not appear as the authorial voice. This relaxation of the "we" norm appears consistent with the journal's heterodox, essayistic scholarly tradition.

**Classification:** RELAXATION (ABS 3; observed in practice, not stated in guidelines). Note: confirmed from one article in a history-of-thought special issue; authors of multi-authored empirical CJE papers should verify against recent comparable articles.

### Direct Quotation

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Direct quotation is extremely rare; paraphrase with attribution is the norm.

**CJE practice:** Extended block quotation from primary and secondary sources is integral to argumentation, particularly in history of thought and theoretical papers. The article examined uses approximately 30+ direct quotations, many as primary evidence for interpretive claims. This is not incidental but constitutive of the genre.

**Classification:** RELAXATION (ABS 3; PRACTICE ONLY — not stated in guidelines, but consistent with the journal's history-of-thought and critical-theory publishing scope).

### Footnote Use

**Guideline:** "Footnotes should be kept to a minimum."

**Practice:** The article examined contains 46 substantive footnotes across 42 pages. Many carry extended arguments, textual evidence, and qualifications that are integral to the analysis. This is a systematic gap between stated guideline and observed practice in longer theoretical papers.

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY for the minimum-use instruction; PRACTICE ONLY for the norm of extensive substantive footnotes. Authors should expect that a revision process may not enforce strict minimisation for theory and history papers.

### Introduction Rhetoric

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** ABS 4*/4 introductions follow a strict sequence: hook → research question → approach and data → quantitative results preview → enumerated contributions → roadmap. Contributions are explicitly enumerated; results are announced quantitatively.

**CJE practice:** The introduction is more essayistic. There is no enumerated contributions list, no quantitative results preview (theoretical papers have no quantities to announce), and the roadmap is brief and functional rather than formal ("I proceed as follows. In section 3, I examine..."). The paper's contribution is stated as a thesis, not as a numbered list. This relaxation is consistent with ABS 3 tier and the journal's heterodox scope.

**Classification:** RELAXATION (ABS 3; PRACTICE ONLY).

### Mathematics and Econometrics Policy

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Silent on any restriction; quantitative methods are standard at the 4*/4 level.

**CJE policy:** The journal imposes an explicit institutional constraint. Mathematics and econometrics must be used only when necessary, must not compromise realistic analysis, must be made accessible to non-mathematical readers in the main text, and must be relegated to an appendix. Violations trigger desk rejection before peer review. This is a hard constraint with no equivalent in the field baseline.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (stated in guidelines; confirmed by complete absence of formal content in the examined article; desk-rejection mechanism confirms enforcement).

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting math-heavy work without accessibility provisions.** The journal's mathematics/econometrics policy is enforced at desk stage. A paper that treats econometric or formal content as self-sufficient — without translating the core argument into discursive prose — will be rejected before reaching peer review. If math is necessary, technical derivations go in an appendix, and the main text must carry the argument in plain language.

2. **Writing "we" in a sole-authored paper.** Observed practice uses "I." Defaulting to the field-wide "we" norm may read as incongruous at CJE, though this is a soft stylistic matter rather than a hard rule.

3. **Treating the 10,000-word limit as firm.** The "not normally exceed" framing is genuine — special issues and longer theoretical papers appear to exceed it — but submitting a long paper without a strong case for its length is a risk. Keep well under 10,000 words unless the work genuinely requires more.

4. **Submitting mainstream-methods work without connecting it to the journal's heterodox and critical framing.** The journal's scope is explicitly heterodox; editors assess whether submissions engage with its traditions (Marx, Keynes, Kalecki, institutional, post-Keynesian, etc.). A paper that reads as a standard applied econometrics exercise with no engagement with critical perspectives is unlikely to pass preliminary review regardless of its technical quality.
