# Journal Writing Skill: Financial Accountability & Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell
- **Scope:** Financial accountability, accounting, performance management, and financial and resource management in public sector, nonprofit, and government organizations. Corporate/private sector accounting is outside scope.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14680408/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 full text (Manes Rossi et al. 2024, DOI: 10.1111/faam.12424); 1 metadata-only (Funck 2025, DOI: 10.1111/faam.12420)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section ordering. Mandatory end-matter sections not in FIELD_SKILL: standalone **Conflicts of Interest** declaration and **Data Availability Statement** are required for all articles. [ENFORCED]

### Length
**8,000 words maximum**, inclusive of the reference list and space equivalence for illustrations. "Occasionally longer, if justifiable." This is substantially shorter than typical ABS 4*/4 accounting journals. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No structured subheadings (Purpose/Design/Findings/Originality). No explicit word limit found in guidelines, but observed abstracts are moderate length (~150–250 words). [ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

### Citations
Author-date in text: (Author Year) or Author (Year). Reference list follows Chicago author-date format:

> Last, F., and F. Last. Year. "Article Title." *Journal Name* Vol, no. Issue: Page–Page.

Note the "no." abbreviation for issue number and colon before page range — not APA or Harvard style. [ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

### Headings
Arabic numerals + pipe separator + title case: **"1 | Introduction"**, **"2 | Data and Methods"**, etc. Not Roman numerals, not ALL CAPS. Subsections follow the same style. [PRACTICE ONLY — observed in article; not specified in accessible guidelines]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Word count includes space equivalence for illustrations.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Private/corporate sector topics are outside scope — submissions should concern government, public services, or nonprofit organizations.
- No submission fees required (submission is free).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Scope and Phenomena

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL covers the full accounting field including corporate financial reporting, auditing, and management accounting in private firms.

**This journal:** Exclusively public sector, nonprofit, and government. All research questions, data, and contexts must fall within this domain. Theoretical frameworks are correspondingly public-sector-specific: New Public Management (NPM), post-NPM, public value theory, stewardship theory in public/nonprofit contexts, legitimacy theory, and institutional theory applied to public organizations feature prominently. Frameworks common in corporate accounting (signaling theory applied to capital markets, contracting theory, earnings management) are absent.

**Classification:** ENFORCED — scope is formally defined.

### Methodological Pluralism

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL baseline reflects a quantitative/archival dominant tradition. In AOS, qualitative/interpretive work is a distinct minority strand. Other ABS 4*/4 accounting journals are predominantly quantitative.

**This journal:** Wide-ranging methods are explicitly welcomed and equally common in practice: case studies, historical analysis, structured literature reviews, interpretive fieldwork, content analysis, and statistical/quantitative models all appear in the same volume alongside one another. Qualitative and interpretive work is mainstream, not a minority tradition. The hypothesis-testing structure described in FIELD_SKILL is common for quantitative articles but absent for qualitative ones — and qualitative articles at FAM do not carry the apologetic framing sometimes found in hybrid journals.

**Classification:** RELAXATION — ABS 3 journal relaxes the quantitative-dominant norm of the field baseline.

### Heading Format

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that Roman numerals + ALL CAPS (AAA journals) or Arabic numerals + title case are both found across the field; no specific separator convention is described.

**This journal:** The specific format "**N | Heading Title**" (pipe separator between number and heading text, title case) is observed in published articles. Example: "3 | Embedding Sustainability Into Operations".

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY — observed in article; guidelines page was inaccessible for confirmation.

### Word Limit

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify word limits, which vary by journal.

**This journal:** 8,000 words inclusive of references and illustration space equivalence. This is notably compact relative to ABS 4* accounting journals. Contributions typically must be tightly scoped; papers that include extensive robustness checks, large variable definition appendices, or lengthy hypothesis development sections common in top-tier archival accounting journals will not fit.

**Classification:** ENFORCED.

### Mandatory End-Matter Statements

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that data availability and ethics statements are "increasingly required" but treats them as optional/emerging.

**This journal:** Standalone **Conflicts of Interest** and **Data Availability Statement** sections are required for all articles and appear as named sections after the main text and before the reference list. Authors are expected to archive data in a public repository. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Word limit violation.** Authors accustomed to ABS 4* journals (which routinely publish 15,000–20,000 word manuscripts) will over-write. References count toward the 8,000-word limit — keep the reference list lean and robustness checks minimal or omit them.

2. **Wrong domain.** Submitting research on corporate financial reporting, capital markets, or private-sector management accounting. The journal does not publish this work regardless of quality.

3. **Hypothesis-testing structure imposed on qualitative papers.** Qualitative, case study, and historical papers do not need formal hypotheses or H1/H2 structure. The journal is methodologically pluralist; forcing a positivist structure onto interpretive work will read as inappropriate.

4. **Neglecting mandatory end-matter.** Missing the standalone Conflicts of Interest statement or Data Availability Statement will require revision at desk-check. Both are mandatory, not optional.
