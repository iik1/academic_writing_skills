- **Scope:** Theoretical and empirical research in financial economics, covering capital markets, financial institutions, corporate finance, corporate governance, and organizational economics.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No required section order beyond standard academic structure is specified. CRediT author contribution statements are required by Elsevier and appear after the main text.

### Length
No explicit word or page limits are stated in the guidelines. Published articles in recent issues run approximately 19–28 pages.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No explicit word limit stated in guidelines. Observed abstracts in recent issues are approximately 120–180 words — toward the lower end of the FIELD_SKILL 150–250 word norm. **Label: PRACTICE ONLY** (shorter abstracts typical in practice; no guideline specification).

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical format confirmed in both guidelines and articles (e.g., "Friedman and Savage (1948)"). **FIELD DEFAULT.**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Significance notation (* p<0.10, ** p<0.05, *** p<0.01) is standard.

### Submission Fee
**US$850** for unsolicited new manuscripts (before VAT). A **$400 partial refund** applies to desk-rejected papers, unless the paper was previously rejected by JFE or the author has accumulated four desk rejections within 12 months. The full fee is **refunded if the editorial decision takes more than 120 days**. This fee has no analog in FIELD_SKILL. **Label: ENFORCED.**

### Generative AI Declaration
Authors who used generative AI tools must include a dedicated declaration section titled (e.g.) "Declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing process," placed **before the References section**. Authors remain fully accountable for accuracy and originality; AI cannot substitute for human authorship. If no AI was used, no section is needed. **Label: ENFORCED.**

### Inclusive Language
Elsevier policy requires inclusive language throughout: avoid descriptors implying superiority based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability. Use plural nouns as default to avoid gendered pronouns. Do not make assumptions about reader beliefs. **Label: GUIDELINE ONLY** (stated in guidelines; compliance not independently confirmed in articles accessed).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI-generated references are explicitly flagged as unreliable ("can be incorrect or fabricated"); authors must verify all references.
- Authorship changes (additions, removals, reordering) are only permitted before acceptance and require editor approval.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Abstract Length (Practice)
**Field norm:** 150–250 words. **JFE practice:** Observed abstracts cluster around 120–180 words, somewhat shorter than the field norm. **Label: PRACTICE ONLY.** Do not pad the abstract; concise is preferred.

### Submission Fee Structure
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address submission fees. **JFE:** Mandatory US$850 submission fee with partial refund rules and a 120-day turnaround guarantee. This is a hard operational constraint affecting when and how to submit, not a writing style issue, but authors must be aware before submitting. **Label: ENFORCED.**

### Generative AI Section
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address AI declarations. **JFE (Elsevier policy):** A named disclosure section before References is mandatory if AI tools were used in writing or research. This is a structural addition not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. **Label: ENFORCED.**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting without accounting for the fee structure.** The $850 fee applies to all new unsolicited submissions. Revise-and-resubmit invitations from prior desk rejections at JFE affect refund eligibility. Budget and track submission history accordingly.
2. **Omitting the AI declaration.** If any AI writing or research assistance was used, the declaration section before References is mandatory. Omitting it is a compliance failure under Elsevier policy.
3. **Abstract padding.** JFE abstracts in practice run shorter (~120–150 words) than the broader field norm. A dense, precise abstract is preferred over a padded one that strains to reach 250 words.
4. **Authorship changes post-submission.** Adding or removing authors after submission requires explicit editor approval and is only permitted before acceptance. Confirm authorship before submitting.
