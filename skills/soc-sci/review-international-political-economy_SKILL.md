# Journal Writing Skill: Review of International Political Economy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Routledge / Taylor & Francis
- **Scope:** Covers international political economy from a plurality of perspectives: international trade and finance, production and consumption, global governance and regulation. Articles typically bridge political science and economics, engaging with power, state-firm relations, and global structures.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/rrip20/about-this-journal
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Rikap 2024, DOI 10.1080/09692290.2024.2365757; Doppen, Calcara & De Bièvre 2024, DOI 10.1080/09692290.2024.2368601)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No fixed mandatory section sequence specified in guidelines. Practice follows standard SOC SCI structure. End-matter sections required in this order: Notes → Acknowledgements → Disclosure statement → Funding → Notes on contributor → References.

### Length
**12,000 words maximum** for research articles. [ENFORCED — stated in guidelines; articles in volume 31(6) run 25–29 pages consistent with this limit]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose, labeled "ABSTRACT" in bold small caps. **Maximum 250 words.** No structured subheadings. Followed immediately by a labeled "KEYWORDS" block (see Citations section). [ENFORCED]

### Keywords
A labeled "KEYWORDS" block is required immediately after the abstract, with terms separated by semicolons. Minimum 3 keywords; observed practice is 5–6 terms. [ENFORCED — confirmed in both articles]

### Citations
**APA author-date format throughout.** In-text: `(Smith, 2020)` or `(Smith & Jones, 2020)` with comma between author and year. Multiple citations separated by semicolons within parentheses. Reference list: APA format with hanging indent; journal names italicised; DOIs included where available. [ENFORCED — confirmed in both articles]

### Headings
Main section headings: unnumbered, bold. Subsection headings: unnumbered, bold italic. No numerical prefixes at any level. [ENFORCED by practice — consistent across both articles]

### Tables and Figures
Source attribution line required beneath each table and figure (e.g., "Source: Author's analysis from Crunchbase."). No specific formatting constraints beyond standard T&F typesetting.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None identified in accessible guidelines beyond standard T&F policies (originality, not under review elsewhere, ethical compliance, conflict of interest disclosure required).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where RIPE deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Style (Reference Format)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL identifies author-date parenthetical as dominant but does not specify APA vs. other formats.
**RIPE practice:** APA format is specifically required — parenthetical `(Author, Year)` with comma, and APA reference list with DOIs. This rules out Chicago author-date or footnote-based citation formats common in adjacent political science outlets.
**Label:** ENFORCED

### Keywords
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify keyword requirements.
**RIPE practice:** A labeled "KEYWORDS" block (minimum 3 terms, semicolon-separated) is required immediately after the abstract. Both articles carry this block. Omitting it is a submission error.
**Label:** ENFORCED

### Numbered Endnotes
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify footnote vs. endnote placement.
**RIPE practice:** Substantive content notes appear as a numbered "Notes" section at the end of the article, placed before References — not as page-bottom footnotes. Superscript numbers appear in the text; note content is collected at the end. Both articles follow this pattern.
**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Closing Section Label
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL expects "Discussion," "Conclusion," or a merged closing section.
**RIPE practice:** "Final remarks" observed as an accepted alternative label (Rikap 2024); "Conclusions" also observed (Doppen et al. 2024). Neither carries a separate Discussion section — interpretation and synthesis are woven into a single closing section. This is a permitted variation per FIELD_SKILL but the merged Discussion/Conclusion with non-standard label is characteristic.
**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Section Road-Map in Introduction
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes CARS structure and sequential framing but does not specify road-map paragraphs.
**RIPE practice:** Both articles close the introduction with an explicit paragraph signposting subsequent sections by name (e.g., "Section 'X' draws on... Section 'Y' presents..."). This appears to be a strong norm in the journal.
**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing keyword block:** RIPE requires a labeled "KEYWORDS" line after the abstract; omitting it or embedding keywords within the abstract text is a submission error.
2. **Non-APA citation format:** Political science authors accustomed to Chicago footnotes or Chicago author-date must convert fully to APA parenthetical format, including the comma between author and year and APA reference list style.
3. **Abstract or manuscript overlength:** The 250-word abstract cap and 12,000-word article limit are both enforced; IPE papers with extensive theoretical framing and empirical detail regularly require tight editing to comply.
4. **Page-bottom footnotes:** RIPE collects content notes in an end-of-article "Notes" section rather than as page-bottom footnotes; submitting in a format with page-bottom footnotes may require reformatting at revision stage.
