# Journal Writing Skill: Manufacturing & Service Operations Management (M&SOM)

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** INFORMS (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences)
- **Scope:** Research on the management of operations in manufacturing and service sectors; covers analytical modeling, empirical analysis, and behavioral studies. The journal has a pronounced analytical/modeling orientation relative to ABS 4* peers.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/msom/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (An, Li, Moseley & Ravi, "The Nonstationary Newsvendor with (and Without) Predictions," M&SOM Articles in Advance, 2025; DOI 10.1287/msom.2024.1168)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Section names and ordering are not prescribed; paper type determines structure.

### Length
**32 pages maximum** (all-inclusive: body, references, tables, graphs, appendices) when typeset using the INFORMS LaTeX or Word style files. Submissions must use double spacing defined as ≤33 lines per page, 1-inch margins on all sides, single column, 11-point font. — **ENFORCED**

**Online supplement:** Restricted to ≤16 manuscript pages for all new submissions. Proofs, additional analyses, and robustness tables should be placed here when they would push the main manuscript over the limit. — **ENFORCED**

### Abstract
**Structured abstract required, ≤300 words.** Three labeled fields are mandatory:

1. **Problem definition:** What is your research problem?
2. **Methodology/results:** What is the research method and the key findings?
3. **Managerial implications:** How can academics, managers, or decision makers benefit?

These field labels appear in bold inline at the start of each part. The abstract is a single paragraph with the three parts running together, not presented as a bulleted list. — **ENFORCED** (confirmed in published articles)

This requirement is more prescriptive than the FIELD_SKILL baseline, which leaves abstract format as a journal-specific matter.

### Citations
INFORMS author-year style throughout. In-text format: author surname(s) + year, no comma between name and year — e.g., `(Levi et al. 2015)` or `Levi et al. (2015)`. Reference list is alphabetical by first author surname; journal names are abbreviated per INFORMS house style (e.g., *Management Sci.*, *Oper. Res.*, *Math. Oper. Res.*). Author initials follow last name with no comma between co-authors. — **ENFORCED**

The in-text convention matches the FIELD_SKILL "author-date (APA-adjacent)" description; the reference list format (abbreviated journal names, INFORMS house style) is publisher-specific and not implied by the FIELD_SKILL description.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Numbered section and subsection headings are standard (e.g., §1.1, §2.3); used for both major and minor divisions.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables and figures are embedded in the manuscript for submission; color figures are accepted (labeled "Color online" in published output).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Bottom-of-page footnotes are prohibited.** The guidelines state that subsidiary material should be incorporated into the main text in parentheses. Note: published analytical modeling articles routinely use numbered endnotes (collected at end of paper before references); the prohibition applies to bottom-of-page placement. — **GUIDELINE ONLY** for endnotes (see Observed Deltas below)
- Systematic automated downloading is prohibited per INFORMS terms.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structured Abstract as a Writing Requirement
**Field norm:** Whether a structured abstract is required is a journal-specific matter not covered by FIELD_SKILL.

**M&SOM practice:** The three-part structured abstract (Problem definition / Methodology/results / Managerial implications) is a hard formatting requirement, not merely a stylistic preference. The Managerial implications field in the abstract is effectively mandatory even for highly analytical papers where managerial takeaways may be modest. In practice, the abstract's Managerial implications entry is sometimes brief and fairly general for theoretical modeling papers. — **ENFORCED**

### Footnotes vs. Endnotes
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address footnote policy.

**M&SOM guideline:** Prohibits footnotes; subsidiary material should be parenthetical in text.

**M&SOM practice:** Published analytical and modeling papers use numbered endnotes (end-of-paper, before References) extensively — the spot-checked article contained 17 such endnotes carrying substantive caveats, proofs, and comparisons. The prohibition appears to apply specifically to bottom-of-page footnotes; endnotes collected at the paper's end are accepted in practice. Authors submitting with endnotes rather than parenthetical insertions should expect no desk rejection on this basis in modeling papers. — **GUIDELINE ONLY** (guideline prohibits footnotes; endnotes appear in published articles)

### Managerial Implications: Abstract vs. Body
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL treats a dedicated managerial implications subsection in the body as near-universal across the OPS&TECH field.

**M&SOM practice:** In analytical and modeling papers, managerial implications are frequently addressed in the structured abstract's Managerial implications field and in the Conclusion, without a separately headed "Managerial Implications" subsection in the body. The abstract field substitutes for (or supplements) the body section convention. For empirical papers this delta may not apply. — **PRACTICE ONLY** (observed in analytical paper; may vary by paper type)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting an unstructured abstract.** The three-part structured abstract is not optional. Omitting any of the three labeled fields (Problem definition, Methodology/results, Managerial implications) or exceeding 300 words is likely to draw an immediate revision request or desk rejection.

2. **Exceeding the 32-page limit before appending the supplement.** The page cap is all-inclusive (references, tables, appendices). Material that would push the manuscript over 32 pages must go into the online supplement, which itself is capped at 16 pages.

3. **Writing a thin Managerial implications entry.** The structured abstract requires an explicit managerial implications field even for highly theoretical or analytical papers. A vague one-sentence entry signals a mismatch with journal scope; reviewers also expect managerial relevance to be developed somewhere in the paper's body (Conclusion at minimum).

4. **Using bottom-of-page footnotes.** Even if the content belongs in an endnote, bottom-of-page footnotes are prohibited. Collect notes at the end of the paper before the reference list, or incorporate the material parenthetically in text.
