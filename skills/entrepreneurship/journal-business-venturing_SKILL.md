## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are enforceable requirements from the journal's official guidelines.

### Structure

**GUIDELINE ONLY:** The journal mentions standard sections (Abstract, Keywords, Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results, Conclusions, Tables/Figures) but does not mandate a specific section order or label set.

Theory sections should "lay the foundation for further work by extending the background provided in the introduction."

No explicit IMRaD enforcement found in guidelines.

### Length

**ENFORCED:** Maximum 35 double-spaced pages in 12pt font, including title page, abstract, tables, and figures. References excluded from page count.

### Abstract

**ENFORCED:** 100 words or less. Format (structured vs. unstructured) not specified in guidelines.

**Guideline note:** Avoid non-standard abbreviations; define essential abbreviations at first use within the abstract.

### Executive Summary

**ENFORCED:** Not required at initial submission. Required once a revision is requested.

**Content requirements (from search results):** Should concisely indicate:
- The gap in the literature
- Why the gap is important
- How the paper addresses it
- Contributions to theory and practice

Word limit not specified.

### Highlights

**ENFORCED:** Mandatory at submission. Short collection of bullet points capturing:
- Novel results
- New methods used during the study

Increases article discoverability via search engines. Submit as separate editable file with "highlights" in filename.

### Citations

**ENFORCED:** elsarticle-num style (numbered references in square brackets).

- References numbered in order of appearance
- Author names may appear in text, but reference number must always be provided
- DOIs recommended
- Journal names abbreviated per LTWA (List of Title Word Abbreviations)
- At submission, any consistent style accepted; journal will apply house style at proof stage

### Headings

**Not specified** in accessible guidelines.

### Tables and Figures

**ENFORCED:** Figures and images generated using AI must be disclosed.

Submit all content as editable source files (.doc/.docx for Word, .tex for LaTeX). PDFs not acceptable.

**GUIDELINE ONLY:** Single-column layout for Word; double-column permitted for LaTeX.

### Language and Tone

**ENFORCED:** Use inclusive language that:
- Conveys respect to all people
- Avoids bias, stereotypes, slang
- Avoids gendered pronouns; use plural nouns as default
- Free from assumptions about age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, disability, health condition

### Explicit Prohibitions

**ENFORCED:**
- AI tools "must never be used as a substitute for human critical thinking"
- AI tools cannot be listed as authors or co-authors
- Authorship changes generally prohibited after acceptance
- Figures/images generated by AI without disclosure (violates transparency requirement)

## Additional Requirements (Author Guidelines)

### Submission Materials

**Title page (separate):**
- Full author names, affiliations
- City, state/province, zip code, country
- Phone and email for all authors
- No author-identifying information elsewhere in manuscript (blind review)

**ENFORCED:** Manuscripts violating anonymity returned without review.

### Author Contributions

**ENFORCED:** Corresponding author must acknowledge co-author contributions using CRediT (Contributor Roles Taxonomy).

### Data Transparency

**ENFORCED:** Authors must either:
- Deposit research data in a relevant repository and cite/link to it, OR
- Provide a statement explaining why data cannot be shared

### Generative AI Disclosure

**ENFORCED:** Mandatory declaration of generative AI use at submission (if applicable).

### Competing Interests

**ENFORCED:** Declaration required.

## Common Pitfalls

Based on guideline emphasis and submission requirements:

1. **Submitting without highlights:** Highlights are mandatory, not optional. Missing highlights file will delay review.

2. **Exceeding 100-word abstract limit:** Hard cap enforced. Abstracts >100 words likely flagged at editorial triage.

3. **Including author-identifying information outside title page:** Manuscripts violating blind review anonymity are returned without review. Check acknowledgments, file metadata, and examples for self-identifying references.

4. **Submitting non-editable files:** PDFs not accepted. Provide .doc/.docx or .tex source files with editable tables/figures.

5. **Omitting data statement:** Required even if data cannot be shared (must explain why).

6. **Undisclosed AI use:** Generative AI use in figures, text generation, or analysis must be declared at submission.
