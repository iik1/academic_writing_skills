# Journal Writing Skill: Transportation Science

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** INFORMS (PubsOnLine)
- **Scope:** Theoretical and applied research on the planning, design, and operations of transportation and logistics systems; covers routing, network design, traffic flow, scheduling, and freight/passenger system optimisation.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/trsc/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1287/trsc.2024.0750 — Celik et al., "Exact Two-Step Benders Decomposition for the TWATSP"; 10.1287/trsc.2023.0241 — Scroccaro et al., "Inverse Optimization for Routing Problems")

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No prescribed section sequence beyond standard OR/MS structure. Authors must provide a brief area-fit statement and contribution summary at submission (administrative, not manuscript-internal).

### Length
**35 pages maximum** for the main manuscript (including all references, tables, and figures), plus **15 pages maximum** for appendices, when formatted in journal style. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose, **300 words maximum**. No labeled fields. Must function as an index: should summarise principal results and conclusions, not merely describe the paper's purpose. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-year format: in-text as (Author Year) or Author (Year). References listed **alphabetically by author name** at end of paper. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Color figures publish online in color but are **converted to black and white for print** unless authors agree to pay additional color-printing costs. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
**Footnotes are prohibited.** Any subsidiary material that would otherwise appear in a footnote must be incorporated into the main text, possibly in parentheses or brackets. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Footnote Policy
**Field norm:** Not explicitly addressed; footnotes are uncommon but not categorically banned.  
**This journal:** Explicitly and categorically prohibited. All subsidiary material must be integrated into the running text.  
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Page and Appendix Limits
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify length limits.  
**This journal:** 35 pages (main body including references/tables/figures) + 15 pages (appendices), in journal format. These are hard submission caps, not targets.  
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a word limit.  
**This journal:** 300 words maximum; must summarise principal results and conclusions (not just state the problem and approach).  
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Color Figure Handling
**Field norm:** Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL.  
**This journal:** Online publication retains color; print is black and white by default. Authors must plan figures to be legible in grayscale.  
**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding the page budget.** The 35-page cap covers *all* references, tables, and figures in the main body. Authors accustomed to journals with separate reference limits will run over.
2. **Using footnotes.** Footnotes are categorically banned; reviewers may flag even a single one. Move all subsidiary remarks into parenthetical text or drop them.
3. **Figures that fail in grayscale.** Figures relying on color to distinguish lines or regions will be unreadable in the print version. Use line style, marker shape, or pattern fills as primary differentiators.
4. **Overlong abstracts.** The abstract must both index the paper *and* summarise principal results within 300 words. Spending the word budget on background motivation at the expense of findings is a common failure mode.
