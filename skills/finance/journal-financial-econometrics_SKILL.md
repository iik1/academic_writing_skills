# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Econometrics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (Society of Financial Econometrics — SoFiE)
- **Scope:** Econometric methodology and empirical methods for financial markets; focuses on statistical/econometric innovation with financial applications rather than causal identification in the style of mainstream empirical finance.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/jfec/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1093/jjfinec/nbag002; 10.1093/jjfinec/nbag003)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Required submission order: title page → abstract → text → figure legends → references → notes (endnotes) → tables → figures. Note that endnotes follow references in the submission file. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section content otherwise.

### Length
Maximum 40 double-spaced typewritten pages. No explicit word count stated. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; **100 words maximum**. This is substantially shorter than the field baseline of 150–250 words. Keywords: 2–6 required. JEL classification codes recommended but not required. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date parenthetical format per Chicago Manual of Style, 15th edition. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. All publicly available datasets must be cited with DOI or unique identifier as a full reference entry — this is an additional requirement beyond typical field practice. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (Arabic numerals).

### Tables and Figures
- Tables: no vertical rules; one table per page in submission; descriptive title required; self-explanatory with footnotes. [ENFORCED]
- Figures: minimum 300 dpi at final size; preferred formats TIFF/EPS (images) or Word/Excel/PowerPoint (graphs). [ENFORCED]
- **Alt text required** for every figure for accessibility, submitted under figure legends. This requirement is absent from FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Simultaneous submission to another journal is prohibited.
- Manuscripts screened for image manipulation and papermill activity.
- Footnotes must NOT be placed at the bottom of cited pages; they must appear as endnotes on a separate page after the text. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** Statistical significance reported with stars and economic magnitude translated into interpretable terms (percentage of mean, basis points, etc.).

**This journal:** The two spot-checked articles are methodological papers developing estimators and asymptotic theory. Results are reported through convergence theorems, asymptotic distributions, and simulation MSE/coverage tables rather than regression coefficient tables with significance stars. Economic magnitude discussion is minimal or absent; the benchmark for "significance" is statistical validity of the estimation procedure, not economic effect size. This is a structural feature of the journal's methodological orientation, not a relaxation of rigor.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (RELAXATION relative to FIELD_SKILL economic-magnitude norm, inherent to the journal's methodological scope)

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** Standard empirical paper structure (Introduction → Data → Empirical Analysis → Conclusion).

**This journal:** Papers frequently follow a theory-first structure: Introduction → [Model/Methodology] → [Asymptotic Theory / Simulation Study] → [Empirical Application] → Conclusion. A "Mathematical Annex" or "Supplemental Material" section for proofs is common, appearing after the conclusion. The empirical application, when present, often occupies a single section and is positioned as validation rather than the main contribution.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
1. **Endnotes required:** Footnotes must be collected as endnotes on a separate page after the text, not placed at the foot of each page. Confirmed in articles (superscript numbering linking to a Footnotes/Notes section). [ENFORCED]

2. **Funding section:** A separate "Funding" section is required immediately before the Acknowledgments, using standardized format: "This work was supported by [full agency name] [grant number xxxx]." This is a structural requirement absent from FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED]

3. **Data/code replication package:** For empirical papers, submission of source data and code files is a condition of acceptance (not merely encouraged). Authors must request exemption explicitly in the cover letter. [ENFORCED]

4. **Submission fee:** $65 USD required at submission; waived for SoFiE members. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Abstract over 100 words.** The 100-word cap is roughly half the field baseline. Abstracts drafted to field norms will need aggressive cuts before submission.
2. **Footnotes at page bottom.** Finance and econometrics papers routinely use page-bottom footnotes; this journal requires endnotes. Submitting with page-bottom footnotes violates the formatting guidelines.
3. **Missing funding section.** The required "Funding" section (with exact format) before acknowledgments is easy to overlook; omitting it or merging it into acknowledgments does not comply.
4. **Treating the empirical application as the primary contribution.** The journal's scope is econometric methodology; reviewers expect the estimator, test, or model to be the central contribution, with the empirical section serving as illustration or validation. Papers structured around an empirical question with methodology as a supporting tool may face desk rejection.
