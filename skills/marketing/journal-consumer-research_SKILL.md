## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
**Label: ENFORCED**

Required sections in order:
1. Manuscript Title
2. Consumer Relevance and Contribution Statement (new submissions only, 300 words maximum)
3. Abstract and Keywords
4. Main Text (with tables/figures embedded)
5. Appendixes (if applicable)
6. References

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** The Consumer Relevance and Contribution Statement is a journal-specific requirement not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. It must appear before the abstract and articulate both consumer relevance and theoretical contribution in 300 words maximum. This section is excluded from the manuscript file during peer review.

Author note and data collection statements are submitted separately through the system, not included in the manuscript file.

### Length
**Label: ENFORCED**

- Maximum 60 double-spaced pages for entire document (includes all sections, tables, figures, appendixes)
- Abstract: maximum 200 words
- Consumer Relevance and Contribution Statement: maximum 300 words
- Keywords: 3-6 required
- Web appendix does not count toward page limit

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** FIELD_SKILL indicates abstracts "typically 150–250 words"; JCR enforces a hard maximum of 200 words. The 60-page limit is strict ("cannot exceed 60-page limit under any circumstance").

### Abstract
**Label: ENFORCED**

- Unstructured
- Maximum 200 words (hard limit)
- Must address: Topic/Issue, Approach/Methods, Results/Findings, Implications/Conclusions
- No citations, links, tables, or figures permitted
- Cannot include information outside the article
- Must be substantive enough for reviewers to assess expertise

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** Narrower word limit (200 vs 150-250). Explicit prohibition on citations in abstract is stated more forcefully than FIELD_SKILL.

### Citations
**Label: ENFORCED**

- Standard APA style (consistent with FIELD_SKILL)
- "There must be a reference for every citation and a citation for every reference"
- Double-spaced with half-inch hanging indent
- Author names retained in references (not anonymized)

No delta from FIELD_SKILL.

### Headings
**Label: ENFORCED**

**No numbered headings permitted.** Three hierarchical levels allowed:

- **Primary:** ALL-CAPS, centered, bold, extra double space before/after
- **Secondary:** Title Case, flushed left, extra double space before/after
- **Tertiary:** Title Case, indented, italicized, period-punctuated, extra double space before

Title case follows Chicago Manual of Style: capitalize first/last words and major words; lowercase articles, coordinating conjunctions, prepositions ≤4 letters.

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify numbered vs unnumbered headings. JCR explicitly prohibits numbered headings and prescribes specific formatting for each hierarchy level.

### Tables and Figures
**Label: ENFORCED**

- Must be embedded in main text at first mention, not placed at end or in separate files
- Sequential numbering (TABLE 1, FIGURE 1)
- Short descriptive titles required
- In-text references by number only; **prohibits directional references** ("above," "below," "preceding," "following")
- Figures: sans serif typefaces only; 95% confidence intervals required if error bars included
- High-resolution images not required during review (but required post-acceptance)

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify embedded vs end-of-document placement. JCR requires embedding and explicitly prohibits directional references, which is unusual and stricter than field norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
**Label: ENFORCED**

- **No numbered headings**
- **No endnotes** (cannot be used under any circumstance)
- **Footnotes strongly discouraged**
- No page numbers, running heads, headers, footers in manuscript
- No underlining
- No line numbering
- No author identification anywhere in manuscript during review (includes institutional names, acknowledgments, funding)
- **No "we found that..." phrasing** — guidelines state "Avoid 'we found that...' phrasing; use direct statements"
- No separate title page
- Cannot exceed 60-page limit

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** The prohibition on "we found that..." directly contradicts FIELD_SKILL, which lists "We find that..." and "Our results show that..." as standard result-reporting phrases. This is a major stylistic divergence. The endnote prohibition and footnote discouragement are also stricter than FIELD_SKILL, which mentions footnotes carry methodological details without discouraging them.

### Formatting
**Label: ENFORCED**

- File format: Word, Rich Text, or PDF (LaTeX users must convert to PDF)
- Maximum 40 MB file size
- US letter (8.5″ × 11″), one-inch margins
- Times New Roman 12-point, single-column, portrait orientation
- Double-spaced main text, left-justified
- No special characters in file names

Follows FIELD_SKILL norms for academic formatting.

### Data and Transparency
**Label: ENFORCED**

- Data collection statement required for all submissions (submitted via system Step 6, not in manuscript)
- Data/materials link or exemption request required for invited revisions; encouraged for new submissions
- Pre-registration links and OSF links encouraged but not required
- All external links must be completely anonymized during review

**Delta from FIELD_SKILL:** Data collection statement is a universal requirement not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL.

### Consumer Relevance and Contribution Statement

**ENFORCED** (journal-specific requirement)

Required for all new submissions:
- Separate section before the abstract
- Maximum 300 words
- Must articulate both consumer relevance and theoretical contribution
- Excluded from manuscript file during peer review (submitted separately in system)
- Appears in final published version

**FIELD_SKILL coverage:** Not mentioned. This is a journal-specific innovation not standard across MKT field.

**Classification rationale:** Explicitly required by submission system; designated section with word limit and specific content mandate. Classified as ENFORCED despite inability to verify in published articles.

### Heading Hierarchy and Numbering

**ENFORCED**

**Prohibition on numbered headings** (e.g., no "1. Introduction", "2.1 Theoretical Framework")

Three-level hierarchy with prescribed formatting:
- Primary: ALL-CAPS, centered, bold
- Secondary: Title Case, left-aligned
- Tertiary: Title Case, indented, italicized, period-punctuated

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify numbered vs unnumbered. FIELD_SKILL examples show section names without numbers but does not explicitly prohibit numbering.

**JCR deviation:** Explicit prohibition on numbered headings.

**Classification rationale:** Clear guideline mandate. Standard practice across OUP social science journals. Classified as ENFORCED.

### Footnotes and Endnotes

**ENFORCED**

- Endnotes: **prohibited entirely** ("cannot be used")
- Footnotes: **strongly discouraged** (guideline states "footnotes strongly discouraged")

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** "Footnotes carry methodological details, alternative analyses, or literature qualifications that would interrupt the main argument." FIELD_SKILL treats footnotes as a standard feature without discouragement.

**JCR deviation:** Much stricter stance against both endnotes and footnotes.

**Classification rationale:** Absolute prohibition on endnotes; strong editorial guidance against footnotes. Classified as ENFORCED.

### Table and Figure Placement

**ENFORCED**

Tables and figures must be **embedded in main text** at first mention, not collected at end of manuscript.

Directional references prohibited: cannot use "above," "below," "preceding," "following" to refer to tables/figures; must use number references only ("Table 1 shows...").

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify embedded vs end-of-document placement. Field practice varies.

**JCR deviation:** Requires embedding and prohibits directional language.

**Classification rationale:** Explicit guideline requirement for submission formatting. Classified as ENFORCED for submission phase.

## Common Pitfalls

Based on explicit guideline warnings and prohibitions:

1. **Exceeding the 60-page limit** — Guidelines state this "cannot" happen "under any circumstance." Authors must use web appendix for supplementary material. Including too many studies or extensive robustness checks in main text risks rejection on length grounds alone.

2. **Author identification during review** — Accidentally including institutional names, acknowledgments, or insufficiently anonymized citations/URLs. Guidelines require generic descriptive terms ("a large Midwestern university") and checking file properties for hidden metadata.

3. **Using "we found that..." phrasing** — Guideline explicitly flags this construction as undesirable. Authors accustomed to standard MKT phrasing ("We find that...") must adapt to direct statement style if following guidelines literally.

4. **Placing tables/figures at end of manuscript** — Submission system may flag or reject manuscripts with tables/figures not embedded at first mention in text. This differs from some journals that accept end-of-document placement for review.

5. **Forgetting the Consumer Relevance and Contribution Statement** — New submissions require this 300-word section. Omitting it or exceeding the word limit will delay review.

6. **Using footnotes for substantive content** — Given strong discouragement, authors should integrate footnoted material into main text or relegate to web appendix. Reviewers or editors may request removal.
