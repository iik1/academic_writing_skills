# Journal Writing Skill: Human Resource Management Journal

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** HRM
- **Publisher:** Wiley
- **Scope:** Business and management journal focused on management of people and workplace issues/tensions. Multidisciplinary approach to advancing theory through empirical evidence, conceptual consolidation, literature reviews, and methods development.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/17488583
- **Field baseline:** None available - HRM field baseline not yet generated

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
**ENFORCED** - Required manuscript elements:
- Title: Short, informative, containing major keywords
- Abstract: Five-sentence structure (see below)
- Introduction: 3-4 paragraph structure establishing conversation, gap, purpose, approach
- Theory/Literature Review: Targeted and theoretically driven (not exhaustive)
- Methods: Transparent, justified, paradigm-appropriate
- Results/Findings: Complete statistical reporting or rich qualitative description
- Discussion: Theoretical contribution, limitations, future research
- Implications: Must address both theory AND practice

**ENFORCED** - Must NOT include:
- Research not focused on HRM aspects
- Pure organizational psychology without HRM connection
- Papers not engaging with theory
- Student-only samples
- Small-scale studies
- AI-written manuscripts
- Pure replication studies

### Length
No explicit word limit stated in editorial. Qualitative papers noted to require more pages than quantitative studies. Journal uses online appendices for supplementary material.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED** - Five-sentence recommended structure:
1. Establish common ground from prior research
2. Establish complication or gap in research
3. Establish why gap needs to be understood better
4. Establish findings and how they resolve gap
5. Establish importance for managers and scholars

**ENFORCED** - Should be written as "teaser" not summary. Written last after manuscript completion.

### Citations
**ENFORCED** - APA reference style

### Headings
No specific heading conventions specified beyond standard sections (Introduction, Theory, Methods, Results, Discussion).

### Tables and Figures
**GUIDELINE ONLY** - Descriptive statistics typically presented in table format
**GUIDELINE ONLY** - Visual representations (graphs, plots, figures) encouraged for clarity
**ENFORCED** - For quantitative: Must report model fit statistics in tables
**ENFORCED** - For qualitative: Can use tables, figures, quotes, excerpts to support findings

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED:**
- Do not report new data in Discussion section
- No AI-generated content without disclosure
- No plagiarism (checked via CrossCheck/Turnitin)
- No bypassing ethical requirements
- No manipulation checks in experimental design without justification
- No uncontextualized research
- No papers clearly written for another journal without substantial revision

## Journal-Specific Requirements (No Field Baseline for Comparison)

### Peer Review
**ENFORCED** - Double-blind peer review. Manuscripts must be anonymized with separate title page containing author details.

### Theoretical Contribution
**ENFORCED** - All submissions must make substantive contribution to theory development. Cannot simply state contribution without explicating HOW (extend, develop, boundary conditions).

### Practical Implications
**ENFORCED** - Must include direct or indirect practical implications. Cannot be vague "awareness raising" - must be actionable and specific.

### Context
**ENFORCED** - Context must be explicitly engaged:
- Multi-level consideration (macro, meso, micro)
- Clear description of investigator, data source, and phenomenon contexts
- Justification for context selection
- Discussion of boundary conditions

### Methods Section Requirements

**For Quantitative Studies - ENFORCED:**
- Data screening procedures if applicable
- Descriptive statistics table with correlations, means, SD
- Sample size and p-value thresholds clearly stated
- For multilevel: ICC1, ICC2 values with justification
- For SEM: Model specifications, estimation methods, fit statistics, performance assessment
- Complete statistical reporting: coefficients, confidence intervals, effect sizes, p-values
- Supplementary analyses clearly justified

**For Qualitative Studies - ENFORCED:**
- Explicit statement of qualitative method tradition and paradigm
- Context of investigator clearly described
- Reflexivity statement for researcher positionality
- Clear description of how findings derive from evidence (quotes, excerpts)
- Triangulation explained if multiple sources/methods used
- Findings organized thematically with clear link to research questions
- Can combine Findings and Discussion if interpretation intertwined with presentation

### Introduction Structure
**GUIDELINE ONLY** - Recommended four-paragraph structure:
1. Engagement with scholarly discourse (cite 2-3 key scholars in conversation)
2. "However..." - pivot to gap or divergence
3. State paper objective plainly and directly
4. For empirical: present context and findings; for conceptual: elucidate contributions

### Writing Style
**ENFORCED:**
- Must be compelling and clear
- No "this has never been done before" without explaining significance
- No "fills a gap" without explaining how gap hindered understanding
- Contributions must show novelty/originality with clear justification
- Discussion must follow logically from Results (no "overarching")

### Data and Transparency
**ENFORCED:**
- Methods must allow inferential reproducibility
- Supplementary materials encouraged (can use anonymized dropbox links)
- Clear documentation of:
  - Data exclusion criteria and methods
  - Missing data handling
  - Outlier treatment
  - Assumption violations and corrections

## Common Pitfalls

Based on editorial guidance, common reasons for desk rejection:

1. **Poor empirical base** - Insufficient sample size, inappropriate sampling, weak data quality
2. **Lack of novelty** - Incremental additions to well-established models without clear contribution
3. **Weak theoretical engagement** - Not engaging with theory or failing to articulate contribution
4. **Methodological issues** - Poor research design, inappropriate analysis, lack of rigor
5. **Inadequate contextualization** - Studying new context but saying nothing about it
6. **Lack of fit with journal** - Organizational psychology focus without HRM connection
7. **Poor presentation** - Not adhering to submission guidelines, excessive grammatical errors
8. **Plagiarism** - Failing similarity checks, AI-generated content without disclosure
9. **Thin contributions** - Multiple small contributions rather than focused significant contribution
10. **No practical implications** - Theoretical work without any application to practice

## Special Considerations

### Use of Online Platforms (MTurk, Prolific)
Journal accepts but requires:
- Verification of participant identities (IP analysis, threat scores)
- Attention checks and data quality filters
- Ethical treatment and fair compensation
- Transparent reporting of platform used
- Discussion of limitations

### AI Usage
**ENFORCED** - Must disclose AI tool usage:
- Document extent and manner of AI use
- Maintain transparency and credibility
- Provide human oversight and verification
- Cannot be AI-written manuscripts
- Risk of inadvertent plagiarism must be addressed

### Contextual Expertise
**GUIDELINE ONLY** - For research in less-familiar contexts:
- Develop contextual expertise through immersion
- Build collaborative networks with local scholars
- Demonstrate understanding beyond surface-level description
- Engage with indigenous features and local constructs
