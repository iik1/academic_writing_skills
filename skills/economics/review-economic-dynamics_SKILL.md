# Journal Writing Skill: Review of Economic Dynamics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier, on behalf of the Society for Economic Dynamics
- **Scope:** Meritorious original contributions to dynamic economics across any area of economics meeting "the highest standards of scientific research." The journal's scope is broad but framed by the SED's view that economics is unified by scientific approach; papers need not be explicitly computational or theoretical but should connect to dynamic phenomena.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/journal/10942025/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Liu, Liu & Xue, "Habit Formation and News-driven Business Cycles," RED Vol. 56, April 2025, DOI: 10.1016/j.red.2025.101273 — abstract/metadata via ScienceDirect; Anstreicher, "Family Resources and Human Capital in Economic Downturns," CES WP 24-15 — full working paper, structural paper representative of RED's empirical-structural genre)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section ordering is prescribed beyond standard academic structure. Required submission elements: title page, abstract, keywords, highlights, main text, references, data availability statement. An appendix or online appendix for proofs and robustness is expected but not mandated in a specific form.

### Length
No word or page limit is stated in accessible guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured narrative. No word limit stated in accessible guidelines. Keywords are required as a separate field. FIELD DEFAULT on format; keyword field is ENFORCED.

### Citations
Author-year (parenthetical), standard economics format. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Arabic numerals with decimal subsection notation (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2, etc.) observed in practice.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Table and figure notes are required to explain variables, clustering, and sample restrictions.

### Highlights
**ENFORCED — not in FIELD_SKILL.** Elsevier requires a Highlights section submitted separately from the abstract: 3–5 bullet points, each no longer than 85 characters (including spaces), describing the key novel results or conclusions of the article. These appear on the ScienceDirect article page. Failure to include highlights will block submission processing. Do not confuse with the abstract; highlights are outcome-focused telegrams, not methodological summaries.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance.
- Manuscripts previously published (other than as preprints, abstracts, theses, or registered reports) are not eligible.
- Competing interests, funding sources, and generative AI use must be declared.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
None observed in articles. Both empirical-structural and theory papers follow the FIELD_SKILL canonical structures.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Highlights requirement (ENFORCED).** The only confirmed writing-relevant delta. Every submission must include a separate Highlights field with 3–5 bullets of ≤85 characters each. Best practice: write highlights after the paper is complete; each bullet should state a concrete finding or contribution, not a methodological description. Example acceptable format: "Middle-class children bear the largest college attainment losses in recessions." Avoid restating abstract sentences verbatim.

**Submission fee (ENFORCED, administrative).** $195 per new submission. Reduced to $100 if all co-authors are full-time students. Free for resubmissions following two or more prior rounds at RED. This is not a writing norm but affects submission planning.

**Dynamic framing of contributions (PRACTICE ONLY — RELAXATION context).** As an ABS 3 journal whose scope is explicitly "dynamic economics," papers should frame at least one contribution in terms of dynamics, intertemporal mechanisms, or equilibrium dynamics — even for empirical papers. This is a soft expectation inferred from scope, not a stated requirement, and does not constitute a new norm beyond FIELD_SKILL's contribution-framing guidance.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting Highlights.** The Highlights field is a hard submission requirement. Authors accustomed to journals without this Elsevier feature frequently omit it or paste the abstract instead. Write 3–5 crisp result statements of ≤85 characters each.

2. **Framing the contribution without a dynamic hook.** RED's identity is "dynamic economics." Contributions framed purely in static terms — even for otherwise strong empirical papers — may face scope-based rejection. Connect at least one finding to intertemporal behavior, life-cycle dynamics, business cycle mechanisms, or equilibrium dynamics.

3. **Ignoring the data availability statement.** Elsevier requires an explicit statement about data and code availability. This is a submission check item, not a stylistic choice. Even if data are confidential (e.g., restricted-use Census data), the statement must explain the access pathway or restriction.

4. **Expecting double-blind review.** RED uses single-anonymized review (authors are known to reviewers). This does not affect manuscript text but matters for cover letter conventions and self-citation practices — the standard FIELD_SKILL norm of open self-citation applies without modification.
