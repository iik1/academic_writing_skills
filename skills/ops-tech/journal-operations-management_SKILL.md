# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Operations Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Wiley (on behalf of ASCM - Association for Supply Chain Management)
- **Scope:** Original, empirical operations and supply chain management research demonstrating both academic and practical relevance. Explicitly does NOT publish purely analytical models or optimization techniques.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.jom-hub.com/submissions/author-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (full-text access unavailable across all platforms)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
**ENFORCED:** Required sections in order:
1. Abstract
2. Keywords
3. Introduction
4. Literature Review/Theory Development
5. Materials/Data Collection and Analysis Methods
6. Results
7. Discussion
8. Conclusions

Note: Section names are more prescriptive than FIELD_SKILL's flexible naming. "Literature Review/Theory Development" is required (not "Theory and Hypotheses"). "Materials/Data Collection and Analysis Methods" is required (not simply "Methodology" or "Methods").

### Length
**ENFORCED:** Typically under 40 pages (double-spaced, 12-point font, one-inch margins, single column). Longer manuscripts may be returned for streamlining before peer review.

### Abstract
**GUIDELINE ONLY:** Word limit and structure (structured vs. unstructured) not specified in accessible guidelines. Keywords required but count not specified.

### Citations
**ENFORCED:** APA (American Psychological Association) style explicitly required. This is more specific than FIELD_SKILL's "APA-adjacent" characterization. DOI use highly encouraged. In-text citations must include page numbers for direct quotations. References must be consistent in format.

### Headings
**GUIDELINE ONLY:** No specific heading level or formatting requirements found in guidelines beyond required section names.

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED - MAJOR DEVIATION FROM COMMON PRACTICE:**
- Tables and figures must appear "at the point of reference (not at the end)"
- Captions placed directly BELOW figures
- Captions placed directly ABOVE tables
- This differs from common submission practice where tables/figures are placed at manuscript end

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED:**
- No purely analytical models or optimization techniques (empirical research only)
- No headers or footers permitted
- Minimal use of footnotes allowed
- Manuscripts without proper anonymization may be desk-rejected
- Running title must not exceed 40 characters
- Plagiarism limits: no single source should account for >1% of wording; overall similarity >15% requires explanation in cover letter

### Formatting
**ENFORCED:**
- Double-spaced throughout
- Single column (required for peer review)
- 12-point font
- One-inch margins on all sides
- Pages must be numbered
- Serial comma usage per APA guidelines
- Latin abbreviations per APA conventions

## Common Pitfalls

Based on explicit guidelines and prohibitions:

1. **Submitting analytical models:** JOM explicitly does not publish purely analytical models or optimization techniques. Papers must use empirical research methods.

2. **Tables/figures at manuscript end:** Unlike many journal submission formats, JOM requires tables and figures embedded at point of reference, not collected at the end.

3. **Exceeding similarity thresholds:** Authors should pre-check with plagiarism software (e.g., iThenticate, Turnitin). Single-source similarity >1% or overall similarity >15% will trigger scrutiny.

4. **Insufficient anonymization:** Manuscripts with identifiers in the text or embedded metadata may be desk-rejected. All files must be stripped of author identifiers.

5. **Overly long manuscripts:** Manuscripts significantly exceeding 40 pages may be returned before peer review for streamlining.
