## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**Delta — Explicit named implications sections at Discussion level (PRACTICE ONLY)**

FIELD_SKILL states that a subsection addressing managerial implications is "near-universal" but does not specify how theoretical and practical implications should be separated or named.

This journal shows a consistent pattern across all 3 articles of separating theoretical and practical implications into distinct, labelled structural units. These appear either as:
- Standalone sections at the same level as Discussion (e.g., "Implications for theory" / "Implications for practice" in Article 1), or
- Named subsections within Discussion (e.g., "Theoretical contributions" / "Practical implications" in Articles 2–3).

One article (Article 3) additionally included a "Methodological contributions" subsection in Discussion alongside the theory and practice units.

This is a more explicit decomposition than FIELD_SKILL anticipates. Authors should plan for — and label — theory and practice implications as distinct units rather than treating them as a single combined block.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting structured abstract fields.** The Emerald structured format (Purpose / Design/methodology/approach / Findings / Originality/value) is required and differs from a conventional unstructured abstract. Missing or misnaming fields is a common formatting error.

2. **Merging theory and practice implications.** A combined "Discussion and conclusion" that treats theoretical and managerial implications as one block appears out of pattern for this journal. Plan to separate and label them as distinct units.

3. **Neglecting originality/value framing.** The "Originality/value" abstract field requires an explicit statement of what is novel. This is distinct from the Findings field and should not be collapsed into it.

4. **Assuming quantitative norms from FIELD_SKILL apply without verification.** All articles in the spot-check sample were qualitative or mixed-methods. Whether quantitative papers follow field-standard robustness/endogeneity conventions cannot be confirmed from this sample.
