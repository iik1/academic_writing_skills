## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are enforced requirements from the submission guidelines.

### Structure
**ENFORCED**

Three publication types with distinct structures:

1. **Research Articles** (primary form): Novel, innovative, rigorous original contributions that break new ground, generate substantive insights, and have strong grounding in current or new theory.

2. **Research Commentaries**: Critical evaluation and roadmap for future research on significant questions. Must describe interesting/novel perspective, explain why it's needed and missing in current research, present directions for future research and practice.

3. **Research Notes**: Shorter contributions (see length limits below).

No specific required section order stated in guidelines.

### Length
**ENFORCED**

- **Research Articles**: Maximum 32 pages of text; maximum 38 pages total including references, appendixes, tables, figures
- **Research Notes**: Maximum 20 pages total; up to 8 pages of figures and tables

### Abstract
**ENFORCED - DUAL ABSTRACT REQUIREMENT**

1. **Main abstract**: Maximum 300 words
2. **Practice-oriented abstract**: 200 words or fewer; will be published in "Research Spotlights" feature to explain, promote, and share the article

This dual-abstract requirement is highly distinctive and likely unique to ISR among IS journals.

### Citations
**ENFORCED**

- **Style**: Author-year format in text, e.g., (Norman 1977) or Norman (1977)
- **Reference list**: Alphabetical by author name at end of paper
- **Format conformance**: Must conform to journal style (specific style guide not named in available guidelines)

### Formatting Requirements
**ENFORCED**

- Double-spaced throughout (including abstract, references, footnotes)
- Minimum 11-point font (standard font)
- 1-inch margins on all four sides
- Footnotes: Numbered consecutively, indicated by superscript numbers; material that would normally be footnotes should be in body text when it doesn't impair readability

### Keywords
**ENFORCED**

Authors must select keywords describing:
- Theoretical orientation
- Methodological orientation

Keywords appear beneath the abstract and are used for Senior Editor, Associate Editor, and reviewer selection. Available keyword list provided in ScholarOne Manuscripts submission system.

### Double-Blind Review Requirements
**ENFORCED**

- Remove explicit indications of authors' names and institutions
- Remove all acknowledgments
- Write paper to avoid revealing author identity

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED**

- Manuscript must not have been published previously
- Manuscript must not be currently submitted elsewhere
- Working paper postings must be disclosed at final acceptance

## Common Pitfalls

Based on guidelines, likely submission mistakes include:

1. **Missing the practice-oriented abstract**: Submitting only the standard 300-word abstract without the required 200-word practice-oriented version for Research Spotlights.

2. **Exceeding page limits**: Going beyond 32 pages of text or 38 pages total; particularly for Research Notes (20 pages max).

3. **Breaking anonymity**: Failing to fully remove identifying information for double-blind review (author names, institutions, acknowledgments, self-identifying citations).

4. **Incomplete keyword selection**: Not providing keywords for both theoretical AND methodological orientation, which are used for editor/reviewer assignment.
