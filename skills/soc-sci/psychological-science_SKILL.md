# Journal Writing Skill: Psychological Science

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE / Association for Psychological Science (APS)
- **Scope:** Empirical and theoretical research across all areas of scientific psychology (cognitive, social, developmental, health, neuroscience). Broad audience emphasis: preference for work of general interest beyond sub-domain specialisms.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.psychologicalscience.org/publications/psychological_science/ps-submissions
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Ren & Schaumberg, 2024, DOI: 10.1177/09567976241239935)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure

Research Articles follow a conventional empirical sequence, but the Introduction carries no explicit heading—the manuscript opens directly with body text. Multi-study papers use numbered sub-studies (Study 1, Study 2 …) each with their own **Method** and **Results** subsections, followed by a single **General Discussion**. Any interim introductions and discussions bracketing individual studies count toward the word limit along with the global introduction and conclusion.

Required end-matter section titled **Transparency** (in this order): Action Editor, Editor, Author Contributions (CRediT taxonomy), Declaration of Conflicting Interests, Funding, Open Practices. This section is mandatory for all articles. [ENFORCED]

**Explicit prohibition:** Meta-analyses and other forms of systematic evidence synthesis are typically not considered for publication.

### Length

Research Articles: Introduction + Discussion + Footnotes + Acknowledgments + Appendices combined ≤ **2,000 words**. The Methods section is explicitly excluded from this count. All interim introductions and discussions in multi-study manuscripts are included in this limit. [ENFORCED]

Registered Reports: Stage 1 Introduction ≤ 1,000 words; Stage 2 Introduction ≤ 1,000 words, Discussion ≤ 1,000 words; Methods and Results have no stated limit. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract

Unstructured single paragraph, ≤ **150 words**. Must explicitly state: (1) sample size(s) for each reported study, (2) participant population(s), and (3) any important limitations of the research. Does not count toward the 2,000-word limit. [ENFORCED]

### Statement of Relevance

All Research Articles must include a **Statement of Relevance** (≤ 150 words) placed immediately after the abstract. It is a plain-language, non-technical summary written for journalists, students, or non-specialist readers—not a second abstract. It should convey why the questions asked and the findings matter beyond psychology laboratories. Does not count toward the word limit. [ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

### Keywords

5–7 keywords listed under the abstract. Open-science status terms ("open data," "open materials," "preregistered") appear as keywords in published articles when applicable. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Citations

APA 7th edition throughout (headings, references, abbreviations, symbols). Minor deviations are tolerated at initial submission; final and revised manuscripts must comply fully. [ENFORCED]

### Headings

APA 7th edition heading levels. Level 1 (e.g., "Study 1," "General Discussion") centred bold. Level 2 (e.g., "Method," "Results") flush-left bold italic. Level 3 sub-headings (e.g., "Participants and procedure.") bold italic, inline at start of paragraph. [ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

### Tables and Figures

Tables and figures must be **embedded within the manuscript near where they are discussed**, not placed at the end. This explicitly overrides the APA 7th default of placing all tables and figures at the end. [ENFORCED]

Figure typography: labels and axis numbers in Helvetica Neue 57 Condensed (or Helvetica/Arial as fallback); panel labels (a, b, c) lowercase 18 pt; main axis labels 10 pt; figure title 12 pt; legend keys 9 pt. [ENFORCED]

### Open Practices (effective January 2024)

Open data and open materials are **required**, not optional. Exemptions are considered case-by-case. Preregistration is not required but is a positive factor in editorial evaluation. A **Research Transparency Statement** must be submitted with all empirical manuscripts and is shared with editors and reviewers. [ENFORCED]

Note: Manuscripts submitted before January 2024 were eligible for Open Data, Open Materials, and Preregistration badges; this badge system no longer applies to new submissions.

### STAR Editors

A dedicated team of Statistics, Transparency, and Rigor (STAR) editors performs transparency checks and reproducibility checks on all manuscripts as a standard part of peer review, in coordination with the Action Editor. [ENFORCED — journal process]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Statistical Reporting

**Field norm:** Effect sizes, CIs, and substantive magnitudes "expected" at the ABS 4 level.

**This journal:** Stricter and explicit. Effect sizes are required for all major results. **95% confidence intervals** are required in place of standard deviations or standard errors around mean dependent variables. Sample sizes and participant populations must appear in the abstract. Confirmed in every table and figure in the spot-checked article: Cohen's *d* with 95% CI reported for all *t*-tests, eta² for ANOVAs, *B* (SE) for regressions. [ENFORCED]

### Structural Deviations

**Field norm:** Canonical sequence with optional separate Literature Review section; no prescribed sub-study structure.

**This journal:** Two journal-specific structural elements override the field baseline:

1. **Statement of Relevance** — a required 150-word plain-language box placed after the abstract. No equivalent exists in the field norm. [ENFORCED]
2. **Numbered multi-study format** — the dominant structure for empirical articles is numbered studies (Study 1, 2 … N) each with their own Method and Results, closing with a General Discussion. The introduction integrates literature review without a separate section heading; it opens without a heading. [PRACTICE ONLY — confirmed in article; consistent with APA 7th conventions for experimental psychology]

### Transparency and Open Science

**Field norm:** "Ethics statements and data availability notes are increasingly standard"; pre-registration "increasingly valued."

**This journal:** Mandatory structured **Transparency** end-matter section required in every published article, listing Action Editor, Editor, CRediT Author Contributions, Conflicts, Funding, and a detailed Open Practices statement. Open data and materials are required (not merely encouraged) from January 2024. Research Transparency Statement required at submission. [ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Violating the 2,000-word cap on non-Methods text.** The Methods exclusion is generous, but the Introduction and Discussion together must stay within 2,000 words including footnotes, acknowledgments, and appendices. In multi-study papers, interim introductions and interim discussions also count—authors routinely underestimate cumulative word load.

2. **Omitting the Statement of Relevance or treating it as a second abstract.** The Statement of Relevance must be written for non-specialists; a technical restatement of the abstract will be flagged at desk review.

3. **Submitting without a Research Transparency Statement.** From January 2024 this is required at submission for all empirical work and is evaluated by STAR editors alongside the scientific content.

4. **Reporting SDs/SEs instead of 95% CIs around means.** The journal explicitly requires 95% confidence intervals; using standard errors or standard deviations for central-tendency uncertainty violates a stated guideline and is caught by STAR review.
