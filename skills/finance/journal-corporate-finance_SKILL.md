# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Corporate Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (North-Holland)
- **Scope:** Publishes high-quality empirical and theoretical papers in corporate finance, including financial structure, governance, payout policy, labor, innovation, risk management, and intersections with macro, asset pricing, fintech, and law. Shorter format papers are explicitly welcomed alongside standard-length articles.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-corporate-finance/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (DOI: 10.1016/j.jcorpfin.2026.103004; DOI: 10.1016/j.jcorpfin.2025.102941)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section sequence is specified beyond standard academic conventions. The journal explicitly notes that shorter format papers are accepted, meaning the canonical five-section structure is not rigidly enforced. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Length
No hard word or page limit stated in accessible guidelines. Published articles observed ranging from 24 to 68 pages, confirming the absence of a strict upper bound. The journal's explicit invitation of "shorter format papers" signals that brevity is valued. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Abstract
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (single unstructured paragraph). Word limit not specified in accessible guidelines text; Elsevier's standard ceiling of 250–300 words applies by convention. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Author-year parenthetical citations in text; Elsevier Harvard format in reference list (e.g., Surname, I., Year. Title. Journal Abbreviation volume, pages). [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standard Elsevier formatting conventions apply. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Highlights
Elsevier requires a Highlights section submitted separately from the main manuscript: 3–5 bullet points, each no longer than 85 characters (including spaces), capturing the core findings of the article. This is a mandatory Elsevier submission element listed in the guidelines table of contents. Not present in FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED]

### Submission Fee
A non-refundable submission fee of USD 340 applies to all new submissions. Open-access publishing carries an additional Article Publishing Charge of USD 4,520. Neither fee is mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Generative AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. AI use in the research or writing must be declared upon submission.
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance.
- Simultaneous submission to other journals is prohibited.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Shorter format papers:** The journal scope page explicitly invites "shorter format papers" alongside standard articles. FIELD_SKILL does not address article-length tiers. Authors writing shorter, more focused empirical notes need not pad to a canonical structure. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

**Highlights:** Published articles at Elsevier carry a Highlights box on the article landing page. Authors must draft 3–5 bullets (≤85 characters each) summarising the main results. This is a submission deliverable entirely absent from FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Desk rejection speed:** The journal reports a median of 5 days from submission to first decision, indicating an aggressive desk-rejection policy. Fit with scope and quality threshold must be evident on the first read; a paper that does not immediately signal its contribution will not survive to review. This is a practical implication not discussed in FIELD_SKILL.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Omitting Highlights.** Elsevier's submission system requires the Highlights file as a separate upload. Forgetting it is a common submission error that triggers a desk-side correction request.
2. **Misjudging fit for desk rejection.** With a 5-day first-decision turnaround, the editorial team desk-rejects aggressively. Papers must make their corporate finance contribution and identification strategy legible in the abstract and first two paragraphs of the introduction.
3. **Submitting without the submission fee.** The USD 340 fee is non-refundable and required at submission; failure to pay prevents processing.
4. **Over-padding a short paper.** The journal's explicit welcome of shorter format papers means authors with tight, focused contributions should not inflate their paper to match a canonical 40-page structure.
