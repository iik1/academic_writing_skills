## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** NHH (Norwegian School of Economics ranking)

- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are enforceable requirements stated in the journal's author guidelines.

### Structure
**ENFORCED** - Standard empirical research article format:
- Abstract (with keywords)
- Introduction / Literature Review
- Theoretical Framework (when applicable)
- Method (Setting, Participants, Data Collection, Data Analysis)
- Results
- Discussion
- Conclusions and Implications
- Acknowledgments
- References
- Appendices (as needed for transcription conventions, instruments, etc.)

### Length
**ENFORCED** - 8,000 to 10,000 words including tables, notes, appendices, and references. Longer or shorter manuscripts accepted depending on merit, but this range is "currently requested."

### Abstract
**ENFORCED** - Up to 200 words maximum. Unstructured (single paragraph). Must be followed by up to 6 keywords.

### Citations
**ENFORCED** - APA style (6th edition referenced in guidelines, though current practice likely uses 7th edition). The journal explicitly adheres to APA definitions of author contributorship and responsibilities. Authors of quantitative studies encouraged to report effect sizes per APA manual recommendations.

Examples from observed article:
- Cazden, C. (2001). *Classroom discourse: The language of teaching and learning* (2nd ed.). Portsmouth, NH: Heinemann.
- Dafouz, E., & Llinares García, A. (2008). The role of repetition in CLIL teacher discourse. *International CLIL Research Journal*, 1, 50-59.
- In-text: author-date format (e.g., "Maroney, 1995")

### Headings
**PRACTICE ONLY** - Hierarchical structure observed in articles:
- **PRIMARY HEADINGS** (all caps or bold)
- **Secondary Headings** (title case, bold)
- **Tertiary headings** (sentence case or italic, for subsections within results)

Data extracts often numbered (EXTRACT 1, EXTRACT 2, etc.) with line-by-line transcription.

### Tables and Figures
**GUIDELINE NOT SPECIFIC** - Guidelines mention tables are counted in word limit but provide no specific formatting requirements. Standard academic presentation expected.

### Format Requirements
**ENFORCED** - 
- American letter-sized paper (8½ × 11 inches)
- 12-point font
- File formats: MS Word, WordPerfect, or RTF (rich text format)
- Electronic submission via Wiley Authors system

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED** - Authors discouraged from submitting multiple manuscripts to the journal until an editorial decision has been made on the first submission.

## Observed Conventions (from article analysis)

> These patterns were observed in the spot-checked article. Without a FIELD_SKILL baseline, classification as "delta" or "norm" is not possible.

### Voice and Register
**OBSERVED** - Third-person academic voice throughout. Formal scholarly register. Examples from examined article:
- "The study sets out to describe..."
- "Our data suggest qualitative differences..."

Mixed use of third person with occasional first-person plural ("our") when describing the research team's actions. Predominantly passive voice for methodology ("data were collected") with active voice increasing in discussion sections.

### Statistical Reporting
**OBSERVED** - For quantitative studies: descriptive statistics reported (e.g., "14 out of 15 MTs"), with emphasis on effect sizes per APA guidance. Sample sizes and study contexts described with specific numbers (e.g., "over 800 native English-speaking language assistants," "300 primary schools").

Qualitative studies employ systematic coding schemes with explicit labels (e.g., XPN for explanation, REC for recasting, REP for repetition).

### Citation Density
**OBSERVED** - Literature reviews are extensive with high citation density in theoretical framework sections. Empirical sections less densely cited, focusing on data presentation.

### Methodological Transparency
**OBSERVED** - Strong emphasis on methodological detail:
- Explicit data triangulation (e.g., video recordings combined with live observation)
- Stimulated recall interviews for participant validation
- Transcription conventions detailed in appendix
- Clear coding procedures and analytical frameworks

### Pedagogical Orientation
**OBSERVED** - "Conclusions and Implications" section standard, with explicit discussion of practical applications for language teaching and learning. The journal emphasizes connections between research findings and classroom practice.

## Common Pitfalls

Based on submission guidelines and journal emphasis:

1. **Exceeding word limit without justification** - The 8,000-10,000 word range is explicitly "requested," and manuscripts that "deviate substantially" may be rejected during in-house review. Calculate total including all tables, notes, appendices, and references.

2. **Weak pedagogical implications** - Given the journal's mission to serve both researchers and teachers, manuscripts must explicitly address practical implications for language education. Pure theoretical contributions without applied dimension may not align with journal scope.

3. **Insufficient effect size reporting in quantitative work** - The guidelines specifically mention APA's encouragement of effect sizes. Quantitative submissions reporting only p-values without effect sizes and confidence intervals will be seen as incomplete.

4. **Simultaneous submissions** - The journal explicitly discourages submitting additional manuscripts while one is under review.
