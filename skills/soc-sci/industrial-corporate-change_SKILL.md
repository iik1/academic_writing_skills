# Journal Writing Skill: Industrial and Corporate Change

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary journal covering technological change, innovation dynamics, industrial organisation, and corporate evolution, situated at the intersection of economics and social science.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/icc/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (doi:10.1093/icc/dtae017; doi:10.1093/icc/dtad032)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section sequence specified beyond standard empirical structure.

### Length
**10,000 words maximum** ("should not normally exceed"). This is a firm editorial expectation. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
**Unstructured single paragraph, maximum 80 words.** No labeled fields (Background/Methods/Results). The 80-word ceiling is notably tight — roughly half the limit common in SOC SCI field journals. Confirmed in both spot-checked articles: plain prose paragraphs well under 100 words. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
**Author-year style throughout.** Narrative form: Author (Year); parenthetical form: (Author Year). No numbered references. [ENFORCED — confirmed in both articles]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Figures must be submitted at **300 dpi minimum** in tiff, bmp, jpeg, or eps format. [ENFORCED]

### JEL Codes
Authors must provide **JEL classification codes** at submission. JEL codes appear in published articles alongside a keywords section. This is an economics-community convention not present in core SOC SCI outlets. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Editors **explicitly discourage multiple concurrent submissions from the same author** and reserve the right to reject on that basis.
- Manuscript must be in English.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Economics-Adjacent Positioning
**Field norm:** SOC SCI journals at this level do not use JEL codes; methodological identity is drawn from sociology, political science, or human geography.  
**This journal:** Despite ABS SOC SCI classification, ICC is economics-adjacent. Published articles include JEL codes and keywords. Quantitative work follows econometric conventions (gravity models, panel regressions, standard-error clustering) rather than causal-inference framing typical of political science outlets. Authors should frame contributions in terms legible to industrial economics and innovation studies audiences, not only mainstream social science. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify an abstract word limit.  
**This journal:** 80-word hard ceiling imposes extreme compression. A single-sentence contribution statement followed by two or three sentences of method and finding is the practical maximum. [ENFORCED]

### Word Limit
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify word limits.  
**This journal:** 10,000-word ceiling. Tighter than many ABS 3 SOC SCI outlets. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Overlong abstracts.** The 80-word limit is easy to exceed; drafting a first abstract of 150–200 words and then cutting is the standard failure mode. Write the abstract last and budget ruthlessly.
2. **Missing JEL codes.** Authors from sociology or political science backgrounds may be unfamiliar with JEL classification. Omitting codes at submission is a procedural error that delays review.
3. **Wrong audience framing.** ICC sits between economics and social science. Framing a paper solely in sociological or political-science terms without engaging innovation economics or industrial organisation literature risks misalignment with the editorial community and typical reviewers.
4. **Exceeding 10,000 words.** The limit is stated as a norm, not an absolute, but submissions meaningfully over length signal poor discipline and risk desk rejection.
