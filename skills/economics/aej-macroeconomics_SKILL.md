# Journal Writing Skill: American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** American Economic Association
- **Scope:** Publishes research across all of macroeconomics: business cycles, growth, monetary and fiscal policy, international macro, and labor market dynamics. Empirical, theoretical, and structural papers all fall within scope.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/mac/submissions/guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (10.1257/mac.20220153, 10.1257/mac.20220034, 10.1257/mac.20200022)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. In-paper appendices must appear on separate pages after the main text. Supplemental appendix material is posted online and not edited or typeset; the submitted paper (including any in-paper appendices) must be fully self-contained — readers must be able to understand all findings without consulting the supplemental appendix. This is stronger than FIELD_SKILL's observation that a supplemental appendix is "expected": here, critical material in a supplemental appendix is a submission error.

### Length
**ENFORCED.** Manuscript must not exceed 40 pages formatted in 11-point font, 1.5 spacing, 1-inch margins, or 45 pages in 12-point font with the same spacing and margins. The page count includes all figures, tables, references, and in-paper appendices. (Supplemental appendices posted online are excluded from this count.) The average published article runs 34–35 typeset pages. The guidelines explicitly state that "shorter, impactful papers will also be given full consideration." FIELD_SKILL does not specify page limits.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED.** 100 words or fewer. Unstructured (no required fields). Confirmed in practice: published abstracts in the April 2026 and January 2026 issues consistently fall at or below 100 words (spot-checked abstracts counted 94 and 97 words respectively). The NBER working paper version of the same articles often carries abstracts of 150–250 words; authors must substantially compress before submission. FIELD_SKILL specifies no abstract word limit.

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style, Author-Date format. Consistent with FIELD_SKILL's author-year norm and confirmed in practice. No deviation from field baseline.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-mandated heading style; practice confirms Arabic-numeral sections with decimal subsections (e.g., 2.1, 2.2).

### Tables and Figures
Count toward the page limit. Supplemental appendix may contain non-essential robustness checks and alternative specifications that would otherwise push the manuscript over the page cap.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI software may not be listed as an author; any AI use must be disclosed at submission.
- Papers under simultaneous review elsewhere are ineligible.
- Papers previously rejected by AEJ: Macroeconomics may not be resubmitted.
- Field experiments must be pre-registered in the AEA RCT Registry.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Abstract Compression Discipline
**ENFORCED.** The 100-word cap forces a compression discipline not present in FIELD_SKILL. In practice, the abstract must cover the question, method, and main quantitative finding in a single short paragraph. Working paper abstracts routinely exceed this limit by 50–150 words; revision for submission requires cutting all hedges, motivational framing, and secondary results. The published abstract reads as a tight statement of contribution rather than a mini-introduction.

### Self-Contained Main Paper (Supplemental Appendix Role)
**ENFORCED.** FIELD_SKILL treats the supplemental appendix as the normal home for proofs, robustness checks, and data description. AEJ: Macroeconomics requires the main paper (plus in-paper appendices, which count toward the page cap) to stand alone. Material in the online supplemental appendix is explicitly positioned as non-essential. This creates a structural trade-off absent from FIELD_SKILL: authors must balance page-cap compliance against the self-containment requirement, which in practice means proofs and robustness go in a typeset in-paper appendix (within the 40/45-page budget) or in the online supplement (if genuinely non-essential).

### Replication Package
**ENFORCED.** Data, code, and replication details are a mandatory condition of acceptance, not a best practice. FIELD_SKILL does not mention replication requirements.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Over-length abstract from the working paper version.** Authors who submit the abstract they circulated as a working paper will exceed 100 words. Trim to the quantitative core: question, method, and main finding only.

2. **Critical material relegated to the online supplemental appendix.** The paper must be self-contained. If a reader would need the supplement to follow the identification argument or the main robustness check, that material belongs in the main text or an in-paper appendix — at the cost of the page budget.

3. **Underestimating what counts in the page limit.** Every figure, table, reference list entry, and in-paper appendix page counts toward the 40/45-page ceiling. Papers near the limit should budget pages explicitly before writing begins.

4. **Resubmitting a previously rejected manuscript.** The guidelines explicitly prohibit resubmission of papers already rejected by AEJ: Macroeconomics. This is stricter than most AEA journal policies.
