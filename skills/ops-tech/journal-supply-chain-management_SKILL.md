# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Supply Chain Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Wiley (on behalf of Institute for Supply Management)
- **Scope:** High-quality empirical, conceptual, and theoretical research in supply chain management, with emphasis on novel theoretical contributions and practical relevance.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/1745493x/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1111/jscm.12313 — Nikookar et al. 2024, qualitative/conceptual; 10.1111/jscm.12328 — Cornelissen et al. 2024, conceptual)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No required section sequence beyond standard paper-type conventions.

### Length
Word ranges by paper type (all-in: abstract, body, references, tables, figures, appendices):
- **Conceptual articles:** 8,000–10,000 words
- **Qualitative or quantitative articles:** 9,000–13,000 words
- **Social Impact / Short Paper:** 4,000–6,000 words

These are guidelines, not hard caps. Co-Editors-in-Chief evaluate submissions on a **contribution-to-length ratio**; higher-contribution work is permitted more pages. Deviations must be justified in the cover letter. Label: ENFORCED (ranges) / GUIDELINE ONLY (as hard upper limit).

### Abstract
- **Length:** ~175 words (no strict cap, but 175 is the stated target)
- **Format:** Unstructured single paragraph — no labeled fields
- **Required content (three elements):** (1) background situation that led to the research; (2) the theoretical puzzle or dilemma the research addresses; (3) key findings or conclusions across theoretical, managerial, and societal dimensions
- **Voice:** Third-person required. Use "the authors challenge," "this article discusses," "the findings demonstrate" — **not** "we challenge," "we discuss," "our findings." This is an explicit guideline requirement. Label: ENFORCED

### Citations
APA style (most recent edition). Author-date format with comma separating author and year (e.g., Smith, 2020). Three or more authors: first author + "et al." Multiple citations in one parenthetical: listed alphabetically, separated by semicolons. Label: ENFORCED. Aligns with FIELD_SKILL author-date norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables and figures count toward the word total.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Manuscripts must be fully anonymized before submission (no author names in document metadata or body).
- All authors must have an ORCID registered in the submission system.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register — Abstract Only

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** First-person plural ("we") is the standard authorial voice, including for describing research design and findings, for single- and multi-authored work alike.

**JSCM requirement:** The abstract must be written in third person. "We" is explicitly prohibited in the abstract. First-person "we" is appropriate in the body text and follows FIELD_SKILL norm there. The deviation is confined to the abstract. Label: **ENFORCED** (stated in guidelines; confirmed in both spot-checked articles — Nikookar 2024 uses "this research translates," "The findings are sense-checked"; Cornelissen 2024 uses "The authors reflect").

**Practical consequence:** A manuscript with an abstract opening "We investigate..." must be rewritten to "This article investigates..." or "The authors investigate..." before submission.

### Abstract Content Structure

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify required content elements for the abstract.

**JSCM requirement:** The abstract must explicitly address three elements: background situation, theoretical puzzle/dilemma, and key findings covering theoretical, managerial, and societal dimensions. An abstract that covers only theoretical framing and findings — omitting managerial and societal takeaways — is incomplete by journal standards. Label: **ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using "we" in the abstract.** The body follows FIELD_SKILL first-person norm, but the abstract requires third-person voice. Authors who write consistently in "we" throughout will need to rewrite only the abstract before submission.

2. **Abstract missing the societal dimension.** The guidelines require findings to address theoretical, managerial, *and* societal implications. Abstracts that cover theory and practice but omit societal relevance are formally incomplete.

3. **Misreading word limits as hard caps.** The ranges (e.g., 9,000–13,000 for empirical papers) are evaluated relative to contribution quality, not enforced mechanically. A 14,000-word paper with an outstanding contribution may pass initial review; a 10,000-word paper with thin contribution may not. Length justification in the cover letter is expected when outside the range.

4. **Submitting without ORCID.** All co-authors must have registered ORCIDs entered in Manuscript Central. Missing ORCIDs are a procedural blocker at submission.
