# Journal Writing Skill: Accounting and Business Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis (Informa UK Limited)
- **Scope:** Any area of accounting broadly defined, including corporate governance, auditing, and taxation. Explicit scope exclusion: focus must be accounting, not corporate finance or general management. Welcomes both quantitative and qualitative approaches.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/rabr20 (detailed instructions-for-authors page returned 403; guidelines below derived from publicly accessible journal scope page and article spot-checks)
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 — Zeff & Dyckman (2020), 10.1080/00014788.2020.1731115 (full text); Amin et al. (2024), 10.1080/00014788.2022.2116384 (abstract); Li (2024), 10.1080/00014788.2022.2145555 (abstract)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Length
Not confirmed from accessible sources. No word or page limit identified.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. FIELD DEFAULT. No structured-abstract labels observed in any spot-checked article. Keywords listed after abstract, separated by semicolons (e.g., "Keywords: auditing; professional skepticism; nudge"). JEL codes not confirmed as required.

### Citations
Taylor & Francis Chicago author-date style. [ENFORCED]
- In-text: (Author Year) — no comma between name and year; e.g., "(Taplin 1969)" or "(Parker 1997, p. 59)". Consistent with FIELD_SKILL's "(Author Year)" description.
- Reference list: year follows author name with comma, is NOT placed in parentheses. Format: Author, I., Year. Title. *Journal*, Volume (Issue), Pages.
  - Example: `Hopwood, A.G., 2007. Whither accounting research? *The Accounting Review*, 82 (5), 1365–1374.`
  - Example: `Edwards, J.R., 2019. *A History of Corporate Financial Reporting in Britain*. New York, NY: Routledge.`
- Note: reference list format differs from APA 7th (where year appears in parentheses). Authors submitting from an APA workflow must reformat.

### Headings
Numbered Arabic numerals with Title Case. Subsections use decimal numbering (1., 1.1., 1.2., etc.). [ENFORCED via practice] Consistent with JAE/JAR style noted in FIELD_SKILL; differs from TAR Roman-numeral ALL-CAPS style.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Scope**: "The focus must be accounting, rather than (corporate) finance or general management." Papers straying into corporate finance or strategy without a clear accounting contribution will be out of scope. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**RELAXATION — GUIDELINE ONLY.** The journal scope page explicitly requires papers to be "written in a way that makes them intelligible to a wide range of academics and, where appropriate, practitioners" with "presentation being as elegant and economical as possible, avoiding unnecessary words, numbers or symbols." This is a departing mandate from the dense, technically specialist register of ABS 4*/4 accounting journals. Implication: jargon-heavy notation, gratuitous methodological complexity, and impenetrable prose that would pass at top-tier journals may be flagged as failing ABR's accessibility standard. The requirement for economy of expression ("avoiding unnecessary words, numbers or symbols") suggests reviewers will push back on overloaded tables and excessive notation.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Scope creep into corporate finance.** The explicit prohibition on corporate finance as the focus is enforced. Papers examining capital structure, asset pricing, or M&A without a clear accounting angle (e.g., earnings quality, auditing, disclosure, tax, governance-as-accounting) will be rejected out of scope.
2. **Reformatting references from APA.** Authors working in APA workflows will produce "(Author, Year)" in-text and year-in-parentheses reference lists. ABR uses Chicago author-date: no comma between name and year in-text, and year is NOT parenthesized in the reference list. Check every reference before submission.
3. **Excessive technical density.** ABR's "intelligible to practitioners" mandate means reviewers may flag papers that are impenetrably technical without a clear payoff for the broader accounting community. The ABS 4*/4 strategy of front-loading identification strategy details without framing implications may land differently here.
4. **Omitting keywords.** Observed in published articles; list keywords after the abstract, separated by semicolons.
