# Operations Research — Journal-Specific Writing Skill

This file records only the deltas from the `orandmansci-field-writing-style` field baseline. Apply the baseline in full, then apply the overrides and additions below.

---

## 1. Manuscript Metadata Block (ENFORCED)

Operations Research requires three metadata fields between the abstract and the first section body, in this order:

1. **Key words** — labeled `Key words :` (with space before colon), followed by a comma-separated list of terms.
2. **Subject classifications** — labeled `Subject classifications :`, listing INFORMS OR/MS subject taxonomy codes in lowercase italics (e.g., *programming: nonlinear: quadratic, algorithms, applications*).
3. **Area of review** — labeled `Area of review :`, followed by the editorial area designation in italics (e.g., *Optimization*).

These fields are not part of the abstract and must appear as a separate block immediately after the abstract. Do not omit any of the three. The subject classifications must use the official INFORMS OR/MS taxonomy; do not substitute with keyword strings or JEL codes.

---

## 2. Section Numbering (PRACTICE ONLY)

Published OR articles use three-level decimal section numbering: `1`, `1.1`, `1.1.1`. Follows the FIELD_SKILL norm of numbered headings but decimal style is the dominant practice. Avoid lettered appendix-style subsections within the main body.

---

## 3. Literature Review Placement (PRACTICE ONLY)

In OR, the literature review is commonly embedded within the Introduction as a dedicated subsection (e.g., Section 1.2 "Related Literature") rather than as a standalone section. The FIELD_SKILL norm is compatible with this; prefer the embedded placement for OR submissions unless the literature review is unusually extensive.

---

## 4. Acknowledgments (PRACTICE ONLY)

Acknowledgments are brief (1–3 sentences) and conventionally thank anonymous referees and the associate editor by role, not by name. Example phrasing: *"The authors thank two anonymous referees and the associate editor for their helpful comments."*

---

## 5. Author ORCID (PRACTICE ONLY)

Authors routinely include ORCID identifiers in the affiliation block. This is not enforced but is standard practice in recent OR articles.

---

## 6. Online Supplement / E-Companion (GUIDELINE ONLY)

INFORMS journals support an e-companion system for supplementary material (proofs, additional experiments, data). When used, the e-companion is referenced in the main text as "Online Appendix" or "the e-companion" and is submitted as a separate file. Not all OR articles use an e-companion; integrated appendices within the main document are also acceptable. Label the choice clearly.

---

## 7. All Other Dimensions

All other writing dimensions — including abstract style, voice, citation density, mathematical exposition, statistical reporting, tables and figures, and reference formatting — follow the `orandmansci-field-writing-style` baseline without modification.
