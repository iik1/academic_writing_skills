# Journal Writing Skill: Global Strategy Journal

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** STRAT
- **Publisher:** Wiley (in partnership with Strategic Management Society)
- **Scope:** Strategy in context: the interaction between the global context and organizational strategies. Accepts quantitative, qualitative, mixed-method, and theoretical/conceptual work. Single-country studies acceptable if they analyze a global strategy dimension. Multi-country studies must explicitly analyze the influence of country characteristics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.strategicmanagement.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/GSJ-Author-Instructions-May-2023-1.pdf (last updated May 2023)
- **Field baseline:** strat-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (DOI: 10.1002/gsj.1516, Grego et al. 2024, full text; second article inaccessible)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Submission order: title page (separate file) → dual abstract → body text → references → appendices → tables → figures. Tables and figures must NOT be embedded in text at submission; they go after the references in the order mentioned in the paper, each with an in-text placement instruction (e.g., "***Insert Table 1 about here***"). Follows FIELD_SKILL section sequence for empirical and conceptual papers.

### Length
Strongly suggested: ~30 pages double-spaced body text, **40 pages total** including references, figures, and tables. The 40-page limit applies to Research Articles, Special Issue Articles, Commentaries, and Perspectives. References should be single-spaced to conserve space. [GUIDELINE ONLY — phrased as "strongly suggest," but noncompliant manuscripts may be returned.]

### Abstract
**Two mandatory abstracts** — this is the most distinctive structural requirement. ENFORCED (confirmed in published articles; both labeled in bold as "Research Summary:" and "Managerial Summary:" within the abstract block).

1. **Research summary:** One paragraph, ≤125 words. Precise summary of the entire paper (not just conclusions); must stand alone.
2. **Managerial summary:** One paragraph, ≤125 words. Same scope, but written in plain, non-academic language for business practitioners. Academic jargon must be avoided.

No citations in either abstract. Five (5) keywords required.

The managerial summary is a standalone abstract, not a subsection within the Discussion. This differs from the FIELD_SKILL baseline, which describes a managerial implications subsection inside the Discussion.

### Citations
APA author-date. Six or more authors: first author et al. Five or fewer authors: all names at first citation, et al. thereafter. Multiple co-cited works listed alphabetically, separated by semicolons. Ampersands (&) used in parenthetical citations and reference list, but NOT in running text. Unpublished/personal work may be cited in text but not included in the reference list. All references must have a corresponding in-text citation and vice versa.

### Headings
Three levels, unnumbered at submission:
- **L1:** ALL CAPS, bold, center aligned (e.g., RESULTS)
- **L2:** Sentence case with main words capitalized, bold, left aligned (e.g., Descriptive Statistics and Correlation Matrix)
- **L3:** Sentence case, bold, italics, ending with a full stop, run-on with paragraph text (e.g., *Independent variable.* Text continues here…)

Note: Wiley's production typesetting adds numeric prefixes (1 |, 2.1 |, etc.) to headings in the published PDF. This is a production artifact; authors follow the unnumbered format above at submission.

### Tables and Figures
- Tables and figures submitted at end of manuscript (after references); not embedded in text.
- Each requires an in-text placement instruction: "***Insert Table 1 about here***"
- Table name placed ABOVE the table: **TABLE 1** Name of table
- Figure name placed BELOW the figure: **FIGURE 1** Name of figure
- Tables must be editable (not images/pictures)
- Figures: minimum 300 dpi; preferred formats .tiff and .png; prints in B&W, color online if submitted in color
- Appendix tables/figures use letters not numbers (Table A, Figure B)

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **No p-value asterisks** (*, **, ***) — exact p-values required instead
- **No data snooping or p-hacking** — explicitly named and prohibited; authors must not search for significant coefficients and then form hypotheses to fit them
- No author names, affiliations, or acknowledgements in the main document
- No citations in either abstract
- No Track Changes or comment bubbles visible in the submission
- Tables not submitted as images

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where GSJ deviates from the field baseline.

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that practice varies — "some require exact p-values or confidence intervals without asterisks; others permit conventional significance markers."

**GSJ practice:** Exact p-values and standard errors are required for all statistical studies; asterisks are explicitly prohibited. The guidelines direct discussion toward confidence intervals and interpretation of the probability of observed results. Effect sizes and material significance (magnitude) must be addressed and interpreted — not merely noted as expected, but required as a named policy. Endogeneity must be explicitly acknowledged if relevant; causal inference may still have value even when perfect identification is impossible, but the attempt must be documented.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (stated in guidelines with explicit prohibition on asterisks and explicit requirement for effect size interpretation).

### Dual Abstract — Managerial Summary
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes a "managerial implications" subsection within the Discussion, and notes that some outlets require a separate managerial abstract; it leaves this as journal-dependent.

**GSJ practice:** GSJ mandates both a research summary (≤125 words, academic) and a managerial summary (≤125 words, plain language for practitioners) as two standalone abstracts — not a section within the paper. The managerial summary must be written without academic jargon. This is structurally distinct from a "managerial implications" subsection.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (confirmed in published articles; manuscripts returned if missing).

### Voice and Register
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (first-person plural "we" dominant throughout, including single-authored papers in practice).

### Citation Density
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Structural Deviations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section sequence (empirical and conceptual papers).

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Omitting the managerial summary or writing it in academic language.** The requirement for a separate 125-word plain-language practitioner abstract is distinctive to GSJ and SMS journals. Authors coming from other STRAT outlets typically have no equivalent; the managerial summary must be genuinely accessible to non-academics, not a paraphrase of the research summary.
2. **Using p-value asterisks.** GSJ explicitly prohibits significance markers. Authors accustomed to asterisk tables must reformat all results tables and replace asterisks with exact p-values; reviewers from GSJ will flag this immediately.
3. **Reporting statistical significance without material significance.** The guidelines frame effect size interpretation as a distinct policy requirement. Reporting only p-values — even exact ones — without addressing the magnitude and practical importance of findings is non-compliant.
4. **Exceeding the 40-page total.** The 40-page limit includes references, tables, and figures. Authors calculating page counts for body text alone will undercount. References are single-spaced to help conserve space.
