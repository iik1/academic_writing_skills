# Journal Writing Skill: Accounting Forum

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis (Informa UK Limited)
- **Scope:** Broad interdisciplinary accounting journal. Publishes across all accounting sub-fields — financial, management, social and environmental, governance, taxation, auditing, ethics, accounting education, and accounting information systems. Explicitly welcomes both quantitative/archival and qualitative/interpretive/critical methodologies with equal standing.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/action/authorSubmission?journalCode=racc20&page=instructions (requires institutional login; submission via Editorial Manager at editorialmanager.com/racc)
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2
  - Ali, Adegbite & Nguyen (2021), "Ownership structure and political spending disclosure," 10.1080/01559982.2021.1929006 [archival/quantitative]
  - Lima et al. (2024), "Is it just a little flu?", 10.1080/01559982.2022.2149441 [critical/qualitative]

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> Guidelines page was not directly accessible (403 on Tandfonline; EZProxy requires active session). Constraints below are derived from observed articles and confirmed secondary sources. Items flagged PRACTICE ONLY are observed but not verified against the live guidelines document.

### Structure
No single prescribed section order. Quantitative/archival papers observed using: Introduction → Literature Review → Theoretical Framework & Hypotheses → Methodology → Results & Discussion → [Additional Analyses] → Conclusion. Qualitative/critical papers use thematic structures (Introduction → Theoretical Framework → Methodological Trajectory → Findings/Themes → Discussions and Conclusions). See Structural Deviations below.

### Length
Not confirmed from guidelines. Articles observed range from ~25–32 printed pages.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. A separate KEYWORDS field (approximately 5 terms) appears after the abstract as a distinct metadata field. No structured sub-headings (Purpose/Design/Findings) observed in either article. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Citations
APA author-date style. In-text: "(Author, Year)" or "Author (Year)". Multiple citations separated by semicolons. Reference list uses full APA format with DOIs appended. [ENFORCED — confirmed via secondary source and both articles]

### Headings
Arabic numeral decimal system: 1., 2., 2.1., 2.1.1. Title-case headings. No Roman numerals, no ALL-CAPS headings. [PRACTICE ONLY — observed consistently in both articles]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables include panel labels and variable definitions in notes.

### Explicit Prohibitions
Not confirmed (guidelines not accessible).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**Delta 1 — Separate Literature Review section (quantitative papers).**
FIELD_SKILL: Literature review is embedded within a single "Hypothesis Development / Theory / Background" section.
This journal: Archival/quantitative papers use a distinct standalone "Literature review" section (reviewing prior empirical findings) followed by a separate "Theoretical framework and hypotheses development" section (building the causal argument and stating formal hypotheses). These are treated as two sequential sections, not one merged section. Classification: PRACTICE ONLY.

**Delta 2 — Results and Discussion integrated; separate standalone Conclusion.**
FIELD_SKILL: "Discussion and Conclusion" are typically merged into a single final section.
This journal (archival paper): "Results and discussions" is one merged section mid-paper; a separate "Conclusion" section closes the paper. The qualitative paper uses "Discussions and conclusions" merged at the end, aligning with FIELD_SKILL. Not a universal pattern — author should check comparable articles in the target sub-field. Classification: PRACTICE ONLY.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**"ACCEPTED BY [editor name]" metadata field.**
Every published article displays the name of the handling editor (e.g., "ACCEPTED BY: Giovanna Michelon", "ACCEPTED BY: Leonardo Rinaldi") as part of the article header. This reflects the journal's multi-editor structure, where different editors handle different research strands (quantitative governance, critical/social accounting, etc.). Authors should consider which editor's remit their paper falls under when positioning the contribution, as the journal's editorial team is explicitly divided by sub-field. Classification: PRACTICE ONLY.

**Genuine methodological pluralism.**
Unlike the FIELD_SKILL baseline (which describes norms derived from quantitative-dominant ABS 4*/4 journals), Accounting Forum publishes critical, interpretive, and qualitative work as a first-class strand — not as a minority tradition. A meaningful proportion of articles use no quantitative methods at all. The FIELD_SKILL's guidance on AOS interpretive papers applies here to a broader range of papers. Authors submitting qualitative/critical work should not feel pressure to adopt quantitative framing conventions. Classification: PRACTICE ONLY (RELAXATION of baseline's quantitative-centric structural norms for non-quantitative submissions).

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Merging Literature Review into Hypothesis Development.** In quantitative submissions, reviewers at this journal appear to expect a freestanding Literature Review section that surveys the empirical landscape before the theory/hypotheses section begins. Collapsing these (as is common at JAE or JAR) may feel under-developed here.

2. **Ignoring the multi-editor structure.** The journal has distinct editors for different research traditions. A paper on social and environmental accounting submitted without acknowledging the critical accounting literature may be routed to a mismatched editor. Align the contribution statement with the relevant strand's intellectual community.

3. **Applying quantitative structural norms to qualitative work.** Accounting Forum is not a predominantly quantitative journal. Qualitative submissions should follow interpretive/critical structural conventions (theoretical framework, methodological trajectory, thematic findings) rather than the hypothesis-test structure of the field baseline.

4. **Assuming the journal's ABS 3 rating signals lower rigor.** The journal publishes genuinely theoretical and methodologically rigorous work across traditions. ABS 3 here reflects the journal's breadth and generalist positioning, not lower standards on any given methodological axis.
