# Journal Writing Skill: Scandinavian Journal of Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell (on behalf of the Scandinavian Journal of Economics)
- **Scope:** General-interest economics journal; accepts manuscripts in all areas of economics from international contributors. Also publishes Nobel Prize surveys and special thematic issues.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://sje.scholasticahq.com/for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Beņkovskis, Tkačevs & Vilerts, "Did job retention schemes save jobs during the COVID-19 pandemic?" SJE 128:73–103, 2026; Bastos, Monteiro & Straume, "The division of revenues from unexpected demand shocks," SJE 128:33–72, 2026)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No prescribed section order is mandated.

### Length
Manuscripts are **preferred to be 35–45 pages**, including references, figures, and tables. Formatting requirements: 12-point standard font (e.g., Times New Roman), margins of at least 2.5 cm (1 inch), true double-spacing (not 1.5-spacing), black ink only. Label: **GUIDELINE ONLY** (stated as a preference, not a hard limit).

### Abstract
**Unstructured, ≤100 words.** The title page must also include JEL classification codes and keywords as separate fields (not embedded in the abstract). Label: **ENFORCED** — confirmed in both spot-checked articles (abstracts are short, unstructured, with JEL codes and keywords listed below).

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical format. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Confirmed in both articles.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Both articles use numbered section headings (Arabic numerals).

### Tables and Figures
**Black-and-white only.** Guidelines state figures and tables must be in black-and-white, using standard solutions that can be easily understood by the reader. Label: **ENFORCED**. This applies at submission stage; color figures not permitted.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Color figures and tables are prohibited.
- Submissions must be PDF at submission stage (LaTeX/Word formatting guidelines apply post-acceptance).
- A **non-refundable submission fee of USD 135** is required at submission. This is unusual at this ABS tier and is worth flagging in cover letter planning.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Standalone Literature Review section in empirical papers:** FIELD_SKILL treats standalone Related Literature sections as more common in theory papers, typically omitted or integrated into the Introduction for empirical papers. Article 1 (Beņkovskis et al.) uses a standalone "Literature Review" as Section 2 in an empirical paper; Article 2 (Bastos et al.) integrates related literature as a labeled subsection within the Introduction. Both approaches are attested at this journal.

**Delta:** A standalone literature section at position 2 is an accepted practice for empirical papers at this journal, not merely a theory-paper convention as FIELD_SKILL implies. However, integration within the Introduction is equally attested.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY — observed in articles; not stated in guidelines.

**ABS note:** This is a RELAXATION of the implicit FIELD_SKILL norm (which treats standalone literature sections in empirical papers as less common). At ABS 3, this broader tolerance is expected.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 100 words.** The 100-word cap is strict and noticeably tighter than the implicit field norm. Drafts targeting top-5 journals often run 150–200-word abstracts; these must be cut substantially before submission.
2. **Color figures submitted.** The black-and-white requirement applies at submission. Converting figures late in preparation is error-prone; design figures in grayscale from the outset.
3. **Forgetting the submission fee.** The USD 135 fee is non-refundable. Ensure the paper clears an internal suitability check before submitting to avoid unnecessary cost.
4. **Omitting JEL codes or keywords from the title page.** Both are required as distinct fields on the title page, separate from the abstract body.
