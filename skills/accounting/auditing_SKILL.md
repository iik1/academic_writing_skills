# Journal Writing Skill: Auditing: A Journal of Practice & Theory

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** American Accounting Association (AAA)
- **Scope:** Publishes original research improving auditing theory or methodology, discussion of current issues in auditing practice, and surveys of developments in auditing and related fields. "Auditing" is interpreted broadly to include internal and external auditing and other attestation activities.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://aaahq.org/Research/Journals/Auditing-A-Journal-of-Practice-and-Theory/Guide-for-Authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Bowlin, Flint, Richardson, and Van Landuyt, "When Do Audit Clients' Stakeholders Prompt Auditor Leniency?" working paper May 2025, forthcoming AJPT)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section order. Level 1 headings are centered, bold, ALL-CAPS, numbered with Roman numerals (I. INTRODUCTION, II. …). Level 2 headings are flush left, bold, title case. Level 3 headings are flush left, bold, italic, title case. Level 4 headings are paragraph-indented, bold, lowercase. Confirmed in article: I. INTRODUCTION, II. BACKGROUND, THEORY, AND HYPOTHESES, III. RESEARCH DESIGN, IV. RESULTS, V. CONCLUSION. Final section titled "CONCLUSION" (not "Discussion and Conclusion"). **[ENFORCED]**

Surveys, instruments, questionnaires, and experimental materials must be submitted as a separate file with author-identifying information removed. **[ENFORCED]**

### Length
**7,000 words** for standard empirical/experimental/analytical papers (including references, figures, tables, and appendices). **12,000 words** for qualitative research papers. This is substantially tighter than the 55-page norm at other AAA journals and is the most important submission constraint this journal imposes. **[ENFORCED]**

### Abstract
- Maximum **150 words**; placed on a separate page immediately preceding the text
- Must be **nonmathematical** and **easily readable**
- Must explicitly **emphasize findings and implications for both practice and theory** — not just contribution to the literature
- Labeled inline as "**ABSTRACT:**" in bold (not a section heading)
- Keywords and JEL codes appear as separate labeled fields immediately after the abstract, along with a Data Availability statement
- **[ENFORCED]**

### Citations
Author-date style throughout. In parenthetical citations, all author surnames are spelled out for up to approximately four authors (e.g., "(Alicke, Bucking, Zell, and Davis 2008)"); "et al." is used for five or more authors. In prose, "et al." is used for three or more authors. No "and" between last two authors in parentheticals — surnames are comma-separated with "and" only before the final surname. **[PRACTICE ONLY — confirmed in article; AAA guidelines show examples only for 1–2 authors]**

### Headings
AAA Roman-numeral/ALL-CAPS Level 1 system (see Structure above). **[ENFORCED]**

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures are numbered with Arabic numerals and require complete titles. Each must be referenced in the text. Tables created in Word or Excel table editor. Figures submitted as separate image files (EPS, TIFF, PDF, JPEG, PNG). **[ENFORCED]**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Papers focused on **audit education** must be submitted to *Issues in Accounting Education* instead. **[ENFORCED]**
- No identifying author information anywhere in the manuscript file (title page submitted separately for double-blind review). **[ENFORCED]**
- No use of AI-generated text without disclosure; AI use must be described transparently. **[ENFORCED]**
- Gender-neutral language required throughout. **[ENFORCED]**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Contribution Framing (Practice-and-Theory Dual Frame)
**Field norm:** Contributions framed as empirical evidence, novel design, new data, theoretical insight, or practical implications — typically in a single contributions paragraph.

**This journal:** Because the journal's name and scope explicitly bridge practice and theory, contributions are expected to address both dimensions explicitly. In the spot-checked article, contributions are structured as: "From a theoretical perspective… From a practical standpoint…" with a separate sentence addressing auditing standards setters. Reviewers at this journal expect the manuscript to articulate what the findings mean for audit practitioners, regulators (PCAOB, IAASB, AICPA), or auditing standards — not just for the academic literature.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY — observed in article, implied by journal scope but not stated as a structural requirement in guidelines.

### Auditing Standards as Institutional Backdrop
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify how institutional context is handled.

**This journal:** Papers routinely situate their research question within current or proposed auditing standards (PCAOB, IAASB, AICPA). References to specific auditing standards (e.g., AS 2405, ISA 250) and standard-setting activity appear in the introduction and conclusion as part of motivation and implications, not just in methods. This is a domain practice that reviewers expect.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Abstract Content Requirement (Practice and Theory Emphasis)
**Field norm:** Abstract states research question, method, key findings, main contribution.

**This journal:** Abstract must explicitly emphasize implications for both practice and theory. This is a stated guideline requirement that goes beyond the field baseline's abstract norm.

**Classification:** ENFORCED — overrides FIELD_SKILL abstract norm where FIELD_SKILL omits this requirement.

### Word Limit
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify word/page limits.

**This journal:** 7,000 words (standard papers); 12,000 words (qualitative papers). This is one of the tightest word limits among accounting journals and frequently requires authors to move detailed robustness analyses to online appendices.

**Classification:** ENFORCED — not covered by FIELD_SKILL.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Exceeding 7,000 words.** Papers submitted over the word limit are likely desk-rejected or returned without review. Move robustness checks and extended variable definitions to appendices early in the writing process.
2. **Abstract focused only on theory.** The guidelines require emphasis on implications for *both* practice and theory. Abstracts that discuss only scholarly contribution will not meet the stated requirement.
3. **Missing practice implications in contributions.** Papers that position contributions solely within the academic literature — without addressing what findings mean for auditors, firms, regulators, or standards-setters — are misaligned with this journal's mission. The practice-and-theory framing should appear in the introduction, conclusion, and abstract.
4. **Submitting audit education papers.** The journal explicitly redirects audit education research to *Issues in Accounting Education*. Submitting such papers will result in immediate rejection.
