# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Wiley / American Finance Association
- **Scope:** Flagship journal of the AFA; publishes leading research across all major fields of financial economics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://afajof.org/submissions/ and https://afajof.org/wp-content/uploads/files/policies-and-guidelines/JF_Style_-_February_2017.pdf
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (DOI: 10.1111/jofi.13318 — "A q Theory of Internal Capital Markets," JF Vol. LXXIX No. 2, April 2024; NBER WP 31138 / JF forthcoming — "Deposit Franchise Runs")

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Manuscript order: (1) title page with author names and abstract, (2) conflict-of-interest disclosure statement (each author named individually), (3) manuscript body with internal appendices and references, (4) optional Internet Appendix (at end of same PDF, new page, labeled prominently). The manuscript must be self-contained without reliance on the Internet Appendix. The introductory section must emphasize incremental contributions; the article must end with a brief nontechnical summary statement of the main conclusions. [ENFORCED]

### Length
60 pages maximum. Includes internal appendices, reference lists, figures, and tables. Excludes disclosure statement pages and Internet Appendix. Exceeding this limit triggers automatic desk rejection. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, maximum 100 words, appears on the title page. Double-spaced, slightly indented, block-justified. No structured fields. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Parenthetical form uses double parentheses with no comma: `(Tufano (1996))` or `(Fama and French (1993))`. Inline form: `Tufano (1996)`. This differs from the FIELD_SKILL baseline which uses `(Fama and French, 1993)` with a comma. "Et al." applies when a source has **four or more** authors (not the common three-or-more threshold). References to data sources within the body or tables must be italicized. Research/consulting firms do not need to be referenced. Personal communications remain as footnotes. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Sections: **Roman numerals** (I, II, III, IV…). Subsections: **letters** (A, B, C…). Sub-subsections: letter-number (A.1., A.2., B.1., etc.). This is the opposite of the FIELD_SKILL baseline (Arabic numerals throughout). The introduction is **neither numbered nor titled**—no heading appears before the first paragraph. The first paragraph of the introduction is not indented; all subsequent paragraphs are indented. Section I is the first numbered section after the introduction. Appendices are lettered A, B, C; tables within an appendix are labeled AI, AII; figures and equations in an appendix are numbered A1, A2. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Tables are numbered with **Roman numerals** (Table I, Table II…) and must have a title and descriptive legend. The legend must define all variable notation and briefly explain the table. Table footnotes are **strictly limited to technical information** (significance levels); all descriptive information belongs in the legend. If the legend exceeds 300 words, lengthy construction details move to a separate definitions table or appendix. Portrait orientation, maximum 8 columns, 12-point text. Figures are numbered with **Arabic numerals** (Figure 1, Figure 2…) and must have a caption; use color figures for the online version. Location of tables and figures must be indicated in the text margin. [ENFORCED]

### Footnotes
The initial author footnote is marked with an asterisk (*) and appears at the bottom of the title page. The **total number of footnotes must not exceed the total number of pages** in the article. [ENFORCED]

### Reference List Format
References appear on a separate page at the end, unnumbered, double-spaced. Format: Last name, First name, year, title, journal volume, pages — with **no parentheses around the year** and **full first names required for all authors**. Example: `Scholes, Myron, 1991, Stock and compensation, Journal of Finance 46, 803–823.` Multi-author: `Wright, Brian D., and Jeffrey C. Williams, 1989, ...`. This is more specific than FIELD_SKILL describes. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Disclosure statements in separate files: prohibited
- Disclosure statements placed in acknowledgment footnotes: prohibited
- Submissions exceeding 60 pages: automatic desk rejection
- Resubmissions of previously rejected manuscripts: automatic rejection
- Simultaneous submission to another journal: prohibited
- Italics on Latin terms (e.g., *ceteris paribus* should appear as ceteris paribus) [ENFORCED]
- Starting a sentence with notation (i.e., variables) [ENFORCED]
- "See, e.g.,": use "See, for example," instead [ENFORCED]
- "i.e." in running text: use "that is," instead (parenthetical "(i.e., ...)" is acceptable) [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Section Numbering
**Field norm:** Arabic numerals throughout (1, 2, 3; 2.1, 2.2); Roman numerals appear "only rarely."
**JF practice:** Roman numerals for all sections (I, II, III); letters for subsections (A, B, C); letter-number for sub-subsections (A.1., A.2.). Confirmed in published articles. [ENFORCED]

### Introduction Heading
**Field norm:** "Introduction" or "1. Introduction" as an explicit heading.
**JF practice:** No heading and no number precede the introduction. The text begins immediately after the abstract. Confirmed in published articles. [ENFORCED]

### Citation Parenthetical Format
**Field norm:** `(Fama and French, 1993)` — comma between author and year.
**JF practice:** `(Fama and French (1993))` — double parentheses, no comma. Inline: `Fama and French (1993)`. Confirmed in published articles. [ENFORCED]

### Et al. Threshold
**Field norm (implicit):** Et al. commonly applied at three or more authors.
**JF rule:** Et al. applies at **four or more** authors. [ENFORCED]

### Citation Density in Introduction
**Field norm:** High; multiple citations per paragraph typical in the introduction.
**JF guideline:** "Citations should be kept to the minimum required to clarify and highlight your contributions."
**JF practice:** Published articles maintain standard high citation density in the introduction. [GUIDELINE ONLY — the written instruction conflicts with observed practice; treat as an aspirational editorial preference, not a structural constraint.]

### Table Footnotes vs. Legend
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Table notes document sample, dependent variable, unit of observation, clustering choices, and significance thresholds.
**JF practice:** Table footnotes are restricted to technical information only (significance levels). All sample descriptions, variable definitions, and methodological notes go in the legend. [ENFORCED]

### Table vs. Figure Numbering
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify whether tables and figures share a numbering system.
**JF rule:** Tables use Roman numerals; figures use Arabic numerals. They are numbered in separate sequences. [ENFORCED]

### Footnote Quantity Cap
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL imposes no footnote count limit.
**JF rule:** Total footnotes cannot exceed total page count. [ENFORCED]

### Conflict of Interest Disclosure
**Field norm:** Not described in FIELD_SKILL.
**JF rule:** Mandatory standalone disclosure section, placed immediately after the title page, naming each author individually. Cannot appear in the acknowledgment footnote. [ENFORCED]

### Reference List Format
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL specifies author-year format but not the precise reference list layout.
**JF rule:** Full first names for all authors; year placed after author names without parentheses; specific punctuation and ordering conventions (see Hard Constraints above). [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Arabic section numbers.** Submitting with "1. Introduction / 2. Data / 3. Results" will conflict with JF style. All sections need Roman numerals; the introduction gets no heading at all.

2. **Comma in parenthetical citations.** Writing `(Fama and French, 1993)` instead of `(Fama and French (1993))` is a common import from APA or other journals. The double-parenthesis, no-comma format is JF-specific.

3. **Overloaded table footnotes.** Authors from other journals habitually put sample period, clustering choices, and variable descriptions in table footnotes. JF restricts footnotes to significance notation; everything else must appear in the legend.

4. **Footnote proliferation.** Long empirical papers with many footnotes may inadvertently exceed the page-count cap. The number of footnotes must not exceed the number of pages in the manuscript.

5. **Disclosure statement placement.** Placing the conflict-of-interest disclosure in the acknowledgment footnote (standard practice at many journals) is explicitly prohibited. It must appear as a separate section immediately after the title page.
