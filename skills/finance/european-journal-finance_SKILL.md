# Journal Writing Skill: The European Journal of Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Routledge / Informa UK Limited (Taylor & Francis Group)
- **Scope:** Full range of theoretical and empirical topics in finance, with emphasis on European interests and concerns. Publishes both quantitative empirical work and institutional/regulatory finance.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/action/authorSubmission?journalCode=rejf20&page=instructions (requires institutional login; direct access blocked)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for canonical empirical structure. No evidence of mandatory section list in guidelines.

### Length
Not confirmed from guidelines. No explicit word or page limit could be retrieved.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. Keywords appear as a separate field. ARTICLE HISTORY (received/accepted dates) appears on the published page alongside the abstract — a Taylor & Francis platform standard, not an author-supplied field. No word limit confirmed. | Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (format confirmed; word limit unverified)

### Citations
**ENFORCED — DELTA from FIELD_SKILL.** The journal uses Taylor & Francis Chicago Author-Date style. Parenthetical in-text citations omit the comma between author and year: `(Cover and Kleit 2014)`, `(Dunham 2019; Prager 2014)`. FIELD_SKILL baseline uses `(Fama and French, 1993)` with a comma (APA/Harvard convention). At this journal, no comma is used. Non-parenthetical form: `Leyshon et al. (2004)` — year in parentheses, author outside.

Reference list format follows Chicago Author-Date:
`Lastname, F., and F. Lastname. Year. "Article Title." Journal Name Volume (Issue): pages.`
Example: `Allen, F., A. Demirguc-Kunt, L. Klapper, M. Soledad, and M. Peria. 2016. "The Foundations of Financial Inclusion." Journal of Financial Intermediation 27:1–30.`
DOIs are included where available. Multiple authors listed in full (no "et al." truncation in the reference list).

### Headings
Arabic numerals with period: `1. Introduction`, `2. Pertinent literature and hypothesis development`, `3. Data and methodology`. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on numbering convention.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Significance notation observed: `*` p<0.10, `**` p<0.05 (two-level). The third level (`***` p<0.01) was not present in the article checked; this may reflect author choice rather than a journal constraint.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None identified from accessible sources.

### Publisher-Mandated Fields (Taylor & Francis Platform)
The following sections appear in all published articles regardless of author instructions — they are required by the publisher:
- **Disclosure statement**: e.g., "No potential conflict of interest was reported by the author(s)."
- **Notes on contributors**: Short author bios (2–4 sentences each). This section is absent in ABS 4*/4 finance journals and is a Taylor & Francis requirement specific to this platform. Authors should prepare these.
- **ORCID**: Listed per contributing author where available.
Label: ENFORCED (platform standard)

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citations
**ENFORCED delta.** Chicago Author-Date format, no comma between author and year in parenthetical citations. FIELD_SKILL baseline reflects APA/Harvard convention with comma. Every in-text parenthetical citation at this journal omits the comma: `(Smith 2020)` not `(Smith, 2020)`. The reference list format also differs: year follows author directly (`Lastname, F. 2020.`), matching Chicago rather than APA conventions. Failure to match this style is immediately visible to editors and reviewers.

### Structural Deviations
**RELAXATION (ABS 3).** Two departures from ABS 4*/4 norms were observed in practice:

1. **Standalone literature section**: Section 2 was titled "Pertinent literature and hypothesis development" — a named, numbered section integrating the literature review and hypothesis derivation. FIELD_SKILL states that a standalone Literature Review section is "essentially never used" in ABS 4*/4 finance journals; at ABS 3, this structure appears to be acceptable. Label: PRACTICE ONLY

2. **Separate Discussion and Conclusions sections**: The article contained both a "5. Discussion" section and a "6. Conclusions" section. FIELD_SKILL notes that a separate Discussion section "is uncommon in mainstream empirical finance papers." At this journal, separating the two is observed practice. Label: PRACTICE ONLY / RELAXATION

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Notes on contributors** (ENFORCED by publisher): Author biographical notes appear in every published article. These are absent in top-tier finance journals. Authors submitting to this journal should draft 3–5 sentence bios covering their institutional affiliation and research interests.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Comma in citations**: Using `(Smith, 2020)` instead of `(Smith 2020)` reveals unfamiliarity with the journal's style. This is the most immediately visible formatting error and affects every citation in a typical paper.

2. **Omitting Notes on contributors**: This Taylor & Francis requirement is absent in ABS 4*/4 journals and is easy to forget. Missing contributor bios will require revision at the copy-editing stage.

3. **Merging Discussion into Conclusions**: While standard at ABS 4* level, EJF practice accommodates a separate Discussion section. Authors may use either structure; the separate structure is at least as common here as the merged one.

4. **Assuming APA/Harvard reference format**: The reference list format differs from APA: year position, punctuation conventions, and title quoting follow Chicago, not APA. Using a reference manager set to APA for this journal will produce incorrectly formatted entries.
