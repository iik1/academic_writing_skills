# Management Accounting Research — Journal-Specific Writing Skill

**Journal:** Management Accounting Research (MAR)
**Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
**ISSN:** 1044-5005
**ABS 2024:** 3
**Field:** ACCOUNT
**Scope:** International forum for management accounting research; explicitly welcomes archival, case study, experimental, field study, survey, analytical, and thought-piece formats.

---

## Hard Constraints (Elsevier-specific — not in FIELD_SKILL)

### Highlights — ENFORCED
Every submission must include **Highlights**: 3–5 bullet points (each ≤ 85 characters including spaces) summarizing the novel findings of the article. Place in a separate section of the manuscript file, labeled "Highlights," before the abstract. These are indexed by ScienceDirect and are not optional.

### Data Availability Statement — ENFORCED
A Data Availability statement is required in every published article. Place it as a labeled section before the References. When data are confidential (common in field/case research), an acceptable formulation is: *"The data that has been used is confidential."* FIELD_SKILL does not address this requirement.

### Inclusive Language — GUIDELINE ONLY
Elsevier's inclusive-language policy applies. Use gender-neutral terms; avoid language that implies assumptions about ability, age, ethnicity, or cultural background. No specific MAR enforcement mechanism observed in articles.

## Structural Deltas

### Heading Numbering — PRACTICE ONLY
Both sampled articles use **Arabic decimal numbering** throughout: top-level sections as `1.`, `2.`, `3.`; subsections as `2.1.`, `2.2.`; third-level as `2.1.1.` Heading text uses sentence case (capitalize first word and proper nouns only). Confirmed across quantitative-survey and qualitative case-study article types.

### Separate Discussion and Conclusion Sections — PRACTICE ONLY
FIELD_SKILL notes that Discussion and Conclusion are "typically merged" in top accounting journals. In MAR, qualitative and case-study articles routinely maintain **separate Discussion and Conclusion sections**. Observed structure in Vuorenmaa (2024): Section 5 = "Discussion," Section 6 = "Conclusions and suggestions for further studies." The Conclusion section in this format may explicitly invite future research directions, which is less common in FIELD_SKILL's archival-journal baseline.

For quantitative articles, Gerged et al. (2023) omits a standalone Discussion and moves directly from "4. Findings" to "5. Conclusion," closer to FIELD_SKILL norms. Method type appears to govern structure choice.

### Results Section Label — PRACTICE ONLY
The results section is labeled **"Findings"** (quantitative: "4. Findings"; qualitative: "4. Case study findings"), not "Results," "Empirical Analysis," or "Empirical Results." Use "Findings" as the default label unless the article type strongly implies otherwise.

## Voice Delta

### Single-Author Voice — PRACTICE ONLY
FIELD_SKILL notes that single-authored papers *may* use first-person "I." In the single-authored MAR article sampled (Vuorenmaa 2024), the author consistently uses **"this study" / "this paper"** rather than "I." The impersonal authorial stance appears conventional in MAR even for solo-authored work.

---

## Scope-Driven Relaxations

### Methods Scope — RELAXATION
FIELD_SKILL norms (built from TAR, AOS, JAE, JAR) emphasize identification strategy, causal inference language, and extensive robustness checks for archival work. MAR's stated scope explicitly encompasses case studies, field studies, surveys, analytical modeling, and thought pieces alongside archival methods. For non-archival formats, FIELD_SKILL's causal-inference conventions (instrumental variables, difference-in-differences framing, extensive robustness tables) are **not expected**. Match the methodological rigor norms to the method type, not to the archival default.

### Abstract Length — RELAXATION
No word limit for the abstract is stated in MAR's author guidelines. Observed range across sampled articles: ~160 words (quantitative) to ~280 words (qualitative). FIELD_SKILL's norm of tight, structured abstracts is not enforced here. Qualitative articles in particular tend toward longer, narrative abstracts. Abstracts are **unstructured** (no labeled IMRaD subheadings).

## Dimensions with No Delta (FIELD DEFAULT)

- **Voice for multi-authored papers:** "we" throughout — consistent with FIELD_SKILL.
- **Citation style:** Author-date in-text (Smith, 2020); full reference list at end — consistent with FIELD_SKILL. No numeric or footnote citations observed.
- **Hedging conventions:** Standard epistemic hedging ("suggests," "may indicate," "consistent with") — no observable departure from FIELD_SKILL.
- **Citation density:** Comparable to FIELD_SKILL norms; no evidence of sparser or denser practice.
- **Keywords:** 4–6 keywords required (Elsevier standard); placed after abstract.

---

## Qualitative-Specific Notes (Supplementary)

In qualitative/case-study articles, **direct interview quotations are presented in italics** as primary evidentiary units (not merely illustrative). Quotations are embedded within the findings narrative. An **Acknowledgments** section appears standard when institutional or grant support exists.
