# Journal Writing Skill: International Journal of Contemporary Hospitality Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Emerald Publishing
- **Scope:** Peer-reviewed research on the management of hospitality and tourism businesses worldwide, covering strategic management, operations, marketing, finance, and HR management.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.emeraldgrouppublishing.com/journal/ijchm (403 from external IPs; guidelines embedded in journal homepage)
- **Field baseline:** tourism-transport-hospitality-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1108/IJCHM-09-2023-1344 — quantitative time-lag survey; 10.1108/IJCHM-01-2024-0034 — text-mining/mixed methods)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for full research articles, with the exception that IJCHM also accepts a formally defined article type — **Critical Reflection Papers (CRPs)** — with its own structural requirements (see Length below). Regular articles follow the canonical Introduction → Literature Review → Method → Results → Discussion → Conclusion sequence.

### Length
- **Research articles:** Word limit not confirmed from publicly accessible guidelines; the Emerald guidelines page returned access errors. Authors should verify via the ScholarOne submission portal (https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ijchm).
- **Critical Reflection Papers (CRPs):** Maximum **7,000 words** including references, tables, figures, and appendices; maximum **30 references**; maximum **3 tables** and **3 figures**. Label: ENFORCED.

### Abstract
**Structured abstract, maximum 250 words.** Required and optional labeled fields (Emerald standard):
- Purpose *(required)*
- Design/methodology/approach *(required)*
- Findings *(required)*
- Research limitations/implications *(optional)*
- Practical implications *(included in both spot-checked articles — functionally expected)*
- Social implications *(optional)*
- Originality/value *(required)*

**Label: ENFORCED.** The FIELD_SKILL baseline does not specify the abstract as structured with named fields; this is a hard override. The inclusion of **Practical implications** as an explicit labeled abstract field — separate from Originality/value — is more specific than the field baseline implies and appeared in both spot-checked articles.

### Citations
**Emerald Harvard style** — not APA. The in-text format is identical to APA (author, year), but the reference list differs materially:

| Element | Emerald Harvard | APA |
|---|---|---|
| Multiple authors | "and" | "&" |
| Article titles | In double quotation marks | No quotation marks |
| Volume/issue | "Vol. X No. Y, pp. Z–ZZ" | "X(Y), Z–ZZ" |
| DOI prefix | "doi:" | "https://doi.org/" |

Label: ENFORCED. This overrides the FIELD_SKILL description of "Author–year (APA)"; the in-text format is the same but the reference list format is distinct.

### Headings
Decimal-numbered subsections (2.1, 2.2, 2.3; 3.1, 3.2, etc.) are consistently observed across spot-checked articles. Not explicitly confirmed in publicly accessible guidelines. Label: PRACTICE ONLY.

### Tables and Figures
CRPs: maximum 3 tables and 3 figures. Label: ENFORCED for CRPs. No additional constraint confirmed for regular articles beyond Emerald's standard word-count rule (each table or figure adds 280 words to the count).

### Explicit Prohibitions
No prohibitions beyond standard Emerald editorial policies could be confirmed from publicly accessible materials. CRP guidelines prohibit exceeding the 30-reference cap.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Discussion and Conclusion are typically separate sections in full research articles at the ABS 4 level.

**IJCHM practice (ABS 3 RELAXATION):** Both spot-checked articles merged Discussion and Conclusion under a single top-level heading, with implications and limitations appearing as numbered subsections within that merged section (e.g., "5.1 Conclusions / 5.2 Theoretical implications / 5.3 Practical implications / 5.4 Limitations and future research"). This reduces structural rigour relative to the field baseline but is consistent with ABS 3 norms.

Label: **RELAXATION** — observed in both spot-checked articles; consistent pattern, not an isolated case.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Critical Reflection Papers (CRPs):** IJCHM formally distinguishes CRPs as a separate article type with their own strict constraints (7,000 words, 30 references, 3 tables/figures). CRPs target critical synthesis of existing research, methods, or theories — not primary data collection. This article type is not addressed in the FIELD_SKILL baseline.

Label: **ENFORCED.**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using APA reference list format instead of Emerald Harvard.** The in-text citation looks the same, but the reference list is materially different: article titles must be in double quotation marks, "&" must become "and", and volume/issue format must follow "Vol. X No. Y, pp. Z–ZZ". Submitting an APA-formatted reference list is a formatting error.

2. **Omitting Practical implications from the structured abstract.** The abstract requires structured labeled fields; Practical implications appeared as an explicit field in both spot-checked articles and is functionally expected, not merely optional. An abstract that lacks this field is likely to be flagged.

3. **Submitting a CRP over the 7,000-word or 30-reference cap.** CRP length and reference limits are hard constraints. Authors conflating CRP requirements with standard article requirements risk immediate desk rejection.

4. **Treating Discussion and Conclusion as rigidly separate.** Unlike higher-ranked field journals, IJCHM articles routinely merge these into a single section with subsections. Reviewers are unlikely to penalise a merged section, but equally unlikely to require separation.
