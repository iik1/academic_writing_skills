# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Marketing

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MKT
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publishing / American Marketing Association
- **Scope:** Premier outlet for substantive research in marketing bridging theory and application. Publishes empirical (quantitative and experimental), conceptual, and methodological work with explicit managerial orientation.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/jmx
- **Field baseline:** mkt-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (journal is part of FIELD_SKILL baseline; access limitations prevented article review)

**Note:** Journal of Marketing is one of six ABS 4*/4 journals used to derive the MKT_FIELD_SKILL baseline. Deviations from field norms are minimal; this skill documents journal-specific hard constraints only.

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override or specify FIELD_SKILL where applicable.

### Structure
**ENFORCED**
- Minimum 2 files required: (1) Title page with author information, acknowledgments, statements; (2) Main document (title, abstract, keywords, text, references, appendices)
- Optional 3rd file: Web Appendix as separate PDF
- Main document page 1: Title, Abstract (200 words max), Keywords (up to 8)
- No "Introduction" heading for opening section (explicit prohibition)
- Maximum 3 heading levels throughout manuscript

### Length
**ENFORCED**
- **50 pages maximum** (inclusive of main text, tables, figures, footnotes, references, and print appendices)
- Applies at all review stages
- Web Appendix does NOT count toward 50-page limit

### Abstract
**ENFORCED**
- 200 words maximum (hard limit)
- Unstructured format
- Third-person voice required ("the authors," not "we")

### Keywords
**ENFORCED**
- Up to 8 keywords maximum

### Citations
**ENFORCED**
- Author-year format: (Author Year) or Author (Year)
- **Full first names** in reference list (not initials)
- Up to 3 authors: list all; 4+ authors: "first author et al." in text
- Multiple citations: alphabetical order, semicolon-separated
- References: alphabetical by first author surname
- Upon acceptance: up to 20 authors listed in references; 21+ use "et al."

### Headings
**ENFORCED**
- **Maximum 3 levels** (no more)
- **No section numbering** (explicit prohibition)
- Level 1 (Primary): Centered, title-case, bold, extra returns before/after
- Level 2 (Secondary): Flush left, title-case, bold + italic (minimum 2 per section required)
- Level 3 (Tertiary): Left-justified, sentence-case, italic, own line above paragraph
- **No "Introduction" heading** for opening commentary (explicit prohibition)
- No single secondary heading within a subsection (must have minimum 2)

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED**
- Placed within text at first mention (not at end)
- Consecutive numbering by mention order
- Titles must reflect take-away message
- **Tables: Actual *p*-values required** (not asterisks; e.g., *p* = .032)
- Tables: Standard errors must be included
- Tables: Must be editable Word format (not inserted as images)
- Figures: Actual *p*-values required
- Figures: Error bars where applicable
- Figures: Arial font preferred
- Figures: Both axes labeled; full y-axis scale range for experiments
- Color printing costs borne by authors; grayscale must work if color not paid for (20-80% levels, ≥20% difference between elements)

### Formatting
**ENFORCED**
- 12-point Times New Roman font throughout (including references)
- Double-spaced (tables and references may be single-spaced)
- 1-inch margins all sides
- No page numbers, headers, or footers on submission

### Footnotes
**ENFORCED**
- Sparse use: typically ≤10 total
- Maximum 40 words per footnote

### Mathematical Notation
**ENFORCED**
- Centered equations; numbered equations in parentheses flush right
- Variables in roman (not italic/bold), except: italic *p* (probability), italic *l* (variable), bold matrices/vectors
- Abbreviations: SD, SE, M (standardized)
- No leading zero (.97 not 0.97)
- Maximum 3 decimal places
- Actual *p*-values, standard errors, and effect sizes required

### Language and Tone
**ENFORCED**
- Active voice preferred over passive
- Third-person in abstract; first-person plural ("we") in main text follows FIELD_SKILL
- **Inclusive language requirements:**
  - Singular "they" (not "he/she")
  - "Race and ethnicity" (not "race/ethnicity")
  - Full gender demographics provided in text
  - Avoid gendered or biased language
- AI-generated content must be identified in Methods and noted in Acknowledgments; AI tools cannot be listed as authors

### Author Anonymity
**ENFORCED**
- Double-anonymized peer review
- No identifying information in main document or Web Appendix
- General location references only ("large public university," "Fortune 500 company")
- No geographic disclosure of data collection sites

### Web Appendix (if used)
**ENFORCED**
- Separate PDF file only
- First page: title "Web Appendix(ix)", paper title, table of contents with page numbers, disclosure statement
- Author names/contact added only upon acceptance
- Times New Roman 12-point; same heading styles as main paper
- Multiple sections: labeled "Web Appendix A," etc., each on new page
- Page numbers required (1, 2, 3...)
- Tables/figures: "Table W1," "Figure W2," etc.
- Equations: W1, W2, WN sequence
- Does NOT count toward 50-page limit
- Must be mentioned in main text; callouts in alphanumeric order
- No copyediting by journal; must be publication-ready

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED**
- No submissions via mail or email (online portal only)
- No page numbers, headers, or footers on submission
- No table images (must be editable Word tables)
- No more than 3 heading levels
- No section numbering
- No "Introduction" heading
- No single secondary heading within a subsection
- No asterisk notation for significance (use actual *p*-values)
- No leading zeros in statistics
- Self-plagiarism prohibited (reusing own published work without citation)

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

**Note:** Full-text articles were inaccessible due to paywall restrictions. Analysis based on author guidelines only. Journal of Marketing is part of the FIELD_SKILL baseline, so no deviations are expected in practice.

### Statistical Reporting
**ENFORCED (guidelines) / Likely PRACTICE ONLY (to be verified)**

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Marketing Science uses asterisks (***/**/*); behavioral journals (JCR, JCP) use APA with actual p-values.

**This journal:** Author guidelines explicitly require actual *p*-values (not asterisks) in both tables and figures. This aligns with the behavioral/experimental strand of MKT_FIELD_SKILL rather than the Marketing Science econometric strand.

**Classification:** ENFORCED — Guidelines state: "Actual *p*-values required (not asterisks)."

**How to apply:** In all results tables and figures, report exact p-values (e.g., *p* = .032) rather than significance stars. Include standard errors in tables.

### Abstract Voice
**ENFORCED**

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Abstracts typically unstructured; voice not specified.

**This journal:** Third-person voice required in abstract ("the authors," not "we").

**Classification:** ENFORCED — Stated explicitly in guidelines.

**How to apply:** Write abstracts in third person even though main text uses "we." Example: "The authors find that..." rather than "We find that..." in abstract only.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding the 50-page limit.** This is a hard cap inclusive of everything except Web Appendix. Count carefully; Web Appendix material does not save main-text pages unless content is truly supplemental.

2. **Using asterisks for significance levels in tables.** Guidelines explicitly require actual *p*-values. Reporting "***p < .001" will be flagged. Report "*p* = .008" instead.

3. **Including an "Introduction" heading.** The guidelines explicitly prohibit this. Begin main text immediately after keywords with no heading.

4. **Exceeding 200 words in abstract.** This is a hard limit enforced at submission. Ruthlessly edit abstracts to fit.

5. **Using "we" in the abstract.** Abstract must be third person ("the authors find...") even though main text uses "we" throughout.

6. **Single secondary headings.** If you use a Level 2 heading within a section, you must have at least two. A single secondary heading is prohibited.

7. **Forgetting to anonymize the main document.** Even obvious self-citations like "As we showed in Author (2023)..." must be generalized to "As prior work has shown..." with no identifying details. Check affiliations, acknowledgments, and geographic specifics.

8. **Inserting tables as images.** Tables must be editable Word tables. Screenshots or pasted images will be rejected.
