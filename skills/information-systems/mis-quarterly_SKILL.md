## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are mandatory requirements from official submission guidelines.

### Structure
**First section:** Must have a descriptive heading and is NEVER labeled "Introduction". (ENFORCED - stated in guidelines)

**Final section:** Not specified in guidelines for main MIS Quarterly (MISQ Executive uses "Concluding Comments").

**Section organization:** Authors should frame technical material accessibly; specialized terms must be defined clearly and briefly; technical material should be in appendices where possible. (GUIDELINE ONLY)

### Length
**Multiple submission categories with different limits:**
- Research Articles: Specific limit not confirmed (guidelines state "maximum lengths include ALL content")
- Research Notes: Half the length of research articles (GUIDELINE ONLY)
- Theory-Generative Research Synthesis: 65 pages maximum (GUIDELINE ONLY)
- Theory Development: 55 pages maximum (GUIDELINE ONLY)

**Critical:** All page/word limits include text, tables, figures, references, AND appendices. Submissions exceeding limits may be returned or rejected. All primary evidence and main claims must exist within manuscript page limits. (ENFORCED)

**Supplementary Materials:** Generally discouraged. (GUIDELINE ONLY)

### Abstract
**Length:** 150-300 words (typically 6-12 sentences based on analysis of published articles) (PRACTICE ONLY - observed but not in guidelines)

**Format:** Unstructured but follows a consistent four-part discourse pattern (PRACTICE ONLY):
1. Sentence 1: Introduce the topic and establish importance
2. Sentence 2 (often): Identify theoretical or empirical gap
3. Sentence 3+: Describe research approach/methodology addressing the gap
4. Middle sentences: Provide empirical/technical specifics
5. Final sentence: Articulate implications "for researchers and practitioners"

**Theory requirement:** Theory is mandatory for MISQ publication. Abstracts typically reference named theoretical frameworks. (ENFORCED - confirmed across sources)

### Citations
**Style:** Parenthetical author-date format (ENFORCED)

**In-text format:**
- Single author: (Paz 2003)
- Two authors: (Chi and Bernstein 2009)
- Three or more: (Xu et al. 2014)
- Multiple sources: (Chi and Bernstein 2009; Paz 2003)

**Reference list format:**
- **Journal articles:** Author(s). Year. "Article Title," *Journal Title* (Volume:Issue), pp. page range.
- **Books:** Author(s). Year. *Book Title*, Location: Publisher.
- **Book chapters:** Author(s). Year. "Chapter Title," in *Book Title*, Editor(s) (eds.), Location: Publisher, pp. page range.
- Article titles in quotation marks with sentence case
- Journal/book names in italics
- All authors listed in references (no et al.)

### Headings
First section must use descriptive heading, never "Introduction". No other specific heading conventions documented. (ENFORCED for first section only)

### Tables and Figures
All figures and tables must be numbered sequentially and explicitly mentioned in the text. (GUIDELINE ONLY - not verified in articles)

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Do NOT submit work already published elsewhere (ENFORCED)
- Do NOT submit work under review at another journal (ENFORCED)
- Do NOT exceed page limits with any content, including appendices (ENFORCED)
- Do NOT use supplementary materials for primary evidence (ENFORCED)
- Do NOT submit underdeveloped papers; review previous issues first (GUIDELINE ONLY)

## Common Pitfalls

Based on editorial guidance and submission guidelines:

1. **Labeling first section "Introduction":** Explicitly prohibited. Use a descriptive heading that reflects the content.

2. **Submitting without reading recent issues:** Guidelines strongly recommend reviewing recent MISQ articles to understand quality expectations and conventions.

3. **Exceeding page limits with "supplementary materials":** All primary evidence must be within the manuscript. Supplementary materials are discouraged.

4. **Writing for specialists only:** MISQ requires accessibility to a broad IS audience. Define specialized terms; move technical details to appendices.

5. **Weak theoretical grounding:** Theory is non-negotiable at MISQ. Abstracts and introductions must engage with established theoretical frameworks.

6. **Missing practitioner implications:** Abstracts typically conclude with implications "for researchers and practitioners" - omitting practitioner relevance may weaken submission.

## Research Notes

**Key insight from Abe Handler's analysis:** "Sentence one of an MISQ abstract often introduces the topic of the paper... Sentence two identifies either a theoretical gap or an empirical gap... Theory is a requirement for publishing in IS, especially at MISQ."

**Critical requirement:** Maximum lengths include ALL content. No exceptions for references or appendices.

**Review obligation:** By submitting, authors agree to review up to 3 papers for MISQ within one year if invited.

**Transparency requirement:** MISQ requires authors to declare research transparency during submission, documenting study design, data, and analysis appropriately for the research genre.
