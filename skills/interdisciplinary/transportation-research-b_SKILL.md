# Journal Writing Skill: Transportation Research, Series B: Methodological

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Publishes papers on methodological aspects of transportation systems, explicitly requiring mathematical analysis. Covers traffic flow theory, network design and optimization, queuing, logistics, statistical modelling, and transportation economics. Non-mathematical papers are outside scope.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/transportation-research-part-b-methodological/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** tourism-transport-hospitality-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (DOI: 10.1016/j.trb.2026.103461; DOI: 10.1016/j.trb.2026.103460; arXiv:2505.24029, published TR-B vol. 201, 2025)

No additional mandatory section order stated in accessible guidelines. A standalone Literature Review section is absent in all spot-checked articles.

### Length

### Citations
**Numbered brackets — ENFORCED delta from FIELD_SKILL.**

The field baseline states "Author–year (APA) is used across all journals in this field." This journal uses numbered citation format [1], [2], [3] throughout. Confirmed in all three spot-checked articles. The in-text citation is a bracketed integer; the reference list is numbered sequentially in order of first appearance.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Multi-level numbered headings (1, 2, 2.1, 2.1.1) observed in spot-checked articles.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Equations are numbered sequentially and referenced by number in the text.

### Highlights
Required (standard Elsevier practice). Four highlights observed in each of the two ScienceDirect articles; Elsevier standard is 3–5 bullets, each a maximum of 85 characters excluding spaces. Each bullet states a core finding or contribution concisely.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Simultaneous submission to another journal is prohibited.
- Authorship changes after acceptance are not permitted without editor approval and written confirmation from all authors.

### Mandatory Declarations (Required Before or With Submission)
- Generative AI use disclosure: a dedicated statement required in the manuscript, placed before the references section.
- Competing interests declaration (via Elsevier declarations tool).
- Funding sources statement.
- Inclusive language throughout: avoid bias, stereotypes, and gendered constructions.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Style — ENFORCED
- **Field norm:** Author–year (APA) throughout.
- **This journal:** Numbered brackets [1], [2]. In-text citations are integer references in square brackets; reference list is numbered in order of appearance, not alphabetical.
- **Classification:** ENFORCED. Confirmed in all three spot-checked articles.

### Scope Narrowing — PRACTICE ONLY
- **Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes the field as spanning multiple methodological cultures including qualitative, survey-based, experimental, and mathematical modelling papers.
- **This journal:** Exclusively mathematical/methodological. The guide explicitly states it publishes papers "particularly those that require mathematical analysis." All conventions in FIELD_SKILL that apply to qualitative papers, multi-study experimental papers, or survey-based papers (measurement scales, CFA, grounded theory, participant quotation) are irrelevant to this journal. Only the mathematical modelling sub-conventions from FIELD_SKILL apply here.
- **Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (scope stated in guidelines; implications for which FIELD_SKILL sub-conventions apply is an inference).

### Formal Mathematical Statements — PRACTICE ONLY
- **Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes mathematical modelling papers use "formal definitions, lemmas, proofs, and propositions" but does not specify their prevalence.
- **This journal:** Named formal statements (e.g., Proposition 1) with in-text proof or reference to proof appear in spot-checked articles. Numbered equations referenced by number in prose are universal. This is the expected mode of presenting analytical results; verbal narration alone is insufficient for theoretical contributions.
- **Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using author-year citations.** The field baseline and APA convention do not apply here. Submitting with "(Smith, 2020)" in-text citations will require reformatting to numbered brackets [1].
2. **Submitting non-mathematical work.** The journal's explicit scope restriction to papers requiring mathematical analysis means that empirical papers relying solely on statistical regression, survey instruments, or qualitative methods will be desk-rejected regardless of quality.
3. **Omitting mandatory declarations.** AI use disclosure (placed before references), competing interests, and funding statements are required for all submissions; missing any of these is a common administrative rejection trigger.
4. **Presenting theoretical results without formal structure.** Analytical results (existence, optimality, convergence properties) should be stated as named formal elements (Proposition, Theorem, Lemma) rather than embedded informally in prose.
