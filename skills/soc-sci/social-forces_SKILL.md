# Journal Writing Skill: Social Forces

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (with UNC Chapel Hill Department of Sociology)
- **Scope:** General sociological inquiry with an interdisciplinary reach into psychology, anthropology, political science, history, and economics. Targets a broad social science readership.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (10.1093/sf/soaf069; 10.1093/sf/soaf068; 10.1093/sf/soaf048)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm, with one addition: a dedicated **Methods section is required** for any manuscript involving human subjects research. [ENFORCED]

### Length
**10,000 words maximum**, counting all text, endnotes, and references together. This is a tight all-inclusive cap. [ENFORCED]

**Maximum 10 panels** total across all tables and figures combined. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No mandated word limit. Guidelines frame it permissively: "summarize the article's quest with or without conclusions." References must not appear in the abstract. In practice, abstracts run approximately 150–200 words. [ENFORCED: format; no word ceiling]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style, 17th edition, author-date system. Spell out all author first names in the reference list. **Page numbers are required only for direct quotations**—not for paraphrase or general citations. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Use headings; avoid abbreviations within heading text. Hierarchical structure (H2–H4) is supported. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
**Avoid vertical lines in tables.** Tables must be submitted in editable format. Figures require .eps, .ai, .pdf, or .tiff at minimum 72 DPI. Alt text required for all visual elements. [ENFORCED: no vertical lines; GUIDELINE ONLY: format specs]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No simultaneous submission elsewhere
- No ghost authorship
- No excessive self-citation or citation manipulation
- No page-number citations except for direct quotations

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Notes Format
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify footnotes vs. endnotes.
**Social Forces practice:** All three spot-checked articles use **footnotes** (not endnotes), despite the guidelines referring to "endnotes" when defining the word count. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Length Constraint
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify word limits.
**Social Forces:** 10,000-word all-inclusive cap (text + endnotes + references) is notably tight for quantitative work with extensive robustness tables. Authors routinely move supplementary material to a separate online appendix, which does not count toward the limit. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Exceeding the word cap.** The 10,000-word limit is all-inclusive (text + endnotes + references). Writers who draft to a "typical sociology" length of 12,000–14,000 words will need substantial cuts or must move material to an online appendix.
2. **Over-citing with page numbers.** Chicago author-date at Social Forces requires page numbers only for direct quotations. Adding page numbers to paraphrased citations departs from house style.
3. **Vertical lines in tables.** The guideline explicitly prohibits them; tables imported from statistical software often include them by default.
4. **Panel count creep.** The 10-panel cap covers tables and figures jointly. Quantitative papers with multiple robustness specifications must plan the table budget early and use combined or appendix panels where possible.
