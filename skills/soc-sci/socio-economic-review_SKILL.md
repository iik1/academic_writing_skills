# Journal Writing Skill: Socio-Economic Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (on behalf of the Society for the Advancement of Socio-Economics)
- **Scope:** Comparative and institutional research on the relationships between economy, polity, and society; welcomes quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-methods work. Strongly interdisciplinary, spanning economic sociology, comparative political economy, and heterodox economics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/ser/pages/Guidelines_For_Authors
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1093/ser/mwae059; 10.1093/ser/mwaf033)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandatory section sequence prescribed beyond standard scholarly conventions. Three separate files required at submission: (1) anonymous title page with author details, word count, and acknowledgements; (2) anonymous main document with title, abstract, keywords, JEL codes, and body; (3) each table and figure as a separate file.

### Length
6,000–10,000 words including references, excluding abstract, tables, figures, and appendices. Both the floor and ceiling are binding: manuscripts outside this range will be returned. Tables and figures should not require more than 4 additional journal pages (editors may grant exceptions). [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; maximum 150 words. No structured fields (Background, Methods, etc.). [ENFORCED]

### Keywords
Two keyword sets are required: (a) up to 6 SER-specific keywords selected from the journal's designated keyword list (downloadable PDF); (b) up to 3 JEL classification codes, formatted as the code followed by its label (e.g., "J31 Wage level and structure"). Free-choice keywords are not accepted; authors must use SER's keyword vocabulary. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date system throughout. Guidelines explicitly require attention to citation accuracy. [ENFORCED — consistent with FIELD_SKILL norm]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (no journal-prescribed heading style stated in guidelines). In practice, numbered sections and subsections (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2, etc.) appear in both quantitative and qualitative articles reviewed. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
Each table and figure submitted as a separate file (Word, Excel, or EPS). Numbered in Arabic numerals and referenced by number in text. Alt text required for each figure, placed below the figure legend with the prefix "Alt text:". Colour figures published online at no charge. Tables/figures should not exceed 4 additional journal pages. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Footnotes and endnotes are prohibited.** Material that would normally appear in notes must be integrated into the main text or placed in a clearly labelled appendix. [ENFORCED — confirmed by absence in all reviewed articles]
- Submission as PDF is prohibited; files must be .doc or .rtf.
- Previously published work is not accepted (working papers permitted under specific conditions; working paper versions cannot be updated after journal acceptance).
- Self-references must be removed from the anonymous main document.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** No specific heading numbering convention is prescribed.
**This journal:** Both reviewed articles use a numbered heading hierarchy (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2, etc.) across quantitative and qualitative work. This appears to be the prevailing practice regardless of methodology. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**No footnotes or endnotes:** FIELD_SKILL does not address footnotes; SER prohibits them entirely. Substantive qualifications, methodological asides, and data details that would typically appear in footnotes must be woven into the main text or moved to an appendix. This has a real effect on prose density: methods sections and robustness discussions carry more weight than at journals that permit extensive footnoting. [ENFORCED]

**Dual keyword requirement:** SER requires both a domain-specific keyword set (from its own controlled vocabulary list) and JEL codes. JEL codes are unusual for a primarily sociological outlet; they reflect the journal's positioning at the economics–sociology interface. Authors from sociology backgrounds unfamiliar with JEL classification should consult the AEA's JEL code browser before submission. [ENFORCED]

**Minimum word count:** The 6,000-word floor is a binding constraint uncommon in social science journals. Short empirical letters or research notes do not fit this outlet. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Footnotes.** The prohibition is absolute. Authors accustomed to journals that allow extensive substantive footnotes (e.g., political science outlets) will need to restructure their argumentation. Budget word count for methodological qualifications that would otherwise live in notes.
2. **Wrong keywords.** SER requires selection from its own keyword list PDF, not free-choice terms. Submitting arbitrary keywords will trigger a revision request before the paper enters review.
3. **Missing JEL codes.** Required regardless of methodological tradition. Sociologists and human geographers unfamiliar with JEL classification routinely omit them.
4. **Word count including references.** The 10,000-word ceiling includes the reference list. Dense citation practice (common in the literature-review-heavy style SER publishes) can push a manuscript over limit; budget accordingly.
