# Journal Writing Skill: Games and Economic Behavior

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** General-interest game theory and its applications across social, behavioral, mathematical, and biological sciences. Accepts work from adjacent fields (IO, computer science, biology) when it advances game-theoretic understanding. ~15% acceptance rate; roughly one-third desk-rejected.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/games-and-economic-behavior/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Balzer & Schneider 2023, GEB 73:2 [arXiv:2201.01149]; Hart & Nisan 2018, GEB 108 [arXiv:1305.4874])

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandated section order beyond standard article elements (title page, abstract, keywords, body, references). A Declaration of Generative AI Use is required before the references if AI tools were used in manuscript preparation.

### Length
Not explicitly stated in accessible guidelines. Spot-checked articles run 23–25 pages including references, consistent with a compact theory-paper norm, but no hard limit was found.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. JEL classification codes are required and appear immediately after the abstract text (not on a cover page). Observed length: approximately 100–130 words. No stated word limit found in guidelines.
**Label:** ENFORCED (JEL codes); PRACTICE ONLY (word length)

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical in text; alphabetical reference list. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Elsevier's standard economics reference format applies: Author, F. (year), "Title," *Journal* Volume, pages.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (Arabic numerals for sections and subsections). Bold run-in paragraph headings within sections are common in published theory papers and are not prohibited.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Language Requirements
- **Inclusive language** is required: avoid assumptions based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability; use plural nouns as a default for gender neutrality. **Label: ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- No authorship changes after acceptance.
- Papers must not have been previously published (preprints and theses excepted).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Roadmap Paragraph
**Field norm:** A section-by-section roadmap at the end of the introduction is near-universal ("The paper proceeds as follows…").
**GEB practice:** Both spot-checked articles omit the roadmap entirely. The introduction previews main results but does not enumerate sections. Given GEB's compact, theory-focused papers, the roadmap appears to be treated as optional filler rather than a required element.
**Label:** RELAXATION (PRACTICE ONLY — not stated in guidelines; observed in 2/2 spot-checked articles)

### Conclusion Section
**Field norm:** A conclusion section is expected; it restates findings, draws implications, acknowledges limitations, and suggests future work.
**GEB practice:** One article replaces the conclusion with a brief "Final Remarks" section (~1 page, no new results, no limitations paragraph); the other article has no conclusion section at all — the paper ends with the final proof and references. A separate Discussion section is absent in both.
**Label:** RELAXATION (PRACTICE ONLY — not stated in guidelines; observed in 2/2 spot-checked articles)

## Dimensions With No Delta
- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural throughout)
- Register and formality: FIELD DEFAULT (high formality; formal theorem/proof environments in theory papers as noted in baseline)
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT
- Statistical reporting: FIELD DEFAULT (not applicable to theory papers; quantitative claims stated precisely)
- Hedging intensity: FIELD DEFAULT (low; derived results stated directly)
- Positioning relative to prior work: FIELD DEFAULT (explicit comparison; "Related Literature" typically handled as bold run-in heading within introduction rather than standalone section, which is within baseline norms)

---

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Including a roadmap paragraph.** GEB theory papers routinely omit it; including one may read as padding in a compact journal where editors value density.
2. **Writing a full conclusion section.** GEB papers often end with brief "Final Remarks" or no conclusion at all. A long conclusion restating results already previewed in the introduction is inconsistent with observed practice.
3. **Omitting JEL codes.** They are required and belong immediately after the abstract text, not only on a cover page.
4. **Scope mismatch.** Editors explicitly require authors to explain how a submission advances game theory's frontiers; industrial organization or applied theory papers must frame their contribution in game-theoretic terms to pass the desk-rejection filter.
