## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict. Based on author guidelines only; article practices not verified.

### Structure
**ENFORCED**: Standard empirical structure expected: Abstract, Introduction, [Hypotheses if applicable], Methods, Results, Discussion, References. Appropriate headings should appear throughout (e.g., Methods, Results, Discussion). Headings must NOT be numbered.

### Length
**GUIDELINE ONLY**: Most manuscripts should be shorter than 50 pages including references, tables, and figures. Exceptions made for meta-analyses and multi-study reports. Articles deemed too long for their contribution will be returned without review.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED**: 
- Maximum 200 words
- Unstructured format
- Must briefly summarize: purpose, hypotheses, methods, key findings, and implications
- Followed by up to 3 keywords (required)

### Citations
**ENFORCED**: APA Publication Manual (7th edition) style. Author-date method for in-text citations (author's last name and year). DOI numbers must be included for all referenced articles.

### Headings
**ENFORCED**: Headings must not be numbered. Use APA-style hierarchy without numbers.

### Tables and Figures
**GUIDELINE ONLY**: Must be included in the blinded submission. No specific formatting requirements stated beyond APA guidelines.

### Format Requirements
**ENFORCED**:
- Double-spaced
- 12-point Times New Roman font
- Manuscript must be blinded for double-blind review
- Remove all identifying information
- Free format submission accepted for initial submission (.doc, .docx, or .rtf)
- Single document including abstract, keywords, main text, references, tables, figures, appendices

### Data Sharing
**ENFORCED**: Authors must provide a data availability statement describing the availability or absence of shared data. Data sharing encouraged via publicly available repositories unless prevented by ethical, privacy, or confidentiality concerns. Not mandatory but expected.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Numbered headings (explicitly prohibited)
- Identifying information in blinded manuscripts
- Missing DOIs in reference list

## Common Pitfalls

**GUIDELINE-DERIVED** (not verified from editorial desk rejections or published guidance):

1. **Exceeding abstract limit**: 200-word cap is strict. Authors accustomed to 250-300 word abstracts may exceed limit.

2. **Numbered headings**: Explicitly prohibited but common in other journals. Authors using templates from other venues may include numbers.

3. **Missing DOIs**: Explicitly required for all referenced articles. Easy to overlook for older sources.

4. **Inadequate blinding**: Double-blind review requires removing all identifying information. Common errors: dataset names, institutional details, self-citations that reveal identity.

5. **Length without contribution**: Returning papers "too long for their contribution" suggests editors actively screen for efficiency. Verbose writing or excessive peripheral content may trigger desk rejection.
