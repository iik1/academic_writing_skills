# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of the European Economic Association

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (European Economic Association)
- **Scope:** General-interest economics journal publishing high-quality theoretical and empirical research across all fields of economics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/jeea/pages/Author_Guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Halvorsen et al. 2024 vol. 22; Yotzov et al. 2025 vol. 24)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific deviations observed.

### Length
**ENFORCED**
- Maximum 40 pages including figures, tables, and references (1.5 line spacing)
- Alternative: 50 pages maximum for double-spaced manuscripts
- 12-point font required (Times New Roman or similar)
- 1-inch margins on all sides

### Abstract
Single-paragraph, unstructured format. Observed abstracts ranged 110-160 words; specific word limit not stated in accessible guidelines. **PRACTICE ONLY**

### Citations
Author-year format. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

**ENFORCED**
- Unpublished papers and manuscripts must include institutional affiliation in citation

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Both Arabic and Roman numerals acceptable; Arabic numerals with decimal subsections observed in practice (1, 2, 3; 2.1, 2.2, etc.).

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED**
- Figure captions must appear below the figure, with notes integrated into the caption
- Table captions must appear above the table; notes appear below the table
- Alt text required for all images, graphs, and tables (accessibility requirement)

### Mathematical Notation
**ENFORCED**
- All math letters must be consistently italicized throughout the paper, including in figures and tables
- Fractions should use slash notation with parentheses: (numerator)/(denominator), e.g., (x + 1)/(y + 8)

### Language and Style
**ENFORCED**
- Papers must be in English
- Use double quotation marks, not single
- Latin expressions (e.g., "et al.") should NOT be italicized

### Data and Replication
**ENFORCED (journal-specific, highly stringent)**
- JEEA will publish papers only if data are clearly and precisely documented, systematically checked by the JEEA Data Editor, and readily available for replication
- Authors of accepted papers with empirical work, simulations, or experimental work must provide data, code, and computational details sufficient for replication prior to publication
- The JEEA Data Editor verifies replication; formal acceptance contingent on successful verification

### Author Contributions
**ENFORCED**
- Authors encouraged to state how author order was determined
- Random author order symbol (ⓡ) encouraged where applicable
- Contribution statements encouraged; required for papers with 5 or more coauthors

### Submission Format
**ENFORCED**
- PDF format only for initial submission
- Membership in European Economic Association required to submit
- Submission fee applies

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Falsification or fabrication of data
- Plagiarism, including duplicate publication of authors' own work without proper citation
- Misappropriation of others' work

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Data availability non-compliance**: JEEA's data editor verification is unusually stringent for the field. Authors must provide replication packages prior to formal acceptance, not at publication. Failure to prepare data and code for verification will block acceptance even after conditional acceptance.

2. **Page limit miscalculation**: The 40-page limit applies to 1.5-spaced manuscripts with 12-point font and 1-inch margins, including all figures, tables, and references. Authors accustomed to journals with separate appendix limits should note that JEEA's limit is comprehensive.

3. **Math notation inconsistency**: Math letters must be italicized everywhere, including figure axes and table headers. Inconsistency between main text and figures is a common source of revision requests.

4. **Missing institutional affiliations in citations**: Unpublished papers, working papers, and manuscripts must cite institutional affiliation. Author-year format alone is insufficient for unpublished sources.
