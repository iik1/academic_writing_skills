## Hard Constraints — Deviations from Field Baseline

### 1. Abstract Format — PRACTICE ONLY

**What EAR does**: Abstract is headed by the word "ABSTRACT" set in small caps as an explicit section label (not just blank space before the text). The abstract itself is unstructured (single paragraph, no subheadings). Keywords appear immediately after the abstract body, labeled "**Keywords:**" in bold, as a run-on list.

**Field baseline**: Unstructured abstract is the ACCOUNT field norm, but the explicit small-caps "ABSTRACT" label and bolded "Keywords:" inline marker are EAR-specific presentational conventions.

**Observed word count**: ~150 words in both articles checked. No explicit limit confirmed (guidelines inaccessible).

**Delta action**: Match the small-caps label and bolded Keywords format. Do not exceed ~150 words unless the submission system explicitly allows more.

---

### 2. Section Heading System — PRACTICE ONLY

**What EAR does**:  
- Level 1 headings: Arabic numeral + period + space + title, bold. Example: **1. Introduction**, **3. Method**, **5. Concluding Discussion**  
- Level 2 headings: Decimal notation, italic. Example: *3.1. Overview and Setting*, *2.1. Earnings Management Strategies and Motives*  
- Level 3 headings (when present): Decimal to second level, italic. Example: *4.5.1. Are firms responding to takeover pressures or poor performance?*

**Field baseline**: The ACCOUNT baseline does not specify heading numbering style; TAR/JAE use unnumbered or Roman-numeral headings. EAR's Arabic decimal system is a distinct house convention.

**Delta action**: Use Arabic numerals throughout the heading hierarchy. Do not use Roman numerals. Do not use unnumbered section titles.

---

### 3. Post-Text Mandatory Sections — ENFORCED

**What EAR does**: All articles carry a set of mandatory sections after the reference list, in this order:  
1. Ethics statement (when human subjects or data ethics are relevant — may be omitted for purely archival work with no human subjects)  
2. Acknowledgements  
3. Disclosure statement (conflicts of interest)  
4. Funding  
5. ORCID (author identifiers, when applicable)  
6. Supplemental data statement (when online supplementary materials are provided)

**Field baseline**: Post-text compliance sections are not part of the ACCOUNT field baseline (which covers article body only). These are Taylor & Francis platform requirements enforced at submission.

**Delta action**: Always include Disclosure statement and Funding sections, even if the content is "The authors report there are no competing interests to declare" and "This research received no specific grant from any funding agency." Omitting these sections will likely trigger editorial desk rejection.

---

### 4. Final Section Naming — PRACTICE ONLY

**What EAR does**: The concluding section merges discussion and conclusion into a single section but uses non-standard titles:  
- Behavioral/field study type: "Concluding Discussion"  
- Archival type: "Concluding Remarks"

**Field baseline**: The ACCOUNT baseline permits "Discussion and Conclusion" or "Conclusion" as the final section. EAR avoids these standard labels in favor of the "Concluding [X]" formulation.

**Delta action**: Prefer "Concluding Discussion" (for papers with extensive interpretation) or "Concluding Remarks" (for papers with terse wrap-up) over "Discussion and Conclusion" or plain "Conclusion."

---

### 5. Citation Format — PRACTICE ONLY

**What EAR does**: APA author-date in-text citations, reference list formatted to APA with DOIs included where available.

**Field baseline**: Author-date with APA is standard across most ACCOUNT journals. Confirmed as consistent with field norm — no delta — but noting that DOI inclusion in references is expected by EAR's Taylor & Francis submission system.

**Delta action**: None beyond field baseline. Ensure all reference list entries include DOI where the article has one.

---

### 6. Statistical Reporting — METHOD-SPECIFIC, NOT JOURNAL-SPECIFIC

**What EAR does**: Reporting conventions vary by paper type and are not standardized at the journal level:  
- Behavioral/experimental papers: Unstandardized coefficient (B), SE in parentheses, standardized β, partial η²  
- Archival papers: Coefficient with p-value in parentheses on the line below; regression equations written out formally and numbered

**Field baseline**: The ACCOUNT baseline specifies archival-convention reporting. EAR extends this to accommodate behavioral reporting conventions without conflict.

**Delta action**: Match the reporting convention to your paper's methodology. EAR does not impose a single statistical table format. Archival papers should follow the archival convention in the field baseline.

---

### 7. Scope Breadth — RELAXATION

**What EAR does**: EAR explicitly positions itself as a "diverse paradigm" journal welcoming qualitative, interpretive, critical, mixed-methods, and non-US empirical settings. Papers making non-causal, interpretive, or theoretical contributions are in scope.

**Field baseline**: The ACCOUNT field baseline (derived from ABS 4*/4 journals like TAR, JAE, CAR) emphasizes large-sample archival empirical work and causal identification. That remains the dominant paradigm but is not the only accepted paradigm at EAR.

**Delta action**: Causal identification language ("identify the causal effect of X on Y") is appropriate for archival papers but not required for qualitative or theoretical submissions. Match rhetorical register to paper type. Do not import ABS 4* archival framing onto interpretive submissions.

## Dimensions with No Delta (Field Default)

The following ACCOUNT field baseline conventions were observed and confirmed as consistent in both articles checked. No EAR-specific override applies:

- **Voice**: Third person, formal register; no first-person singular
- **Paragraph style**: Topic-sentence-led; deductive structure within paragraphs
- **Argumentation**: Gap → contribution → structure → findings in Introduction; hypothesis derivation from prior literature in archival papers
- **Hedging**: Standard academic hedging ("suggests," "consistent with," "provides evidence that")
- **Citation density**: Moderate-to-high in literature sections; lighter in methods and results
