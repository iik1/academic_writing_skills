# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Health Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Economic analyses of the value, production, or distribution of health or healthcare; papers must advance health economics knowledge or directly inform health policy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-health-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Siciliani, Wen & Gaughan, PMC12284584; Smart, Powell, Pacula et al., PMC11392605)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section order. Both full-text articles confirm flexible structure: one includes a separate Discussion section between Results and Conclusion; the other does not. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Length
No word or page limits. The guidelines state explicitly: "We publish papers of any length. There is no separate submission channel for short papers, which are encouraged." GUIDELINE ONLY (stated policy, not a writing-style constraint).

### Abstract
Unstructured prose abstract. No structured format (e.g., Background/Methods/Results/Conclusions headers) is required or observed. No stated word limit; observed abstracts run approximately 150–250 words. ENFORCED (confirmed by practice across all abstracts reviewed).

### Citations
Author-year format. Confirmed in guidelines and in both full-text articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
No journal-mandated heading conventions. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Standard Elsevier requirements. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
The journal does not publish the following, and desk-rejects them (~75% desk-rejection rate):
- Opinion pieces
- Systematic reviews
- Cost-of-illness studies
- Applications of existing methods lacking methodological innovation
- Time series analyses without independent economic contribution
- Epidemiological studies without substantive economic analysis

**ENFORCED.** These define the scope boundary more tightly than any ABS 3 ECON journal would be expected to—the journal screens for economics methodology, not health content alone.

### Required Declarations (ENFORCED)
Elsevier-mandated elements that must appear in every submission:
- Declaration of competing interests
- Funding source disclosure
- CRediT authorship contribution statement
- Declaration of generative AI use (if applicable; placed before the References section)

### Language
Inclusive language is required throughout. Authors must avoid descriptors implying superiority based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability status. ENFORCED.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Separate Discussion section: PRACTICE ONLY.** One of two articles examined (Siciliani et al.) included a distinct Discussion section between Results and Conclusion; the other (Smart et al.) did not. FIELD_SKILL notes practice varies—this confirms the norm holds at JHE. No guideline mandates or prohibits a Discussion section.

**Literature review placement: PRACTICE ONLY.** Smart et al. integrated a dedicated "Existing literature" subsection within the Background/framework section rather than in the Introduction. This diverges slightly from the FIELD_SKILL default of surveying the literature within the Introduction, but the difference is organizational rather than rhetorical.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**JEL codes: optional.** Guidelines do not mention JEL classification codes as a requirement. One article listed them (Smart et al.: H75, I18, K32); the other (Siciliani et al.) did not. PRACTICE ONLY. Authors may include JEL codes but are not required to. This is atypical for an economics journal.

**Generative AI disclosure: ENFORCED.** A formal section titled "Declaration of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing process" must appear immediately before the References if AI tools were used. This is not covered by FIELD_SKILL.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting prohibited paper types.** Cost-of-illness studies, systematic reviews, and epidemiological papers without a clear economics methodology or identification strategy will be desk-rejected. The journal's scope is narrower than its subject area suggests: health content is necessary but not sufficient.
2. **Using a structured abstract.** Clinical health journals often require a structured abstract (BACKGROUND / METHODS / RESULTS). JHE does not; submitting one signals unfamiliarity with the outlet.
3. **Omitting the AI disclosure section.** If any AI-assisted writing tool was used, a formal declaration section is required before the References. Omitting it violates Elsevier policy.
4. **Assuming JEL codes are required.** Authors from general economics backgrounds may include JEL codes as a default; at JHE this is optional. Including them does no harm, but their absence is not a deficiency.
