# Journal Writing Skill: Review of Economics and Statistics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** MIT Press
- **Scope:** Long-standing general journal of applied (especially quantitative) empirical economics, covering microeconomics, labor, health, development, macroeconomics, and related empirical methods. Does not publish purely theoretical work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://direct.mit.edu/rest/pages/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Ferrara & Fishback, NBER WP 26936, published REStat 106(5) 2024; Alsan & Yang, NBER WP 24731, published REStat 106(6) 2024)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandated section order beyond standard empirical conventions.

### Length
No stated page limit for full-length articles. **Short Papers** (a separate submission type introduced December 2019) are capped at **6,000 words and 5 exhibits**. For each exhibit fewer than five, authors may add up to 200 words toward the limit; a paper with zero exhibits may run up to 7,000 words. Label: ENFORCED.

### Abstract
Unstructured; **maximum 100 words**; must be "suitable for the Journal of Economic Literature" (i.e., self-contained, jargon-light). Label: ENFORCED. FIELD_SKILL does not specify a word limit; this cap is binding and tighter than many comparable ABS 4 outlets.

### Citations
Author-year in-text citations (name, year, optionally page numbers). Reference list at end: journals require publication name, volume, month, year, and page range; books require city, publisher, year, and editors or page range where relevant. This is consistent with the FIELD_SKILL author-year norm. Label: FIELD DEFAULT, format details ENFORCED.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Both Roman and Arabic numerals observed in spot-checked articles; guidelines do not mandate one system.

### Tables and Figures
Formatting guidelines (double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman, 1-inch margins) apply to the **entire manuscript including tables and figures**. Label: ENFORCED for resubmissions; implied for initial submissions.

### JEL Codes
Each submission must include JEL classification codes from the EconLit Subject Descriptors system. Editor assignments are determined by JEL codes. Label: ENFORCED. Not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL baseline.

### Data and Replication
Authors of accepted papers must post data and code (or document data access arrangements) and provide a detailed readme file sufficient for replication. Proprietary data must be flagged at submission. Label: ENFORCED post-acceptance.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated beyond standard review conventions. Submission fee ($125, nonrefundable) is required at time of new submission; waived for authors with a current individual subscription.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a word count.
**This journal:** 100-word hard cap, enforced. Both spot-checked articles conform: the published Alsan & Yang abstract is ≈85 words; Ferrara & Fishback NBER version runs slightly longer but the published abstract is trimmed to comply. Writers accustomed to 150–200-word abstracts standard at other ABS 4 outlets must cut substantially.
**Label:** ENFORCED.

### Short Papers Submission Type
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes a single article format.
**This journal:** A formal Short Papers category exists with strict word and exhibit caps (see Length above). Short Papers go through the same peer review as full papers. This is not a note or comment format — it is a full empirical contribution in compressed form.
**Label:** ENFORCED.

### JEL Code Requirement
**Field norm:** JEL codes are common practice but FIELD_SKILL treats them as outside its scope.
**This journal:** Mandatory at submission; used for editor routing. Omitting them delays processing.
**Label:** ENFORCED.

### Manuscript Formatting
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify typographic conventions.
**This journal:** Double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman (or similar serif), 1-inch margins throughout — including abstract, footnotes, tables, figures, and references.
**Label:** ENFORCED.

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT
- Active vs. passive: FIELD DEFAULT
- Sentence and paragraph style: FIELD DEFAULT
- Introduction structure (hook → question → approach → results → contributions → roadmap): FIELD DEFAULT — roadmap paragraph confirmed in Alsan & Yang ("Our paper proceeds as follows...")
- Citation style (author-year in-text, paraphrase over quotation): FIELD DEFAULT
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT
- Statistical reporting (magnitudes + significance, economic significance expected): FIELD DEFAULT — both articles report percentage-point magnitudes with significance indicators; table notes use stars (* p<0.10, ** p<0.05, *** p<0.01)
- Identification strategy as required element: FIELD DEFAULT
- Robustness checks expected: FIELD DEFAULT
- Null results treated as results: FIELD DEFAULT
- Hedging intensity (low; defend via identification, not verbal hedging): FIELD DEFAULT
- Footnotes for supplementary material (numbered, in-text position): FIELD DEFAULT
- Standalone online appendix expected: FIELD DEFAULT

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 100 words.** The cap is binding and unusually tight. Abstracts that announce results quantitatively within 100 words require deliberate compression — cut motivating context, not findings.
2. **Missing or wrong JEL codes.** Editor routing depends on them; an ill-chosen code can delay review or send the paper to a mismatched editor.
3. **Submitting a Short Paper that exceeds 6,000 words + 5 exhibits.** The limit is strictly enforced; the word–exhibit trade-off (200 words per exhibit foregone) must be tracked explicitly.
4. **No replication package plan for empirical work.** The data availability policy is enforced post-acceptance; authors using proprietary data should document their access arrangements and partial replication strategy at submission to avoid revision delays.
