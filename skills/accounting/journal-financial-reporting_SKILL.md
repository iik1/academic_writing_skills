# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Reporting

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** American Accounting Association (AAA)
- **Scope:** Publishes research on financial reporting — production, dissemination, and analysis of information from a firm's financial accounting system. Covers standard-setting, disclosure, analyst behavior, intermediary roles, and decision-maker use of financial data. Explicitly welcomes non-mainstream methodologies and short-form articles.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://aaahq.org/Research/Journals/Journal-of-Financial-Reporting/Guide-for-Authors; https://aaahq.org/Research/Journals/Manuscript-Preparation-Guide
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (full-text access unavailable via direct fetch or EZProxy; all constraints below derive from AAA Manuscript Preparation Guide and JFR Editorial Policy)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Manuscript must be submitted in this order: (1) title page (separate Word file), (2) abstract, (3) AI Disclosure Statement, (4) article text beginning with "I. INTRODUCTION", (5) references, (6) numbered figures (captions only in text file; figures as separate files), (7) numbered tables, (8) exhibits, (9) appendices.

JFR accepts four content types beyond standard original discovery articles:
- **Replications**: Must be concise with limited literature review; new sample must match or exceed original in strength.
- **Perspectives & Discussions**: Thought-provoking pieces on emerging issues; mostly solicited but unsolicited considered.
- **Comments**: Require prior contact with original authors before submission.
- **Commentaries**: 1–2 pages; may accompany published articles to address conflicting evidence or provide context.

Short articles are explicitly welcome for all content types. [ENFORCED]

### Length
55 pages maximum, all-inclusive: text, references, figures, tables, and appendices combined. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; ≤150 words; must "concisely inform the reader of the manuscript's topic, its methods, and its findings." The abstract (and the introduction) should be "relatively nontechnical, yet clear" — accessible to the broad accounting research community, not only sub-area specialists. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date style: "(Author Year)" in text — no comma between author and year. Two authors: "(Fehr and Schmidt 2003)". Three or more: "(Smith et al. 2003)" after first full citation. Reference list alphabetical by surname; DOIs or URLs required when available; no abbreviated journal titles. [ENFORCED — consistent with field norm but format is more explicit]

### Headings
Level 1: centered, bold, ALL CAPS, Roman numeral (e.g., "I. INTRODUCTION", "II. HYPOTHESIS DEVELOPMENT"). Level 2: flush left, bold, title case. Level 3: flush left, bold, italic, title case. Level 4: paragraph indent, bold, lowercase. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Tables use Arabic numerals with complete titles; must be referenced in text; created in Word or Excel (not formatted with spaces/tabs). Figures submitted as separate files at final submission (EPS preferred; also TIFF, PDF, JPEG, PNG; line art at 600 dpi; halftones at 264 dpi). Alt-text required for all tables and figures for accessibility. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Single authors **must not** use the editorial "we." [ENFORCED — overrides FIELD_SKILL, which states "we" is dominant regardless of author count]
- AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) cannot be listed as author or co-author.
- Reviewers and editors must not upload manuscripts into generative AI tools.
- Footnotes are for extensions of information only; they may not serve as citation or documentation vehicles.
- No abbreviated journal titles in references.
- No wingdings, geometric shapes, or images as mathematical notation.
- No application files (CorelDraw, Word, etc.) submitted as figure files.
- No images of people without express written permission.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register
**Field norm:** "We" is the dominant convention *regardless of author count.*
**This journal:** Single-authored papers must use impersonal constructions or third-person ("this paper," "the analysis"). The editorial "we" is explicitly prohibited for single authors. [ENFORCED — tightening of FIELD_SKILL norm]

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes one canonical structure for archival/experimental papers.
**This journal:** Four additional content types are explicitly supported: replications (concise, limited lit review), perspectives/discussions, comments (require prior author contact), and commentaries (1–2 pages). These are not marginal exceptions — the editorial policy actively solicits them. Short articles across all types are explicitly welcome. Replication articles deviate structurally by design: abbreviated literature review is expected, not a weakness. [ENFORCED]

### AI Disclosure
**Field norm:** Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL.
**This journal:** A "Declaration of Generative AI and AI-Assisted Technologies" statement is required in every submission. It must appear after the abstract and before the body text. It must be updated on every revision submission. [ENFORCED]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Gender-neutral language:** Authors are explicitly encouraged to use gender-neutral language throughout. FIELD_SKILL does not address this. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

**Footnote scope:** Footnotes are limited to extensions of information (elaboration, caveats). They cannot be used for in-text documentation or citation purposes. FIELD_SKILL does not address footnote use explicitly. [ENFORCED]

**Submission fee:** $75 (AAA members); $125 (non-members). Non-refundable. Required at initial submission.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Single-author "we"**: Using editorial "we" in a sole-authored paper violates a hard prohibition. This is the single most important voice deviation from FIELD_SKILL.
2. **Missing AI disclosure**: Every submission requires a separate AI disclosure statement placed after the abstract. Omitting it or placing it elsewhere will require revision before review.
3. **Over-elaborating replications**: JFR replications are expected to be concise with a limited literature review. Treating a replication like a full-length original discovery article signals a genre mismatch.
4. **Page budget miscalculation**: The 55-page cap includes references, figures, tables, and appendices. Authors who count only text pages will be over limit.
