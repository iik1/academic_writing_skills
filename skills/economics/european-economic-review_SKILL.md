# Journal Writing Skill: European Economic Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** General-interest economics journal covering theoretical and empirical research across all areas of economics, established 1969. Welcomes work with European context but is not restricted to it.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/journal/00142921/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Bellani, Berriochoa, Kapteina & Schwerdt, IZA DP 17099, published in EER — confirmed published in European Economic Review)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section order beyond abstract, keywords, and highlights preceding the main text. Appendix expected for robustness and supplementary material.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured plain paragraph. JEL classification and keywords listed separately below the abstract (confirmed in practice). No word limit specified in guidelines. FIELD DEFAULT for format; JEL + keywords placement is standard Elsevier. ENFORCED.

### Highlights
Required for all submissions. Elsevier standard: 3–5 bullet points, maximum 85 characters each (including spaces). Should capture novel results and/or new methods. Highlights appear separately from the abstract in the submission system and are displayed on the article landing page. ENFORCED — this element has no equivalent in FIELD_SKILL and is a mandatory addition.

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (author-year parenthetical, confirmed in practice). No deviation observed.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Arabic numerals used in practice. No journal-specific heading rules stated.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific formatting rules beyond standard Elsevier requirements.

### Submission Fee
€125 non-refundable fee for all new submissions. Reduced rates: €100 for PhD students as corresponding author; €50 for Research4Life Group B country authors; full waiver for Research4Life Group A country authors. VAT applies for EU authors. ENFORCED — fee is due at submission regardless of outcome.

### Generative AI Declaration
Authors must declare use of any generative AI tools in their work. AI tools may not be listed as authors. Applies to text and to figures/images/artwork. ENFORCED.

### Authorship Freeze
Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance. ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Multiple, concurrent, or redundant publication prohibited.
- AI tools prohibited from being listed as authors.
- Authorship changes prohibited after acceptance.

### Originality Standard
Guidelines state work must be "original in motivation or modelling." This phrasing — OR rather than AND — is a RELAXATION relative to ABS 4*/4 journals, where originality in both motivation and contribution is typically expected. At ABS 3, a well-executed empirical study on a new context or a clean replication with a novel dataset may qualify, even if the methodology is entirely standard.

### Inclusive Language
Guidelines require inclusive language: avoid assumptions about reader beliefs, avoid bias and stereotypes. Standard Elsevier boilerplate. GUIDELINE ONLY — not systematically enforced at the text level beyond Elsevier's general standards.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
None observed. The one article examined (Bellani et al.) follows the standard empirical paper structure: Introduction → Institutional Background → Data and Empirical Strategy → Results → Mechanisms → Concluding Remarks. Section titled "Concluding Remarks" rather than "Conclusion" — within FIELD_SKILL norm (no standard naming convention).

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Highlights (mandatory addition):** ABS 4*/4 journals in ECON do not require highlights. EER, as an Elsevier journal, mandates 3–5 highlights of ≤85 characters each. These are written as declarative sentences summarising the paper's novel findings (not method descriptions). This is the most operationally significant delta for submission preparation. ENFORCED.

**Submission fee:** No ABS 4*/4 ECON journal charges a submission fee. EER charges €125 per submission. Authors must budget for this, and multiple-round revision workflows are costlier if resubmitting as a new submission. ENFORCED.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Forgetting highlights.** Submissions without highlights will be incomplete in the Elsevier submission system. Draft 3–5 declarative highlights (≤85 characters each) before submitting. Do not copy abstract sentences; they typically exceed the character limit.
2. **Submitting work that is neither motivationally nor methodologically novel.** The "original in motivation or modelling" standard is permissive, but still requires one clear dimension of originality. Straightforward replications of prior studies on different countries without a novel research question will be desk-rejected.
3. **US-centric framing with no European angle.** EER is a general economics journal but its positioning and editorial board are European. Papers with no European data, context, or institutional relevance are at a disadvantage relative to similarly sized journals without a European identity.
4. **Authorship list not finalised before submission.** The journal prohibits authorship changes after acceptance. Ensure the full author list, order, and corresponding author are finalised before the paper is submitted, not just before acceptance.
