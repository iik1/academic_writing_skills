PHASE 3 - ABSTRACTION RULES (MANDATORY)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

RULE 1 - NO QUOTATIONS
Do not quote any article or author. All observations must be
paraphrased into general field-level statements.

RULE 2 - NO JOURNAL-SPECIFIC DETAILS
Do not name specific journals in the body of FIELD_SKILL.md
(the sources table is the only exception).
Do not include word limits, page limits, submission requirements,
or publisher formatting rules.

RULE 3 - NORMATIVE FRAMING ONLY
Every claim must be framed as a norm or expectation:
  CORRECT: "At the ABS 4*/4 level, reviewers expect..."
  CORRECT: "Leading journals in this field require..."
  WRONG:   "Some articles use..."
  WRONG:   "One journal requires..."

RULE 4 - MARK UNCERTAINTY
Where norms vary rather than converge, write:
  "Practice varies: [describe range]. No consensus norm exists."

RULE 5 - PREFER RANGE OVER POINT ESTIMATES
  CORRECT: "Citation density in the introduction is high."
  CORRECT: "Sentences are typically medium to long."
  WRONG:   "Introductions cite 40-60 sources."
  WRONG:   "Sentences average 28 words."
