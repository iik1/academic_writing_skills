# Journal Writing Skill: Academy of Management Journal

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MGMT
- **Publisher:** Academy of Management
- **Scope:** Empirical research (quantitative, qualitative, field, laboratory, meta-analytic, mixed methods) that advances management theory and informs practice. Must offer strong empirical and theoretical contributions. Submissions without substantial empirical component will not be reviewed.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aom.org/publications/journals/publishing-with-aom/author-and-reviewer-resources/
- **Field baseline:** mgmt-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (paywall access limitations; analysis based on official Style Guide 2014)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No required section order specified beyond standard empirical article structure. See FIELD_SKILL for quantitative/qualitative/theoretical templates.

**ENFORCED**

### Length
No specific word/page limit stated in Style Guide. Refer to "Information for Contributors" on journal website for current length guidelines.

### Abstract
- **Maximum 200 words** (unstructured)
- Goes on page 2 (separate from title/author page)
- No citations in abstract

**ENFORCED**

### Citations
APA style as per FIELD_SKILL. Specific requirements:
- Alphabetical order in parenthetical citations
- Et al. for 3-6 authors after first citation; for 7+ authors use et al. even on first citation
- Page numbers format: (Author, Year: page)

**ENFORCED**

### Headings
**Exactly three levels only.** Mandatory formatting:

1. **First level**: ALL CAPITAL LETTERS, centered, boldface
2. **Second level**: Title-Style Capitalization, Flush Left, boldface
3. **Third level**: First word capitalized, indented, italicized, boldface, run into paragraph

Do not skip levels. Use second- and third-level headings in sets of two or more.

**ENFORCED**

### Tables and Figures
- Preferred format: Microsoft Word (also WordPerfect, PDF acceptable)
- **NOT acceptable**: straight Excel files (must convert to Word/PDF first)
- Columns read down, not across
- **Two decimal places for all statistics** (required)
- **Correlations in lower-left corners** of correlation tables
- Superscript lowercase letters for table footnotes
- Significance levels in stack below regular footnotes:
  - † p < .10
  - * p < .05
  - ** p < .01
- **No color in figures**
- Variable names must match text (no abbreviations/code names)
- Place all tables/figures at end with position markers in text: `--------Insert Table 2 about here--------`

**ENFORCED**

### Hypotheses
- Fully and separately state each hypothesis
- **Present tense**
- Numbered label (Hypothesis 1, Hypothesis 1a, etc.)
- **Indented block format, italic type**

Example format from Style Guide:
> *Hypothesis 1a. Concise writing has a positive relationship to publication.*

**ENFORCED**

### Footnotes
- **Footnotes on respective pages** (NOT endnotes)

**ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No color in figures
- No exact survey reproductions in appendixes
- No abbreviations for concept/variable names (use full words)
- No "talking in math" in regular text (e.g., write "We surveyed 100 employees," not "We surveyed n = 100 employees")
- No anthropomorphism (inanimate entities acting as humans)
- No author-identifying information in submissions under review
- Avoid sexist or biased language
- No code names in tables/figures

**ENFORCED**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Statistical Reporting

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL does not specify decimal place requirements for statistics.

**AMJ requirement**: **Two decimal places for all statistics** reported in tables.

**Classification**: ENFORCED

**How to apply**: When preparing tables, round all coefficients, standard errors, R², and other statistics to exactly two decimal places. This applies to regression tables, correlation matrices, and all quantitative results tables.

---

### Table Formatting: Correlation Placement

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL does not specify where correlations appear in tables.

**AMJ requirement**: **Correlations must be in lower-left corners** of correlation tables.

**Classification**: ENFORCED

**How to apply**: In correlation/descriptive statistics tables, place correlation coefficients below the diagonal (lower-left triangle). Means, standard deviations, and other descriptive statistics typically appear in columns to the right.

---

### Hypotheses Formatting

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL states hypotheses should be "set off in indented blocks, in italic type" and numbered.

**AMJ adds specificity**: Present tense required; must be "fully and separately stated."

**Classification**: ENFORCED (specification of field norm)

**How to apply**: Each hypothesis gets its own indented italic block. Do not combine hypotheses. Use present tense ("has," "is," "influences") not future tense. Number distinctly (H1, H1a, H1b, H2, etc.).

---

### Footnotes vs. Endnotes

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL mentions footnotes are used but doesn't specify placement.

**AMJ requirement**: Footnotes must be **on their respective pages**, explicitly NOT endnotes.

**Classification**: ENFORCED

**How to apply**: Use Word's footnote function (not endnote). Footnotes appear at bottom of the page where referenced, not collected at article end.

---

### Active Voice and First Person

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL states "First-person plural ('we') is the default" and "Active voice is used selectively."

**AMJ strengthens this**: "Write in the active voice...instead of the passive voice" and "Use the first person ('I' or 'we') to describe what you, or you and your coauthors, did."

**Classification**: ENFORCED (emphatic statement of field norm)

**How to apply**: Default to active voice and first-person constructions throughout. The Style Guide explicitly states this makes it "easy for readers to see who did what." Examples from guide:
- Prefer: "Earley (1989) found that two items lacked factor validity"
- Avoid: "Two items were found to lack factor validity by Earley (1989)"

---

### Mathematical Reporting

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL does not address mathematical notation in running text.

**AMJ requirement**: Do not "talk in math" in regular text. Use words. Reserve symbols for results reporting and formulas. Italicize customary symbols (p, r, b, F, Z). Spaces on either side of equals signs, minus signs, etc.

**Classification**: ENFORCED

**How to apply**: Write "We surveyed 100 employees" not "We surveyed n = 100 employees." When reporting results in parentheses, symbols are acceptable: "(β = 0.06, p < .05)."

---

### Variable Naming in Tables

**Field norm**: FIELD_SKILL does not specifically address variable naming conventions in tables.

**AMJ requirement**: "Use the same name for each variable that you use in your text. Don't use code names and abbreviations."

Example from Style Guide:
- Desirable: "Profitability"
- Undesirable: "PRFT"

**Classification**: ENFORCED

**How to apply**: Tables must use full, readable variable names that match exactly what appears in the manuscript text. No abbreviations, acronyms, or database codes.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using endnotes instead of footnotes**: AMJ explicitly requires footnotes on respective pages, not collected endnotes. Use Word's footnote feature.

2. **Color figures**: Submitted figures must be black-and-white/grayscale. No color.

3. **Inconsistent decimal places**: All statistics in tables must have exactly two decimal places (.06, not .057 or .1).

4. **Excel tables**: Straight Excel files are not acceptable. Convert to Word or PDF before submission.

5. **Abbreviations in tables**: Variable names like "PRFT," "EMP_SAT," or "ROA" will be rejected. Use "Profitability," "Employee satisfaction," "Return on assets."

6. **Wrong heading hierarchy**: AMJ uses exactly three levels with specific formatting. Do not use four levels or skip from level 1 to level 3.

7. **Math in prose**: Do not write "n = 250" or "M = 3.4" in running text. Write "250 employees" or "mean of 3.4."

8. **Passive voice dominance**: AMJ Style Guide explicitly prefers active voice and first person. "We collected data" not "Data were collected."
