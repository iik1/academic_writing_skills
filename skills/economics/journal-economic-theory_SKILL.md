# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Economic Theory

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Publishes original theoretical economics research across all fields; welcomes complementary empirical, experimental, quantitative, and computational work when grounded in theory.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-economic-theory/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 full text (He & Libgober 2025, "Misspecified Learning and Evolutionary Stability," JET 230:106082, arXiv:2509.16067); citation format cross-checked against Brandt et al. 2025 (JET 230:106096, arXiv:2305.10286)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
No mandatory section sequence beyond standard academic organisation. A dedicated **AI Use Disclosure** section is required immediately before the References when generative AI tools were used in preparing the manuscript. The Elsevier "Highlights" feature (3–5 bullet points of key findings) is available but optional.

### Length
No word or page limits stated. ENFORCED: none applicable.

### Abstract
Unstructured; no word limit specified in guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Citations
Guidelines do not mandate a specific citation style. In practice: author-year format in-text (e.g., "Esponda and Pouzo (2016)") and an alphabetically ordered author-year bibliography. Reference list format: Last, F. and F. Last (Year): "Title," *Journal*, Volume, Pages. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Arabic numerals with decimal subsections (1, 1.1, 1.2, 2, 2.1, ...) observed in published theory papers. FIELD_SKILL permits either Roman or Arabic; decimal subsections are not mentioned there. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. High-resolution figures required; captions required. Authors must disclose use of generative AI to create any figure or image (covered by the AI disclosure section). ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. ENFORCED.
- Simultaneous submission to other journals prohibited. ENFORCED.
- Authorship changes after acceptance require editor approval via formal request. ENFORCED.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**Related Literature placement (theory papers):** FIELD_SKILL states that a standalone Related Literature section is "more common" in theory papers. In the one theory paper examined, Related Literature appears as a numbered subsection (§1.4) within the Introduction, not as a standalone section. This is inconsistent with the field norm for theory papers but consistent with the FIELD_SKILL rule for shorter empirical papers. Authors should not assume a standalone Related Literature section is expected or preferred at JET.
- **Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY

**AI Use Disclosure section:** A dedicated prose section disclosing which AI tools were used and for what purposes is required immediately before the References section when applicable. This structural element has no counterpart in FIELD_SKILL.
- **Classification:** ENFORCED

### Voice and Register

**Inclusive language mandate:** The guide explicitly requires gender-neutral, non-discriminatory language throughout the manuscript, including preference for plural nouns over gendered pronouns. This is a stated editorial requirement; FIELD_SKILL does not address it.
- **Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (stated in guidelines; not distinguishable at the article level)

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural "we" throughout, including sole-authored papers)
- Citation format: FIELD DEFAULT (author-year in-text and bibliography)
- Statistical and quantitative reporting: FIELD DEFAULT
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT
- Hedging intensity: FIELD DEFAULT
- Introduction structure (hook → question → approach → results → contributions → roadmap): FIELD DEFAULT
- Conclusion norms: FIELD DEFAULT
- Theorem/proposition/lemma environments: FIELD DEFAULT (numbered sequentially)
- Peer review: Single-blind (minimum two reviewers); not a writing norm but noted for submission planning

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting AI disclosure:** If any AI tool was used in drafting, editing, or generating figures, a dedicated disclosure section must appear before References. Omitting this section violates stated policy even if use was minimal.
2. **Standalone Related Literature in theory papers:** Theory authors accustomed to placing Related Literature as its own top-level section may find this is not the observed JET practice. Embedding it as a subsection of the Introduction (§1.x) appears to be the journal norm.
3. **Assuming a word or page limit exists:** JET states no such limits. Overly brief papers that omit proofs or appendix material to fit an assumed limit are not appropriate; completeness is expected.
