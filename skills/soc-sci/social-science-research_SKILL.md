# Journal Writing Skill: Social Science Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Quantitative empirical research testing social science theory; cross-disciplinary methods that advance theory-testing across social science disciplines. Meta-analysis reviews also accepted.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/social-science-research/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (DOI: 10.1016/j.ssresearch.2026.103329; DOI: 10.1016/j.ssresearch.2026.103325)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for quantitative work (Introduction, Theory/Literature, Data and Methods, Results, Discussion/Conclusion). No journal-mandated section labels beyond what the quantitative tradition requires.

### Length
Not specified in guidelines. No word or page limit stated.

### Abstract
Unstructured; maximum 250 words. References in the abstract should be avoided; if essential, cite author(s) and year(s) only. **ENFORCED.**

### Citations
ASA (American Sociological Association) style throughout. Author-date in-text citations confirmed in published articles. **ENFORCED.**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Keywords
1–7 keywords required; prefer single-word terms over multi-word phrases. **ENFORCED.**

### Declarations Required
Authors must complete declarations for: competing interests, funding sources, generative AI use in manuscript preparation, and sex/gender-based analyses (where applicable). **ENFORCED.**

### Explicit Prohibitions
The following submission types face explicit desk rejection:
- Qualitative studies
- Theoretical papers (no empirical component)
- Simulated-data studies
- Descriptive or exploratory work (no theory test)
- Research notes
- Policy evaluation studies
- Narrative literature reviews (meta-analysis is the only accepted review format)
- Non-experimental studies using convenience samples ("generally considered below publication standards")

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations — Scope Restriction

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL covers a methodologically diverse field including qualitative, mixed methods, ethnographic, critical theory, and quantitative work.

**This journal:** Strictly quantitative empirical only. The journal's scope excludes the entire qualitative and theoretical tradition that FIELD_SKILL treats as legitimate. Submitting any qualitative, interpretive, or purely theoretical manuscript will result in desk rejection.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Structural Deviations — Sample Quality Threshold

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not impose a threshold on sample type.

**This journal:** Non-experimental studies using convenience samples are explicitly flagged as generally below publication standards. Studies using this design should either employ an experimental design or use probability-sample data.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Structural Deviations — Review Format

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not restrict the format of review articles.

**This journal:** Only meta-analysis is accepted as a review article format. Narrative or systematic literature reviews without quantitative synthesis are excluded.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting qualitative or mixed-methods work.** The journal is strictly quantitative; any qualitative component triggers desk rejection regardless of theoretical contribution.
2. **Using a convenience sample in a non-experimental design.** This is one of the few explicit quality thresholds in the guidelines. Use probability samples or experimental random assignment.
3. **Submitting a narrative literature review.** Only meta-analyses are accepted as review articles. A well-structured narrative review will be desk-rejected.
4. **Abstract over 250 words or containing a reference list.** Keep the abstract self-contained and within the stated limit; references in the abstract are discouraged and must be author-year format if included.
