# Journal Writing Skill: Omega

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (Pergamon)
- **Scope:** Operations Analytics — data-driven and problem-driven models addressing practical optimization and business analytics challenges. Preference given to papers with implications for the practice of management.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/omega/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Bakker, Dunke & Nickel, "A structuring review on multi-stage optimization under uncertainty," Omega, 2019 ARTICLE IN PRESS — author copy via Rotman/UofT seminar page)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Sections must be explicitly numbered using a decimal hierarchy: 1, 2, 1.1, 1.1.1, 1.1.2, etc. Required front-matter elements: Title page, Abstract, Keywords, Highlights, (optional) Graphical Abstract. Confirmed in the spot-checked article. [ENFORCED]

Special paper types with different rules: **Short Communications** ("Memoranda") capped at 10 pages; **Feedback** (brief correspondence addressing limitations in recent Omega publications) — both are journal-specific formats absent from FIELD_SKILL.

### Length
Regular articles: 35 pages maximum. Short Communications: 10 pages. No explicit word-count limit stated; page limit is the operative constraint. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured (no required field labels). Must stand alone; references cited in the abstract must be given in full. Confirmed unstructured in spot-checked article. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Numbered sequential style: [1], [1,2], [1–4]. In-text by superscript or bracket number; reference list ordered by first appearance. This differs from the author-year conventions used by the ABS 4*/4 journals underpinning FIELD_SKILL (Management Science, Operations Research). [ENFORCED — confirmed in articles]

### Headings
Decimal-numbered hierarchy mandatory (1, 1.1, 1.1.1). Confirmed in spot-checked article. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Graphical abstract encouraged (optional).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Submissions of **purely theoretical papers are explicitly discouraged** ("Submissions of purely theoretical papers are discouraged" — editorial policy stated on journal homepage). [ENFORCED]
- No prior publication except as preprint, abstract, or thesis.
- No simultaneous submission elsewhere.
- Generative AI use in manuscript preparation must be declared.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**Field norm:** High and consistent formality throughout; technically dense prose calibrated to expert readers.

**Omega:** The guidelines explicitly require manuscripts be "written in a manner that makes it accessible to a wide-ranging readership of academics, practitioners, and managers." This is a documented RELAXATION of the dense, expert-only register at ABS 4*/4 level. In practice this means: technical content should be contextualized with intuitive business or operational framing; heavy mathematical notation without managerial interpretation is at risk of rejection.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (stated requirement); RELAXATION relative to FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Scope and Theoretical Contribution

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL profiles the OR&MANSCI field as spanning from purely theoretical/mathematical contributions to empirically grounded applied work; both are valid at ABS 4*/4 level.

**Omega:** Pure theory is explicitly discouraged. Papers must demonstrate "implications for the practice of management." Theoretical and methodological contributions are accepted but must be grounded in or validated against practical problems. No standalone deductive proofs without connection to an applied decision problem. This is not a new norm — it is a documented scope restriction relative to the full field baseline.

**Classification:** ENFORCED; RELAXATION of what the field accepts.

### Citation Density

Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Numbered format changes the visual appearance of citations but the section-by-section density pattern (high in Introduction/Related Work, lower in Results/Appendix) follows FIELD_SKILL.

### Structural Deviations

**Numbered sections:** Decimal hierarchy (1, 1.1, 1.1.1) is mandated and confirmed in articles. FIELD_SKILL does not require numbering.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

**Highlights:** 3–5 bullet points (maximum 85 characters per bullet, including spaces) required as a separate submission element. These are not part of the abstract and are used for search indexing. FIELD_SKILL makes no mention of highlights.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Memoranda / Short Communications:** A distinct 10-page article format for brief scientific reports. Not present in FIELD_SKILL.
**Feedback column:** A correspondence format for critiques of recent Omega articles. Not present in FIELD_SKILL.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting a purely mathematical paper.** If the contribution is a theorem or algorithm with no application validation and no management-practice interpretation, the paper will be out of scope. Omega requires a real-world decision problem or data context.

2. **Using author-year citations.** Omega uses numbered sequential [1]–[4] citations. Submitting with APA/Chicago author-year format (as used in Management Science or Operations Research) requires reformatting.

3. **Omitting Highlights.** Highlights (3–5 bullets, ≤85 characters each) are a required submission element. Missing them is a technical desk-rejection trigger.

4. **Writing for an expert-only audience.** Unlike ABS 4*/4 venues where technical density is a signal of rigor, Omega requires that prose remain accessible to practitioners and managers. Papers that never translate formal results into decision-relevant language risk negative handling decisions.
