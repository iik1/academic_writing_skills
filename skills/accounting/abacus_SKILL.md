# Journal Writing Skill: Abacus

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell on behalf of the Accounting Foundation, University of Sydney
- **Scope:** Quarterly journal covering accounting, corporate finance, and business studies; publishes archival empirical papers, analytical work, commentary essays, and forum discussions. Broader scope than the pure-accounting ABS 4* journals in the FIELD_SKILL baseline.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14676281/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Oh & Penman 2024, DOI 10.1111/abac.12307, accessed via SSRN preprint SSRN-id3616871; published version pages 23–48 of Abacus Vol. 60 No. 1)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Manuscript file should open with title, abstract, and keywords; body sections follow. No required section template is specified beyond these opening elements.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; **maximum 250 words**; followed immediately by **3–5 keywords listed in alphabetical order**. Label: ENFORCED.

### Citations
Author-date in-text format. Guideline example: `(Jones, 1962, p. 21)`. For three or more authors, cite as first author + "et al." — e.g., `(Smith et al., 2020)`. Label: ENFORCED.

Reference list format is highly distinctive (see Observed Deltas below).

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm in article practice (Arabic numerals observed: 1. Introduction, 2. …, 3. …, 4. Conclusion).

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables carry panel labels and variable definitions in notes; t-statistics in parentheses below coefficients.

### Explicit Prohibitions
Manuscript must contain no author-identifying information (double-anonymous review). ORCID registration required at submission. A peer-review processing fee of AUD 75 is charged once a paper advances past desk review.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Reference List Format
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify reference list formatting beyond "author-date" style.

**Abacus practice:** Reference list uses a distinctive house style observed consistently in published work:
- Author surnames in **ALL CAPS** followed by initials: `DECHOW, P. M.`
- Multiple authors separated by **semicolons**; "and" before the final author: `DECHOW, P. M.; S. P. KOTHARI and R. L. WATTS.`
- Article titles in **single quotation marks**: `'The Relation between Earnings and Cash Flows.'`
- Journal names in regular (non-italic) text
- Format for journal articles: `SURNAME, I. N.; I. N. SURNAME and I. N. SURNAME. 'Title.' Journal Name Volume (Year): first–last.`
- Working papers cited with SSRN URL and year at end

Label: ENFORCED (observed in accepted/published article; consistent with the journal's historical style as an Australian accounting journal).

### Scope and Genre Mix
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes norms for empirical and experimental archival papers; commentary and essay genres are not addressed.

**Abacus practice (RELAXATION):** The journal regularly publishes short commentary essays (e.g., Ball 2024, 12 pp.), invited forum responses, and policy-oriented analytical pieces alongside standard archival papers. These non-archival genres do not follow the hypothesis-development structure described in FIELD_SKILL and may use a more essayistic register with fewer formal section divisions.

Label: PRACTICE ONLY — authors submitting archival empirical papers should follow FIELD_SKILL structure; authors submitting commentary or analytical essays should follow the journal's more flexible genre conventions.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Reference list formatting:** Authors unfamiliar with Abacus house style often submit references in standard APA or Chicago format. The journal uses ALL-CAPS surnames and single-quoted article titles — reformat the reference list before submission.

2. **Keywords not alphabetized:** The guideline explicitly requires 3–5 keywords in alphabetical order. Submitting keywords in any other order (e.g., by importance or topic) will require revision.

3. **Treating Abacus as a pure empirical journal:** The journal publishes essays, commentaries, and analytical critiques alongside archival papers. Structure and length norms differ by genre; an essay submission should not be padded with hypothesis-testing machinery.

4. **Abstract length:** The 250-word cap is firm. Abstracts from top-tier journals that were revised for resubmission often run longer; trim before submitting.
