# Journal Writing Skill: European Financial Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell on behalf of the European Financial Management Association (EFMA)
- **Scope:** International empirical and theoretical finance with emphasis on European markets, institutions, and regulation; welcomes work on corporate finance, asset pricing, banking, and financial markets with European relevance.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.efmaefm.org/0EFMJOURNAL/EFM%20Author%20Guidlines%20.pdf
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Fu, Jenkinson, Newton & Xie, 2024, DOI: 10.1111/eufm.12460)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No prescribed section order beyond standard finance conventions.

### Length
**55 pages maximum**, inclusive of references, appendices, tables, and figures. [ENFORCED — confirmed in practice; Fu et al. runs 28 journal pages.]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, **≤100 words**. This is materially stricter than the field baseline (150–250 words). Keywords (up to 5) and JEL classification (at least one code) appear as separate fields below the abstract, not within the abstract body. [ENFORCED — confirmed at 91 words in spot-checked article.]

### Citations
**APA 6th edition** throughout. In-text: author-year parenthetical with ampersand inside parentheses — e.g., "(Fama & French, 1993)" — and "and" in narrative — e.g., "Fama and French (1993) show…." Reference list: "Author, I. (Year). Title. *Journal*, Vol(Issue), pages." This differs from the JF/JFE reference format (which uses commas throughout with no parentheses around year). [ENFORCED — confirmed in reference list of spot-checked article.]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Arabic numerals with subsections (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2, etc.). Published articles display a pipe separator (e.g., "2 | DATA") added by Wiley in production; authors need not replicate this in manuscripts.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standard errors in parentheses; */**/*** notation for 10%/5%/1% significance documented in table notes.

### First-Page Requirements
The first page must contain, in order: (a) title; (b) author names **listed strictly in alphabetical order by surname** with email addresses; (c) abstract ≤100 words; (d) up to five keywords; (e) at least one JEL code; (f) data availability statement; (g) conflict of interest statement; (h) funding statement. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
None explicitly stated beyond standard ethics (no simultaneous submission, no duplicate publication).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** 150–250 words (FIELD_SKILL baseline for ABS 4*/4 journals).
**This journal:** ≤100 words. Authors must compress the research question, method, and key finding into a single very short paragraph. Economic magnitudes are typically omitted from the abstract and reserved for the introduction.
**Classification:** ENFORCED (stricter than field baseline; this is a tightening, not a relaxation).

### Citation / Reference List Format
**Field norm:** Author-year parenthetical in text; reference list format unspecified in FIELD_SKILL (it varies across ABS 4* journals).
**This journal:** Explicitly requires APA 6th edition. The reference list format — "Author, I. (Year). Title. *Journal*, Vol(Issue), pages." — differs from the finance-standard JF/JFE style that uses commas throughout without parenthesised years. Authors accustomed to JFE submission format must reformat their reference lists.
**Classification:** ENFORCED.

### Author Name Order
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify author ordering conventions.
**This journal:** Author names must be listed **strictly in alphabetical order by surname**. This is mandatory and journal-specific, overriding any contribution-based or seniority-based ordering conventions the authors might prefer.
**Classification:** ENFORCED — confirmed in spot-checked article (Fu, Jenkinson, Newton, Xie).

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract too long.** Authors calibrated to ABS 4* journals (150–250 words) will over-write the abstract. Draft at ≤100 words from the start; do not compress at the end.
2. **Wrong author order.** Listing authors by contribution or seniority is a desk-rejection risk. Alphabetize by surname unconditionally, regardless of relative contributions.
3. **Wrong reference format.** Using JF/JFE-style references (commas throughout, year after journal name) instead of APA 6th (year in parentheses after authors, volume/issue notation) will require a full reference-list reformat before submission.
4. **Missing first-page statements.** Data availability, conflict of interest, and funding statements are required on the first page. These are not optional appendix items.
