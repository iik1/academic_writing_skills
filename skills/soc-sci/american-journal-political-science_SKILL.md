# Journal Writing Skill: American Journal of Political Science

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley on behalf of the Midwest Political Science Association
- **Scope:** Leading general journal in political science covering all subfields, empirical and normative.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://ajps.org/guidelines-for-manuscripts/manuscript-preparation/
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Schaeffer, Krakowski & Olsen, 10.1111/ajps.12933, AJPS 2024)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Full articles and research notes have no mandated section order beyond a title page with abstract and an alphabetical reference list. The three accepted submission genres impose structural constraints:
- **Full Article**: original argument or analysis, max 10,000 words.
- **Research Note**: methodology papers and meta-analyses only, max 4,000 words.
- **Correspondence**: critical essays challenging a previously published AJPS article only, max 4,000 words. Correspondence is not a venue for new empirical work.

Manuscripts lacking original arguments or analytical rigor will not be sent for review.

### Length
**10,000 words maximum** for full articles (ENFORCED). Word count includes: main text, footnotes/endnotes, parenthetical references, appendices destined for print, and table/figure titles and notes. Word count **excludes**: title page, abstract, reference list, online-only supporting information, and mathematical notation.

### Abstract
**150 words maximum**, unstructured (no labeled fields). Must address: (1) the research stream/area the paper contributes to, (2) the specific topic, (3) the analytical strategy, (4) the findings, and (5) implications. Label: ENFORCED. Observed articles conform closely; abstract runs approximately 120–145 words.

### Citations
Authors must follow either the **APSA Style Manual for Political Science (2023)** or **The Chicago Manual of Style (18th edition)**. Two journal-specific requirements override default APSA/Chicago practice:
1. **Full first and last names** in the reference list (e.g., "Schaeffer, Merlin" not "Schaeffer, M."). Initials alone are prohibited. (ENFORCED)
2. **Self-citations must use third person** ("Brooks et al. (2000) examined…" not "we previously showed…"). (ENFORCED)

In-text citations use author-date format: (Author Year) or Author (Year). Reference list entries follow APSA format with full names, year after author, article title in quotation marks, journal in italics.

### Headings
FIELD_SKILL norm applies for heading hierarchy. Observed practice in published articles: major section headings are typeset in ALL CAPS (e.g., THEORETICAL BACKGROUND, RESULTS, CONCLUSION). Sub-headings appear in mixed case. This is a publisher typesetting convention, not author-controlled. (PRACTICE ONLY)

### Tables and Figures
Must be numbered consecutively and be legible and high quality. Inserted into manuscript body for review. Statistical significance threshold for table annotation: **p ≤ 0.05** (explicitly stated in guidelines). (ENFORCED)

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No author-identifying information anywhere in the submission (names, affiliations, acknowledgments, presentation history, grant acknowledgments). Double-blind review enforced strictly.
- No simultaneous submissions to other journals.
- No resubmission of manuscripts previously reviewed by AJPS.
- No manuscripts without IRB approval where human subjects are involved.
- Word count violations result in automatic non-review without exception.
- No replication materials exemption without pre-submission editorial approval.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** Separate Discussion and Conclusion sections are common in quantitative work; practice varies.

**This journal:** The dominant structural pattern in published articles omits a standalone Discussion section. The typical sequence is Introduction → Theory/Literature Review → Data and Methods → Results → Conclusion. The Conclusion section carries both interpretive and summarising functions that FIELD_SKILL assigns to a separate Discussion. (PRACTICE ONLY — observed in spot-checked article; guidelines do not mandate this structure.)

### Replication Materials
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that replication materials and data availability statements are "increasingly standard." No specific deposit requirement.

**This journal:** Replication/verification materials are **mandatory** and must be deposited in the **AJPS Dataverse** (Harvard Dataverse Network) before publication. A standardised "Verification Materials" statement citing the Dataverse DOI appears prominently in published articles, adjacent to the abstract. Exemptions require pre-submission editorial approval. (ENFORCED)

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL expects effect sizes, confidence intervals, and substantive magnitudes alongside significance. p ≤ 0.05 is standard but not explicitly codified.

**This journal:** Guidelines explicitly codify **p ≤ 0.05** as the significance threshold for table annotations. Observed articles report both 90% and 95% confidence intervals on figures, consistent with a more demanding standard for uncertainty disclosure than the field baseline alone would imply. (ENFORCED for the threshold; PRACTICE ONLY for dual-CI reporting.)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Word count miscalculation.** The 10,000-word cap includes footnotes, parenthetical citations, and print appendices — but not the reference list or online appendix. Authors routinely undercount by omitting footnotes. Verify the count against the guidelines definition before submission.
2. **Abbreviated names in references.** The journal explicitly prohibits "Last, F." format. Every reference entry must spell out the author's full first name. Manuscripts formatted with standard APA or abbreviated APSA style will need reference list revision.
3. **Self-citation in first person.** Using "as we showed in X (2020)" is prohibited; must write "as X et al. (2020) showed." Violations undermine the double-blind process.
4. **Missing Dataverse deposit.** Replication materials must be deposited and a Dataverse DOI obtained before the manuscript can be accepted. Treating replication as a post-acceptance task causes avoidable delays; deposit should be prepared in parallel with final revisions.
