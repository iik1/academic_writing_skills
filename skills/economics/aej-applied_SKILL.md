# Journal Writing Skill: American Economic Journal: Applied Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** American Economic Association
- **Scope:** Applied microeconomics; empirical papers using causal identification strategies on labor, health, education, development, and related topics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/app/submissions/guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1257/app.20230647; 10.1257/app.20240271)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. In-paper appendices must begin on a new page after the main text and before the references list. The paper must be self-contained without consulting supplemental materials; supplemental appendices are permitted for non-essential extensions but are not edited or typeset by the journal. [ENFORCED]

### Length
Maximum 40 typeset pages when formatted in 11-point font, 1.5 spacing, and 1-inch margins; or 45 pages in 12-point font, 1.5 spacing. The limit includes all figures, tables, references, and appendices. The journal's average published article runs 32–33 typeset pages. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; 100 words or fewer. No required fields. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style Author-Date format is the stated recommendation. In practice, AEA house style omits the comma between author and year in in-text citations: "(Smith 2026)" not "(Smith, 2026)". Reference list format follows AEA convention: Author. Year. "Title." Journal Volume (Issue): Pages. [GUIDELINE ONLY for software choice; ENFORCED for AEA house format in published articles]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Published articles use Roman numerals for main sections and capital letters (A, B, C…) for subsections.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI software may not be listed as an author; any AI assistance in manuscript preparation must be disclosed.
- Papers simultaneously under consideration elsewhere are ineligible.
- Papers previously rejected by AEJ: Applied Economics may not be resubmitted.
- Field experiments must be registered in the AEA RCT Registry prior to submission.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Voice and Register
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** First-person plural ("we") is near-universal, including in sole-authored papers.

**This journal:** First-person singular ("I") is used and published in sole-authored papers. Confirmed in Smith (2026): "I study," "I analyze," "I argue" throughout the abstract and body. Multi-authored papers follow the field norm of "we." Note that "This paper" is sometimes used as a framing device at the opening of the abstract or conclusion alongside "we" in multi-authored work, but "we" dominates the body. [PRACTICE ONLY]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 100 words.** The limit is firm. At the field baseline there is no stated abstract word limit, so authors used to other ABS 4 outlets may not trim aggressively enough.
2. **Appendix placed after references.** AEJ Applied requires in-paper appendices to appear before the references list, which differs from many journals where references come first.
3. **Paper not self-contained.** The journal explicitly requires that the main text stand alone without supplemental materials. Readers should not need to consult the online appendix to follow the argument.
4. **Sole-authored papers written in "we."** The field baseline favors plural even for sole-authored work, but AEJ Applied publishes "I" in sole-authored papers. Using "we" for a single-author paper is not wrong here, but "I" is the observed norm and avoids the awkwardness of false plurality.
