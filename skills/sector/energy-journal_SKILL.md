# Journal Writing Skill: The Energy Journal

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR (Energy Economics)
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications on behalf of the International Association for Energy Economics (IAEE)
- **Scope:** Theoretical and applied energy economics. The central focus must be the economics of energy. Papers where energy issues carry no economic analysis, where environmental topics are treated without explicit energy content, or where energy is peripheral to an economic argument are rejected at desk.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/ENJ (Sage); supporting policy: https://www.iaee.org/publications/energy-journal
- **Field baseline:** sector-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Kilian, Rapson & Schipper, "The Impact of the 2022 Oil Embargo and Price Cap on Russian Oil Prices," The Energy Journal, 2026, 47(3); preprint accessed via UC Davis faculty page)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No fixed section sequence is mandated, but numbered sections with numbered subsections (e.g., 2, 2.1, 2.2) are the operative norm in published articles (see Observed Deltas). A separate standalone literature review section is not prescribed and is absent from observed articles; literature engagement is integrated into numbered content sections. Article types: Research Article, Energy Perspectives (invitation-only), Book Review.

### Length
**9,500 words maximum**, including abstract, footnotes, and references. Equivalently stated as 25–35 pages double-spaced. This cap is notably tight: references and notes count toward the limit. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured, **≤150 words**. No structured fields (Background / Methods / Results) are prescribed or used. JEL classification codes and a keyword list follow the abstract on the title page. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
**Chicago author-date system** — not APA. This conflicts with the APA (author-year) norm stated in FIELD_SKILL. In-text: `(Cook et al., 2023)` or `Cook et al. (2023)`. Reference list: alphabetical, full first names, year placed after journal name rather than after author names. Example format:

> Borenstein, Severin and Ryan Kellogg, "The Incidence of an Oil Glut: Who Benefits from Cheap Crude Oil in the Midwest?," *The Energy Journal*, 2014, 35(1), 15–34.

Key formatting markers: full first names (not initials), year after journal name, commas throughout, "and" not "&". [ENFORCED — overrides FIELD_SKILL APA norm]

### Headings
Sections numbered with Arabic numerals; subsections numbered decimally (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2, ...). No heading-level formatting rules stated in guidelines, but numbered hierarchy is consistently used in published work. [PRACTICE ONLY for numbering convention]

### Tables and Figures
No explicit rules stated in accessible guidelines beyond standard submission requirements. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for reporting/interpretation separation.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Manuscripts where "energy issues carry no economic implications analysed" — rejected at desk.
- Manuscripts on "environmental topics in which no energy issues are explicitly dealt with" — rejected at desk.
- Manuscripts on "economic issues where energy plays just a minor part" — rejected at desk.
- No more than 12 complex equations; mathematical expressions set in italic type, equations numbered consecutively flush right. [ENFORCED]
- Simultaneous submission or prior publication elsewhere not permitted (standard originality warranty required).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Format
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** APA author-year (initials, parenthetical year after author names, "&").
**This journal:** Chicago author-date — full first names, year placed after journal name, "and" throughout, commas as separators. The in-text format is functionally equivalent (author-year), but the reference-list format differs materially.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Required Metadata: JEL Codes and Keywords
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** JEL codes are not mentioned; the FIELD_SKILL field has no JEL classification tradition.
**This journal:** Both a keyword list and JEL codes are required on the title page, immediately following the abstract. Observed in all checked articles.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Section Structure: Numbered Hierarchy, No Standalone Literature Review
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Canonical sequence Introduction → Literature Review → Method → Results → Discussion → Conclusion; standalone literature review is standard.
**This journal:** Articles use numbered sections throughout (1 Introduction, 2 [topic], 2.1, 2.2... N Conclusions). A standalone "Literature Review" section is absent; prior literature is engaged within numbered content sections and explicitly within the Introduction's contribution statement. This matches the quantitative-economics paper structure, not the tourism/management paper structure in FIELD_SKILL.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (guidelines do not prescribe structure; observed consistently)

### Practical/Managerial Implications: Absent
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Both theoretical and practical/managerial implications are expected, with substantial weight on practitioner guidance in applied papers.
**This journal:** Managerial or practitioner implications sections are absent. Policy implications may be discussed within the conclusion, but as an academic economics journal the audience is researchers, regulators, and analysts — not managers. A dedicated "managerial implications" subsection would be anomalous.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY — RELAXATION of FIELD_SKILL norm

### Equation Density and Notation
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Mathematical notation ranges widely; the field baseline notes this is absent from behavioural and interpretive papers.
**This journal:** Explicit cap of 12 complex equations. Mathematical expressions in italic; equations numbered flush right. This applies to empirical and theoretical papers alike. Calibrated structural models, reduced-form regressions, and theoretical derivations are all common article types.
**Classification:** ENFORCED (equation cap); FIELD DEFAULT for notation conventions (already within FIELD_SKILL mathematical culture range)

## Dimensions With No Delta
- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural "we" throughout)
- Formal academic register: FIELD DEFAULT
- Hedging and epistemic caution: FIELD DEFAULT (moderate; causal claims restricted to designs that support them)
- Discussion and conclusion: FIELD DEFAULT (conclusions section restates findings, engages prior work, states limitations and future directions; merged or separate from discussion depending on paper type)
- Statistical reporting: FIELD DEFAULT (coefficients, standard errors, model fit reported in tables; precise numerical reporting expected; null results reported)
- Past tense in methods: FIELD DEFAULT
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT (high in introduction and conclusions, low in results)

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Word budget**: At 9,500 words including references and footnotes, authors frequently underestimate how much the reference list and notes consume. Budget approximately 800–1,200 words for a 30-reference list; plan the body accordingly.
2. **Chicago vs APA reference list**: Authors from management, tourism, or sociology backgrounds who default to APA will produce incorrect reference-list entries (wrong year placement, initials instead of full names). Check every entry against the Energy Journal reference format.
3. **Missing JEL codes**: Omitting JEL classification codes from the title page is a common desk-rejection trigger; they are not optional.
4. **Scope mismatch at desk review**: The editors apply the scope prohibition strictly. Papers on climate policy, environmental regulation, or general macroeconomics that do not foreground energy economics are rejected without peer review. The economic analysis of energy must be the stated centre of the paper, not a motivation or application.
