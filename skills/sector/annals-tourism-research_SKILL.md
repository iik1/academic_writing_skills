# Journal Writing Skill: Annals of Tourism Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** Tourism, Transport and Hospitality
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Broad social-scientific study of tourism, including theoretical, empirical, qualitative, and conceptual work; one of the field's most generalist and prestigious outlets
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/annals-of-tourism-research/publish/guide-for-authors

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for full research articles. The journal additionally defines three short-format article types with distinct structural expectations (see Length below).

### Length
All word counts are **inclusive of tables, figures, and references** — an unusually strict inclusive counting rule:

| Format | Word Limit | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Full research article | 6,000–10,000 words | Inclusive of tables, figures, references |
| Research Note | ≤ 3,000 words | Preliminary findings, methodological developments |
| Commentary | ≤ 3,000 words | Response to a recently published article; deep expertise required |
| Viewpoint | ≤ 1,000 words | Opinion on current scholarship; **requires advance editor-in-chief approval before submission** |

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Abstract
Guidelines do not specify a word limit or required format. In practice, the abstract is an unstructured single paragraph. Highlights (4–5 bullet points) and a Graphical abstract appear as standard Elsevier submission components alongside, not within, the abstract text.

**Abstract text: PRACTICE ONLY (unstructured single paragraph)**
**Highlights: ENFORCED (required Elsevier submission element, 4–5 bullets observed consistently)**
**Graphical abstract: ENFORCED (present in all articles accessed; listed as a submission section in guidelines)**

### Citations
Guidelines do not state citation style explicitly. Author–year (APA) is the observed format consistent with field norms.

**Label: FIELD DEFAULT**

### Headings
Guidelines do not specify heading hierarchy or numbering. No heading-specific deviations identified.

**Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.**

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures count toward the word limit. The guidelines recommend expressing key findings graphically where possible.

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Work simultaneously under review elsewhere may not be submitted.
- Previously published work is excluded except preprints, abstracts, lectures, theses, or registered reports.
- Generative AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors and may not be cited as sources.
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance.
- A Generative AI Use Declaration is required at the end of the manuscript if AI was used in preparation. **Label: ENFORCED**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Graphical abstract and Highlights are standard submission elements.** The FIELD_SKILL baseline notes Highlights as "optional but common" in Elsevier journals. At ATR, both Highlights (4–5 bullets) and a Graphical abstract are present in all published articles examined and are treated as required components, not optional. Authors should prepare these as integral parts of the submission rather than additions.

**Label: ENFORCED**

**Viewpoints require advance editor-in-chief approval** before a manuscript can be submitted. This pre-submission gate has no parallel in the FIELD_SKILL baseline and distinguishes ATR from journals that accept opinion-format pieces through standard submission.

**Label: ENFORCED (Guideline only for the pre-approval process; the viewpoint format itself is FIELD DEFAULT)**

### Length Constraint

The inclusive word count (tables + figures + references counted toward the 6,000–10,000 ceiling) is a tighter constraint than implied by FIELD_SKILL, which does not address word limits. A full-length empirical paper with three tables and a reference list of 70–80 items will consume roughly 1,500–2,500 words of the budget on non-prose elements, leaving approximately 4,000–8,000 words of running text. Authors must budget accordingly.

**Label: ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Over-length submissions.** Authors accustomed to journals with 8,000–10,000-word limits on prose alone may inadvertently exceed ATR's ceiling when tables and references are counted in. Budget the word count before drafting, not after.

2. **Submitting a Viewpoint without prior editor approval.** The pre-submission approval requirement for Viewpoints is easy to overlook; submitting without it will result in desk rejection.

3. **Missing or underprepared Highlights and Graphical abstract.** Both are required Elsevier submission elements. Highlights should be 4–5 standalone declarative sentences summarising the paper's contributions; the Graphical abstract should communicate the study visually. Treating these as afterthoughts risks incomplete submissions.

4. **Inadequate multidisciplinary accessibility.** ATR explicitly requires that articles be written for a multidisciplinary audience. Specialist jargon that is routine in a sub-field (e.g., psychometric terminology in an experimental paper, remote sensing nomenclature in a geospatial paper) needs plain-language explanation or replacement.
