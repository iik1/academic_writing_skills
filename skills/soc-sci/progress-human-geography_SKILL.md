# Journal Writing Skill: Progress in Human Geography

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications
- **Scope:** Critical reviews of philosophical, conceptual, theoretical, topical, methodological, ethical, and political issues in human geography. Explicitly excludes empirical research and detailed case accounts.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/phg
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (via Academia.edu, older article; structural patterns confirmed via web sources for 2024 articles)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required template, but heading levels must be clear and sections clearly defined. The journal publishes multiple distinct manuscript types, each with different typical structures:
- **Review articles / Progress Reports**: critical synthetic reviews with thematic section headings (no IMRaD); Progress Reports are typically commissioned and run ~4,000 words.
- **Perspectives, Opinions**: shorter argumentative pieces.
- **Book Review Symposia / Book Review Essays**: extended multi-author critiques with author response.

There is no standard Results or Methods section — the journal does not accept empirical work. This is a hard constraint that overrides the FIELD_SKILL assumed structure (Introduction → Theory → Data/Methods → Results → Discussion). Authors must use a review/essay structure instead.

### Length
**ENFORCED.** Papers must be 4,000–8,000 words, inclusive of endnotes, excluding bibliography. Progress Reports are typically ~4,000 words; full review articles up to 8,000. This is a tight ceiling with a stated floor.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED.** Unstructured abstract of exactly 100 words, placed between the title and main body. Must concisely state purpose, major findings, and conclusions. FIELD_SKILL specifies no word count for abstracts.

### Citations
**ENFORCED.** Sage Harvard author-date system. Page references use a colon (not a comma): e.g., "Author, year: page" (Smith, 2020: 45). Every in-text citation must have a corresponding reference list entry and vice versa.

### Keywords
**ENFORCED.** Minimum 5 keywords, listed immediately after the abstract. FIELD_SKILL does not specify keyword placement or minimum count.

### Headings
Author's discretion; no prescribed heading scheme. Heading levels must be visually distinct. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm in all other respects.

### Tables and Figures
No journal-specific rules stated. Given the scope exclusion of empirical work, data tables and results figures are absent in practice. Conceptual diagrams appear occasionally.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **No empirical research**: the journal explicitly does not publish results of empirical work.
- **No detailed case accounts**: individual case studies are excluded regardless of theoretical framing.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Canonical structure is Introduction → Theory/Literature Review → Data and Methods → Results → Discussion/Conclusion, with robustness checks and supplementary materials common in quantitative work.

**This journal:** IMRaD structure is absent. Articles are review essays or critical surveys organised around thematic or argumentative headings. There are no Data/Methods, Results, or Robustness sections. The standard progression is: Introduction (establishing territory and gap in the literature) → thematic analytical sections (each engaging a strand of scholarship) → Conclusion (synthesising and projecting directions). This is not a relaxation of FIELD_SKILL norms — it is a categorically different genre. **ENFORCED.**

### Statistical Reporting
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Tables and figures carry the primary evidential burden in quantitative work; effect sizes and confidence intervals expected.

**This journal:** Statistical reporting is absent in practice because no empirical work is published. Evidential burden rests entirely on literature synthesis and theoretical argumentation. **ENFORCED** (scope prohibition).

### Citation Density
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** High in introduction and literature review; moderate in discussion.

**This journal:** Citation density is uniformly high throughout, including in sections that in empirical journals would be Results or Discussion. Because the entire article is a literature review or critical synthesis, every analytical claim is anchored to cited scholarship. There is no citation-light Results section. **PRACTICE ONLY** (implied by scope, not stated in guidelines).

### Voice and Register
FIELD_SKILL norm broadly holds, but the critical/theoretical register is consistently at the more essayistic end of the range described in FIELD_SKILL's qualitative and human geography provisions. First-person singular is standard for single-authored pieces and is used to advance explicit argumentative positions ("I argue," "I would suggest") rather than merely to describe procedures. **PRACTICE ONLY.**

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting empirical work.** The journal explicitly rejects results-based articles, regardless of how theoretically rich the framing is. Any manuscript with a Data/Methods section, regression output, or case study narrative will be desk-rejected.
2. **Exceeding the word ceiling.** The 8,000-word limit includes endnotes but excludes the bibliography. Authors who write to 8,000 words and then add extensive notes will breach the limit.
3. **Abstract overlength.** The 100-word abstract limit is firm and lower than many authors expect. Abstracts exceeding this are a common formatting error.
4. **Missing keywords.** A minimum of 5 keywords must appear immediately after the abstract; omitting them or placing them elsewhere is a formatting error.
