# Journal Writing Skill: Decision Sciences

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley (on behalf of Decision Sciences Institute)
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary research on managerial decision-making across accounting, analytics, economics, finance, information systems, international business, logistics, management, marketing, operations, production, and supply chain. Particular interest in analytics. Broader than the typical OR/MANSCI field baseline.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/15405915/homepage/forauthors.html (Manuscript Preparation Guide PDF linked from https://decisionsciences.org/journal/)
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Chintapalli & Vakharia, 10.1111/deci.12595; Paul, Craig & Bendoly, 10.1111/deci.12558)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standalone "Relevant Literature" section observed in practice (not embedded in Introduction); "Implications and Conclusions" as merged final section also observed. Both are within FIELD_SKILL's permitted range.

### Length
Maximum 40 double-spaced pages including references, tables, and figures. **Fast Track department**: maximum 25 double-spaced pages. Label: ENFORCED

### Abstract
Unstructured (no labeled fields). No word limit confirmed from accessible guidelines text; sister journal DSJIE uses 250 words. Label: PRACTICE ONLY (unstructured format confirmed in articles; word limit not confirmed from DSJ guidelines directly)

### Citations
APA author-year format. In-text: (Author, Year); multiple authors use ampersand in parenthetical citations — (Brown & Sambamurthy, 1999) — but "and" when authors are named in running text. Three-plus authors: cite all on first use; use "et al." on subsequent uses. Alphabetical then chronological ordering for multiple citations. Label: ENFORCED

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (no journal-specific deviations confirmed).

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (no journal-specific deviations confirmed from accessible materials).

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated in accessible guidelines beyond standard dual-submission and anonymisation requirements.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Scope and Audience Orientation
**Field norm:** OR&MANSCI writing targets an audience of specialists in operations research and management science; technical density is calibrated for that readership.

**This journal:** The guidelines explicitly state that Decision Sciences "is a business journal, and its audience is primarily business school faculty." Authors "are asked to write their manuscripts with our audience in mind." This means papers from affiliated fields (e.g., IS, finance, marketing) are welcome and, crucially, manuscripts must be accessible to generalist business school readers rather than narrow OR/MANSCI specialists. Technical depth should be accompanied by clear managerial and/or theoretical framing aimed at a cross-disciplinary business school audience.

**Classification:** ENFORCED — RELAXATION. ABS 3 relaxation; does not redefine FIELD_SKILL baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL specifies no explicit page cap.

**This journal:** The 40-page double-spaced hard limit (including all back matter) is a binding constraint. At the ABS 4*/4 level, supplementary materials absorb overflow; here, the page count covers the full submission package. Fast Track submissions face a tighter 25-page limit with a 5-week review cycle (Conditional Acceptance, Minor Revision, or Reject only).

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
Two new editorial departments expand the submission types beyond the standard empirical/analytical paper:
- **Department of Replication Studies**: Accepts exact, methods-only, bounded conceptual extension, and transformative replication studies published as refereed Research Notes. This submission type has no FIELD_SKILL analogue.
- **Department of Fast Track**: Short, high-impact papers with strong practical motivation, timeliness, or broad audience potential. Papers must clear at least one of four distinctive-perspective criteria (timely topic, new research directions, real-world problem, broad media appeal). Published identically to regular papers (not as Research Notes).

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Ignoring the business faculty audience**: Papers written at pure OR/MANSCI specialist density without explicating managerial relevance or cross-disciplinary implications risk rejection. The audience expectation is broader than the field baseline.
2. **Exceeding the page limit**: 40 double-spaced pages is a hard cap covering all back matter. Unlike ABS 4*/4 outlets, there is no separate online supplement allowance described in accessible guidelines that would permit overflow.
3. **Submitting a narrow technical contribution without business grounding**: DSJ's scope explicitly spans IS, marketing, finance, and accounting alongside operations. Pure mathematical papers with no evident business-school relevance are outside the journal's stated profile.
4. **Misusing the Fast Track department**: Fast Track requires one of four explicit "distinctive perspective" criteria (timeliness, new research directions, real-world practical problem with generalizable findings, or media-level broad appeal). Submitting a standard short paper without meeting these criteria is misaligned.
