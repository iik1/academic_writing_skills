# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Business Logistics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Wiley Periodicals LLC (on behalf of CSCMP)
- **Scope:** Empirical supply chain management research on any supply chain activities that shape how organizations create and deliver value; seeks rigorous, relevant, and resonant research that informs theory and practice.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/21581592/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Patrucco et al. 2025, DOI: 10.1111/jbl.70037, via Strathclyde repository; citation style corroborated by Paperpile citation-style analysis)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section sequence specified in guidelines; the standard empirical sequence (Introduction → Theory/Hypotheses → Methodology → Results → Discussion → Conclusions) is observed in published articles.

### Length
No explicit word count specified for regular articles. Editors explicitly state they pay close attention to the **contribution-to-length ratio** — lean manuscripts are favored. For the "To the Point" special-issue format: 3,000–4,000 words (figures and tables excluded). [GUIDELINE ONLY for regular articles; ENFORCED for special-issue format]

### Abstract
**Unstructured plain paragraph. Maximum 200 words.** No labeled subsections (Purpose, Design/Methodology, Findings, etc.). [ENFORCED — confirmed in guidelines and observed in articles]

### Title
**Maximum 25 words.** [ENFORCED]

### Citations
**Chicago author-date style** — not APA. Deviates from FIELD_SKILL's "APA-adjacent" norm.
- In-text: no comma between author and year — `(Lee 2004)` not `(Lee, 2004)`. Multiple citations separated by semicolons: `(Falcone et al. 2022; Roscoe et al. 2022)`.
- Reference list: `Author(s). Year. "Article Title in Title Case." Journal Name Volume, no. Issue: Pages.` (e.g., `Adobor, H. 2020. "Supply Chain Resilience: An Adaptive Cycle Approach." International Journal of Logistics Management 31, no. 3: 443–463.`)
- Book chapter format: `Author(s). Year. "Chapter Title." In Book Title, edited by Editor(s), pages. Place: Publisher.`
[ENFORCED — observed consistently in published articles]

### Headings
Numbered section hierarchy (1, 2, 2.1, 2.2.1, etc.) observed in published articles. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Figures and tables do not count toward the word limit in the "To the Point" special-issue format; this principle appears to apply broadly.

### Submission Requirements
- Submit via ScholarOne: https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jbl
- Select one or more **methodology keyword(s)** and one or more **topical keyword(s)** during submission
- Double-blind review; authors must not reveal identity in the manuscript
- Conflict of interest declaration required at submission
- Copyright assignment required prior to production
- 12-point font, 1-inch margins, double spacing (formatting requirement)

### Explicit Prohibitions
No self-identifying information in the manuscript (anonymization for double-blind review).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Style
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Author-date (APA-adjacent) — `(Lee, 2004)` with comma; reference list in APA format with sentence case for article titles.
**JBL practice:** Chicago author-date — `(Lee 2004)` without comma; reference list uses title case for article titles, year placed immediately after author name, volume/issue formatted as `31, no. 3: 443–463`. This is a consistent, system-level departure from the field baseline.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Abstract
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Does not specify structured vs. unstructured; treated as journal-specific.
**JBL constraint:** Unstructured plain paragraph, maximum 200 words. No labeled sections.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Title Length
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Not specified.
**JBL constraint:** Maximum 25 words.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Contribution-to-Length Ratio
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Not specified.
**JBL editorial emphasis:** Editors explicitly monitor contribution-to-length ratio. This signals a preference for tighter manuscripts than general field norms imply — padding or over-long literature reviews are a specific desk-rejection risk.
**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY

### Empirical Scope
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Accepts conceptual, analytical, qualitative, and empirical work.
**JBL emphasis:** Explicitly self-described as "an empirical supply chain management journal." Analytical modelling and purely conceptual papers are outside the primary scope. Accepted methodology types named in submission portal include: archival, case study/structured interview, econometric modelling, ethnography, experimental design, optimization, simulation, survey.
**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (ABS 3 relaxation note: this is a scope constraint, not a tightening of writing norms)

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Wrong citation style**: Using APA (`(Lee, 2004)`) instead of Chicago author-date (`(Lee 2004)`) is the single most visible formatting error — no comma, and reference list format differs substantially.
2. **Abstract too long or structured**: JBL requires an unstructured paragraph ≤200 words. Importing a structured abstract from another submission (with Purpose/Findings headers) will require full rewrite.
3. **Over-long manuscripts**: Editors explicitly penalize poor contribution-to-length ratios. Extensive literature reviews or method appendices included in the main text are a red flag; move robustness material to online supplementary files.
4. **Non-empirical submissions**: Purely conceptual or analytical modelling papers are outside the journal's stated empirical focus and are likely to face desk rejection.
