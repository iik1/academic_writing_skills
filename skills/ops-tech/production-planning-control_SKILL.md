# Journal Writing Skill: Production Planning and Control

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis (Informa UK Limited)
- **Scope:** Operations management across all industries, with explicit emphasis on research that addresses emerging industrial needs and guides managerial practice. Qualitative, quantitative, mixed-methods, modelling, and case study papers are all welcomed; any method must be explicitly justified as appropriate to the industrial problem studied.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/action/authorSubmission?journalCode=tppc20&page=instructions (direct access blocked; key requirements sourced from journal description and article practice)
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 full-text (Mirzaei, Mabin & Zwikael, DOI: 10.1080/09537287.2024.2349231)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict. Note: the official guidelines page was inaccessible during preparation of this skill. Hard constraints below are derived from the journal's published description and confirmed article practice. Word limits and abstract length could not be confirmed from primary guidelines; the constraints that could be confirmed are noted accordingly.

### Structure
No mandatory section sequence is prescribed beyond paper type conventions. Any research method is acceptable if explicitly justified. FIELD_SKILL norms apply for section structure by paper type.

**ENFORCED delta:** Every published paper must include a "Notes on Contributors" section (see below under Observed Deltas).

### Length
Word limits not confirmed from primary guidelines. Special issue calls specify 6,000 words; this may not reflect the regular submission limit. Observed full-text qualitative articles run approximately 8,000–9,000 words excluding references.

### Abstract
**PRACTICE ONLY.** Unstructured plain paragraph — no labeled subfields (Purpose, Design, Findings, etc.) — confirmed in published articles. Keywords listed separately below the abstract. Abstract length limit not confirmed from primary guidelines.

### Citations
**ENFORCED.** Taylor & Francis Chicago author-date house style, confirmed in practice:
- In-text: (Author Year) — no comma between author name and year; "and" (not "&") for two authors; "et al." for three or more
- Reference list: first author inverted (Last, F.), subsequent authors in normal order (F. Last), connected with "and". Year follows author list with a preceding period: `. 2012.` Article titles in double quotation marks. Journal name in italics (in typeset output). Volume (Issue): pages. DOI included.

Example:
> Bashir, H., U. Ojiako, A. Marshall, M. Chipulu, and A. A. Yousif. 2022. "The Analysis of Information Flow Interdependencies within Projects." *Production Planning & Control* 33 (1): 20–36. https://doi.org/10.1080/09537287.2020.1821115.

This format differs from APA: no comma in-text, no parentheses around the year in the reference list, "and" rather than "&", only first author inverted.

### Headings
**PRACTICE ONLY.** First-level headings: bold. Second-level headings: bold italic. Both appear in green in the typeset journal layout (a production-level convention, not author-controlled). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None confirmed from guidelines directly. Industry applicability is an explicit review criterion ("applicability in industry, besides originality and scientific rigour"): papers perceived as purely academic with no industrial relevance risk desk rejection.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Author Biographies with Photographs

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address author biographies or photos.

**PPC requirement:** Every published paper includes a "Notes on Contributors" section with a professional headshot photograph of each author and a biographical paragraph (approximately 100–150 words per author). Bios are written in third person and cover current institutional affiliation, research specialisation, past career highlights, and notable publications or awards. This is explicitly stated in the journal's published description: "pictures and bios of authors together with their contact details are provided with each paper."

Authors must prepare this material at submission or revision, consistent with Taylor & Francis submission requirements.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (stated in journal description and universally confirmed in practice)

### Managerial Implications Placement

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL states a dedicated managerial implications subsection is "near-universal" across ABS 4*/4 journals.

**PPC practice (ABS 3 relaxation):** Managerial and theoretical implications are combined into a single subsection within Discussion (e.g., "Customizing project management methodologies – theoretical and practical implications") rather than appearing as a standalone "Managerial Implications" section. Practical relevance remains substantively present — the journal's review criteria explicitly weight industrial applicability — but the formal separation into a dedicated section is not enforced.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY, RELAXATION (practical relevance required; standalone section not required)

### Industry-Applicability Review Standard

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL expects practical relevance to be made explicit but does not state it is a gating review criterion.

**PPC standard:** The journal explicitly states that reviewers assess "relevance to the field, particularly applicability in industry, besides originality and scientific rigour." Industrial applicability is a co-equal criterion alongside scientific rigour, not a secondary consideration. Papers that cannot articulate an industrial problem motivating the research are at heightened risk of rejection on relevance grounds.

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (stated in journal description; harder to observe as a structural pattern in articles)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing "Notes on Contributors."** The journal requires a biographical paragraph and professional photo for every author. Submitting without this material — or including only an institutional affiliation — does not meet the journal's requirements.

2. **Wrong citation format.** The Taylor & Francis Chicago author-date style is not produced by default in APA or Harvard reference manager outputs. Key deviations: no comma in-text, year is bare in the reference list (not parenthesised), only first author is inverted, "and" replaces "&" before the final author. Verify the reference list manually.

3. **Insufficient industrial framing.** Reviewers explicitly score papers on "applicability in industry." A paper that frames its motivation and contributions purely in theoretical terms — without anchoring the problem in an industrial need or managerial implication — will likely be rejected on relevance grounds at this journal even if it passes the scientific rigour threshold.

4. **Overly long abstract with structured subheadings.** Published articles use a plain unstructured paragraph. Adding Purpose/Design/Findings labels (common in some Emerald journals) is inconsistent with observed practice at PPC.
