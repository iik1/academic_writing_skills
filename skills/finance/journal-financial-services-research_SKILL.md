# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Services Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Springer
- **Scope:** Empirical and theoretical research on the demand, supply, regulation, and pricing of financial services broadly defined — banking, insurance, capital markets, FinTech, consumer and corporate finance, and related regulation and macro-financial policy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://link.springer.com/journal/10693/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1007/s10693-023-00410-w; 10.1007/s10693-024-00433-x)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section order specified in guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm in practice.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, 150–250 words. Must not contain undefined abbreviations or unspecified references. 4–6 keywords required as a separate field. **ENFORCED.** Abstract word range aligns with FIELD_SKILL; the keyword count (4–6) is an additional hard requirement not specified at field level.

### Citations
Springer house style: author-date **without** comma. In-text format is `(Author Year)` — e.g., `(van den Heuvel 2006)` or `(Rosch and Caparon 2004; van den Heuvel 2006)`. Reference list format: `Author(s) (Year) Title. Journal Volume:Pages`. **ENFORCED. Overrides FIELD_SKILL**, which uses `(Fama and French, 1993)` with a comma.

### Headings
Arabic numeral section numbering (1, 2, 2.1, etc.). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Standard asterisk notation for significance (*, **, *** at 10%, 5%, 1%). Permission required for previously published material. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Explicit Prohibitions
Undefined abbreviations in the abstract are prohibited. Manuscripts must be anonymized for double-blind review. Data Availability Statement is required for all original research articles.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**Standalone Literature Review section — RELAXATION (PRACTICE ONLY)**

FIELD_SKILL norm: a standalone "Literature Review" section is essentially never used in mainstream finance journals; related literature is either embedded in the introduction or labeled as a subsection within it.

JFSR practice: a separate, numbered section titled "Literature Review" (or similar) appears as the second major section in at least one published article (Citterio et al. 2025, Vol. 68(2)). This is tolerated at the ABS 3 level and is consistent with the journal's broader scope (bridging banking, insurance, and policy literatures that benefit from explicit taxonomic review). It is not required and not mentioned in the guidelines.

### Citations

**No comma in author-date citations — ENFORCED**

FIELD_SKILL norm: `(Fama and French, 1993)` — comma between author name and year.

JFSR (Springer house style): `(Author Year)` — no comma. Confirmed in published articles and in the journal's citation style specification. This applies to both parenthetical and narrative citation forms: `Boot et al. (2021)` not `Boot et al., (2021)`. The reference list also follows Springer format, which differs from the APA-adjacent style common at JF/JFE/RFS.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Comma in citations.** Authors accustomed to JF, JFE, or RFS will habitually write `(Fama and French, 1993)`. JFSR requires `(Fama and French 1993)`. This will be copyedited but is worth correcting at submission.

2. **Missing keywords.** JFSR requires 4–6 keywords as a separate field. A submission without them may be returned before review.

3. **No Data Availability Statement.** This is required for all original research articles and is a formal submission requirement, not optional.

4. **Treating the standalone literature review as prohibited.** At this ABS 3 journal, a separate Literature Review section is observed in practice and is not penalized, unlike at ABS 4*/4 journals where it signals methodological immaturity.
