# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Financial Intermediation

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Financial intermediation, investment banking, corporate finance, financial contracting, financial regulation, and credit markets. Strong emphasis on banking and credit markets relative to other ABS 4 finance journals.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-financial-intermediation/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Guettler, Naeem, Norden & Van Doornik, 2024, "Pre-publication revisions of bank financial statements: A novel way to monitor banks?" JFI 58, 101073 — accessed via BIS Working Paper 1777)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. The article reviewed followed: Introduction → Institutional Background → Data (with subsections) → Empirical Analysis (with subsections, including robustness) → Conclusions → References → Online Appendix.

### Length
No explicit page or word limit identified in the accessible guidelines.

### Submission Fee
**$500 USD non-refundable submission fee** required before the paper is considered. Fee is not refunded on desk rejection. [ENFORCED]

### Review Process
**Single-blind:** author identity is disclosed to reviewers. [ENFORCED]
**Active desk-rejection policy:** the journal states that a significant fraction of submissions are rejected without external review. The median time from submission to first decision is approximately 2 days. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No structured fields (Background/Methods/Results). No explicit word limit identified; the spot-checked article used approximately 130 words, somewhat below the 150–250 word FIELD_SKILL range. [PRACTICE ONLY — single observation]

### Citations
In-text format: author-year **without** a comma between author name and year (e.g., "Acharya et al. 2009" and "(Acharya et al. 2009; Bischof et al. 2021)"). This differs slightly from the FIELD_SKILL example "(Fama and French, 1993)" which includes a comma. The difference is a publisher (Elsevier) formatting convention. [PRACTICE ONLY]

Reference list format follows Elsevier author-year style: "Last, F., Last, F., Year. Title. *Journal* Volume, Pages." Year immediately follows author list with a period. Journal names italicized. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Headings
Main section headings: bold, numbered with Arabic numerals (e.g., **1. Introduction**, **2. Institutional background**).
Subsection headings: italicized, numbered (e.g., *3.1 Data sources*, *4.2 Machine learning exercise*). This is the Elsevier journal convention; it is a visual formatting difference from unformatted headings but does not affect argumentation or content structure. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables carry descriptive notes defining sample, variables, clustering choices, and significance thresholds. Standard asterisk notation: *, **, *** for 10%, 5%, 1%.

### Highlights
Elsevier requires a **Highlights** section (3–5 bullet points summarizing the article's novel findings). This appears as a separate submission component, not in the manuscript body. [GUIDELINE ONLY — not visible in published working paper]

### Generative AI Disclosure
If AI writing tools are used, a disclosure statement must appear in a dedicated section immediately before the References. AI tools cannot be listed as authors. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No simultaneous submission elsewhere
- No AI listed as author
- No authorship changes after acceptance

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Submission Economics (not covered by FIELD_SKILL)
FIELD_SKILL is silent on submission fees. JFI charges $500 USD non-refundable at submission, regardless of outcome. This is a concrete cost that affects submission strategy: do not submit speculatively to JFI.

### Review Transparency
FIELD_SKILL does not specify review blinding. JFI is **single-blind**: reviewers know who the authors are. Authors cannot mask affiliation in the manuscript.

### Citation Punctuation
FIELD_SKILL baseline example uses a comma before the year: "(Fama and French, 1993)". JFI (Elsevier) practice omits the comma: "(Fama and French 1993)". This is a minor publisher-level convention.

### Highlights Component
FIELD_SKILL does not mention a Highlights requirement. JFI requires 3–5 bullet-point highlights as a separate submission element summarizing novel results. These are displayed on the ScienceDirect article page and should be written to function as standalone discovery statements.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting without the $500 fee.** The fee is required before consideration and is non-refundable on desk rejection. Budget for this cost and submit only when the paper is genuinely ready.
2. **Ignoring the active desk-rejection policy.** The editorial team rejects a substantial fraction of papers without review, with a median first decision of ~2 days. Papers outside the journal's scope (banking, intermediation, financial contracting) or below threshold on contribution clarity are likely desk-rejected. The introduction must signal fit and contribution immediately.
3. **Omitting the Highlights section.** Elsevier requires 3–5 discovery-statement bullet points as a separate submission file. This is easy to forget but will delay processing.
4. **Using a comma in author-year citations.** The Elsevier/JFI convention is "Smith et al. 2020" without a comma, not "Smith et al., 2020." Apply consistently throughout.
