# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of the Association for Information Systems

- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Manuscript Categories and Length

JAIS accepts seven submission types with distinct word limits:

- **Research Articles, Theory, Literature Reviews, Foundational Research:** 15,000 words maximum (longer manuscripts "are very likely to receive extra scrutiny" and may be shortened)
- **Research Perspectives:** ~10,000 words
- **Editorials:** 6,000-8,000 words
- **Policy and Impact:** 5,000-7,000 words
- **Absolute maximum:** 65 pages including everything (exceeding this = desk rejection)

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Structure

**Required heading format:**
- Numbered sections up to 3 levels maximum
- 1 First-Level Heading
- 1.1 Second-Level Subheading
- 1.1.1 Third-Level Subheading
- Title case for all headings (capitalize main words)

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Abstract

**Format:** Not specified in guidelines. Observed practice shows unstructured narrative abstracts (~110 words in spot-checked editorial). No word limit stated. No required fields or structure mandated.

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT (no journal-specific requirement found)

### Formatting Requirements

- **Font/spacing:** Double-spaced, Times New Roman 12pt for submissions
- **Orientation:** Portrait only in body; landscape tables/figures must go in appendix
- **Data appendices:** SEM studies must include full correlation matrix or covariance matrix in appendix

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Citations

**Style:** APA 7th edition with three JAIS-specific deviations:

1. **No DOIs:** "JAIS does not routinely use DOIs"
2. **No page numbers for conference proceedings**
3. **No "retrieved from" for URLs:** "do not use 'retrieved from,' simply give the URL"
4. **Conference presentations:** No dates and locations required

**In-text citation rules:**
- 1-2 authors: use all names every time
- 3+ authors: first author et al. from first citation onward
- Use "&" in parenthetical citations; use "and" when authors are part of sentence

**Classification:** ENFORCED (deviations from APA) / GUIDELINE ONLY (for base APA adherence)

### Tables and Figures

- **Table captions:** Above the table, title case for caption
- **Figure captions:** Below the figure, title case for caption
- **Table formatting:** Border lines (1/4 pt) between cells, bold header row, sentence case in cells
- **Table notes:** In last row of table
- **Width:** Must not exceed portrait page width

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Language Requirements

"The official language of the Journal of AIS is English. Therefore all submissions need to be checked and edited for correct English before submission."

**Specific conventions:**
- American spelling (organization, emphasize, color, behavior)
- "Data" as plural noun
- Oxford (serial) comma required
- That/which distinction upheld (essential clauses use "that" with no comma; nonessential use ", which")
- Avoid "as" for causation (use "because"); avoid "due to" (use "because of")
- Compound modifiers: hyphenate before noun, no hyphen after noun
- No hyphens with -ly adverbs
- Numerals for 10+; spell out if starting sentence
- Always numeral + "%" for percentages
- Em dashes (—) for auxiliary information; hyphens (-) for ranges; no en dashes

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Cover Letter Requirements

Must include:
- Manuscript category
- Word count
- 2-3 Senior Editor nominations
- Conflict of interest disclosures
- Related publications declaration
- **Generative AI transparency document**

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Explicit Prohibitions

- Double-blind review: author names should not appear obviously
- At least one author must be AIS member
- Concurrent submission to other journals
- Landscape orientation in body (appendix only)

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Voice and Tense: JAIS Preference for Active First Person

### Active Voice with First Person

JAIS explicitly encourages active voice with first-person pronouns. From the style guide:

> "Historically, publications have eschewed using 'I,' 'we,' and other such words to maintain objectivity. However, **JAIS prefers that authors use 'I,' 'we,' and so on because they are clearer and more succinct.**"

**Examples from style guide:**
- Preferred: "We analyzed..." vs. Passive: "...was analyzed"
- Preferred: "Table 1 presents..." vs. Passive: "...are presented in Table 1"

**Observed practice:** Dennis (2019) editorial uses extensive first person:
- "I've seen a gradual shift..."
- "My objective in this essay is to argue..."
- "I believe this obsession..."
- "When I began my career..."
- "I was part of a group..."
- "I am a product of my generation..."

**Classification:** ENFORCED (explicit guideline with strong preference stated)

**WHY THIS MATTERS:** This may differ from older IS conventions or journals in other fields that avoid first person. JAIS takes a clear position that first person improves clarity.

### Tense Conventions

**Present tense for:**
- What the paper does: "This paper explores...", "We define...", "In Section 1, we summarize..."
- What other papers do: "Holsapple (1995) discusses..."
- Results and findings: "The data suggest that...", "The model proposes..."

**Past tense for:**
- Signal phrases about studies: "Walker (2001) found..."
- Methodology and data collection: "We conducted...", "We analyzed...", "We asked respondents..."
- Historical events: "In 1990, Smith (1990) coined the term..."
- What participants said: "Interview participants reported..."

**Present tense for models:** "Because the model comprises [present tense]..."

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Quotation and Emphasis

### Quote Format

- **All quotes:** Double quotation marks
- **Quotes in quotes:** Single quotation marks only (never use single quotes for emphasis)
- **Page references:** Required for all direct quotes unless source lacks page numbers
- **Long quotes (40+ words):** Indent and italicize; do not italicize the citation; period before citation
- **Capitalization changes:** Made silently (no square brackets to mark)
- **Ellipses:** No brackets around ellipses marking deleted text
- **Square brackets:** Only for text/punctuation added to quote

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Emphasis

"Please use minimal emphasis. When doing so, use italics or quotes."

For quotes with added emphasis: "(emphasis added)" before citation
For quotes with original emphasis: "(emphasis in original)"

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Capitalization Conventions

**Capitalize:**
- "Appendix," "Section," "Table," "Figure" when referring to specific items (e.g., Figure 1, Section 3)

**Do NOT capitalize:**
- Job titles/positions: "president of the United States," "senior editor," "vice president of IBM"
  - Exception: when used as part of a name ("President Lincoln")
- Models, theories, laws, statistical procedures: "technology acceptance model (TAM)," "partial least squares (PLS)," "self-determination theory (SDT)," "structural equation modeling (SEM)"
  - Exception: proper names within: "Chronbach's alpha"
- Field/discipline names: "information systems," "accounting," "neuroIS"

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Reference Section

### General Format

APA 7th edition with JAIS modifications noted above.

**Capitalization rules:**
- **Sentence-style** for: article titles, book titles, conference paper titles, presentation titles, chapter titles
- **Headline-style** for: journal titles, conference proceeding titles, conference names

**Other conventions:**
- Use "&" to separate author names in references
- Use "&" in publisher names
- Include publisher name but NOT city
- Use "et al." in references only when there are 21+ authors

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Stylistic Conventions

### Abbreviations

- Spell out on first use with abbreviation in parentheses
- No periods in abbreviations: US, UK, FBI (not U.S., U.K., F.B.I.)
- Exception: initials in names need periods and spaces: "J. Q. Smith"

### Hyphens and Dashes

- **Em dashes (—):** Set off auxiliary information
- **Hyphens (-):** Number/date ranges (77-80, January-February)
- **No en dashes (–)** for any purpose
- **Compound prefixes:** Close up without hyphen (interrelation, nonnormative, underexamined, preexisting)
- **Exception:** Hyphenate co- with people (co-worker, co-author, co-editor) and certain vowel combinations (co-opt, meta-analysis, multi-item, quasi-experimental)
- **Special case:** "editor-in-chief" is hyphenated per APA

### Footnotes

- Superscript number after punctuation (periods, commas, quotation marks)
- No endnotes (footnotes only)
- Left-justified text, one space after superscript number
- No line spacing between footnotes

### Key Terms

Introduce with quotation marks or italics; thereafter no distinction.

### Numbers

- Numerals for 10+
- Spell out if beginning sentence
- **Always numerals for data analysis results** regardless of value or position

**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Observed Patterns (Not in Guidelines)

Based on spot-check of Dennis (2019) editorial:

**Abstract:** Unstructured narrative format, ~110 words, no labeled sections

**Section numbering:** Simple integers (1, 2, 3, 4) for main sections; editorial format did not use subsections

**Citation integration:** Natural integration into sentences; moderate density in introduction; references span IS, management, and business education literature

**Acknowledgments:** Unnumbered section after main text, before references

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Passive voice when active is clearer:** JAIS explicitly prefers active voice with "we" and "I" — do not default to passive constructions to sound academic

2. **Exceeding word limits:** Manuscripts over 15,000 words face "extra scrutiny" and may be shortened; over 65 pages total will not be reviewed

3. **Using DOIs in references:** JAIS does not routinely use DOIs despite APA 7th standard — omit them

4. **Landscape tables/figures in body:** Must be moved to appendix — body must be portrait only

5. **Missing Generative AI transparency document:** Required as of 2021 style guide revision

6. **Forgetting SEM data appendix:** Full correlation or covariance matrix required for SEM studies

7. **Capitalizing theories/models:** Write "technology acceptance model (TAM)" not "Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)"

8. **Wrong comma style:** JAIS requires Oxford comma — "x, y, and z" not "x, y and z"
