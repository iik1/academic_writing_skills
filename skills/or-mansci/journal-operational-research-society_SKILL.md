# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of the Operational Research Society

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis (on behalf of The Operational Research Society)
- **Scope:** Publishes the full spectrum of OR including theory, practice, history, and methodology; with an explicit editorial mandate to attract applied case-study work. Papers demonstrating applications of OR to real problems are stated as especially welcome.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/tjor20 (see "Instructions for Authors")
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (DOI: 10.1080/01605682.2025.2537885 — "A soft-constrained multi-objective facility location approach...", full text via White Rose eprints)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section ordering. Numbered sections (1., 2., 3., etc.) consistently observed. No mandated section names beyond standard Introduction and Conclusions. Journal also publishes technical notes, discussions, and book reviews in addition to full-length papers.

### Length
No confirmed explicit word limit found in accessible guidelines. Abstract: 50–200 words (ENFORCED, from archived guidelines; consistent with article evidence).

### Abstract
Unstructured. 50–200 words. Followed by a separate **KEYWORDS** block (observed: 6 terms in spot-checked article; format is a labelled list below the abstract). [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Harvard author-date system. In-text format: (Author, Year) or Author (Year). For 3–6 authors on first citation, give all names in full; thereafter use first author "et al." Reference list in alphabetical order by author surname (or chronologically for same-author group). DOIs included where available. [ENFORCED — stated in guidelines, confirmed in article]

### Headings
Numbered sections and subsections (1., 1.1, 2., etc.). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None found in accessible guidelines.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Contribution Type and Scope (ABS 3 RELAXATION)

**Field norm:** ABS 4*/4 outlets expect contributions framed as formal theoretical advances, algorithmic guarantees, or empirically novel findings. A case study alone — without novel methodology or formal results — is rarely a complete contribution.

**JORS instead:** Applied case-study papers are an explicit and first-class contribution type, not a supplement to theory. Papers can be accepted on the basis of demonstrating practical value, real-world implementation, or meaningful application of established OR methods to a novel domain. The journal's stated scope treats practice-oriented work as equally central to theoretical work. [RELAXATION — stated in journal scope; confirmed by breadth of published article types including sports analytics, scheduling case studies, and multi-criteria decision-making applications]

**Implication:** The Introduction at JORS may give substantially more weight to motivating practical significance rather than identifying a formal theoretical gap. Contribution claims can be framed in terms of applied utility alongside (or instead of) formal novelty.

### Citation Style

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a citation style (explicitly excluded as journal-specific).

**JORS instead:** Harvard author-date format required. [ENFORCED]

### Keywords

**Field norm:** Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL.

**JORS instead:** Keywords are required and appear as a labelled "KEYWORDS" block immediately after the abstract. Approximately 5–6 keywords observed in practice. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Framing contribution purely as theoretical novelty.** JORS actively seeks applied work. A paper with novel algorithms but no empirical or case validation is less suited to this outlet than at ABS 4*/4 venues. Conversely, a real-world implementation paper with limited theoretical originality is a legitimate submission here.

2. **Using numbered or endnote citations.** JORS requires Harvard author-date format throughout. Numbered citation systems (common in some OR fields) are inconsistent with the journal's requirements.

3. **Omitting keywords.** The KEYWORDS block after the abstract is required. Missing it or conflating it with the abstract is a formatting error.

4. **Writing an abstract above 200 words.** The guideline ceiling is 200 words. Applied OR abstracts that summarise problem, method, case setting, and findings can easily overrun; plan the abstract structure accordingly.
