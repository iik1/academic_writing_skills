# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Monetary Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Monetary economics, macroeconomic dynamics, monetary and fiscal policy, financial markets and their interactions with the real economy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-monetary-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (10.1016/j.jmoneco.2026.103921; 10.1016/j.jmoneco.2026.103935; 10.1016/j.jmoneco.2025.103826)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section order is prescribed in the guidelines.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in the author guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured (single paragraph). No word limit is stated in the guidelines. Articles checked show abstracts of approximately 150–170 words. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (no limit enforced, but observed practice clusters near 150–170 words).

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical style. Not stated explicitly in the guidelines but confirmed in all articles checked. Label: PRACTICE ONLY → FIELD DEFAULT (consistent with field baseline).

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Supplementary/online appendix material is explicitly permitted.

### Highlights — ENFORCED
Elsevier requires a **Highlights** file submitted separately from the manuscript. Highlights consist of 3–5 short bullet points (maximum 85 characters each, including spaces) summarising the novel results or key takeaways. They appear on the published article page above the abstract. All three articles confirmed this element (3 bullets in two articles, 4 in the third). Label: ENFORCED.

### Graphical Abstract — GUIDELINE ONLY
A graphical abstract (a single image summarising the article's main finding) is listed as an optional submission item. Not observed in the articles checked. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY.

### AI Use Disclosure — ENFORCED
Authors who used generative AI or AI-assisted tools in writing, editing, or image production must declare this in the manuscript (typically in an Acknowledgements section or dedicated statement). AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. Label: ENFORCED.

### Inclusive Language — GUIDELINE ONLY
The guidelines explicitly require inclusive language: avoid bias related to age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability; prefer gender-neutral phrasing (e.g., plural nouns rather than gendered pronouns); avoid slang and cultural assumptions. This is a stated Elsevier guideline rather than a field convention. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors.
- Previously published work may not be resubmitted (preprints excepted).
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Highlights block:** FIELD_SKILL contains no norm for a pre-abstract highlights element. JME (via Elsevier) requires one. In published articles, this block appears as 3–5 short declarative bullets immediately preceding the abstract on the article page. When preparing a submission, authors must draft these separately; they are not drawn from the abstract. Classification: **ENFORCED**.

**Graphical abstract slot:** An optional single-panel visual summary is available for submission. Not present in field norms. Classification: **GUIDELINE ONLY**.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**AI disclosure block:** FIELD_SKILL does not address AI use disclosure. JME guidelines require a declared statement if generative AI was used in drafting or editing. No equivalent field norm exists. Classification: **ENFORCED**.

**Inclusive language norm:** FIELD_SKILL makes no prescriptive statement about inclusive language. The JME guidelines contain an explicit inclusive-language requirement. Classification: **GUIDELINE ONLY**.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting the Highlights file.** Elsevier's submission system treats Highlights as a required upload item. A submission without one is incomplete. Bullets must be ≤85 characters each and must state novel contributions or findings, not restate the abstract.
2. **Confusing Highlights with the abstract.** Highlights are not a condensed abstract; they should isolate distinct take-aways (e.g., one bullet per key result), and each must stand alone as a short declarative sentence.
3. **Failing to declare AI tool use.** If any AI writing assistant was used at any stage, a disclosure statement is required in the manuscript. Absence of this statement where it is warranted constitutes a guideline violation.
4. **No word limit ≠ no length norm.** The absence of a stated page limit does not mean length is unconstrained. JME articles in the spot-check cluster around standard ABS 4 empirical paper length (~30–50 pages of body text); reviewers at this level penalise bloat.
