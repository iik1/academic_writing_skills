# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of International Money and Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Theoretical and empirical research in international monetary economics and international finance; covers exchange rates, capital flows, monetary policy, international banking, and related topics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-money-and-finance/publish/guide-for-authors

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
Standard academic structure (title page, abstract, keywords, body, references). Requires a **Highlights** section (see below). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Length
No explicit word or page limits stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured narrative paragraph. No word limit stated in guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on content (research question, method, findings). **GUIDELINE ONLY** (no limit enforced).

### Citations
**DEVIATION FROM FIELD_SKILL.** FIELD_SKILL baseline specifies author-year parenthetical format (e.g., "Fama and French, 1993"). This journal uses **numbered bracket citations** in the body text (e.g., [1], [2]) with a correspondingly numbered reference list. Confirmed in two published articles. **ENFORCED.**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Separate guidance applies if generative AI is used to produce visual content (must be disclosed).

### Highlights
Elsevier requires a **Highlights** section, appearing before or alongside the abstract on the published page. These are 3–5 short bullet points (typically one sentence each) summarizing the article's key contributions or novel findings. Not part of FIELD_SKILL. **ENFORCED** (confirmed in both articles checked).

### Submission Fee
US$250 non-refundable submission fee required before manuscripts are considered. **ENFORCED.** Not a writing constraint but a hard pre-submission requirement.

### Inclusive Language
Guidelines explicitly require inclusive language: avoid descriptors based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability; use gender-neutral phrasing as default. **ENFORCED.**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Previously published work (except preprints/theses)
- Simultaneous submission elsewhere
- Authorship changes after acceptance
- Listing AI tools as authors or co-authors

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** Author-year parenthetical throughout (e.g., "Fama and French, 1993" or "(Fama and French, 1993)").
**This journal:** Numbered bracket citations in body text (e.g., [1], [2]) with a numbered reference list. This is an Elsevier-standard format applied consistently in published articles.
**Classification: ENFORCED.** Writers accustomed to JF/JFE/RFS must switch citation style entirely.

### Highlights Section
**Field norm:** Not part of FIELD_SKILL; no equivalent in ABS 4*/4 finance journals.
**This journal:** Elsevier requires a Highlights section — 3–5 short bullet-point sentences capturing the article's key novel findings. These appear on the published abstract page and in database previews. They are distinct from the abstract and must be written as standalone claims, not as continuation of the abstract.
**Classification: ENFORCED.**

### Voice and Register
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Published articles use first-person plural, active voice, confident declarative tone.

### Statistical Reporting
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Quantitative magnitudes reported in abstracts and results; significance levels noted.

### Citation Density
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm overall; introduction-heavy citation pattern observed.

### Structural Deviations
None beyond the Highlights requirement. Standard introduction → data → empirical analysis → conclusion structure observed.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Citation format error.** Authors trained on JF/JFE/RFS will instinctively write "(Author, Year)" in-text. This journal requires numbered brackets [N]. Using author-year citations is inconsistent with published norms and Elsevier's standard template for this journal.

2. **Missing Highlights.** The Highlights section is an Elsevier requirement that does not exist in top-tier US finance journals. Omitting it or confusing it with the abstract is a common formatting error. Write Highlights as 3–5 standalone, complete-sentence bullet points — not fragment teasers.

3. **Forgetting the submission fee.** The US$250 non-refundable fee must accompany submission. Submitting without it will halt the process; the fee is not waived for desk rejections.

4. **AI tool non-disclosure.** If any generative AI tools were used in manuscript preparation (beyond basic grammar/spell check), a dedicated declaration section must appear before the references. Omitting this is an explicit guideline violation.
