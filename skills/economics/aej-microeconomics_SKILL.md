# Journal Writing Skill: American Economic Journal: Microeconomics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** American Economic Association
- **Scope:** Microeconomic theory and applied microeconomics spanning industrial organization, market design, behavioral economics, game theory, labor, and public economics. Publishes both purely theoretical and structural/reduced-form empirical work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/mic/submissions/guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Alaoui & Penta, "Attitudes Towards Success and Failure," AEJ:Mic 18(1) 2026, DOI 10.1257/mic.20240429; Ganglmair, Simcoe & Tarantino, "Learning When to Quit," AEJ:Mic 17(3) 2025, DOI 10.1257/mic.20190321)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Required elements: title, abstract, main text, references. In-paper appendices begin on a new page after the main text and before references. Supplemental appendix is standard and expected.

### Length
Recommended maximum **45 pages**, counting everything: main text, figures, tables, references, and in-paper appendices. Average published length is 36–37 typeset pages. References may be single-spaced; all other content requires 1.5 line spacing; minimum 12-point font; 1-inch margins. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; **100 words or fewer**. This cap is stricter than most ABS 4*/4 outlets. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style author-date format. Confirmed in both spot-check articles as parenthetical author-year (e.g., "(Gordon, 2016; Bloom et al., 2017)"). Aligns with FIELD_SKILL convention. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Simultaneous submission to another journal is ineligible.
- Previously rejected manuscripts from this journal cannot be resubmitted.
- AI software may not be listed as an author. [ENFORCED]
- **Field experiments using randomization must be registered in a publicly accessible registry prior to data collection.** Unregistered RCTs are ineligible regardless of study quality. [ENFORCED]

### Data and Code
Authors must provide a complete replication package (data, code, and documentation sufficient for replication) before acceptance, in compliance with the AEA Data and Code Availability Policy. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 100 words.** AEJ:Mic's cap is notably tighter than most top-field outlets. A draft carried over from a QJE or AER submission will typically need the abstract cut by 30–50%.
2. **Page count overshoot.** The 45-page ceiling includes references, tables, figures, and in-paper appendices—materials that some journals exclude from page counts. Calculate the total carefully before submitting.
3. **Unregistered field experiment.** Any paper using randomization in a field experiment must have been registered in a public registry (e.g., AEA RCT Registry, ClinicalTrials.gov) before data collection began. Post-hoc registration does not satisfy this requirement.
4. **Missing or incomplete replication package.** The AEA requires submission of a full replication package as a condition of acceptance, not merely at final publication. Budget time to assemble clean, documented code and data before the revision stage.
