## 1. Format & Structure

**Numbered section headings — PRACTICE ONLY**
Headings are numbered with Arabic integers and decimals: `1. Introduction`, `2. [Section Title]`, `2.1. [Subsection]`, `2.2.`, etc. Bold formatting used for first- and second-level headings. This contrasts with the unnumbered heading convention common at ABS 4*/4 STRAT outlets (SMJ, AMJ, OS). Apply numbered headings in manuscripts submitted here.

**Third-level headings: italic, unnumbered, inline — PRACTICE ONLY**
Third-level subheadings appear as italicized titles set inline at the start of a paragraph (e.g., *Subsection label.* Body text continues…), not as standalone heading lines. No number assigned. If a third level is needed, use this format rather than a numbered heading or a standalone bold line.

**Separate Discussion and Conclusion sections — RELAXATION**
FIELD_SKILL norm at ABS 4*/4 STRAT journals is a merged Discussion/Conclusion section. Strategy Science uses separate numbered sections: a Discussion section (examining implications, situating findings in the literature) followed by a distinct Conclusion section (scope limitations, future research, brief restatement). Both sections are substantive, not perfunctory. This is a relaxation of the top-journal merged norm; use separate sections here.

## 7. Discussion & Conclusion

**Separate sections — see §1 above (RELAXATION).**
The Discussion section engages with theoretical implications and situates the argument relative to prior literature. It does not simply restate findings. The Conclusion section is brief (roughly 1–2 pages), addresses limitations, suggests future research directions, and closes with a synthetic statement. Do not compress these into a single section when writing for this journal.

## 8. Citations & References

**In-text page citation: colon notation — PRACTICE ONLY**
When citing a specific page, the format observed is `Author (Year: page)` or `(Author Year: page)` — e.g., `Nickerson (2023: 45)` — using a colon between year and page number, with no `p.` or `pp.` prefix. FIELD_SKILL does not specify this level of granularity. Adopt colon notation for this journal; do not write `(Year, p. 45)` or `(Year, pp. 45–47)`.

**Author-date with "&" in running text — FIELD DEFAULT**
No delta. Two-author citations use `&` (not "and") in parenthetical form: `(Smith & Jones 2020)`. In running text: `Smith and Jones (2020)`. Consistent with INFORMS house style and STRAT field norm.

## 9. Tables, Figures & Appendices

**Table placement notation in manuscripts — PRACTICE ONLY**
In the accepted manuscript, tables are not embedded inline but flagged with a centered placement directive: `---Insert Table X about here---`. This is standard manuscript-submission practice (not a final typesetting norm), but confirms the journal accepts, and likely expects, callout-style placement markers rather than inline table embedding in submission files.

**Footnotes: numbered, bottom-of-page — PRACTICE ONLY**
Footnotes are numbered sequentially and appear at the bottom of the relevant page (not endnotes). Used sparingly for substantive elaborations and definitional clarifications, not for citations.

**Watch-out — numbered headings required.** If submitting to this journal after writing in an unnumbered-heading style (e.g., a revision from SMJ or AMJ), convert headings to numbered format per §1.
