# Journal Writing Skill: Transportation Research, Part D: Transport and Environment

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** Tourism, Transport and Hospitality (SECTOR)
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Publishes original research on the environmental impacts of transportation, policy responses to those impacts, and their implications for the design, planning, and management of transportation systems. All submissions must demonstrate generalizable policy and/or methodological relevance.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/transportation-research-part-d-transport-and-environment/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** tourism-transport-hospitality-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1016/j.trd.2026.105328; 10.1016/j.trd.2026.105360)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standard sequence (Introduction → Method → Results → Discussion → Conclusion) observed in both spot-checked articles. No journal-mandated section labels beyond standard Elsevier requirements.

### Length
No explicit manuscript word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
**150 words maximum; unstructured plain paragraph.** No labeled fields (Background, Methods, etc.). The abstract must stand alone and avoid references and uncommon abbreviations. — **ENFORCED**

### Highlights
**Required; 3–5 bullet points; maximum 85 characters per bullet including spaces.** Must be submitted as a separate editable file named with "highlights" in the filename. — **ENFORCED**

### Citations
Author–year format: (Smith, 2020) for single; (Cunningham and Russell, 2000; Giles, 2002) for multiples; (Lange et al., 2009) for three or more authors. Confirmed in guidelines and consistent with FIELD_SKILL norm. — **FIELD DEFAULT**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Graphical abstracts present in spot-checked articles and appear expected by the journal, though not explicitly mandated in accessible guidelines text. High-resolution figures required. — **PRACTICE ONLY**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools must not be listed as an author or co-author
- Generative AI use must be declared upon submission if used in manuscript preparation
- AI-generated references must be verified; fabricated citations are author's responsibility
- No concurrent submission or prior publication (preprints permitted)
- No authorship changes after acceptance

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Environmental and Policy Framing (Structural)
**Field norm:** Practical/managerial implications are expected in tourism and hospitality papers; mathematical transport methodology papers foreground technical extensions.

**This journal:** Because the journal's sole focus is the environmental dimension of transport, *every* article — regardless of methodology — must situate its contribution within an environmental impact or policy response framing. Implications sections must address environmental policy relevance explicitly, not just managerial or operational takeaways. This applies equally to quantitative modelling papers that might otherwise focus on technical extensions. The guidelines state submissions should demonstrate "generalizable policy and methodological relevance to research and practice." — **ENFORCED**

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract word limits (treated as journal-specific).

**This journal:** Hard 150-word cap. At this word count, authors must prioritise findings and policy implications over contextual framing. — **ENFORCED**

### Highlights
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes highlights as "optional but common" for Elsevier journals.

**This journal:** Highlights are required, not optional. 3–5 bullets at ≤85 characters each. Given the 85-character cap, each bullet must deliver a single concrete finding or contribution — descriptive or methodological process bullets will consume the allowance without communicating value. — **ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Abstract overrun.** The 150-word cap is tight for empirical papers with complex methods. Authors accustomed to 200–250-word abstracts routinely exceed it; write the abstract last and cut to fit.
2. **Missing or malformed Highlights file.** Highlights must be a separate submission file. Embedding them in the main manuscript or submitting more than 5 bullets or any bullet exceeding 85 characters triggers desk rejection or revision.
3. **Insufficient environmental framing.** Papers that contribute to transport modelling or behaviour research but do not explicitly connect findings to environmental impact or policy response are out of scope. The implications section must address this dimension even if the core contribution is methodological.
4. **Graphical abstract absent.** Both spot-checked articles included a graphical abstract. While not explicitly mandated in the publicly accessible guidelines text, its absence is likely conspicuous.
