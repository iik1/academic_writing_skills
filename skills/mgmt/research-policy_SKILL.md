## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm with article-type variation. Research articles follow standard empirical article structure. Research Notes (shorter format) may omit components typical of full articles when appropriate.

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT (structure), GUIDELINE ONLY (Research Notes flexibility)

### Length
- **Research Articles:** 8,000-10,000 words
- **Research Notes:** 3,000-5,000 words

These limits are tighter than FIELD_SKILL ranges (10,000-15,000 typical for quantitative work).

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Abstract
Guidelines mention abstract requirement but do not specify word limit or structured vs. unstructured format in accessible sections. Likely follows FIELD_SKILL norm (150-250 words, unstructured acceptable).

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT (format unspecified in guidelines)

### Highlights
**Required:** Authors must provide 3-5 bullet point highlights (85 characters maximum each) summarizing key findings. This is NOT standard in FIELD_SKILL.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Graphical Abstract
Optional. Authors may submit a single image providing a visual summary of main findings.

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (optional feature)

### Citations
Citation style not specified in accessible guideline sections. Elsevier journals typically use numbered reference systems or author-date; MGMT field norm is APA (Author, Year). Likely follows field norm.

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT (unspecified in guidelines)

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT

### Tables and Figures
Guidelines mention sections on tables, figures, and images exist but formatting details not accessible in retrieved content. Standard academic formatting expected.

**Classification:** FIELD DEFAULT

### Data Availability
Data availability statement required when applicable.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **No authorship changes after acceptance** — ENFORCED
- **AI tools cannot be listed as authors or co-authors** — ENFORCED  
- **Generative AI use in content creation must be declared** in methods or acknowledgments with specific tools named and how they were used — ENFORCED
- **Inclusive language required** — ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Exceeding Word Limit
Research Policy's 8,000-10,000 word limit is stricter than typical MGMT outlets. Articles at the FIELD_SKILL norm of 12,000-15,000 words will require substantial cutting.

### 2. Omitting Highlights
The 3-5 highlights (85 characters each) are mandatory. These are distinct from the abstract and must be concise, specific, and communicate key findings independently.

### 3. Undisclosed AI Use
Any use of generative AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) in drafting, editing, or analysis must be explicitly declared. Failure to disclose is a violation of editorial policy.

### 4. Post-Acceptance Authorship Changes
Unlike some journals that allow minor author list modifications, Research Policy prohibits any authorship changes after acceptance. Ensure author list is final before submission reaches acceptance stage.

## Research Note Format

Research notes (3,000-5,000 words) are explicitly recognized as a distinct article type. Per guidelines, they may omit "normal" components of full research articles when appropriate. This suggests:

- Abbreviated literature review acceptable
- Streamlined methods section  
- More focused contribution (single finding vs. multiple contributions)

This flexibility is not standard in FIELD_SKILL and represents a journal-specific accommodation for shorter empirical work.

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (practice not verified)
