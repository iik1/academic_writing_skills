# Journal Writing Skill: Tourism Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Leading international journal covering the full range of tourism research — behavioural, managerial, planning, and policy dimensions. Does not publish transport methodology papers.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for full research articles. The journal formally recognises three distinct submission types, each with its own structural expectations (see Length below). The "Progress in Tourism Research" type is a narrated literature review — not a standard empirical article — and must map the conceptual/theoretical/methodological trajectory of a topic; it explicitly excludes descriptive systematic literature reviews and pure bibliometric studies. Registered Reports are accepted as a fourth type (Stage I pre-registration + Stage II completed study). [ENFORCED]

### Length
Journal-specific word limits not covered by FIELD_SKILL:
- **Research paper:** ~10,000 words [ENFORCED]
- **Research note:** 3,000–5,000 words (small-scale studies on niche topics) [ENFORCED]
- **Progress in Tourism Research:** ~12,000 words [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose confirmed in articles. Specific word limit not recoverable from public guidelines page; standard Elsevier practice is 200–250 words. [GUIDELINE ONLY — exact limit not confirmed]

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (APA author-year). Confirmed consistent with article metadata.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Generative AI use in figures must be declared explicitly in the AI disclosure statement. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Papers based on a single survey with a convenience sample are **likely to be desk-rejected**. [ENFORCED]
- Scale development studies where the scale itself is the entire contribution (without demonstrated theoretical advancement) face desk rejection. [ENFORCED]
- Pure bibliometric studies and descriptive systematic literature reviews are **excluded** from the "Progress in Tourism Research" category. [ENFORCED]
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. [ENFORCED]
- No authorship changes permitted after acceptance. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Inclusive Language as Formal Policy
FIELD_SKILL specifies high academic register and absence of colloquial phrasing but does not flag inclusive language as a formal submission requirement. Tourism Management requires authors to use inclusive language throughout, explicitly avoid bias and cultural stereotypes, write for gender neutrality, and report sex- and gender-based analyses where applicable. [ENFORCED — stated in guidelines, consistent with observed article language]

### Generative AI Disclosure
Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL. Tourism Management requires a mandatory disclosure statement (positioned before the References) whenever AI tools are used in manuscript preparation — including text generation, editing, figure creation, or data analysis. Failure to disclose is treated as an ethical violation. [ENFORCED]

### Double-Blind Review — Anonymisation Obligation
FIELD_SKILL does not specify review modality. Tourism Management uses **double-anonymised** peer review; authors must remove all identifying information from the manuscript file before submission. [ENFORCED]

### Highlights Section
FIELD_SKILL notes highlights as "optional but common" in Elsevier journals. Confirmed present in spot-checked articles (3–5 short bullet points per article). Required by Elsevier house style for this journal. [PRACTICE ONLY — confirmed in articles; specific count/character limit not confirmed from guidelines page]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Single-survey convenience sample** — Explicitly flagged for likely desk rejection. If the entire empirical base is one self-report survey distributed via MTurk, student pools, or similar, the paper will not advance to review.
2. **Scale development as the sole contribution** — A paper whose primary output is a validated measurement scale must also demonstrate how that scale advances theory or answers a substantive research question; the validation itself is insufficient.
3. **Submitting a bibliometric study or plain SLR as "Progress in Tourism Research"** — This category requires a narrated, interpretive review with strong theoretical/methodological positioning, not a PRISMA-style systematic review or citation analysis.
4. **Omitting the AI disclosure** — Even minor AI-assisted editing (grammar, paraphrasing) requires a disclosure statement. The omission is treated as an ethics violation, not an oversight.
