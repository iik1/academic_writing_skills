# Journal Writing Skill: Behavioral Research in Accounting

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** American Accounting Association (AAA)
- **Scope:** Original behavioral research on how accounting affects and is affected by individuals, organizations, and society. Publishes experimental, survey, field, and experimental-economics studies; also welcomes literature reviews, methodological papers, scale validation, and replications. Does NOT publish archival research as primary content.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://aaahq.org/Research/Journals/Behavioral-Research-in-Accounting/Guide-for-Authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Sinnemann, Brkovic, Sabel, and Nüesch, "Rewarding the Right Way: The Effects of Forced Rating Systems on Creative Performance in Groups," BRIA 2026, DOI: 10.2308/BRIA-2024-012)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for general section sequencing. Additionally: an AI use disclosure statement is required immediately after the abstract and before the introduction body, even if no AI was used. IRB approval or exemption must appear as a footnote (not in prose or acknowledgments) for any human subjects research. For experimental papers, the section labeled "METHOD" is conventional (not "Data and Research Design"). Subsection headings such as "Participants and Experimental Procedure" are standard within that section.

### Length
Maximum 55 pages including references, figures, tables, and appendices. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; maximum 150 words. Must convey topic, methods, and findings. Title appears on the abstract page without author names. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style (16th ed.) author-date format. **No comma** between author name and year: (Deutsch 1949), not (Deutsch, 1949). Two authors joined by "and" (not "&"): (Harbring and Irlenbusch 2011). Three-to-five authors: all named on first citation, then "et al." Six or more authors: "et al." throughout. Reference list uses authors' initials not full names; journal titles not abbreviated; DOIs included where available. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Four-level system, explicitly specified:
- **Level 1:** Centered, bold, ALL CAPS, Roman numeral (I. INTRODUCTION)
- **Level 2:** Flush left, bold, title case
- **Level 3:** Flush left, bold, italic, title case
- **Level 4:** Paragraph indent, bold, lowercase

[ENFORCED — confirmed in article]

### Tables and Figures
Means tables must include standard deviations and cell sizes. Test statistics must be accompanied by degrees of freedom. Use "n.s." for non-significant values; "NA" for non-applicable. Two-tailed p-values should be noted in table footnotes. Alt-text required for all tables and figures. Figures submitted as separate files (EPS preferred); scalable vector formats preferred for line art. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Single authors must not use editorial "we."** [ENFORCED — explicit prohibition in guidelines; FIELD_SKILL says "we" is the norm "regardless of author count," which does not apply here for sole-authored work]
- No author self-identification in text or file metadata during review
- No photographic likenesses without express permission
- No links to personal websites, YouTube, or cloud storage in supplemental materials
- No AI tool listed as author or co-author
- AI prohibited from creating or materially altering non-data-driven images/videos
- Manuscripts under simultaneous review elsewhere not accepted

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** "We" is the dominant convention regardless of author count.
**BRIA delta:** Single-authored papers must use "I" or impersonal constructions. Editorial "we" is prohibited for solo authors. Multi-author papers follow the field norm. [ENFORCED]

### Statistical Reporting
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Economic significance increasingly expected alongside statistical significance; precise reporting of coefficients and significance thresholds.
**BRIA delta:** Mandatory completeness requirements stated in editorial policy: standard deviations, cell sizes, and degrees of freedom must be included whenever reporting means or test statistics. This is an explicit requirement, not a best practice. Confirmed in practice: articles report means (Std. Dev.) and independent t-statistics systematically, with two-tailed p-values noted in table footnotes. [ENFORCED]

### Structural Deviations
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Methods section covers sample construction, variable definitions, model specification, descriptive statistics table.
**BRIA delta:** BRIA primarily publishes behavioral (experimental/survey/field) research, not archival. Methods sections are structured around participants, experimental design, manipulation checks, and procedure — not sample selection exclusion criteria or regression specifications. Results are typically reported as means, standard deviations, and t-statistics or ANOVA, not regression coefficients with fixed effects. This structural difference is a scope consequence, not a deviation from FIELD_SKILL's experimental-paper guidance, but authors coming from an archival background should expect the convention to apply here consistently rather than as an exception. [PRACTICE ONLY]

BRIA also accepts manuscript types absent from top ACCOUNT field journals: replications of influential behavioral articles, scale validation studies, and methodological/methods papers. These have non-standard structures not covered by FIELD_SKILL.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
- **AI disclosure block:** A dedicated AI disclosure statement is inserted as a named block between the abstract and introduction in every submission. If no AI tools were used, a statement to that effect is still required. [ENFORCED]
- **IRB footnote:** Human subjects research requires a specific footnote (not prose mention) affirming IRB approval or exemption. At revision stage, full IRB documentation or an institutional letter is required. [ENFORCED]
- **Gender-neutral language:** Explicitly encouraged in guidelines. [GUIDELINE ONLY]
- **ORCID:** Required for submitting author. [ENFORCED — administrative, not stylistic]

## Dimensions With No Delta
- Argumentation and introduction structure: FIELD DEFAULT
- Hypothesis development and formal hypothesis statements (H1, H2, H1a/H1b format): FIELD DEFAULT
- Hedging and epistemic caution: FIELD DEFAULT
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT
- Discussion/conclusion content and tone: FIELD DEFAULT — conclusion section may be labeled "CONCLUSION" rather than "Discussion and Conclusion"; "Limitations and Future Research Directions" appears as a named subsection within it
- Paragraph and sentence style: FIELD DEFAULT
- Tables as primary vehicle for quantitative results, prose for interpretation: FIELD DEFAULT
- Reference list format (alphabetical, DOIs included): FIELD DEFAULT

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Solo authors defaulting to "we":** FIELD_SKILL instills "we" as universal. BRIA explicitly prohibits it for single-authored papers. Check author count before drafting.
2. **Missing standard deviations or degrees of freedom in tables:** BRIA's editorial policy mandates complete quantitative reporting. Omitting these will draw desk rejection or mandatory revision requests.
3. **Framing the paper as archival:** BRIA does not publish archival research as its primary content. A methods section written around sample construction, Compustat/CRSP variables, and OLS regressions with fixed effects is misaligned with the journal's scope and reviewer expectations. Reframe for experimental or survey designs.
4. **No AI disclosure block:** The placement requirement (after abstract, before introduction) is structural. Omitting it — even when no AI was used — is a compliance failure.
