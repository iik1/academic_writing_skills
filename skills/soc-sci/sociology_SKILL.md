# Journal Writing Skill: Sociology

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications (on behalf of the British Sociological Association)
- **Scope:** General sociology journal publishing empirical, theoretical, and methodological articles across all sociological sub-fields using quantitative and qualitative methods; also accepts Research Notes, critical review essays, and book reviews.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/SOC (returned 403 throughout preparation; constraints below are practice-derived only)
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1177/00380385241305570 — Anving et al., 2024, qualitative empirical; 10.1177/00380385241228836 — Piccoli et al., 2024, conceptual/theoretical)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> Author guidelines page returned 403 errors throughout preparation. All constraints below are derived from article observation and must be verified against the live guidelines before submission.

### Structure
Guidelines inaccessible. Observed across both articles: all major sections use **descriptive headings only** — no generic labels ("Literature Review," "Results," "Findings," "Discussion") appear anywhere. End matter follows a consistent order: Acknowledgements/Funding → Ethics statement (empirical work only) → ORCID iDs → Notes → References → Author biographies → Date submitted/Date accepted. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Length
Not confirmed from guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured paragraph, followed immediately by a standalone **"Keywords"** section (bold heading, lower-case comma-separated terms). Both sections appear before the Introduction. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Citations
**SAGE Harvard author-date.** In-text: (Surname, Year) or (Surname and Surname, Year) for two authors; (Surname et al., Year) for three or more; page references use colon-space: (Surname, Year: page). "and" spelled out, never "&". Multiple citations within parentheses separated by semicolons. Reference list format: Surname Initial (Year) Title. *Journal Name* Volume(Issue): pages. For book chapters: Surname Initial (Year) Chapter title. In: Editor Surname Initial (eds) *Book Title*. City: Publisher, pages. Confirmed in both articles. PRACTICE ONLY (guidelines inaccessible).

### Headings
**H1 (main sections): bold.** **H2 (subsections): italic, not bold.** Confirmed across both articles. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Tables and Figures
Tables use a bold "Table N." title line. A "Source:" note appears below the table body. Observed in Article 2. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Explicit Prohibitions
Not confirmed (guidelines inaccessible).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Descriptive Section Headings Throughout
FIELD_SKILL describes qualitative work as using "thematic subsections" in findings, and implies generic labels such as "Literature Review," "Results," or "Discussion" may appear in quantitative and mixed-methods work. In this journal, **no generic section labels appear in any article type** — every major section, including background/literature, theory, analysis, and the penultimate interpretive section that functions as a Discussion, carries a substantive descriptive heading. The "Discussion" function is absorbed into a descriptively titled section before Conclusion, or directly into the Conclusion. Confirmed across both articles (qualitative empirical and conceptual). PRACTICE ONLY.

### Abstract Format
FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract structure. Sociology uses an **unstructured abstract paragraph** followed by a separate **"Keywords"** section (bold heading; terms in lower case, comma-separated). Keywords are not embedded in the abstract text. Confirmed in both articles. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Citation Format (SAGE Harvard)
FIELD_SKILL references author-date citation practice generally. This journal uses **SAGE Harvard** specifically: page references take the form (Author, Year: page) with a colon-space separator; "and" is spelled out between two authors; reference list entries follow the format Surname Initial (Year) Title. *Journal* Volume(Issue): pages, with "In:" preceding book chapter details and "(eds)" for edited volumes. Confirmed across both articles. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Heading Format
FIELD_SKILL does not specify typographic conventions for section headings. This journal uses a **two-level hierarchy**: H1 sections in **bold**, H2 subsections in *italic* (no bold). Confirmed across both articles. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Ethics Statement Placement
FIELD_SKILL describes ethics statements as appearing in or adjacent to the methods section. In empirical articles in this journal, the ethics statement appears as a **standalone bold-headed section** ("Ethics statement") in end matter, positioned after Conclusion and Funding, before Notes and References. Not present in non-empirical articles. PRACTICE ONLY.

### End-Matter Structure
FIELD_SKILL does not specify end-matter conventions. Articles in this journal consistently close with: Acknowledgements/Funding → (Ethics statement for empirical work) → ORCID iDs → Notes → References → **Author biographies** (brief paragraph per author) → **Date submitted / Date accepted** (bold labels). Author biographies and submission/acceptance dates are structural requirements not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. PRACTICE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls
1. Using any generic section label ("Literature Review," "Findings," "Discussion") — this journal uses descriptive headings for every section without exception.
2. Using "&" in citations or failing to spell out "and" between two authors in in-text references.
3. Omitting the Keywords section or embedding keywords in the abstract text rather than under a separate "Keywords" heading.
4. Placing the ethics statement in the Methods section rather than as a standalone end-matter section.
