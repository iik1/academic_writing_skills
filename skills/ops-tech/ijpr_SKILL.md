### Length

### Abstract
Unstructured plain paragraph. No labeled fields (Purpose/Design/Findings etc.). Approximate length in practice: 150–200 words. Keywords are listed immediately after the abstract under a "KEYWORDS" label. [PRACTICE ONLY — guidelines not directly accessed]

### Citations
**Chicago author-date format.** This is a hard deviation from the APA-adjacent norm described in FIELD_SKILL.

In-text: name and year in parentheses, **no comma** between author name and year — e.g., (Smith 2020), not (Smith, 2020). Two-author in-text: (Smith and Jones 2020). Four or more authors: (Smith et al. 2020). Multiple references in one citation: separated by semicolons — (Smith 2020; Jones 2021).

Reference list: alphabetical by first author's last name. Year appears after author list with a full stop, not in parentheses. Format: Last, F., F. Last, and F. Last. Year. "Article Title in Title Case." *Journal Name* Volume(Issue): Pages. https://doi.org/xxx. Note "and" is used before the final author (not "&" as in APA). [ENFORCED — confirmed by Paperpile guide and full-text article]

### Headings
Numbered hierarchical section headings are standard in practice: 1., 2., 2.1., 2.2., 3., etc. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Format

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** "In-text citations follow author-date (APA-adjacent) format throughout." APA uses a comma between author and year — (Smith, 2020) — and "&" before the final author in multi-author references.

**IJPR instead:** Chicago author-date. No comma in in-text citations — (Smith 2020). Uses "and" not "&" in multi-author in-text and reference-list entries. Reference list has year as a freestanding element after the author string (not in parentheses).

**Label:** ENFORCED — confirmed by journal's official citation guide (Paperpile) and by full-text article examination.

### SDG Metadata

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Not addressed.

**IJPR instead:** A "SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS" field appears on the published article page and in the article header, listing mapped SDG labels. This is a Taylor & Francis platform feature that likely requires authors to indicate relevant SDGs at submission.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using APA in-text citation format.** The no-comma Chicago style (Smith 2020) is easily confused with APA (Smith, 2020). Using a comma — or "&" before final authors — signals an APA reference manager template rather than a Taylor & Francis one. Use the journal's Zotero/Mendeley style file.

2. **Treating this as a management journal.** IJPR publishes a high share of analytical modelling, scheduling, and engineering-oriented papers. Framing contributions entirely in management-theory terms without engaging quantitative/technical literature may misalign the paper with the journal's editorial appetite, even though management-science papers are accepted.

3. **Omitting SDG relevance.** Taylor & Francis may require SDG mapping at submission. Identify the most plausible SDG(s) in advance to avoid delays during the submission workflow.

4. **Over-length abstracts.** In practice, abstracts are concise (~150–200 words) and unstructured. Submitting a structured abstract (with labeled sections) or an abstract exceeding ~250 words would be atypical for this outlet.
