# Journal Writing Skill: International Journal of Hospitality Management

- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Research papers, state-of-the-art reviews, and analyses of business practices in the hospitality industry, covering human resources, consumer behaviour, operations, strategic and financial management, technology, and related disciplines.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for full research articles. No additional required section ordering is specified beyond standard Elsevier manuscript components (title page, abstract, keywords, highlights, body, references).

### Length
- **Full articles:** Maximum **9,000 words in total**, including tables, figures, and references. Label: ENFORCED.
- **Research Notes:** Maximum **2,500 words in total**, including tables, figures, and references. Label: ENFORCED.
- Only these two article types are accepted. Commentary, viewpoint, and forum pieces — mentioned in FIELD_SKILL as accepted by leading journals in this field — are **not accepted** at IJHM. Label: ENFORCED.

### Abstract
Format appears unstructured (single paragraph) in all spot-checked articles, ranging approximately 120–180 words. No word limit is specified in the accessible portion of the guidelines. No structured fields (e.g., Purpose, Findings) are required, unlike the Emerald-style structured abstract used by IJCHM. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (format) / no confirmed word limit.

### Citations
**Numbered references in square brackets** (e.g., [1], [2], [3]) are used throughout all spot-checked articles. This is a departure from the FIELD_SKILL baseline, which states that author–year (APA) is used across all journals in this field.

The in-text citation format places bracket numbers at the point of citation; the reference list is ordered by citation sequence, not alphabetically. This citation system could not be confirmed from the accessible portion of the author guidelines due to page truncation, but it was consistent across all three spot-checked articles. Label: PRACTICE ONLY.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific heading conventions confirmed.

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures count toward the total word limit. No additional formatting constraints confirmed beyond standard Elsevier requirements. Label: ENFORCED (word-count inclusion).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Accepted work may not be republished in the same form in any language.
- Inclusive language is required throughout: avoid gender-specific pronouns (prefer plurals) and descriptors implying superiority based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability. Label: ENFORCED.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Author–year (APA) is used across all journals in this field, with alphabetically ordered reference lists.

**IJHM practice:** Numbered citation format [1], [2] with sequentially ordered reference list. Confirmed across all three spot-checked articles spanning different methodological approaches (mixed-methods, multi-criteria modelling, quantitative survey). Label: **PRACTICE ONLY** (consistent across all observed articles; could not confirm from guidelines text due to access limitations).

**Practical consequence:** In-text citations are brackets not parenthetical author–year. The reference list is ordered by first appearance, not alphabetically. A manuscript formatted in APA is formatted incorrectly for IJHM.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Commentary, viewpoint, and forum pieces are accepted by leading journals in this field.

**IJHM constraint:** Only full-length research articles and Research Notes are accepted. No other contribution types are considered. Label: **ENFORCED.**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using APA (author–year) citations instead of numbered references.** The field baseline specifies APA, but IJHM uses numbered [1] [2] format throughout. Submitting a manuscript with parenthetical author–year in-text citations and an alphabetical reference list is inconsistent with observed journal practice and will require reformatting before or after acceptance.

2. **Exceeding the 9,000-word all-in limit.** The word cap includes tables, figures, and references — not just the body text. Authors who mentally exempt tables or references will undershoot the available word budget for the main text. A 9,000-word manuscript with 60 references and three tables is likely over limit.

3. **Attempting to submit a commentary or viewpoint piece.** IJHM does not accept these formats. Only full articles (≤9,000 words) and Research Notes (≤2,500 words) are considered.

4. **Writing a structured abstract with labeled fields.** Unlike Emerald journals in this field, IJHM uses an unstructured single-paragraph abstract. A Purpose / Findings / Originality-value structure is inconsistent with observed practice.
