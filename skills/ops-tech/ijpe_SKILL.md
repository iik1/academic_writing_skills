# Journal Writing Skill: International Journal of Production Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Covers the interface between engineering and management, addressing all aspects of manufacturing, process industries, and production in general, including supply chain management, inventory, scheduling, and production economics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/international-journal-of-production-economics/publish/guide-for-authors

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No prescribed section sequence. Double-blind peer review applies: manuscripts must be fully anonymised at submission (author names, affiliations, self-citations identifying the authors must be removed or masked). [ENFORCED]

### Length
No explicit manuscript word or page limit stated in guidelines. Full manuscript length is unconstrained by formal rule.

### Abstract
Unstructured narrative format; maximum 250 words. No labeled fields (Background, Methods, etc.). Abstract must stand alone: avoid references where possible; if essential, cite author(s) and year. Avoid non-standard abbreviations; define at first mention. [ENFORCED]

### Keywords
1–7 keywords required. Avoid multi-word phrases using "and" or "of". Only well-established abbreviations are acceptable. [ENFORCED]

### Highlights
Mandatory at submission. Elsevier standard: 3–5 bullet points, each no longer than 85 characters (including spaces). Bullets capture the novel results or key findings in plain language. Confirmed in practice: articles show 4 bullets summarising study design and findings. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date (Harvard) format: (Author, Year) for single references; (Author1 et al., Year) for three or more authors; semicolons separating multiple references. Reference list: Author surname, initials, year, full title, journal, volume, pages. Aligns with FIELD_SKILL norm. [ENFORCED / FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific heading hierarchy prescribed.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for content. Generative AI must not be used to create or alter figures and images; any AI-assisted figures require explicit disclosure in the manuscript. [ENFORCED for AI prohibition]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors. [ENFORCED]
- If AI tools were used in manuscript preparation (writing, editing, analysis), a declaration statement must appear before the references section. [ENFORCED]
- Generative AI must not be used to generate or manipulate figures or images. [ENFORCED]
- Inclusive language is required: no bias based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability; use gender-neutral constructions (plural nouns as default). [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Analytical modelling papers are highly prevalent.** FIELD_SKILL characterises quantitative empirical papers as the dominant type in OPS&TECH. In IJPE, analytical and game-theoretic modelling papers (supply chain contract design, platform competition, pricing, coordination under asymmetric information) are at least as prevalent as empirical papers in a given issue. Papers of this type follow the modelling paper structure noted in FIELD_SKILL (Introduction → Model Setup → Analysis → Managerial Insights → Conclusion), and this structure is routinely practiced. Authors submitting empirical papers should not assume they are writing to the modal paper type.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing or malformed Highlights.** IJPE requires 3–5 bullets of ≤85 characters each. Submissions without highlights or with overlong bullets are likely flagged at desk review.
2. **Abstract over 250 words.** The 250-word cap is enforced. Structured abstracts with labeled fields are wrong for this journal.
3. **Insufficient anonymisation.** Double-blind review means all identifying material (author names, affiliations, acknowledging grants tied to named institutions, self-citations with "in our previous work") must be removed from the submitted manuscript.
4. **Undisclosed AI use.** Elsevier requires an explicit AI declaration statement before references if any AI tool was used in preparing the text. Omission may constitute a policy violation.
5. **Calibrating to the wrong paper type.** Given the high prevalence of analytical modelling papers, empirical authors should not assume their structure or level of mathematical formalism sets the norm for the journal. Modelling papers are equally central to IJPE's identity.
