# Journal Writing Skill: Review of Financial Studies

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (Society for Financial Studies)
- **Scope:** Financial economics, broadly defined. Publishes empirical, theoretical, and experimental work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://sfs.org/guidelines-for-submissions/
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm, with one major exception: the introduction roadmap paragraph is **explicitly prohibited** (see below).

### Length
No explicit page limit stated in current guidelines. Manuscript must be double-spaced, maximum 28 lines per page, font size 11pt or larger.

### Abstract
**100-word strict limit.** Submissions with abstracts longer than 100 words are automatically returned without review. ENFORCED.

This is a major deviation from FIELD_SKILL (which specifies 150–250 words). At 100 words, the abstract must be extremely compressed: one sentence on the question, one on the method/data, one or two on findings, one on contribution. No room for elaboration.

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (author-year parenthetical). No alternative format mandated.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for table design and notation. One additional requirement not in FIELD_SKILL: **alt text is required underneath all figure captions**. ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
1. **No roadmap paragraph at the end of the introduction.** The guidelines explicitly prohibit "a boilerplate table of contents paragraph at the end of the paper's introduction." ENFORCED. This directly overrides FIELD_SKILL, which describes the closing roadmap as "near-universal."
2. **No endnotes.** Footnotes are permitted; endnotes are not. GUIDELINE ONLY (not verified in practice).
3. **No separate online appendix files.** Internet appendices must be embedded in the main manuscript PDF, clearly marked, with no author identification. GUIDELINE ONLY.
4. **No scanned PDFs.** Submission must be a native PDF, not a scan of a printed document.
5. **Language quality gate.** Papers will be desk-rejected if the editor believes the grammar places an unfair burden on reviewers. English is required; grammatical perfection is not, but readability is. GUIDELINE ONLY.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Abstract Length

**Field norm:** 150–250 words, single unstructured paragraph.

**RFS:** 100-word hard ceiling, automatically enforced. At this length, authors must omit all hedging, all context-setting, and limit findings to one or two sentences with specific numbers. Every word must carry maximum information density. Label: ENFORCED.

### Introduction — Roadmap Prohibition

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes a closing roadmap sentence as "almost invariably" present — e.g., "The rest of this paper is organized as follows: Section 2 describes the data..."

**RFS:** Explicitly prohibits this. The guidelines single out the "boilerplate table of contents paragraph at the end of the paper's introduction" as a prohibited element. Authors must end the introduction without a section-by-section roadmap. The introduction should flow from motivation through contributions without a structural signpost at the close. Label: ENFORCED.

### Figure Accessibility

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address alt text or accessibility requirements.

**RFS:** Alt text is required underneath all figure captions. This is a publisher-level accessibility requirement that must be addressed before submission. Label: ENFORCED.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract too long.** Exceeding 100 words triggers automatic return. Count before submitting. At 100 words, every sentence must be essential — contributions and implications belong in the introduction, not the abstract.

2. **Roadmap paragraph left in.** Authors accustomed to the field norm of a closing roadmap must actively delete it. This is one of the few formatting items the guidelines call out by name. Ending the introduction on a contribution or implication statement is the expected alternative.

3. **Online appendix submitted as separate file.** Additional materials must be embedded in the main PDF (clearly labeled, e.g., "Internet Appendix"), not uploaded separately. Separate appendix files will trigger a return.

4. **Missing alt text on figures.** This is easy to overlook. Each figure caption must be accompanied by alt text describing the figure's content for accessibility purposes.
