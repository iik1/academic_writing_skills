# Journal Writing Skill: British Accounting Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (official journal of the British Accounting and Finance Association)
- **Scope:** Broad accounting and finance journal accepting analytical, archival, experimental, survey, and qualitative case-study methods; covers financial accounting, management accounting, auditing, and finance topics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/the-british-accounting-review/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Luu, Xu & Zheng, BAR 55(4) 2023, DOI: 10.1016/j.bar.2023.101216; Smith & Cordina, BAR 46(3) 2014, DOI: 10.1016/j.bar.2014.03.002)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Guidelines name Introduction and Conclusions as required sections but do not prescribe a rigid intermediate structure, consistent with the journal's multi-methodology scope.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in the author guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. JEL classification codes and Keywords listed as separate fields below the abstract. No stated word limit. — ENFORCED (confirmed in articles)

### Citations
APA format throughout. In-text parenthetical citations use an ampersand: (Author & Author, Year). Multiple citations in one parenthetical use semicolons. Reference list uses APA style: Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *volume*(issue), pages. — ENFORCED (confirmed in articles; implicit from Elsevier APA house style)

### Headings
Arabic numerals for all section levels (1., 2., 3.; subsections 2.1, 2.2). — PRACTICE ONLY (not stated in guidelines; confirmed in both articles)

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables require titles, descriptions, and footnotes; figures require captions. Indicate if color is needed for print.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Manuscripts under consideration elsewhere are not accepted.
- Republication in English or any other language is prohibited.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where BAR deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Format
**Field norm:** Author-date style (format not specified in FIELD_SKILL).
**BAR practice:** APA format specifically. In-text parenthetical uses ampersand: (Boehmer & Wu, 2013), not "and." Reference list entries follow APA: Author, A. (Year). Title. *Journal*, *vol*(issue), page–page. — ENFORCED

### Scope (Finance Inclusion)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL baseline is derived from pure accounting journals.
**BAR practice:** Explicitly covers finance alongside accounting. Finance-oriented articles (capital markets, short-selling, asset pricing) are published. Contributions to finance literature are expected and credited in the contribution statement. — GUIDELINE ONLY (scope is stated in guidelines; applies to how you frame contributions and position in the literature)

### Inclusive Language
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a stance on gendered language.
**BAR requirement:** Authors must use inclusive language, avoid gendered pronouns, and default to plural nouns to sidestep singular-gendered constructions. — GUIDELINE ONLY

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Wrong citation format:** Using "and" in parenthetical citations — BAR uses APA ampersand format: (Author & Author, Year). Check every in-text citation.
2. **Narrow accounting framing:** BAR spans accounting and finance. If your paper touches capital markets or financial economics, position contributions in both literatures; reviewers from a finance background are common.
3. **Omitting research instruments:** Qualitative and experimental papers must submit survey questionnaires, interview guides, or experiment questionnaires with the manuscript. This is an explicit submission requirement.
4. **Missing author bios in the cover letter:** Each author requires a biography of up to 200 words (affiliation, position, research areas, three strongest publications) in the cover letter — an atypical requirement not found in most accounting journals.
