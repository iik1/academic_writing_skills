# Journal Writing Skill: Academy of Management Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MGMT
- **Publisher:** Academy of Management
- **Scope:** Theory development journal publishing novel, insightful conceptual articles that challenge conventional wisdom concerning all aspects of organizations and their role in society. Publishes only theoretical/conceptual work—no empirical data.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aom.org/publications/journals/publishing-with-aom/author-and-reviewer-resources/author-resources/submitting-to-review/
- **Field baseline:** mgmt-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (guidelines and editorial analysis only; full-text articles inaccessible)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
AMR publishes only theoretical/conceptual articles with no empirical sections. There is no mandated section sequence. Contributors find AMR open to many different formats and styles of presentation. The "five building blocks" framework (Common Ground, Complication, Concern, Course of Action, Contribution) should structure the abstract and introduction, but subsequent sections are named by their substantive theoretical argument, not by generic labels.

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Length
25–30 double-spaced pages, excluding references, tables, and figures. Approximately 12,000 words maximum. Editors consider contribution-to-length ratio; reviewers lose interest in long, rambling, or unnecessarily dense papers.

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Abstract
Unstructured, maximum 200 words. Must integrate the five building blocks: Common Ground, Complication, Concern, Course of Action, and Contribution. No citations in abstract.

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Citations
Modified APA style. Key differences from standard APA:
- Use colon (not comma) before page numbers in citations
- No parentheses around year of publication in book references

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Headings
Three levels, all boldface:
- **Level 1:** ALL CAPS, CENTERED, BOLD
- **Level 2:** Title Case, Flush Left, Bold
- **Level 3:** First word capitalized, indented, italicized, run into paragraph

Headings and subheadings use 1.5 spacing (not double-spaced).

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Tables and Figures
Group all references first, then tables (grouped together), then figures (grouped together), then appendices/supplementary material—regardless of order mentioned in text.

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Formatting
- Font: Times New Roman 12-point (not "Times" or other variants)
- Paper size: Letter (8.5 × 11 inches)
- Body text: Double-spaced
- Margins: 1 inch (Word's Normal margin setting)
- File format: Word document

**Label: ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No empirical data (AMR is theory-only)
- Papers can be submitted to AMR only once
- Papers under review at AMR cannot be under review elsewhere
- AI-generated content must be disclosed in cover letter and acknowledgments

**Label: ENFORCED**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Propositions: Optional, Not Required

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Theoretical/conceptual papers in MGMT develop numbered propositions (P1, P2…) accompanied by explicit verbal reasoning.

**AMR practice:** Formal research propositions are **not required**. AMR is open to many different formats and styles. Between 2010-2012, only 59 of 83 AMR articles used propositions. Editorial guidance warns against formulaic application of propositions and states flexibility in style is valued.

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (explicitly stated in author guidelines but not universally followed in published articles)

**When to apply:** Authors may use propositions if they serve the argument, but should not feel obligated. Alternative formats include process models, typologies, and narrative theoretical development.

---

### Introduction Structure: Five Building Blocks Framework

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Introductions follow CARS model (Establish territory → Establish niche → Occupy niche) with contribution statement typically as numbered list.

**AMR refinement:** Introductions must integrate five building blocks in this sequence:
1. **Common Ground** - Establish current state of literature and shared understanding
2. **Complication** - Identify gaps or limitations
3. **Concern** - Demonstrate why complication matters theoretically and practically
4. **Course of Action** - Explain proposed solution or theoretical approach
5. **Contribution** - Articulate novel impact on the field

These blocks appear in both abstract and introduction (introduction provides expanded version). The conventional sequence may be reformulated when justified to more effectively engage readers.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (found in influential editorial guidance, particularly Lange & Pfarrer 2017 "Sense and Structure" editorial, but not in formal submission requirements)

**When to apply:** Frame every AMR introduction using these five blocks. The abstract must compress all five into ≤200 words.

---

### Heading Hierarchy: Three Levels with Specific Formatting

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** No specific heading format prescribed; APA-style headings common but not standardized across MGMT outlets.

**AMR requirement:** Exactly three levels, all boldface:
- **Level 1:** ALL CAPS, CENTERED, BOLD
- **Level 2:** Title Case, Flush Left, Bold
- **Level 3:** First word capitalized, indented, italicized, run into paragraph

Headings use 1.5 line spacing (body text is double-spaced).

**Classification:** ENFORCED

**When to apply:** Never use more than three heading levels. Never use non-bold headings. Level 1 headings must be all-caps and centered.

---

### Citation Style: Modified APA

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Standard APA in-text citations.

**AMR requirement:** Modified APA:
- Colon (not comma) before page numbers: (Smith, 2020: 45) not (Smith, 2020, p. 45)
- No parentheses around year in book references in the reference list

**Classification:** ENFORCED

**When to apply:** All in-text citations and references.

---

### Abstract Word Limit: 200 Words Maximum

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Abstracts typically 150–250 words.

**AMR requirement:** Maximum 200 words.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

**When to apply:** All submissions. Abstract must be tightly written to fit five building blocks within 200 words.

---

### Length Constraint: 25–30 Pages

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Theoretical/conceptual papers typically 8,000–14,000 words.

**AMR requirement:** 25–30 double-spaced pages excluding references, tables, figures (~12,000 words).

**Classification:** ENFORCED

**When to apply:** Manuscripts significantly longer than 30 pages will be viewed negatively. Editors prioritize contribution-to-length ratio.

## Dimensions With No Delta

- **Voice and person:** FIELD DEFAULT (first-person "we" for multi-authored; "I" acceptable for single-authored theoretical work)
- **Citation density:** FIELD DEFAULT (citation-dense in introduction and theory sections)
- **Tone and register:** FIELD DEFAULT (academic but direct; assertions with appropriate hedging)
- **Discussion/Conclusion structure:** FIELD DEFAULT (brief conclusion restating core contribution)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Including propositions because "theory papers need propositions":** AMR explicitly does not require formal propositions. Many successful AMR papers use process models, typologies, or narrative theoretical development instead. Use propositions only if they serve your argument.

2. **Ignoring the five building blocks in the introduction:** AMR editorial team expects Common Ground → Complication → Concern → Course of Action → Contribution structure in introduction. Omitting "Concern" (why the complication matters) is especially common and problematic—if readers perceive a complication as trivial, they disengage.

3. **Exceeding 30 pages or 200-word abstract limit:** AMR editors and reviewers value concision. Long papers signal poor contribution-to-length ratio. Abstracts over 200 words will be rejected for revision before review.

4. **Using more than three heading levels or wrong formatting:** Level 1 headings must be ALL CAPS, CENTERED, BOLD. Using sentence case or left-aligned Level 1 headings violates house style.

5. **Submitting empirical work:** AMR publishes only theory. If your paper includes hypotheses tested with data, it belongs in AMJ, Organization Science, or another empirical outlet, not AMR.
