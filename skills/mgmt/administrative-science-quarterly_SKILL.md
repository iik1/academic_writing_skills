# Journal Writing Skill: Administrative Science Quarterly

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MGMT
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications
- **Scope:** Publishes theoretical and empirical papers advancing understanding of organizing in contexts such as teams, enterprises, and markets. Interdisciplinary focus on organizational theory.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/asq
- **Field baseline:** mgmt-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 0 (full texts inaccessible; analysis based on comprehensive author guidelines and editorial guidance)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for article types (quantitative deductive, qualitative inductive, theoretical/conceptual, integrative review). No journal-specific section requirements beyond field standard.

### Length
**ENFORCED**: 35-45 pages of text (12-point Times New Roman, double-spaced, 1-inch margins), NOT including references, tables, figures, or appendices. Editors will unsubmit manuscripts that significantly exceed this range and request cuts before review.

**ENFORCED**: Introduction must be 3-5 pages. Shorter than 3 pages suggests insufficient motivation/research question development; longer than 5 pages suggests scope creep.

**PRACTICE ONLY**: Discussion should be 5-7 pages according to editorial guidance.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED**: Informative abstract of 200 words maximum (field baseline is 150-250; ASQ specifies the lower bound).

**ENFORCED**: Must describe: (1) the research question or focus, (2) type of study (empirical, qualitative, field, network study, etc.), and (3) context and country if relevant to understanding (e.g., "Fortune 500 firms," "hospitals in Canada," "factories in China").

**ENFORCED**: Avoid jargon; prioritize readability for interdisciplinary audience.

**ENFORCED**: No citations in abstract (consistent with field norm).

Unstructured format (consistent with field norm).

### Citations
**ENFORCED**: APA style for in-text citations and reference lists (as of January 2025).

**PRACTICE ONLY**: References must match all in-text citations exactly; discrepancies signal carelessness and harm editorial evaluation.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Included in the 35-45 page limit exemption (they do not count toward page count).

### Explicit Prohibitions
**PRACTICE ONLY**: Minimize or eliminate footnotes when possible. Use only when content would interrupt main text flow but is necessary for definitional clarification or methodological detail.

**PRACTICE ONLY**: Avoid opening introduction with excessive citations (establish territory efficiently).

**PRACTICE ONLY**: Do not include findings or methods in the introduction; these belong in dedicated sections.

**PRACTICE ONLY**: Closing paragraph of Discussion should avoid citations; leave readers with broader implications rather than additional literature engagement.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Introduction Length and Structure
**Delta classification**: ENFORCED

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: Introduction typically 800-1,500 words (roughly 3-6 paragraphs).

**ASQ requirement**: Introduction must be 3-5 pages. More prescriptive than field baseline. Rationale provided in editorial guidance: fewer than 3 pages = insufficient explanation of motivation and research question; more than 5 pages = likely trying to do too much.

**How to apply**: Count pages, not words. Double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman. If below 3 pages, expand territory establishment or niche identification. If above 5 pages, move literature detail to Theory section.

---

### Discussion Length
**Delta classification**: PRACTICE ONLY

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: No specific length guidance; Discussion may be standalone or merged with Conclusion.

**ASQ guidance**: Discussion should be 5-7 pages according to editorial blog post. This is observed practice rather than hard guideline requirement.

**How to apply**: Budget approximately 5-7 double-spaced pages for Discussion section. If shorter, consider whether theoretical implications are fully developed; if longer, assess whether content should move to limitations or future research subsection.

---

### Footnote Usage
**Delta classification**: PRACTICE ONLY (relaxation of field norm)

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: "Footnotes are used for definitional clarifications, literature tangents, or methodological details that would interrupt the main text."

**ASQ preference**: "Eliminate footnotes when possible" (editorial guidance). Stronger discouragement than field baseline.

**How to apply**: Default to integrating content into main text or cutting it entirely. Reserve footnotes only for truly essential clarifications that cannot be incorporated without disrupting argument flow. ASQ editors view excessive footnoting as a sign of insufficiently streamlined argumentation.

---

### Abstract Specificity
**Delta classification**: ENFORCED (tightening of field norm)

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: Abstract 150-250 words, states research question/gap, method, main finding, theoretical contribution.

**ASQ requirement**: Abstract maximum 200 words (lower end of field range). Must explicitly identify: (1) research question or focus, (2) study type with methodological descriptor (e.g., "qualitative field study," "network analysis," "longitudinal survey"), (3) context with geographic specificity when relevant.

**How to apply**: Target 180-200 words. Include explicit study-type language ("We conducted a qualitative study of..."; "Using panel data from..."). If study context is not U.S.-based or involves specific organizational type, name it ("cooperatives in Germany," "biotechnology startups," "public hospitals in Canada").

---

### Interdisciplinary Accessibility
**Delta classification**: PRACTICE ONLY (tightening of field norm)

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: "Academic but direct. Assertions are made with appropriate hedging but without excessive qualification."

**ASQ emphasis**: Minimize jargon suitable for interdisciplinary audiences. ASQ positions itself as a journal for organizational theory broadly construed, not narrowly within management subfields.

**How to apply**: When introducing theoretical constructs or methodological approaches specific to one subfield (e.g., "upper echelons theory," "configurational analysis"), provide brief definitional context. Avoid acronyms without spelling out on first use. Write for a reader trained in organizational sociology, strategy, or organizational behavior, but not necessarily your specific niche.

---

### Research Question Placement
**Delta classification**: PRACTICE ONLY

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: Research question stated explicitly, usually near the end of the introduction.

**ASQ guidance**: Research question must be stated within the first 2-3 pages of the manuscript (editorial blog). Slightly more prescriptive timing than field baseline.

**How to apply**: If introduction is 3-5 pages, position research question by page 2-3 at the latest. Do not delay until final paragraph of introduction.

---

### Discussion Closing Style
**Delta classification**: PRACTICE ONLY

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL)**: Discussion "may end with a brief statement about broader significance or future agenda."

**ASQ preference**: "The closing paragraph should avoid citations and leave readers with broader implications" (editorial guidance).

**How to apply**: Final paragraph of Discussion (or Conclusion if separate) should be citationless. Frame the contribution's significance in broad terms without engaging additional literature. This is the rhetorical "so what" moment.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding 45 pages of text.** Editors will unsubmit and request cuts before sending for review. Do not assume "a bit over" is acceptable. Count pages of prose only (exclude references, tables, figures, appendices). If manuscript is 48+ pages, identify cuts before submission.

2. **Insufficient introduction or discussion length.** A 2-page introduction or 3-page discussion signals underdevelopment. ASQ is a top-tier outlet; argumentation must be thorough. If introduction is under 3 pages, expand niche establishment or contribution preview. If discussion is under 5 pages, develop theoretical implications more fully.

3. **Reference list errors.** Editors flag exact matching of references to in-text citations as a quality signal. A missing or extra reference suggests carelessness. Before submission, cross-check every citation against reference list using citation management software or manual audit.

4. **Jargon-heavy abstracts.** ASQ abstracts are read by interdisciplinary audiences. Avoid subfield-specific acronyms (e.g., "RBV," "UET," "RDT") and methodological shorthand (e.g., "DID," "2SLS"). Write out "resource-based view," "upper echelons theory," "difference-in-differences."
