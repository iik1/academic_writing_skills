# Journal Writing Skill: American Sociological Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE on behalf of the American Sociological Association
- **Scope:** Flagship general journal of the ASA. Publishes empirical, theoretical, and methodological work of broad interest to sociologists across all substantive areas.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/ASR
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Reardon, Weathers, Fahle, Jang & Kalogrides 2024, "Is Separate Still Unequal? New Evidence on School Segregation and Racial Academic Achievement Gaps," ASR 89(6); read via CEPA Working Paper wp19-06)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Length
**15,000 words maximum** for the full manuscript submission. Word count includes: main text, notes, references, and tables/figures. (ENFORCED)

### Abstract
**150–200 words**, unstructured flowing prose (no labeled fields or structured subheadings). The abstract should summarise the research question, method, and core findings in continuous paragraphs. Observed practice (Reardon et al.): ~190 words, single paragraph. (ENFORCED)

### Citations
All manuscripts must follow the **ASA Style Guide, 7th edition (2022)**. Key rules that differ from generic author-date practice:

- **In-text format:** (Lastname Year) or Lastname (Year). For direct quotations, add page number with colon: (Lastname Year:p. N). (ENFORCED)
- **Multiple in-text citations:** Separated by semicolons, listed alphabetically by first author: (Jones 2010; Smith 2015; Zhao 2019). (ENFORCED)
- **3+ authors in text:** First in-text citation lists all last names; subsequent citations use first author et al. (ENFORCED)
- **Reference list — full names:** Every author's full first name must be spelled out in the reference list. Initials alone are not permitted. (ENFORCED)
- **Reference list — all authors:** List all authors in the reference list; do not use "et al." regardless of how many authors. (ENFORCED)
- **Reference list — article titles:** Sentence case (only first word and proper nouns capitalised). Journal names in italics, volume in italics, issue in parentheses, page range. (ENFORCED)

### Heading Hierarchy
ASR uses a strict three-level heading system. Authors must replicate this exactly:

| Level | Format | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | ALL CAPS, flush left, own line | `RESULTS` |
| 2 | Italic, flush left, own line | *Segregation Trends* |
| 3 | Italic, indented as paragraph, followed by period; text runs on the same line | *&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cross-sectional estimates.* Text continues here… |

Do not add bold, underline, or numbering to headings at any level. (ENFORCED)

### Manuscript Section Ordering
The prescribed submission order is: Title page / Abstract / Text / Notes / References / Tables / Figures / Appendixes. Each section begins on a new page. (ENFORCED)

### Tables and Figures Placement
Tables and figures must be placed **at the end of the manuscript**, after the References section, in the order: Tables first, then Figures. Each table/figure starts on its own page. Insert a short placeholder at the point of first reference in the text:

> `[Table 1 about here]`
> `[Figure 1 about here]`

Observed practice: placeholders appear as a separate line, on their own paragraph, enclosed in square brackets. (ENFORCED)

### Notes Format
Two mutually exclusive options: **bottom-of-page footnotes** OR a separate **NOTES section** (placed after Text, before References). Do not mix both. Each individual note must not exceed **100 words**. Notes are indicated by superscript numerals in the text. (ENFORCED for 100-word cap and mutual exclusivity; observed practice in spot-checked article used bottom-of-page footnotes)

### Anonymous Review
No author-identifying information anywhere in the submission. This explicitly includes: no author names, no affiliations, no acknowledgments, no grant information, no presentation history. Authors are specifically instructed not to cite their own working papers, dissertations, or unpublished manuscripts, as these would identify them to reviewers. (ENFORCED)

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Tables and Figures Placement
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify placement of tables and figures relative to text; the field norm in quantitative work is figures and tables embedded near their first reference or appended.

**This journal:** Strict end-placement is required — all tables after References, all figures after tables. Text contains only `[Table X about here]` placeholder lines. (ENFORCED)

### Section Ordering
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes a canonical section sequence (Introduction → Theory → Data/Methods → Results → Discussion) but does not prescribe the ordering of the manuscript's physical sections (abstract, notes, references, appendixes).

**This journal:** Physical section order is mandated: Title page / Abstract / Text / Notes / References / Tables / Figures / Appendixes. Authors who default to embedding tables inline or placing notes before references will violate submission requirements. (ENFORCED)

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a house citation style; it notes that author-date format is common across quantitative journals.

**This journal:** ASA Style Guide 7th edition (2022) is mandatory. The most consequential differences from commonly used alternatives (APA, Chicago author-date) are: (a) full first names in reference list rather than initials; (b) all authors listed in reference list, no et al.; (c) article titles in sentence case, not title case; (d) semicolons between multiple in-text citations, listed alphabetically. (ENFORCED)

### Heading System
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a heading hierarchy; it notes that major headings and sub-headings exist but leaves formatting to the outlet.

**This journal:** A precise three-level system is imposed. Level 1 ALL CAPS, Level 2 italic standalone, Level 3 italic run-in with period. No bold or numbered headings at any level. (ENFORCED)

### Notes Word Cap
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify note length limits. Footnotes are described as a common vehicle for caveats, qualifications, and supplementary detail.

**This journal:** Each note is capped at 100 words. Notes that exceed this limit must be either cut, relocated to the appendix, or integrated into the main text. (ENFORCED)

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract length.

**This journal:** 150–200 words, unstructured. Both a floor and a ceiling apply. Observed abstracts run close to the upper limit (~190 words). (ENFORCED)

### Self-Citation of Unpublished Work
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address self-citation of working papers or dissertations.

**This journal:** Citing one's own working papers or dissertations is explicitly prohibited under blind review requirements, as such citations would identify the author. Self-citations to published work may be included but should be handled carefully to preserve anonymity. (ENFORCED)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Inline tables and figures.** The most common formatting error for authors from other disciplines. Tables and figures must not appear in the body text — they go after References, each on its own page. Replace each with a placeholder line `[Table 1 about here]`.
2. **Abbreviated names in references.** ASA style requires full first names in the reference list. Manuscripts prepared with APA, AMA, or abbreviated Chicago style will need all author names expanded. "Smith, J." must become "Smith, John."
3. **Et al. in the reference list.** ASA format lists every author in full regardless of how many there are. Reference management software defaults (which often use et al. for 7+ authors in APA) must be overridden.
4. **Mixed notes and footnotes.** Choosing both bottom-of-page footnotes AND a separate Notes section is a submission error. Pick one format and use it consistently throughout.
5. **Notes exceeding 100 words.** Long caveats that would be routine as footnotes in other journals must be trimmed to under 100 words or moved to the appendix.
6. **Citing working papers or dissertations as self-citations.** Under ASR's anonymous review policy, such citations should be removed. Replace with language such as "see [citation omitted for review]" or integrate the content without the citation.
7. **Heading formatting.** Using bold or numbered headings, or omitting the run-in style for Level 3 heads, deviates from ASR's published format. Level 3 heads are italic, indented as a paragraph indent, followed by a period, with the text continuing on the same line.
