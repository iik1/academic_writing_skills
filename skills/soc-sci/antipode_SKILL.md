# Journal Writing Skill: Antipode

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley (for The Antipode Foundation)
- **Scope:** Critical and radical human geography; theoretical and empirical work grounded in Marxist, socialist, anarchist, anti-racist, anticolonial, feminist, queer, trans*, green, and postcolonial thought. Papers must contribute to a Left politics broadly defined.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://antipodeonline.org/about-the-journal-and-foundation/a-radical-journal-of-geography/
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1111/anti.70026 — Holgersen & Blackwell 2025; 10.1111/anti.13132 — Madden 2025; full-text access blocked, confirmed via abstracts and repository metadata)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No fixed section order is mandated. Theoretical essays without a Methods section are explicitly accommodated ("Formative Essays in Radical Geography" is a recognised article type). Standard empirical articles may include Background, Methods, Findings, and Conclusion but this is not required. The Editorial Collective evaluates logical coherence, not adherence to a prescribed template.

### Length
**9,500 words maximum, inclusive of endnotes and references.** Tables and figures consume page space; authors are advised to account for this when near the limit. Interventions (short commentaries): ~1,500 words. — ENFORCED

### Abstract
Unstructured; approximately 150 words. No required subfields. — ENFORCED

### Citations
**In-text:** Author-date parenthetical format — (Author Year), (Author1 and Author2 Year), (Author et al Year) for three or more authors; no comma between author and year.
**Endnotes:** Used exclusively for textual asides; must NOT be used to house reference citations. Cited with superscript Arabic numerals in text.
**Reference list:** "Free Format" — journal does not prescribe a specific style, but requires: (a) consistent formatting throughout, (b) each entry begins with first author's last name, (c) hanging indents, (d) single-spaced with a blank line between entries, (e) references and endnotes submitted as separate sections.
— ENFORCED

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific heading style prescribed.

### Tables and Figures
No specific format rules stated beyond the caution that tables and figures use page space within the word limit. — ENFORCED (count toward space budget)

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Papers may not be published, in press, or simultaneously submitted elsewhere.
- Endnotes may not be used to house reference citations (references belong in the reference list only).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where Antipode deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register
**Field norm:** Politically-engaged register is permitted in critical human geography outlets; described as a "genuine norm difference" but not a universal expectation.

**Antipode:** Politically-engaged, passionate prose is an explicit editorial expectation, not merely a permitted stylistic option. Guidelines state papers should be "politically-engaged, timely, and passionate." Authors should convey political commitment to a Left politics broadly defined. An essayistic or polemical register—including single-authored first-person framing and overt normative argumentation—is standard rather than exceptional.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Citation Density and Citational Reflexivity
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes standard literature engagement but does not address the demographic composition of cited scholars.

**Antipode:** "Citational reflexivity" is a formal editorial commitment. Authors must actively engage with and cite scholars from historically underrepresented groups, regions, and institutions—particularly Global South scholars and those marginalised within the academy. This is a submission requirement, not optional good practice.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Intersectionality and Political Framing
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL acknowledges that qualitative outlets expect positionality and reflexivity in methods sections.

**Antipode:** Goes further: papers must attend to how class, gender, race, sexuality, ability, and colonialism factor into their argument, regardless of method. This is not limited to a methods section positionality statement—it applies to the analytical framing of the paper itself. Papers that merely describe axes of difference without new or challenging analyses are insufficiently rigorous by journal standards.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL expects a legible empirical progression (Introduction → Theory/Literature → Data and Methods → Results → Discussion), with flexibility in qualitative work.

**Antipode:** Theoretical and conceptual essays that do not follow the empirical research structure are equally legitimate. Many published articles are structured as critical theoretical interventions without a Methods or Results section. A progression of Introduction → theoretical argument → conceptual analysis → implications/conclusion is a standard Antipode article format.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (guidelines imply through the "Formative Essays" category; confirmed by article evidence)

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Cover letter requirement — ENFORCED:** Antipode requires a cover letter of up to 300 words explaining: (a) why the author chose Antipode specifically, (b) how the paper reflects the journal's values and parameters, (c) what ongoing conversations in the journal it engages, and (d) what the paper does and why it matters. This is substantively more demanding than cover letters at most journals.

**Accessibility norm — ENFORCED:** Prose must be accessible to international, English-reading audiences outside the author's specific subfield. Terms, places, groups, and political parties must be defined or contextualised. More explicit than FIELD_SKILL's general high-formality standard.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Treating political register as optional.** Submissions in a neutral, detached academic register risk rejection as insufficiently aligned with the journal's mission. The paper must take a position.
2. **Ignoring citational reflexivity.** A reference list dominated by Anglophone, male, or Global North scholars will draw reviewer attention. Authors should audit citations before submission.
3. **Submitting without a substantive cover letter.** A generic summary does not meet expectations. The cover letter should engage Antipode's specific debates and values directly.
4. **Word count error.** The 9,500-word cap is all-in: body text + endnotes + references. Tables and figures add further space pressure. Authors frequently miscalculate by excluding endnotes or references.
