## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where Financial Management explicitly departs from the field baseline.
> No article full texts were accessible; all deltas derive from official editorial philosophy and the Author Toolkit.

### Robustness Expectations — RELAXATION

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Systematic robustness treatment is "non-negotiable" at ABS 4*/4; reviewers expect alternative samples, variable definitions, clustering strategies, and placebo tests.

**This journal instead:** The official editorial philosophy explicitly states "more weight to the idea and less weight to robustness tests." Original contribution is the primary criterion; exhaustive robustness documentation is not the governing standard.

**Classification:** RELAXATION [GUIDELINE ONLY — not confirmable via article access]

### Motivation Framing — RELAXATION

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Introduction opens by establishing broad, high-stakes economic context with heavy citation density from the outset.

**This journal instead:** "Motivation should come from reality rather than from the literature." The journal's brand is "academic articles that people actually read," prioritizing real-world motivation as the opening move over literature-driven framing.

**Classification:** RELAXATION [GUIDELINE ONLY — not confirmable via article access]

### Tone and Controversy — RELAXATION

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Adversarial or controversial framing is rare and carefully managed; papers present themselves as extending or complementing prior work.

**This journal instead:** FM explicitly welcomes articles that are "bold and controversial (to provoke and further debate in the field)." Debate-provoking framing is an accepted — not merely tolerated — register.

**Classification:** RELAXATION [GUIDELINE ONLY — not confirmable via article access]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Over-investing in robustness sections**: FM's editorial philosophy explicitly de-emphasizes robustness relative to original ideas. A manuscript structured around exhaustive robustness documentation — the ABS 4*/4 standard — may be misaligned with FM's stated priorities.

2. **Literature-first introductions**: FM prefers real-world motivation. An introduction that spends its opening paragraphs reviewing prior literature before motivating the problem may feel misaligned with the editorial brand, even though that structure is standard at higher-ranked finance journals.

3. **Excessive self-citation**: The toolkit explicitly flags this as a submission mistake. Audit self-citations before submitting.

4. **Assuming ABS 4*/4 robustness standards transfer**: FM has explicitly relaxed robustness norms. The journal rewards idea novelty over documentation completeness; reviewers are selected to bring "fresh perspectives" and tolerance for "more risky and innovative" work.
