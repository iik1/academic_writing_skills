# Journal Writing Skill: British Journal of Sociology

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley (on behalf of LSE)
- **Scope:** Leading international sociology journal publishing rigorous original research on all aspects of the discipline, quantitative and qualitative, with a general sociological audience.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14684446/homepage/forauthors.html (also: https://www.lse.ac.uk/sociology/british-journal-of-sociology/notes-to-contributors)
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Calnitsky & Wannamaker, "The revolution next door," BJS 75(5) 2024, via PMC11617795; Stopforth, Connelly & Gayle, "Do you like school?", BJS 75(4) 2024, abstract via White Rose repository)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section order beyond standard academic convention. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section sequencing. Separate Notes section (endnotes) placed after main text and before Bibliography is required when notes are used.

### Length
- **Research articles:** approximately 8,000 words, inclusive of abstract, notes, and bibliography. ENFORCED.
- **Research notes / commentaries:** maximum 2,500 words. ENFORCED.
- Word count on title page must reflect the inclusive count.

### Abstract
Unstructured prose of up to 300 words giving a concise statement of intention, results, and conclusions. No subheadings. Six keywords required, listed in order of importance. ENFORCED.

### Citations
Harvard author-date system throughout. ENFORCED.

**In-text:** (Author Year) with no comma between name and year — e.g., (Giddens 1984); multiple sources separated by semicolons — (Bourdieu 1984; Giddens 1984); four or more authors use et al. — (Klein et al. 2013). No ampersand in in-text form.

**Bibliography format:** distinctive from standard Harvard. Year immediately follows author name; article/chapter titles in single quotation marks; journal and book titles in italics; page numbers preceded by "p." — e.g.:

- Journal article: Nurse, P. 2003 'Systems biology: understanding cells', *Nature* 424(6951): p.883.
- Book: Giddens, A. 1984 *The Constitution of Society*, Cambridge: Polity Press.
- Chapter: Author Year 'Chapter title' in Editor(s) (eds) *Book Title*, Place: Publisher, pp.x–y.

Three or more authors listed with full names in bibliography (no "et al." truncation in reference list).

### Headings
Not prescribed in guidelines. In published quantitative articles: main-section headings appear in bold, all-capitals; numbered decimal subsections (e.g., 4.1, 4.2) used when warranted. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables carry footnotes labeled a, b, c below the table. Figures typically display coefficient plots with confidence intervals in quantitative work.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Notes must be kept to a minimum; discursive footnotes embedded in text are not permitted — all notes must be endnotes.
- Manuscripts must not mix UK and US spelling; one convention must be chosen and applied consistently throughout.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Format
FIELD_SKILL covers citation density and paraphrase norms but does not specify a reference list format. BJS uses a distinctive Harvard variant: year immediately after author in the bibliography (not at the end of the entry), article titles in single quotes, page numbers preceded by "p." This format is non-standard relative to APA or Chicago and must be applied precisely. ENFORCED.

### Notes Convention
BJS explicitly requires endnotes rather than footnotes and instructs authors to minimise their use. FIELD_SKILL does not specify a note placement convention. ENFORCED (confirmed in published articles: Calnitsky & Wannamaker used 13 sequentially numbered endnotes placed before the reference list).

### Spelling Latitude
BJS accepts either UK or US spelling, provided it is applied consistently. For a British sociology journal this is notably permissive; FIELD_SKILL does not address spelling conventions. GUIDELINE ONLY.

### Section Heading Style
Published quantitative articles use bold, all-capitals for main-section headings and numbered decimal subsections. Some prize-winning articles have used colloquial or thematic section titles in place of conventional labels (e.g., "There Goes the Neighborhood" for a literature review section). Neither convention is prescribed in the guidelines. PRACTICE ONLY. As this is an ABS 3 journal, the thematic-heading practice may represent a RELAXATION of the more formal heading norms observable at ABS 4 level.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Inclusive word count**: The 8,000-word limit includes the abstract, notes section, and bibliography. Authors who count only body text will routinely exceed the limit once these sections are added.
2. **Bibliography format errors**: The BJS reference style — year immediately after author, single-quoted article titles, "p." before page numbers — is easily confused with standard Harvard or APA. Submitting with APA or Chicago formatting is a common error.
3. **Missing or mis-ordered keywords**: Six keywords are required and must be listed in order of importance. Omitting keywords or treating order as arbitrary are both departures from the guidelines.
4. **Footnotes instead of endnotes**: BJS requires endnotes. Authors accustomed to footnotes must convert notes to the endnote section placed after the main text and before the bibliography.
