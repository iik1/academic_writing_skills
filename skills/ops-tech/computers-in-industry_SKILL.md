# Journal Writing Skill: Computers in Industry

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Original, application-oriented research on information and communication technology in industry, covering business process integration (ERP, design, manufacturing, supply chain), knowledge-intensive industrial fields, and emerging technologies including AI/ML applied to industrial settings. Papers focused solely on ICT or manufacturing processes without broader relevance are out of scope.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/computers-in-industry/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** opsandtech-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (S0166361524000113; S0166361524000095; S0166361524000101)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section sequence beyond: Abstract, Keywords, Highlights, main body, References, Declarations (competing interests, funding, AI use). Follows conventional research structure (Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion/Conclusion) in practice. A separate Title Page is required for double-blind review.

### Length
- Regular and survey papers: approximately 10,000 words. [ENFORCED]
- Short notes, comments, position papers: no more than 2,000 words. [ENFORCED]
- Individual case studies: may exceed limits where broader relevance is demonstrated. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No labeled subsections required. Word limit not specified in guidelines. [ENFORCED — unstructured format confirmed in all three articles]

### Highlights
**Required.** A short collection of bullet points conveying the core findings. Observed practice: 3–4 bullet points per article. Guidelines list Highlights as a required submission element. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
**Numbered bracket style** — [1], [2], etc. — throughout the manuscript. This overrides the author-date (APA-adjacent) norm in FIELD_SKILL. [ENFORCED — confirmed in all three articles]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. A Graphical Abstract is optional.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Generative AI may not be listed as an author or co-author.
- Authors must declare any generative AI use in manuscript preparation in a dedicated statement before the References section.
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance; generally not considered after submission.

### Mandatory Declarations
Authors must submit a Declarations section covering: competing interests, funding sources, and AI use (even if nil).

### Inclusive Language
Guidelines explicitly require inclusive language throughout. Gender-neutral constructions using plural nouns are specifically mandated (e.g., "they" rather than gendered pronouns). [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** Author-date in-text citations (APA-adjacent), e.g., (Smith, 2020).
**This journal:** Numbered bracket citations [1], [2] in-text, with numbered reference list. Applies throughout all sections. Label: **ENFORCED**

### Highlights
**Field norm:** Not specified in FIELD_SKILL.
**This journal:** Highlights are a required submission element (3–4 bullet points summarising core findings), distinct from the abstract. Label: **ENFORCED**

### Voice and Person
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** First-person plural ("we") as standard authorial voice, including for single-authored work.
**This journal:** Impersonal, third-person constructions dominate in observed articles ("the study interprets," "the framework formalises," passive constructions). First-person plural is not the house norm. Given ABS 3 tier, this is a relaxation of the field norm. Label: **RELAXATION — PRACTICE ONLY** (guidelines silent; observed consistently across all three articles)

### Managerial Implications
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** A dedicated managerial implications subsection is near-universal across the field.
**This journal:** The CS/engineering orientation means papers are evaluated on technical contribution and industrial applicability rather than explicit managerial implications framing. A dedicated "Managerial Implications" section is not a standard structural expectation based on article observation. Label: **RELAXATION — PRACTICE ONLY**

### Scope and Paper Type
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Quantitative empirical, qualitative, analytical modelling, and conceptual papers grounded in OM/SCM/production management theory.
**This journal:** A substantial share of articles are algorithmic, AI/ML, or system-design papers with applied engineering focus. Theory-and-hypotheses structures (H1, H2…) are uncommon; papers more typically report precision/recall/F1 metrics, benchmark comparisons, or system evaluation results rather than regression tables or structural equation models. Label: **PRACTICE ONLY**

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Using author-date citations.** The journal uses numbered [1] style throughout. Submitting a manuscript with (Author, Year) citations will require a complete reformatting pass.
2. **Omitting Highlights.** Highlights are a required element. Missing or insufficient highlights (fewer than 3 points) will trigger a compliance request from the editorial system.
3. **Framing as an OM/SCM theory paper without a computational or ICT component.** The journal's scope centres on ICT applied to industry. Papers without a substantive technology implementation, system design, or AI/ML component are likely out of scope regardless of methodological quality.
4. **Missing Declarations section.** Competing interests, funding, and AI use statements are all required. Omitting any component—even a nil declaration—is a submission compliance failure.
