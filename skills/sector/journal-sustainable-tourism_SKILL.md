# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Sustainable Tourism

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Routledge / Taylor & Francis
- **Scope:** Theoretical, conceptual and empirical research advancing critical understanding of the relationships between tourism and sustainable development, covering economic, social, cultural, political, organisational, and environmental dimensions. Has a distinctive critical/transformative orientation, regularly publishing work on justice, decolonisation, degrowth, and epistemic diversity.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Normative and Advocacy Framing

**Field norm:** "Polemical framing is absent from high-quality work in this field" (FIELD_SKILL, Citation and Literature Engagement section). At ABS 4, argumentation is constructive and measured.

**This journal:** JoST actively publishes work that takes explicit normative positions — advocating for justice, decolonisation, degrowth, and systemic transformation in tourism. Authors are expected to position their arguments within a critical/transformative paradigm. Overtly prescriptive claims ("tourism must...", "we need radical responses") appear in published articles without the heavy hedging that field norms would typically require for causal or prescriptive statements. This is a **RELAXATION** at ABS 3 level: polemical or advocacy-oriented framing is acceptable when grounded in a critical theoretical position, not an error.

Classification: **PRACTICE ONLY**

### Open Science Requirements

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that "Ethical approval statements, data availability declarations, and conflict-of-interest disclosures are now expected" — but does not mention preregistration, preprints, or registered reports.

**This journal:** Since the 2023 editorial ("Open science for sustainable tourism," Vol. 32), JoST has an explicit open science editorial policy. (a) Preprints are actively encouraged; authors are asked to deposit before or at submission. (b) Preregistration is encouraged for confirmatory quantitative work. (c) Registered Reports became an accepted submission type in 2024, following a two-stage review process (Introduction + Methods accepted in principle before data collection). These are currently encouraged rather than required for standard submissions, but represent a distinctive journal expectation.

Classification: **GUIDELINE ONLY** (formally stated in editorial policy; not yet a hard submission requirement for all article types)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting value-neutral descriptive work**: JoST expects research to engage critically with sustainability and take a position on implications. Work that describes without evaluating or advocating is unlikely to fit the journal's transformative mandate.
2. **Over-hedging normative claims**: In critical/advocacy papers, excessive hedging of the paper's own normative stance reads as misaligned with the journal's intellectual culture. Reserve hedging for empirical claims, not theoretical or justice-based arguments.
3. **Ignoring the open science expectation**: Reviewers and editors increasingly expect authors to have considered preregistration, open data, and preprint deposition. Failing to address these signals unfamiliarity with current journal norms even when not formally required.
4. **Structured abstract**: Do not use Emerald-style structured abstracts (Purpose / Design/methodology / Findings / Originality). JoST uses unstructured prose abstracts.
