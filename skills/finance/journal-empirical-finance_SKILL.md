# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Empirical Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** High-quality empirical finance broadly defined — asset pricing, corporate finance, financial econometrics, banking, international finance, market microstructure, and behavioral finance. Theoretical work with clear empirical implications is also within scope. Editors explicitly welcome innovative, controversial, and unusual approaches.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-empirical-finance/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1016/j.jempfin.2024.101002 — "CEO neuroticism and corporate cash holdings"; 10.1016/j.jempfin.2024.101099 — "Forecasting realized betas using predictors indicating structural breaks and asymmetric risk effects", both Vol. 80, Jan. 2025)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Beyond the canonical structure described in FIELD_SKILL, the following elements are required in all submissions:

- **Highlights** — A dedicated section listing 3–5 single-sentence findings, each no longer than 85 characters, placed before the abstract. This is an Elsevier-wide requirement confirmed in all articles checked. Label: ENFORCED
- **Keywords** — 4–6 keywords required. Label: ENFORCED
- **JEL classification codes** — Required. Label: ENFORCED (confirmed in all articles)
- **CRediT author contribution statement** — Required, documenting each author's role. Label: ENFORCED
- **Data availability statement** — Required. Label: ENFORCED
- **Declaration of generative AI** — If any generative AI tool was used in writing or analysis, a disclosure statement must appear as a named section immediately before the References section. If no AI was used, no section is needed. Label: ENFORCED

### Length
No explicit word or page limits stated in the accessible portion of the guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. Observed length in published articles: 65–160 words. The 150-word lower bound in FIELD_SKILL does not apply; shorter abstracts are acceptable. Maximum 250 words (Elsevier standard). Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (upper bound); RELAXATION (lower bound relative to FIELD_SKILL)

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical format, e.g., "Ang et al. (2006)", "Frazzini and Pedersen (2014)". Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Significance star notation (* p<0.10, ** p<0.05, *** p<0.01) confirmed in articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Generative AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Generative AI may not be used by reviewers or editors during peer review.
- Manuscripts must not have been previously published (preprints excepted).
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Highlights section:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify a Highlights section. JEF requires one as a mandatory pre-abstract element containing 3–5 bullets of ≤85 characters each, summarizing core findings. This is the most significant structural addition relative to the field baseline. Label: ENFORCED

**JEL codes:** Not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. Required by JEF and confirmed in all articles. Place after keywords on the published page. Label: ENFORCED

### Abstract Length

FIELD_SKILL states "typically 150–250 words." JEF articles in practice are often substantially shorter (as low as 65 words). The journal does not appear to enforce a lower bound. Label: RELAXATION

### Editorial Tone (Scope Signal)

FIELD_SKILL reflects ABS 4*/4 norms where mainstream empirical papers dominate. JEF's guidelines explicitly state editors welcome "innovative, controversial papers, and unusual approaches" and are interested in work from emerging scholars. This signals a slightly higher tolerance for non-canonical methods and unconventional framing than implied by the field baseline. Authors should not over-hedge methodological novelty as if writing for a more conservative outlet. Label: PRACTICE ONLY

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting Highlights.** The Highlights section is a hard Elsevier requirement that does not exist in most top-4* finance journals. Authors migrating a draft from JF, JFE, or RFS must add this section; it is not optional.

2. **Overly long abstract.** The field baseline cites 150–250 words as typical. JEF abstracts in practice run shorter; a 100-word abstract is normal here. Do not pad to meet the FIELD_SKILL range.

3. **Missing JEL codes.** Required at submission; not adding them is a preventable desk-rejection trigger.

4. **Scope mismatch.** The guidelines explicitly flag rejection of papers that fit better in accounting, purely computational, or purely mathematical finance journals. A paper without meaningful empirical content or empirical implications will be desk-rejected regardless of quality.
