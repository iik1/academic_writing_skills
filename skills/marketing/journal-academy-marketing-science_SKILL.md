## Journal-Specific Notes

### Article Types
JAMS publishes a broad range of methodologies:
- Experimental/behavioral (multi-study designs)
- Quantitative empirical (econometric, field experiments, structural models)
- Meta-analyses (as examined)
- Conceptual/review papers
- Scale development papers
- Method papers

This methodological diversity is noted in FIELD_SKILL.

### Distinctive Emphasis (per FIELD_SKILL baseline)
- **Managerial implications** receive more prominent treatment than in JCR or JMR
- **Structural equation modeling (SEM)** with fit indices (CFI, RMSEA, SRMR) especially common
- **Broad empirical scope** covering B2B, services, branding, and strategy alongside consumer behavior
- **Scale development** papers recognized as distinct article type

These features are already documented in FIELD_SKILL section 11 (Journal-Level Notes).

## Common Pitfalls

Based on FIELD_SKILL guidance and journal positioning:

1. **Insufficient managerial implications:** JAMS expects explicit, substantive discussion of practical implications alongside theoretical contributions. Do not treat this as perfunctory.

2. **Narrow methodological contribution without theoretical insight:** While JAMS values methodological rigor, purely technical contributions without clear marketing theory advancement are insufficient.

3. **Single-study experimental designs:** For behavioral work, JAMS typically expects multi-study designs (3+ studies) demonstrating robustness, mechanism, and boundary conditions.

4. **Weak integration across diverse literatures:** JAMS publishes across multiple marketing domains (consumer, B2B, services, branding). Papers must situate contributions within the appropriate sub-literatures while demonstrating broader relevance.
