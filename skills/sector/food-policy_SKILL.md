# Journal Writing Skill: Food Policy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary empirical and analytical research on food systems, agricultural policy, food security, nutrition policy, and consumer behaviour as they relate to national and international policy debates. Explicit policy relevance is a condition of acceptance, not a bonus.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/food-policy/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** sector-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (10.1016/j.foodpol.2024.102622; 10.1016/j.foodpol.2024.102623; 10.1016/j.foodpol.2023.102568)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Mandatory section sequence for full research articles:
1. Highlights
2. Abstract
3. Introduction — must explicitly flag the paper's policy implications
4. Body sections (methods, results, discussion as appropriate)
5. Conclusions
6. **Policy Implications** — required as a dedicated standalone section; must also be signalled in the Introduction
7. References

The Policy Implications section is non-negotiable. The guidelines state it is required both in the Introduction and as a separate body section. [ENFORCED]

### Length
6,000–10,000 words for complete submissions. Word count includes abstract, endnotes, tables, references, and appendices. [ENFORCED]

**Policy Comments** (a distinct short-form article type): ≤600 words, ≤10 references, ≤1 figure or table; no abstract; no appendices or supplementary material. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured (plain prose, no labelled fields). No explicit word limit stated in the guidelines. [GUIDELINE ONLY — no limit enforced]

### Citations
Numbered in-text citations in brackets (e.g. [1], [2]), with a sequentially numbered reference list. This is the observed practice across spot-checked articles. Specific citation style rules were not fully accessible in the guidelines text retrieved, but numbered format is consistent across checked articles. [PRACTICE ONLY — confirmed in articles; not verified in guidelines text]

This deviates from the sector field baseline, which specifies author–year (APA/Harvard) format.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for heading hierarchy. No journal-specific heading label conventions stated beyond the required "Policy Implications" section name.

### Tables and Figures
Standard Elsevier rules apply. Policy Comments are restricted to at most one figure or table total. [ENFORCED for Policy Comments]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Review and Viewpoint articles are published by invitation only; unsolicited submissions are desk-rejected without review. [ENFORCED]
- No supplementary material or appendices permitted for Policy Comments. [ENFORCED]
- No overlap with previously published work; similarity checks are performed on all submissions. [ENFORCED]
- Authorship changes after acceptance require a formal Authorship Change Request; not permitted unilaterally. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where Food Policy deviates from the sector field baseline.

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** Author–year (APA) parenthetical citations throughout.
**This journal:** Numbered citations in brackets [1], [2], with a sequentially numbered reference list. Confirmed in two spot-checked articles. Authors should not use (Smith, 2020) format.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (guidelines text was not fully accessible to confirm explicit statement, but practice is consistent across checked articles)

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** Practical/managerial implications typically integrated within Discussion or Conclusion; a standalone section is optional.
**This journal:** Policy Implications is a mandatory separate section, positioned after Conclusions, not inside it. The introduction must also explicitly signal the paper's policy contribution. This is a structural requirement, not a presentational preference.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Highlights
**Field norm:** "Optional but common" in Elsevier journals (per FIELD_SKILL).
**This journal:** Required for all full research articles; present in all spot-checked articles.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Accessibility Register
**Field norm:** High formal register; no specific instruction about disciplinary accessibility.
**This journal:** Guidelines explicitly require prose to be "accessible to a social science readership." Given the journal's interdisciplinary audience (economists, nutritionists, agronomists, public health researchers, policy practitioners), technical language from any single discipline should be unpacked rather than assumed.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Using author–year citations.** The numbered-bracket system is the operative format. Submitting with APA in-text citations will require reformatting and signals unfamiliarity with the journal.
2. **Burying policy implications in the Discussion or Conclusion.** The Policy Implications section must appear as a distinct section after Conclusions, and the Introduction must also flag policy relevance. Treating policy discussion as incidental rather than structural is a basis for desk rejection.
3. **Submitting a Review or Viewpoint article without an invitation.** These formats are invitation-only; unsolicited versions are rejected at the desk regardless of quality.
4. **Exceeding the word cap on full submissions.** The 10,000-word ceiling covers all components including references and tables. Submissions that exceed this range, or that use appendices to circumvent the limit, will require cuts before review.
