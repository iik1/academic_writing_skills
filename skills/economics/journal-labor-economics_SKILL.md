# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Labor Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** University of Chicago Press (official journal of the Society of Labor Economists)
- **Scope:** Publishes international research on labor economics, covering supply and demand for labor services, personnel economics, income distribution, unions and collective bargaining, and labor market demographics.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jole/instruct
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Charles, Johnson & Tadjfar, JOLE 44(1), DOI 10.1086/732302; Lang, JOLE 43(2), DOI 10.1086/732772) — full text inaccessible due to paywall; abstracts and metadata reviewed.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No section ordering mandated beyond the title page requirement (names, email addresses, and affiliations of all authors preceding the abstract).

### Length
**20,000-word cap.** A full-page table or figure counts as 500 words. Manuscripts significantly exceeding this limit may be returned before review. FIELD_SKILL does not specify word limits. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
**100 words maximum, unstructured.** The abstract appears on the title page alongside author names, affiliations, and email addresses. No structured fields (no JEL, no keywords section mandated on the title page beyond the abstract itself). FIELD_SKILL does not specify a word limit. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago author-date style (author-year parenthetical). First names written out as in the original publication. References are not anonymized (single-blind review: reviewers know author identities). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on citation format. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Tables numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals. Detailed preparation guidelines provided by the press for artwork and figures. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on substance. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated beyond the length cap. Manuscripts exceeding 20,000 words may be returned without review.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Mandatory Disclosures (not in FIELD_SKILL)

**AI/LLM usage disclosure.** When large language models (e.g., ChatGPT) are used to write or substantially rewrite a manuscript, this must be reported in the acknowledgment footnote, with details of how and where AI was used provided in the article's online appendix. FIELD_SKILL has no norm on this. [ENFORCED]

**Funding disclosure.** Authors must attach a statement disclosing any direct funding that contributed to the research. This statement is published as part of the accepted article, not merely included in the submission cover letter. FIELD_SKILL does not address mandatory published funding disclosure. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding the word count.** At 20,000 words with tables counting as 500 words each, a paper with many tables can hit the limit faster than expected. Check the word count including table equivalents before submission.
2. **Over-length abstract.** The 100-word cap is strict. Economics abstracts at ABS 4* journals often run 150–200 words; cutting to 100 requires deliberate triage of content.
3. **Missing funding disclosure.** Unlike journals where a funding acknowledgment is optional or boilerplate, JOLE requires a formal funding statement that will appear in the published article. Omitting it at submission creates a revision burden.
4. **Undisclosed LLM use.** If AI tools were used in drafting or substantially rewriting the manuscript, disclosure is required both in the acknowledgment note and in the online appendix. Failing to disclose is an ethics violation under the journal's COPE-aligned policies.
