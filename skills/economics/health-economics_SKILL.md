# Journal Writing Skill: Health Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Wiley
- **Scope:** International journal covering all aspects of health economics, including theoretical contributions, empirical studies, and health policy analysis from an economic perspective.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/10991050/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1002/hec.4795; 10.1002/hec.4893)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
The guidelines state the manuscript should "in general, but not necessarily" follow: Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Acknowledgements, References, Tables, Legends and Figures. This is explicitly permissive, not a mandate. The journal also offers Free Format submission. In practice, empirical papers use variants such as Introduction → Empirical Strategy → Data → Results → Conclusions, and theory/applied papers use custom analytic section sequences without a separate Conclusion. **RELAXATION**: structure is less prescribed than FIELD_SKILL implies for either paper type.

### Length
No overall manuscript word limit specified in guidelines. Abstract: 200 words maximum. **ENFORCED.**

### Abstract
Unstructured (no labeled fields such as Background/Methods/Results/Conclusions). Maximum 200 words. Must not contain citations. Keywords required. JEL Classification codes required on the abstract page. Confirmed in both spot-checked articles. **ENFORCED.**

### Citations
Author-year (parenthetical) in text; alphabetical APA-style reference list. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Numbered sections with pipe separator (e.g., "1 | INTRODUCTION") in the published format; subsections numbered hierarchically (3.1, 3.1.1). **PRACTICE ONLY** (typesetting convention; authors need not replicate exact pipe formatting in manuscript).

### Tables and Figures
Guidelines state tables and figures should not be inserted in text but placed at end of manuscript, each on a separate page. This is a **submission-stage requirement** (not the published layout). **ENFORCED** for submission.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Abstracts must not contain citations. **ENFORCED.**
- Clinical trial registration numbers must be included in abstracts reporting trial results (not applicable to most economics papers). **ENFORCED** where applicable.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Mandatory Back-Matter Sections
FIELD_SKILL does not specify back-matter requirements. Health Economics requires two labeled sections before the reference list in every article:
1. **Conflict of Interest Statement** — all authors must declare conflicts or state none exist.
2. **Data Availability Statement** — authors must describe where data can be accessed, including any access restrictions.

Both confirmed in both spot-checked articles. **ENFORCED.**

### JEL Classification
Not covered by FIELD_SKILL (explicitly outside its scope). Health Economics requires JEL codes to appear on the abstract page, alongside Keywords. Confirmed in both articles. **ENFORCED.**

### Abstract Word Limit
FIELD_SKILL does not specify word limits. Health Economics caps abstracts at 200 words. This is tighter than typical economics working paper abstracts (which often run 150–250 words). **ENFORCED.**

### Structural Relaxation: No Mandated Section Order
FIELD_SKILL specifies near-canonical section orders for empirical and theory papers. Health Economics explicitly states the suggested structure need not be followed, and offers Free Format submission. Observed practice confirms flexibility: one article places Empirical Strategy before Data; the theoretical article uses entirely custom sections. Do not assume a FIELD_SKILL-prescribed section order is required or expected here. **RELAXATION.**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 200 words or containing citations.** The 200-word cap is strict and abstracts must be free of references — both are enforced submission requirements.
2. **Omitting JEL codes.** Unlike many field journals where JEL codes are added at submission-system level, Health Economics expects them on the manuscript abstract page itself.
3. **Omitting Conflict of Interest and Data Availability statements.** These are required labeled sections, not optional acknowledgement text. Manuscripts without them will be returned.
4. **Imposing a rigid FIELD_SKILL section schema.** The journal's explicit "not necessarily" language and Free Format option mean reviewers will not penalise unconventional structures — but the abstract, back-matter, and JEL requirements remain non-negotiable regardless of structure chosen.
