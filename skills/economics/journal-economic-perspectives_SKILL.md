# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Economic Perspectives

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** American Economic Association
- **Scope:** Bridges standard research journals and the general-interest press; presents insights from one specialty to economists outside that specialty. All content is fully open access.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/submissions and https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/editorial-policy
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 5 abstracts from AEA metadata pages (full PDFs inaccessible; 403 errors on pubs.aeaweb.org). Spot-check was abstract-level only; deltas below derive primarily from guidelines.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
The journal does not publish standard empirical research papers, theory papers, or literature reviews. Articles are perspective, synthesis, or applied-survey pieces that argue for the author's interpretation of a body of evidence. No required section architecture (identification strategy, robustness appendix, etc.) is mandated. Structure is determined by the argument, not the canonical empirical paper template. **ENFORCED**

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines. Published articles run approximately 20–30 journal pages. Guidelines emphasise a proposal-first process; full manuscripts must be approved in advance.

### Abstract
Unstructured; no required fields or explicit word limit. Spot-checked abstracts ran approximately 80–130 words—shorter and less technically dense than typical AER abstracts. **PRACTICE ONLY**

### Citations
AEA author-year format. In-text: (Author Year). Reference list: Last, First, and Second First Last, Year, "Article Title." *Journal* Volume (Number): Pages. **Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.**

### Headings
**Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.** No journal-mandated heading convention observed.

### Tables and Figures
**Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.** No journal-specific rules stated beyond AEA data/code availability policy.

### Explicit Prohibitions
The journal does **not** publish:
- Book reviews
- Literature reviews (papers whose primary contribution is cataloguing prior work)
- Highly mathematical papers
- Papers on narrowly focused topics inaccessible to the general AEA membership
- **ENFORCED**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**Field norm:** High technical vocabulary density; specialist audience assumed; jargon used freely once introduced.

**JEP instead:** Accessible to "90 percent or more of the AEA membership." Jargon must be explained or avoided; necessary complexity must be made intelligible to non-specialists. The register is closer to the upper end of general-interest academic writing than to the specialist register of AER or JPE. **ENFORCED**

Practical implication: A reader in a different subfield of economics must be able to follow the argument. Subfield-specific estimators, datasets, and institutional details should be briefly explained, not assumed.

### Mathematical Notation

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** No restriction on equations; standard in empirical and theory papers.

**JEP instead:** "Scholarly without relying too heavily on mathematical notation." Guidelines explicitly prefer "economic intuition over mathematical derivations when possible." Equations should appear only where they genuinely clarify an idea that prose cannot convey. **ENFORCED**

### Argumentation and Stance

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Authors state findings, contributions, and claims clearly, but the primary rhetorical move is "here is what the evidence shows."

**JEP instead:** Articles should "argue for the author's point of view" and explain "how recent work shaped that perspective." The appropriate mode is advocacy and synthesis—taking an explicit interpretive or policy position grounded in evidence—not neutral reporting of results. Conclusions may be normative or prescriptive where the evidence supports them. **ENFORCED**

### Structural Deviations

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Canonical empirical paper structure with identification strategy, data section, robustness checks, roadmap paragraph.

**JEP instead:** No required identification strategy section; no expected robustness appendix as a standalone component. The article is organized around the argument, not around methodology sections. Original data work, if present, is subordinate to the interpretive/synthetic contribution. The roadmap paragraph convention is less rigid because the structure is not pre-set. **ENFORCED (by type restriction)**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Specialist-only framing.** Writing for an audience of econometricians or IO specialists rather than the general AEA membership. The test: would a macroeconomist or development economist who is not in your subfield be able to read and engage with every section?

2. **Equation-heavy exposition.** Carrying over the notation-dense style of a research paper. JEP editors actively push back on mathematical derivations that could be replaced by economic intuition.

3. **Literature-survey structure.** Organizing the paper as a comprehensive review ("Section 2 reviews the evidence on X, Section 3 reviews Y…") rather than as an argument. The prohibition on literature reviews is absolute; the contribution must be an interpretation or synthesis, not a catalogue.

4. **Submitting without a proposal.** The journal strongly prefers a 2–5 page proposal before a completed manuscript. Topics are often organized into symposia; a completed paper may arrive too late or overlap with already-commissioned pieces.
