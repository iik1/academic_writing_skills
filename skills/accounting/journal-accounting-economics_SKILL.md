# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Accounting and Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Publishes empirical, analytical, and experimental research that applies economic theory and methods to accounting phenomena. Narrower scope than general accounting journals: manuscripts without an explicit economic-theoretic motivation are outside scope.

### Headings
Arabic numerals with title-case headings. Already noted in FIELD_SKILL as the JAE/JAR convention. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Submission Fee
**USD 750 required for all new unsolicited manuscripts, paid before the submission enters consideration.** Resubmissions are free. Desk rejection without peer review is possible at any time. [ENFORCED — unique to JAE among major accounting journals]

### Review Process
Double-anonymized peer review is required: authors must remove all identifying information from the manuscript. [ENFORCED]

### Generative AI
Any use of generative AI tools in manuscript preparation must be declared in a statement placed immediately before the References section. AI tools may not be listed as authors. [ENFORCED — Elsevier policy applied at this journal]

### Scope Requirement
Manuscripts must apply economic theory to accounting phenomena. Papers lacking explicit economic-theoretic framing are outside scope regardless of empirical quality. [ENFORCED]

### Inclusive Language
Authors are required to use inclusive, gender-neutral language throughout. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No simultaneous submission to another journal.
- No prior publication (preprints, abstracts, and theses are excepted).
- No listing of AI tools as authors or co-authors.
- No authorship changes after acceptance.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Submission Economics (Administrative Delta)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address submission fees.
**JAE practice:** USD 750 non-refundable submission fee required before review begins; resubmissions are free. This has practical implications: (a) do not submit speculative or early-stage work, (b) a rejection without review is financially costly, and (c) resubmission to a revise-and-resubmit is incentivized. [ENFORCED]

### Scope Constraint
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes accounting research broadly, including capital markets, auditing, managerial, and tax research without a required theoretical anchor.
**JAE practice:** An economic-theoretic motivation is a hard scope requirement. Papers using economic frameworks (agency theory, contracting theory, information economics, mechanism design) are favored; purely descriptive archival work without explicit economic grounding is at scope risk. [ENFORCED]

### Review Anonymity
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify blind vs. double-blind across journals.
**JAE practice:** Double-anonymized — reviewers do not know author identities, and authors' manuscripts must be prepared to conceal their identities. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting work without economic-theoretic grounding.** JAE editors desk-reject papers that read as "accounting" rather than "economics of accounting." Explicitly anchor hypotheses in economic theory (agency, contracting, information economics) even when the contribution is primarily empirical.
2. **Forgetting the submission fee.** USD 750 must be paid before submission is processed. Confirm the payment system during submission and budget accordingly; the fee is not refunded regardless of editorial outcome.
3. **Failing to anonymize for double-blind review.** Unlike some accounting journals that use single-blind review, JAE requires full author anonymization. Remove acknowledgments, self-citations that reveal identity, and institutional references from the submitted manuscript.
4. **Omitting the GenAI declaration.** If any AI writing or analysis tool was used, a declaration statement is required immediately before the references. Omitting it violates Elsevier policy and may cause a desk rejection.
