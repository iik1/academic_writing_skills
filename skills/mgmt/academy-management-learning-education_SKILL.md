# Journal Writing Skill: Academy of Management Learning and Education

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** MGMT
- **Publisher:** Academy of Management
- **Scope:** Publishes theory, empirical research, reviews, critiques, and resources addressing management teaching and learning processes. Theory-driven scholarship; NOT a teaching-practice journal publishing descriptive work. Requires rigorous analyses contributing to theoretical debate.

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm with manuscript type variations: conceptual, review, empirical articles, essays, and dialogues. No deviations from standard MGMT field structure observed in guidelines.

**Label:** FIELD DEFAULT

### Length
- **Regular manuscripts:** Up to 40 pages, double-spaced, inclusive of figures, tables, and references
- **Essays:** Up to 35 pages, double-spaced, inclusive of figures, tables, and references (references may be single-spaced)

**Label:** ENFORCED

### Abstract
- No more than 200 words
- Format (structured/unstructured) not specified in available guidelines
- Follows standard AOM practice

**Label:** ENFORCED

### Citations
- AOM author-date citation style (modified APA)
- Citations ordered alphabetically
- Two authors: cite both every time
- Three to six authors: all names first citation, then "et al."
- Seven or more authors: "et al." even for first citation
- Multiple works by same author(s) same year: add "a," "b," etc. after year

**Label:** ENFORCED (AOM-wide standard)

### Headings
- Times New Roman 12-point
- Section headings and subheadings at 1.5 spacing (body text double-spaced)
- Standard formatting otherwise follows FIELD_SKILL

**Label:** ENFORCED

### Tables and Figures
Included in page count. Standard academic formatting. Specific table/figure formatting rules not detailed in accessible guidelines.

**Label:** FIELD DEFAULT

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Purely descriptive teaching-practice manuscripts** are explicitly rejected. AMLE requires rigorous logical and empirical analyses that contribute to theoretical debate.
- Manuscripts must not be under consideration elsewhere or previously published.

**Label:** ENFORCED

### Contribution Requirements

**Field norm:** MGMT field expects 2-4 theoretical contributions framed against existing literatures.

**AMLE requirement:** Three mandatory qualities for excellent manuscripts:
1. Firm grounding in management learning and education (MLE) scholarship
2. Appropriate, well-executed analysis
3. Contribution to both theory AND practice

AMLE is explicitly theory-driven: work must "enhance our ability to explain, predict, or understand phenomena relevant for MLE." Description alone insufficient—manuscripts must explain why and how in ways that improve theory and practice.

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY (strengthened contribution bar specific to AMLE's pedagogical domain)

### Domain Specificity

**Field norm:** MGMT field encompasses broad organizational and management topics.

**AMLE requirement:** All work must address "processes of management teaching and the learning that results from it." Manuscripts must be grounded in management learning and education scholarship specifically, not general management research.

**Label:** ENFORCED

### Page Limits by Type

**Field norm:** MGMT journals typically 10,000-15,000 words (roughly 35-50 pages double-spaced) without strict type-based differentiation.

**AMLE practice:** Differential limits—40 pages for regular manuscripts, 35 pages for essays. References may be single-spaced for essays.

**Label:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting descriptive teaching practice without theoretical contribution.** AMLE explicitly states it is NOT a teaching-practice journal. Describing a course, pedagogy, or curriculum without rigorous analysis and theoretical advancement will be desk-rejected.

2. **Insufficient grounding in MLE scholarship.** General management theory alone is inadequate. Manuscripts must engage with the management learning and education literature specifically.

3. **Failing to address both theory and practice.** AMLE requires dual contribution. Work that advances theory without practical implications for management education, or practice without theoretical grounding, will not meet the bar.

4. **Exceeding page limits.** 40-page hard cap for regular manuscripts, 35 for essays (inclusive of everything except potentially single-spaced references for essays).
