## MANUSCRIPT STRUCTURE

### Article Types & Length [ENFORCED]

**Original Articles**:
- **Max 40 pages** (double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman, 1-inch margins)
- Includes title page, abstract, main text, references, tables, figures, appendices
- Word count guideline: ~10,000-12,000 words

**Research Briefs**:
- **Max 20 pages** (same formatting) + tables + references
- For focused empirical findings or novel methodological contributions

**Review Essays**:
- **Max 40 pages** (same formatting)
- Comprehensive reviews with novel theoretical integration

### Abstract [GUIDELINE ONLY ⚠️]

**Stated guideline**: 100 words, unstructured

**Observed practice**: 
- Neff et al. (2026): ~150-200 words, unstructured
- **DELTA DETECTED**: Published abstracts may exceed 100-word guideline by 50-100%

**Recommendation**: Draft concise abstract (~100 words) per guidelines, but recognize editorial flexibility for slightly longer abstracts if conceptually dense.

### Keywords [ENFORCED]

- **Minimum 2-5 keywords**
- Should reflect main theoretical constructs and empirical context

### Headings & Organization [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Observed structure** (Neff et al., 2026):
1. Introduction (no heading)
2. Theory & Background (with subsections)
3. Hypothesis Development
4. Study Overview (if multi-study design)
5. Study 1: Methods → Results
6. Study 2: Methods → Results
7. Discussion (with subsections: Contributions, Practical Implications, Limitations)
8. Conclusion
9. Appendices (as needed)

**Pattern**: IMRaD variant with explicit theory development section before hypotheses. Multi-study designs present each study sequentially with dedicated methods/results subsections.

## WRITING VOICE & STYLE

### Voice [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Third-person scholarly** with **inclusive "we"** for research team:
- ✅ "We contribute to..."
- ✅ "Our findings suggest..."
- ✅ "We examine..."
- ❌ Avoid first-person singular ("I argue...")
- ❌ Avoid passive voice overuse

### Citation Density [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Heavy front-loading**:
- **Introduction & Theory**: Multiple citations per paragraph (dense theoretical grounding)
- **Hypothesis Development**: Moderate density (supporting each hypothesis with 2-5 key citations)
- **Methods**: Light (primarily methodological references)
- **Results**: Sparse (focus on findings)
- **Discussion**: Moderate-to-heavy (connecting back to literature)

## FORMATTING & SUBMISSION

### File Format [ENFORCED]

- **Preferred**: Microsoft Word (.doc, .docx)
- **Accepted**: LaTeX (must follow journal formatting upon acceptance)

### Typography [ENFORCED]

- **Font**: 12-point Times New Roman
- **Line spacing**: Double-spaced throughout (including references)
- **Margins**: 1 inch (2.54 cm) on all sides
- **Page numbers**: Bottom center or top right

### Anonymization [ENFORCED]

**Double-blind review** — Remove ALL identifying information:
- Author names, affiliations, acknowledgments
- Self-citations must be anonymized: "As [Author, Year] demonstrated..." → "As [BLINDED] demonstrated..."
- File properties metadata
- Grant/funding acknowledgments (add after acceptance)

### Tables & Figures [ENFORCED]

- Place **after references** (not embedded in text)
- Each table/figure on separate page
- Number consecutively (Table 1, Table 2...)
- Provide clear, concise titles
- Include notes below table for abbreviations, significance markers

## STATISTICAL REPORTING

### Inline Reporting [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Format observed** (Neff et al., 2026):
```
B = .20, t = 2.40, p < .05
β = .15, 95% CI [.08, .22]
```

**Pattern**: Coefficient first, test statistic second, significance third. Confidence intervals reported for key effects.

### Tables [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Regression tables** should include:
- Unstandardized coefficients (B)
- Standard errors (SE) in parentheses
- t-values or z-values
- Significance stars (*, **, ***)
- Model fit statistics (R², F-statistic, AIC/BIC if applicable)
- Sample size (N)

**Correlation tables**: Include means, SDs, and bivariate correlations with significance indicators.

### Mediation/Moderation [PRACTICE ONLY]

- Report **bootstrap confidence intervals** (10,000+ iterations standard)
- Use established macros (PROCESS, lavaan) and cite them
- Report indirect effects with 95% CI
- Present path coefficients for all pathways

## THEORETICAL EXPECTATIONS

### Contribution Requirements [PRACTICE ONLY]

ETP prioritizes manuscripts that:
1. **Advance theory**: Not just empirical novelty, but conceptual contribution
2. **Address "so what?"**: Clear implications for entrepreneurship research/practice
3. **Position clearly**: Explicit statement of how findings extend/challenge existing theory

**Example contribution framing** (Neff et al., 2026):
- "We contribute to [literature X] by..."
- "Our findings challenge the assumption that..."
- "This extends [theory Y] by integrating..."

### Theory Section [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Expected elements**:
- Review of relevant theoretical perspectives (Bourdieusian, institutional, psychological, etc.)
- Identification of gaps or tensions in existing research
- Development of conceptual framework
- Explicit linkage between theory and hypotheses

## REFERENCES & CITATIONS

### Style [ENFORCED]

**APA 7th Edition** throughout

**In-text**:
- (Author, Year)
- (Author1 & Author2, Year)
- (Author1 et al., Year) — for 3+ authors

**Reference list**:
- Alphabetical by first author surname
- Hanging indent
- DOI included for all sources with DOI
- Journal titles spelled out (not abbreviated)

### Reference Management [PRACTICE ONLY]

- Typical entrepreneurship manuscript: 60-100 references
- Heavy emphasis on:
  - ETP itself (journal self-citation common)
  - Other ABS 4* ENTRE/MGMT journals (AMJ, AMR, ASQ, SMJ, JBV)
  - Foundational theoretical works
  - Recent empirical studies (<5 years)

## APPENDICES & SUPPLEMENTARY MATERIALS

### Appendices [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Commonly included**:
- Additional robustness checks
- Alternative model specifications
- Supplementary analyses referenced in main text
- Extended descriptive statistics
- Survey instruments or interview protocols (if space allows)

**Format**: Label as Appendix A, Appendix B, etc.

### Online Supplements [GUIDELINE ONLY]

For materials too extensive for print appendices, authors may submit:
- Extended datasets
- Full survey instruments
- Additional robustness tests
- Multimedia materials

**Note**: Check with editor regarding online supplement policies.

## COMMON REJECTION REASONS (INFERRED)

Based on submission guidelines emphasis:

1. **Insufficient theoretical contribution**: Empirical novelty alone is insufficient
2. **Weak positioning**: Failure to clearly articulate gap and contribution
3. **Descriptive findings without explanation**: ETP seeks theory-testing or theory-building
4. **Poor fit**: Research not centrally about entrepreneurship phenomena
5. **Methodological concerns**: Weak research design, insufficient rigor
6. **Anonymization violations**: Any identifiable information triggers desk rejection
