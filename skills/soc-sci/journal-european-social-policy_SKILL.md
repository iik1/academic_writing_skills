# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of European Social Policy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE
- **Scope:** European social policy across welfare states, labour markets, ageing, family policy, migration, pensions, and poverty; welcomes cross-national comparative work, single-country studies with wider theoretical significance, and EU multi-level governance analyses.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/ESP
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Eick & Leruth, JESP 34(2) 2024, DOI 10.1177/09589287241240311; Laruffa & Nullmeier, JESP 2025, PMC11893266)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section sequence is mandated; guidelines ask only that heading levels be clear and sections well-defined. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Length
- **Research articles:** 6,000–10,000 words excluding references. The guidelines specify a soft floor ("normally longer than 6,000 words") as well as a ceiling.
- **Forum contributions:** normally up to 6,000 words.
- **Thematic Review Essays:** normally up to 6,000 words.
- **European Briefing Papers:** normally up to 4,000 words.
- **Tables and figures:** each counted as 350 word-equivalents against the total; authors must reduce prose commensurately. Label: ENFORCED.

### Abstract
Unstructured. Guidelines specify that it should state purpose, major findings, and conclusions; no explicit word limit is stated. In practice, published abstracts run approximately 100–150 words. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY (format stated; word limit not stated).

### Citations
Sage Harvard style required throughout. Parenthetical author-date format in text (Author, Year); reference list alphabetical by surname with full publication details. Label: ENFORCED.

### Headings
No specific rules beyond clarity of levels. Observed practice (both articles): bold for main section headings, italic for sub-headings within sections. Label: PRACTICE ONLY.

### Tables and Figures
Each table or figure reduces the available word budget by 350 words. No other format rules specific to this journal identified. Label: ENFORCED (word-count rule).

### Keywords
Minimum 5 keywords required, listed after the abstract. Label: ENFORCED.

### Optional Plain Language Summary
Guidelines offer an optional plain language title (~50 words) and plain language summary (~300 words) for lay audiences. Not required; not standard in observed articles. Label: GUIDELINE ONLY.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None identified beyond the standard scope restriction: purely non-European or non-comparative work without broader theoretical significance is out of scope.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only list dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**End-matter conventions (PRACTICE ONLY):** Both observed articles include standardised end-matter sections in this order: Declaration of Conflicting Interests → Funding → ORCID iDs → References. One article (qualitative) also includes an Author Biographies section before References. These are published as discrete, labelled sections—more formally delimited than is common across all SOC SCI outlets. Authors should prepare these sections before submission.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**European scope requirement (ENFORCED by scope policy):** Single-country studies are accepted only if they address issues of wider theoretical or comparative significance. The field baseline does not impose a geographic scope restriction. JESP reviewers will assess whether a single-country paper earns its place by speaking to European or cross-national questions.

**Word floor on research articles (ENFORCED):** The 6,000-word minimum is unusual. FIELD_SKILL does not specify minima. Very short empirical papers (e.g., under 6,000 words) are directed toward the Forum or Briefing sections rather than the main article stream.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding word budget without accounting for figures/tables.** The 350-word deduction per figure or table is strictly applied; a paper with five tables effectively has a 8,250-word prose ceiling, not 10,000.
2. **Submitting to the wrong article type.** Short empirical notes (under ~6,000 words) belong in the Forum or European Briefing sections, not the main article stream; misrouting delays peer review.
3. **Insufficient European framing.** A single-country study that does not explicitly connect to comparative welfare state debates or EU-level dynamics will be flagged by reviewers as out of scope regardless of methodological quality.
4. **Missing Sage Harvard compliance.** The journal enforces Sage Harvard; Chicago author-date or APA formatting will require full reformatting at revision stage.
