# Journal Writing Skill: Academy of Management Annals

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MGMT
- **Publisher:** Taylor & Francis (for Academy of Management)
- **Scope:** Publishes integrative reviews of research advances in management; "reviews with an attitude" that challenge assumptions, motivate conceptual integration, and set research agendas
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aom.org/publications/journals/publishing-with-aom/author-and-reviewer-resources/author-resources/submitting-to-annals/
- **Field baseline:** mgmt-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Krlev, Hannigan, & Spicer, 2025, DOI: 10.5465/annals.2021.0051)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
**ENFORCED**: Annals publishes ONLY integrative review articles (no empirical studies, no pure theory). Typical structure observed: Introduction → METHODS (describing review scope and process) → FINDINGS or RESULTS (synthesis) → DISCUSSION → CONCLUSION. Section headings often use caps with descriptive subtitles (e.g., "METHODS: Review Scope, Relevance, and Process"). Future research directions may appear in Discussion or as standalone section—varies by article.

### Length
**PRACTICE ONLY**: Approximately 50 pages typical (some articles shorter; example analyzed was 28 pages of main text). No hard maximum stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED**: Maximum 200 words. Unstructured format standard. Must state what the review does (not just what it covers), its contribution, and implications.

### Citations
**FIELD DEFAULT**: APA style (standard for all AOM journals).

### Headings
**PRACTICE ONLY**: ALL CAPS for major sections with optional descriptive subtitle after colon. Example: "FINDINGS: Understanding Review Directions and Review Purposes."

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Extensive use of synthesis tables common in review articles.

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED**: No empirical data collection studies. No purely theoretical/conceptual work without literature review foundation. The sine qua non is a "thoughtfully bounded, comprehensive, and systematic review of a stream of management research."

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Submission Process: PROPOSAL-FIRST Requirement
**ENFORCED (MAJOR DELTA)**

**Field norm**: Authors submit complete manuscripts directly.

**Annals requirement**: Authors must first submit a PROPOSAL describing their focus, review process, and intended contributions. Only if the proposal is accepted are authors invited to submit a full manuscript. Invitation to submit does not guarantee acceptance.

**Proposal specifications**:
- Maximum 5 pages, single-spaced
- 12-point font, 1-inch margins
- Double-blind peer review by Associate Editors
- Deadlines: April 1 and October 1 (twice yearly)
- Submission via ManuscriptCentral: https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/aomannals

**Why**: Annals seeks to guide authors in developing high-quality integrative reviews that serve specific functions for their fields.

**How to apply**: Before drafting a full review, prepare a focused proposal that (1) identifies the research stream, (2) explains why an integrative review would advance the scholarly conversation, (3) describes the review process, and (4) articulates intended contributions. Consult editors' published guidance on what makes a good review (e.g., Rousseau, 2024; Krlev et al., 2025).

### CV Submission Required
**ENFORCED**

**Field norm**: No CV submitted with manuscript.

**Annals requirement**: All authors must submit current CV with proposal. CVs reviewed only by editors, not by peer reviewers. Author qualifications to provide authoritative review are relevant to editorial decision.

**Why**: The journal assesses whether authors have the expertise to provide a definitive review of the proposed area.

**How to apply**: Include comprehensive CV showing relevant publications and expertise in the proposed review area.

---

### "Reviews With an Attitude" Philosophy
**ENFORCED**

**Field norm**: Reviews synthesize and describe existing knowledge.

**Annals expectation**: Reviews should "move above and beyond descriptions of the field" by:
- Challenging established assumptions and concepts
- Pinpointing problems and factual errors
- Inspiring discussions
- Illuminating avenues for further study
- Motivating conceptual integration
- Setting agendas for future research

Reviews are explicitly framed as "reviews with an attitude" [sic]—having clear purpose and direction relative to the field being reviewed.

**Why**: Annals seeks reviews that actively reshape knowledge infrastructure, not just catalog it.

**How to apply**: Reviews should have reflexive character—specifying what you do WITH existing knowledge, not just what knowledge exists. Identify contested issues, methodological gaps, or conceptual puzzles and take a position. The review should chart a new direction for the field (reshaping, renovating, expanding, or ordering the knowledge base).

---

### Review Methodology Standards
**ENFORCED** (tightening of FIELD_SKILL expectation)

**Field norm**: Review process should be described; systematic methods preferred but not required.

**Annals requirement**: Review must use "systematic methods to reduce bias and create trustworthy knowledge." Must include:
- Clearly stated review scope and relevance
- Explicit search strategy and inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Transparent analytical process
- Documentation of each step

**Why**: Reviews are themselves a form of research requiring methodological rigor.

**How to apply**: Dedicate a substantial METHODS section to documenting review process. Explain how papers were identified, selected, and analyzed. Address potential biases. Make process reproducible. See Rousseau (2024) editorial "Reviews as Research" for detailed guidance.

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural standard)
- Citation density: FIELD DEFAULT (dense in intro/background; discussion situates findings)
- Statistical reporting: NOT APPLICABLE (no empirical data)
- Structural templates: FIELD DEFAULT (review structure aligns with integrative review template in FIELD_SKILL)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting full manuscript without proposal acceptance**: Annals ONLY accepts invited manuscripts. Authors who skip the proposal stage will be desk-rejected.

2. **Descriptive cataloging instead of critical synthesis**: Simply summarizing what exists in a literature is insufficient. Reviews must challenge, integrate, or reshape the field. Lack of "attitude" (critical perspective or directional positioning) is a common rejection reason.

3. **Weak methods section**: Failing to document search strategy, inclusion criteria, and analytical process rigorously. Annals expects review methods to meet standards of trustworthiness comparable to empirical research.

4. **Ignoring field status**: Reviews that "blindly follow a single idealized model or procedure" rather than fitting approach to the current state of the field. Authors should diagnose whether their field needs ordering (scattered knowledge), expanding (moving beyond boundaries), renovating (challenging stagnation), or reshaping (methodological innovation).
