# Journal Writing Skill: Politics and Society

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications
- **Scope:** Established in the late 1960s as an explicitly critical, alternative voice in the social sciences. Publishes original analyses of politics, its social roots, and consequences — spanning theoretical essays, historical investigations, philosophical reflections, and empirical research from multiple disciplines.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://pas-journal.org/pages/faq.php (also https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/PAS)
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Vogel, "Market Governance as a Balance of Power," *Politics & Society* 51(3): 319–336, 2023; DOI: 10.1177/00323292231183834)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section sequence is prescribed. The journal publishes theoretical essays, historical investigations, and empirical research; no IMRaD template is imposed. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for qualitative and theoretical work.

### Length
**12,000 words maximum**, excluding notes, reference lists, and appendixes. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured abstract on the title page. No word limit specified in guidelines. No required subfields (background, methods, results). The title page must also include author name, institution, and email. [ENFORCED — minimal spec]

### Citations
**Chicago endnote style (Chicago Manual of Style, 17th ed.)**: numbered superscript references in text linked to endnotes placed at the end of the manuscript (not footnotes at the bottom of pages). Endnotes are double-spaced. This is a **hard deviation from the SOC SCI field norm** of author-date citation. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Not specified in guidelines. Practice articles use substantive thematic headings rather than generic labels (e.g., not "Literature Review" but a label describing the argument). Follows field norm for qualitative and theoretical work.

### Tables and Figures
Color figures appear online only; authors must pay for color in print or provide grayscale alternatives with revised captions that do not depend on color. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Simultaneous submission: manuscripts under review elsewhere will not be considered.
- Manuscripts that significantly overlap with a forthcoming or existing publication will not be considered.
- Manuscripts without page numbers will be returned without review.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** Author-date (parenthetical) is the dominant convention across SOC SCI journals.
**This journal:** Chicago numbered endnote style throughout. Superscript numbers in text; full citations in endnotes at manuscript end; no in-text "(Author Year)" references. This is the single most consequential submission difference: a manuscript formatted in author-date style will need complete reformatting.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Structural Orientation
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL acknowledges that qualitative and theoretical work uses thematic sections; quantitative work typically follows IMRaD.
**This journal:** The journal's explicit positioning as a critical, multi-disciplinary outlet — accepting theoretical essays, historical investigations, and philosophical reflections alongside empirical work — means that IMRaD structure is rarely appropriate. Even mixed-methods empirical articles (e.g., Etchemendy & Lodola 2024) are framed theoretically from the outset, with theory-building sections prominent before any methods or data discussion. Purely conventional quantitative articles without strong theoretical framing are rare and likely face higher resistance.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (consistent with FIELD_SKILL's own caveat for qualitative/theoretical work, but more uniformly applied here)

### Language Explicitness
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL specifies high formality and precise, economical prose but does not include an explicit language standard.
**This journal:** The journal homepage and submission materials explicitly state that the journal "emphasizes the use of lucid English in its articles." This is a stated editorial preference for clear, accessible prose — not dense jargon. For authors writing in critical theory traditions with heavy technical vocabulary, this signal warrants attention.
**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY (stated but not enforced via manuscript rejection criteria)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting in author-date format.** The journal uses Chicago endnote style. Manuscripts using "(Author Year)" in-text citations are not formatted to journal standard.
2. **Exceeding the word limit before notes and references.** The 12,000-word cap applies to the main text only; notes and reference lists are excluded, but heavy use of endnotes to compensate for main-text length is visible and discouraged.
3. **Missing page numbers.** Manuscripts without page numbers are returned without review — an unusual hard enforcement that catches authors used to journals that accept unnumbered drafts.
4. **Submitting after a deadline.** The journal operates on three annual editorial board meeting cycles (deadlines: August 30, December 30, April 30). Submissions received after a deadline wait for the next cycle; the effective turnaround extends beyond the stated four-month maximum.
