# Journal Writing Skill: Review of Accounting Studies

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Springer
- **Scope:** Empirical, analytical, and theoretical research in financial accounting, managerial accounting, auditing, and taxation. Emphasis on archival and experimental designs.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://link.springer.com/journal/11142/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1007/s11142-025-09882-8; 10.1007/s11142-024-09828-6)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section order prescribed in guidelines.

### Length
No word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; **150-word maximum**. [ENFORCED — guidelines specify; confirmed in published articles]

**Keywords:** 4–6 keywords required as a separate field. [ENFORCED]

**JEL codes:** Required as a separate field. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date in-text, no comma between author name and year: "(Dye 1993)", "(Lennox and Li 2012)", "(Gul et al. 2013)". Multiple citations in one parenthetical separated by semicolons. Reference list: all authors listed up to 20; for groups larger than 20, list first 19, ellipsis, then final author (APA 7 convention). [ENFORCED — guidelines specify author-count rule explicitly; in-text format confirmed in articles]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Articles use Arabic numerals with decimal subsections (2.1, 3.1, 5.3.1).

### Tables and Figures
- Table title appears **above** the table, bold, sequentially numbered: "Table 1 Sample Selection." Notes appear below. Significance notation: \*\*\*, \*\*, \* at 1%, 5%, 10% levels, two-tailed. [ENFORCED — confirmed in articles]
- Figure captions use Springer bold label format: **"Fig. 1"** followed by caption text. [ENFORCED — Springer house style]
- In-paper appendix figures continue main-text numbering (no "A1, A2" labels for in-paper appendices). Online/supplementary appendix figures numbered separately. [ENFORCED]

### Submission Requirements
- **Double-blind review.** Manuscript must be fully anonymized: no author names, affiliations, acknowledgments, or identifying information anywhere in the manuscript body. [ENFORCED]
- **Separate title page** required, containing title, author names, affiliations, corresponding author contact, and all acknowledgments. Acknowledgments must not appear in the manuscript body. [ENFORCED]
- **AI/LLM use must be documented** in the Methods section (or equivalent if no Methods section exists). [ENFORCED]
- **Submission fee: USD 500** required at submission. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Acknowledgments in the manuscript body (must be on title page only).
- Submission without a complete set of editable source files.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Coefficient and p-value or t-statistic reported in prose or parenthetically; no single table layout prescribed.

**This journal (both articles):** Regression tables consistently place the coefficient estimate in the table cell and the t-statistic in parentheses on the row directly below — not p-values, not standard errors. Economic magnitude (as % of unconditional mean or standard deviation) is narrated in prose for every main result. [PRACTICE ONLY — observed in both articles; not stated in guidelines]

### Structural Deviations
**Untabulated robustness:** Both articles extensively cite "untabulated tests" or "results in the online appendix" to document robustness checks not shown in the paper. Online appendix tables are lettered (Table A1, A2, …) and kept separate from numbered in-paper appendices. [PRACTICE ONLY — neither convention is stated in guidelines; both are consistent across articles]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract over 150 words.** The 150-word cap is firm and shorter than most authors expect for a top accounting journal. Cut aggressively; do not carry methodology detail into the abstract.

2. **Acknowledgments in the manuscript body.** Double-blind policy requires all acknowledgments on the title page. Leaving a thanks footnote or author note in the manuscript body is grounds for desk rejection without review.

3. **Missing JEL codes or too few keywords.** Both are required fields; omitting either is a submission error, not a minor formatting issue.

4. **Undocumented AI/LLM use.** The journal explicitly requires disclosure in the Methods section. Treating LLM use as a private drafting aid without disclosure violates stated policy.
