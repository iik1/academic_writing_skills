# Journal Writing Skill: Business Strategy and the Environment

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** ERP Environment and John Wiley & Sons Ltd.
- **Scope:** Corporate environmental management, sustainability strategy, ESG, eco-innovation, green finance, and circular economy. Covers quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-methods work examining how businesses address environmental challenges.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/10990836/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (DOI: 10.1002/bse.4310; DOI: 10.1002/bse.70307)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section sequence. Mandatory terminal sections (beyond references): **Conflicts of Interest** statement required; **Author Contributions** (CRediT taxonomy) strongly expected; **Acknowledgements** where applicable. Supporting Information appendices permitted.

### Length
**8,000 words maximum, excluding references.** This is a firm cap for standard research articles. Systematic reviews observed at greater length; contact editors for review articles.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED.** Unstructured (no labeled fields such as Purpose/Design/Findings). Maximum **150 words**. Bold "ABSTRACT" heading. **No citations permitted in the abstract.** The abstract appears as a shaded box in the published layout.

### Citations
**ENFORCED (observed format).** Guidelines describe "APA citation style," but published articles consistently use Chicago author-date formatting:

- **In-text:** Author Year without comma — `(Richardson et al. 2023)`, `(Broman and Robèrt 2017)`. Multiple citations separated by semicolons.
- **Reference list:** `Last, F., and F. Last. Year. "Article Title." *Journal Name* Volume, no. Issue: Pages. https://doi.org/...` Uses "and" (not "&"), year follows author names without parentheses, issue number preceded by "no.", article titles in quotation marks, journal names italicised.

This diverges from standard APA. Follow the observed Chicago author-date format, not the APA template cited in the guidelines.

### Keywords
**ENFORCED.** Keywords listed immediately after the abstract, introduced by bold **Keywords:** label. Items separated by vertical pipes (`|`). Typically 5–7 keywords. Example: `sustainability | ESG | circular economy | corporate strategy`.

### Headings
**PRACTICE ONLY.** Both articles use a consistent numbered pipe format: `**N | Section Name**` for main sections, `**N.N | Subsection Name**` for subsections, `**N.N.N | Sub-subsection Name**` for third-level headings. All heading numbers bold. This three-level numbered system is strongly observed across articles.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables and figures carry primary evidential burden and require prose narration directing readers to specific elements. Captions follow `FIGURE N |` or `TABLE N |` format (pipe separator, consistent with heading style).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No citations in the abstract.
- Manuscripts must not exceed 8,000 words (excluding references) without editorial agreement.
- AI tool use must be disclosed in the Methods or Acknowledgements section if any portion of the manuscript was developed with AI assistance.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**Field norm:** Multi-authored quantitative and mixed-methods work uses first-person plural "we" throughout.

**This journal:** Both "we" and third-person "this study" are active in published work. Some multi-authored articles (e.g., bse.4310, four authors) use "this study" as the primary agent throughout the paper ("This study applies…", "this study combined…", "this study investigated…"). Others (e.g., bse.70307, four authors) use "we" for active research moves ("we conducted", "we define", "we identify") while also using "this study" for summary framing. Both constructions are accepted; "this study" appears more prominently than in the ABS 4 baseline journals.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (RELAXATION at ABS 3 level — the field norm of "we" is not enforced; "this study" is an accepted alternative or supplement).

### Citation Density

Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Introduction and literature sections carry dense citations; methods moderate; results low; discussion moderate. No delta observed.

### Statistical Reporting

Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for quantitative work. Qualitative and mixed-methods work integrates analysis into findings narratives. No systematic delta observed at two articles.

### Structural Deviations

**Section numbering convention:** The `N | Section Name` heading format (number followed by pipe separator and bold name) is a journal-specific typographic convention not found in the ABS 4 baseline. All three heading levels follow this pattern. Adopt this format when preparing camera-ready or formatted submissions.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (typographic convention observed in all checked articles; not stated in guidelines).

**Explicit contribution enumeration:** Both articles state contributions as numbered or lettered lists within the introduction (e.g., "the contribution of this study is threefold: (i)… (ii)… (iii)…"; "our scholarly contributions are twofold"). This enumerative contribution statement is prominent in the introduction, consistent with the quantitative political science sub-norm in FIELD_SKILL but notable as consistent practice here regardless of methodology.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Mandatory Conflicts of Interest statement:** Present in all checked articles, explicitly declared even when no conflict exists ("The authors declare no conflicts of interest/interests"). This is a required terminal section.

**Classification:** ENFORCED.

**CRediT Author Contributions:** Detailed CRediT taxonomy statements (e.g., "conceptualisation, formal analysis, funding acquisition, investigation, methodology…") appear as a named terminal section. Expected by the publisher.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (observed in Article 1; not visible in Article 2, suggesting variable enforcement — follow Wiley submission system requirements).

**AI disclosure:** Guidelines explicitly require disclosure of AI tool use in Methods or Acknowledgements. Not verifiable from articles (neither disclosed AI use).

**Classification:** GUIDELINE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Abstract length:** The 150-word cap is strict. Abstracts that exceed this or that include citations will require revision; the abstract box in the layout is visually prominent and readers notice over-length.

2. **Citation format mismatch:** Submitting with standard APA parenthetical years `(Smith, 2023)` instead of the journal's Chicago author-date style `(Smith 2023)` requires reformatting at revision. Use the no-comma format from the outset.

3. **Exceeding the word limit:** At 8,000 words excluding references, this cap is tighter than many ABS 4 outlets. Detailed literature reviews and extensive robustness sections will need to be condensed or moved to supplementary materials.

4. **Omitting the Conflicts of Interest statement:** This terminal section is mandatory and must be present even when there are no conflicts to declare. Omitting it is a straightforward desk-rejection trigger at Wiley.
