# Journal Writing Skill: Ecological Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Transdisciplinary journal integrating ecological and economic theory; covers ecosystem valuation, sustainability transitions, environmental policy, and integrated ecological-economic modelling. Explicitly "transdisciplinary in spirit and methodologically open."
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/ecological-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (Li & Wang 2026, DOI 10.1016/j.ecolecon.2026.109016; pii/S0921800925002204; pii/S0921800924003501)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandatory section sequence is specified. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for internal ordering discretion.

### Length
Strict per-category word caps — **ENFORCED**:
- **Analysis** (standard research article): max 8,000 words
- **Commentary**: max 5,000 words or 12 double-spaced pages
- **Surveys**: max 8,000 words or 25 double-spaced pages
- **Methodological and Ideological Options**: max 8,000 words or 25 double-spaced pages
- **News and Views**: max 1,500 words (letters within this category: max 600 words)
- **Book Reviews**: max 1,200 words or 4 double-spaced pages

Selecting the correct submission category is mandatory; each category has a distinct editorial purpose. Most empirical research papers submit as **Analysis**.

### Abstract
Unstructured plain prose — no labeled subsections (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions). Observed length: ~100–200 words. No word limit stated in guidelines, but practice aligns with Elsevier standard of 150–250 words. **PRACTICE ONLY** (length); **GUIDELINE ONLY** (format: guidelines reference abstract without specifying structure).

### Citations
Author-year format confirmed in articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific numbering convention mandated.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for content. Elsevier production standards apply for resolution and file format.

### Highlights
A **Highlights** box is required — standard Elsevier format: 3–5 bullet points, each ≤85 characters including spaces, summarising the core findings. Observed in practice (4 bullets in one article). **ENFORCED.**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Generative AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- AI may not substitute for human critical thinking, expertise, or evaluation in the research or writing process (use must be declared).
- Letters to the editor must not refer to personalities; focus on ideas only.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** First-person plural ("we find", "we show", "we estimate") is near-universal; even sole-authored papers use "we."

**This journal:** Impersonal third-person constructions are common in practice. Two of three articles spot-checked used "This study demonstrates…", "The results reveal…", "This study aims to illuminate…" rather than "we." One article used collective framing ("Our findings reveal…"). The natural science co-tradition within this interdisciplinary journal normalises impersonal voice alongside "we."

**Classification:** RELAXATION (ABS 3 context; both registers are accepted; impersonal voice is not penalised here as it would be at ABS 4/4* econ journals).

**Practical implication:** Either "we" or impersonal third-person is acceptable. Authors coming from economics should not feel compelled to switch; authors from ecology or environmental science need not adopt "we." Avoid mixing both registers within a single paper.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Standard empirical structure: Introduction → Data → Empirical Strategy → Results → Robustness → Conclusion.

**This journal:** No single structure dominates. Interdisciplinary papers frequently include methods sections that cover ecological modelling, biophysical accounting, scenario analysis, or qualitative frameworks alongside or instead of econometric identification. A standalone **Conceptual Framework** or **Theoretical Background** section appears frequently. Pure identification-strategy subsections (as required at ABS 4* ECON) are absent in many papers.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Wrong submission category.** Submitting a standard empirical paper as "Survey" or "Commentary" triggers desk rejection. Research articles go under **Analysis** (8,000-word cap).
2. **Missing Highlights box.** Elsevier's required 3–5 bullet Highlights (≤85 chars each) is not part of FIELD_SKILL and is easily omitted by authors from pure economics backgrounds.
3. **Over-narrowing the framing.** The journal explicitly values transdisciplinary scope. A paper framed purely as a labour or IO economics contribution, without ecological or sustainability relevance, is likely to be seen as out-of-scope regardless of technical quality.
4. **Assuming identification-centred framing is required.** Unlike ABS 4/4* ECON outlets, this journal does not require a causal identification strategy. Well-designed descriptive, accounting, or model-based analyses are acceptable; forcing causal language onto non-experimental work is a style mismatch.
