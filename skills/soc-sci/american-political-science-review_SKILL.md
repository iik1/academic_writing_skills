# Journal Writing Skill: American Political Science Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press (on behalf of American Political Science Association)
- **Scope:** The flagship journal of the APSA, publishing empirical, theoretical, and methodological research across all subfields of political science.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://apsanet.org/publications/journals/american-political-science-review/apsr-submission-guidelines/ and https://apsanet.org/publications/journals/american-political-science-review/apsr-manuscript-preparation-guidelines/
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Myers 2025 "Press Coverage and Accountability in State Legislatures," doi:10.1017/S000305542500022X; Pérez, Lee, and Luna 2025 "Partisans of Color: Asian American and Latino Party ID in an Era of Racialization and Polarization," doi:10.1017/S0003055424001217)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Five named publication tracks, each with its own structural norms:
1. **Research Articles** — standard empirical or theoretical articles; no rigid section prescription beyond field norms
2. **Research Notes** — shorter focused contributions; same structure as Research Articles but tighter
3. **Synthesis** — review + original analysis; submitted as a prospectus or full manuscript; annual October 1 deadline only
4. **Registered Reports** — pre-analysis plan reviewed and accepted before data collection; journal commits to publish if plan is faithfully executed
5. **Replications and Reappraisals** — reevaluation of APSR or influential work; no fixed structure required

Manuscript order for submission: (1) title page with abstract, (2) anonymized manuscript text, (3) footnotes if not embedded, (4) reference list, (5) tables and figures (separate pages, numbered), (6) appendix/supplementary material.

**ENFORCED**

### Length
- Research Articles: no hard cap; editorial expectation is under 11,000 words; most submissions accepted are under 12,000 words
- Research Notes: ≤7,000 words at publication (excluding references and online appendices)
- Synthesis: <11,000 words at publication (soft target; excluding references and online appendices)
- Replications and Reappraisals: no hard cap; journal encourages under 6,000 words
- All manuscripts: word count must appear on front page

**ENFORCED**

### Abstract
Unstructured prose; ≤150 words; must cover research question, methods, and findings/conclusions. No labeled fields. Kept separate from word count calculations.

**ENFORCED**

### Citations
APSA author-date style: (Author Year) or (Author Year, Pages) — no comma between author and year. Multiple sources separated by semicolons. Footnotes are used for substantive commentary only, never for simple citations. If accepted, the APSR production team reformats references for publication, so authors need only be internally consistent at submission.

**ENFORCED**

### Headings
Short, meaningful, parallel in structure and tone. Headline-style capitalisation (all words capitalised except prepositions, conjunctions, articles). No notes appended to headings. Title ≤12 words (combined with subtitle). Hierarchical differentiation required but no prescribed label set.

**GUIDELINE ONLY** (observed in published articles but heading labels vary freely)

### Tables and Figures
Numbered consecutively; each must be referenced in the text; placed on separate pages in submission; titled. No specific formatting template prescribed beyond APSA style.

**ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No endnotes at submission (footnotes only; may convert post-acceptance)
- No boldface or underlining; use italics instead
- Do not append notes to section headings
- Manuscripts must be fully anonymised for blind review (author names and identifying affiliations removed from main text)

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Preregistration Norm — Tightened

**Field norm:** Preregistration "increasingly valued" in quantitative causal inference work.

**APSR:** Preregistration "generally expected" for experiments and for any hypothesis-testing study with a prospective design; the journal implemented a formal Registered Reports track in October 2024 precisely to institutionalise this. Authors are asked to indicate which analyses were preregistered.

**Classification: ENFORCED** (stated in guidelines and reflected in the Registered Reports track)

### Theory and Hypotheses Section Label — APSR Pattern

**Field norm:** Formal hypothesis section is common in quantitative political science but not prescribed.

**APSR practice:** Quantitative articles consistently carry a dedicated section labelled "Conceptualization, Theory, and Hypotheses" (or close variant) that contains explicitly numbered hypotheses (H1, H2, …). This label and the pairing of conceptualisation with hypotheses in a single section is characteristic of the journal.

**Classification: PRACTICE ONLY**

### Multi-Study Experimental Structure

**Field norm:** Standard structure (Data and Methods / Results) assumed.

**APSR practice:** Experimental multi-study papers use numbered study labels ("Studies 1 and 2: …") as top-level section headings rather than splitting into generic Methods and Results sections. Each study contains its own design, analysis, and results subsections.

**Classification: PRACTICE ONLY**

### Handling Editor Attribution

Not a writing norm, but a structural convention invisible to authors at submission: published articles list the name of the handling editor at the foot of the first page. Authors need not do anything; this is added in production.

**Classification: PRACTICE ONLY**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Wrong citation format.** The APSA author-date style (Author Year) with no comma is non-negotiable. Using footnote citations or Chicago notes-bibliography format is a straightforward error that signals unfamiliarity with the journal.

2. **Wrong track selection.** Desk rejections occur when a Research Note is submitted as a Research Article or vice versa, or when a Synthesis is submitted outside the October window. Choose the track deliberately and justify it in the cover letter.

3. **Abstract over 150 words.** The 150-word cap is strict and checked at submission. Abstracts that run long are returned before review.

4. **Missing replication materials or unregistered experiments.** APSR requires replication data/code for all quantitative work and expects preregistration for experiments. Submitting without these, or without noting which analyses were preregistered, is a known submission gap.
