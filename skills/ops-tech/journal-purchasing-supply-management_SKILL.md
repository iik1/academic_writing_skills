# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Purchasing and Supply Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (Pergamon)
- **Scope:** Empirical research on the acquisition of external resources—materials, services, and knowledge—by organisations across sectors; papers must contribute to testing or developing purchasing and supply management (PSM) theory.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-purchasing-and-supply-management/publish/guide-for-authors

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standard empirical section sequence observed in practice: Introduction → Literature Review → Methodology → Results → Discussion → Conclusion. A separate Highlights section (bulleted) is required before the abstract body.

### Length
Not publicly accessible at time of review. Check the guide for authors directly before submission.

### Abstract
Unstructured (plain paragraph). No structured labels (Purpose/Design/Findings) observed in spot-checked articles. Word limit not retrievable from guidelines at time of review. [PRACTICE ONLY — confirmed unstructured format in articles; word limit unconfirmed]

### Citations
Author-date format (Harvard/APA-adjacent), confirmed in spot-checked articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Highlights
A dedicated "Highlights" section consisting of 3–5 bullet points summarising key contributions is required as a standard Elsevier submission element. Appears before the abstract. [ENFORCED — standard for all Elsevier journals, confirmed in both spot-checked articles]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- **Mathematical modelling papers without strong empirical grounding are not accepted.** The journal describes itself as "largely empirically focused." Pure analytical or formal modelling papers — regardless of methodological sophistication — are desk-rejected unless they are grounded in and tested against empirical data. [ENFORCED — stated explicitly in guidelines and consistent with empirical content of spot-checked articles]
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Double-anonymised review: author-identifying information must be removed from manuscripts.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Analytical modelling papers (Model Setup, Analysis, Managerial Insights, Conclusion) are an accepted paper type across the OPS&TECH field.

Conceptual papers must be grounded in and contribute to empirical PSM research.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting modelling-only work.** The journal's explicit prohibition on mathematical modelling papers without empirical grounding is a firm desk-rejection trigger. If your paper is primarily analytical or formal, it belongs elsewhere (e.g., IJOPM or POM modelling tracks).
2. **Scope mismatch.** Research must focus specifically on purchasing, procurement, or supply management as the focal phenomenon. General supply chain, operations, or logistics papers that do not centre on the acquisition of external resources fall outside scope.
3. **Missing Highlights.** Omitting the Elsevier Highlights section (3–5 key takeaway bullets) is an immediate formatting deficiency; it is a required submission element and is not the same as the abstract.
4. **Theoretical-only positioning.** Even theory-development contributions are expected to connect to empirical evidence; purely conceptual papers that do not engage with empirical PSM research are at risk of desk rejection given the journal's stated empirical focus.
