## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
**ENFORCED** - All Research Articles must follow this exact section order:
1. Abstract (structured)
2. Introduction
3. Results
4. Discussion
5. Materials and Methods (placed AFTER Discussion, not before Results)
6. References and Notes
7. Acknowledgments

**KEY DEVIATION:** Science uses "methods-last" structure. Materials and Methods appears AFTER Discussion, often in smaller font. This differs from standard IMRAD format used by most journals.

### Length
**ENFORCED:**
- Main text: maximum 3000 words
- Abstract: 125 words or less
- Display items: 3-5 figures or tables with brief legends
- References: approximately 50 main-text references
- Figure legends: maximum 200 words per legend

### Abstract
**ENFORCED** - Structured abstract with four components in order:
1. **BACKGROUND:** One sentence for general field introduction (comprehensible to non-specialists), then one sentence of specific background for the study
2. **OBJECTIVES/METHODS:** Explanation of why research was done and how
3. **RESULTS:** What was found
4. **CONCLUSIONS:** Main implications in terms comprehensible to all readers

**CRITICAL:** 125-word maximum. No citations or abbreviations allowed in abstract.

### Citations
**ENFORCED** - Numbered citation style with complete bibliographic information:
- In-text: Italicized numbers in parentheses: (1), (2, 3), or (4-6) for ranges
- Reference list: Numbered in order of first citation through text, then tables, figures, reference notes, acknowledgments, and supplementary materials
- Format: All authors listed (do NOT use et al.), full article title, journal abbreviation, volume, inclusive pagination, year
- Example: "E. J. Neer, T. Kozasa, Sites for Gα binding on the G protein β subunit overlap with sites for regulation of phospholipase Cb and adenylyl cyclase. J. Biol. Chem. 273, 16265-16272 (1998)."

### Headings
**ENFORCED:**
- Up to three levels of subheadings permitted
- Level 1: Bold
- Level 2: Bold italic
- Level 3: Italic
- Each heading separated by line break
- Subheadings should be descriptive clauses, not full sentences

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED:**
- 3-5 display items maximum
- Figure legends: double-spaced, numerical order, included in text file
- Figure title: at beginning of legend, NOT in figure itself
- Legend maximum: 200 words
- Table captions: First sentence is brief descriptive title with no citations
- Table footnotes: Use symbols in order (*, †, ‡, §, ǁ, ¶, #, **, ††), not in column heads
- Nomenclature, abbreviations, units must match text
- Figure panels containing data must be mentioned in Results before Materials and Methods

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED:**
- No paragraph breaks in title, author list, or abstract
- No citations in abstract
- No abbreviations in abstract
- No et al. in reference lists - list all authors
- No incomplete citations - must include full titles and inclusive pagination

### Voice and Register
**GUIDELINE ONLY** - Science traditionally advises avoiding first person ("we", "I"). Guideline states: "avoid using the first person in your writing, as Science succeeds in spite of human beings, not because of us, so you want to make it look like your results magically discovered themselves."

**CLASSIFICATION:** GUIDELINE ONLY - This represents traditional guidance, though contemporary scientific publishing trends increasingly favor first-person active voice for clarity. Without article spot-checks, cannot confirm actual practice.

### Statistical Reporting
**FIELD DEFAULT** - No journal-specific statistical reporting requirements found in guidelines beyond general scientific standards. Guidelines do not specify p-value formatting, confidence interval requirements, or effect size reporting beyond standard scientific practice.

### Citation Density
**ENFORCED** - Approximately 50 main-text references recommended for Research Articles. This is a relatively tight constraint given the 3000-word limit (ratio ~60 words per reference).

### Structural Deviations
**ENFORCED - MAJOR DEVIATION:**
1. **Methods-last structure:** Materials and Methods section placed after Discussion, typically in smaller font
2. **Structured abstract:** Four-component format (Background, Objectives/Methods, Results, Conclusions) with strict organization
3. **Single combined References and Notes section:** Not separate sections
4. **No Methods in supplementary materials:** Materials and Methods must provide sufficient replication detail but appears at end of main article

### Manuscript Assembly
**ENFORCED:**
- Preferred format: Single .docx file containing all figures and tables embedded
- Assemble as: Title (no paragraph breaks), Authors (initials first, separated by spaces; family names in full; separated by commas not "and"), Affiliations, Abstract, Main Text, References and Notes, Acknowledgments, then figures with captions
- Submit both .docx and complete PDF

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Methods placement error:** Placing Materials and Methods before Results (standard IMRAD) instead of after Discussion (Science requirement)

2. **Abstract violations:** Exceeding 125 words, including citations, using abbreviations, or failing to structure with Background/Objectives/Results/Conclusions flow

3. **Incomplete references:** Using et al. instead of listing all authors, or providing incomplete pagination

4. **Overly long legends:** Exceeding 200-word limit for figure legends instead of moving detail to Materials and Methods

5. **Word count miscalculation:** Counting abstract or Methods in the 3000-word main text limit (limit applies to Introduction + Results + Discussion only)
