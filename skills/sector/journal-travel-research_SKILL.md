# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Travel Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** Tourism, Transport and Hospitality
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publications
- **Scope:** Premier peer-reviewed journal covering travel and tourism development, management, marketing, economics, and behaviour; founded 1961.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/JTR (PDF mirror: https://www.sagepub.com/docs/default-source/msg/submission-guidelines_-journal-of-travel-research_-sage-journals.pdf)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section labels or ordering beyond the field baseline are specified in the guidelines.

### Length
**10,000 words maximum.** Excludes: title, abstract, keywords, tables, figures, and reference list. There is no minimum. This cap is strict; the guidelines cite page constraints explicitly. — **ENFORCED**

### Abstract
Unstructured continuous paragraph. No specific word limit stated in the guidelines. Must concisely state the purpose, major findings, and conclusions. — **ENFORCED** (consistent with field norm but made explicit by guidelines)

### Keywords
Minimum 4–6 keywords, listed immediately after the abstract. Keywords should be as specific as possible to the research topic. — **ENFORCED**

### Citations
APA (author–year) in-text, APA-formatted reference list. Every in-text citation must have a corresponding reference list entry and vice versa. **References must include a DOI hyperlink; if no DOI exists, include the publisher's URL; if no publisher URL, use an archival URL.** — **ENFORCED**

### Headings
Heading levels must be clear and sections clearly defined. No specific heading template is mandated beyond clarity. Follows APA heading conventions in practice. — follows field norm; no additional constraint.

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures are placed on separate pages at the end of the manuscript file, or uploaded as separate files. Copyright permission required for reproduced material. — **ENFORCED** (placement detail not covered in FIELD_SKILL)

### Submission Format
Word is preferred; LaTeX is also accepted (SAGE LaTeX template available). Submission via ScholarOne Manuscripts (https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jotr). — informational.

### Double-Anonymized Review
Two-file submission is required: (1) a title page containing author names, affiliations, and acknowledgements; (2) the anonymized manuscript containing title, abstract, body, and references — with all author-identifying information removed. — **ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
No explicit writing-style prohibitions beyond standard publication ethics (COPE compliance, no simultaneous submission, no self-plagiarism). The guidelines do not prohibit specific section structures or rhetorical conventions.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### References: DOI Requirement
**Field norm:** APA author–year format; no explicit DOI policy stated in FIELD_SKILL.
**This journal:** Every reference must include a DOI hyperlink. When no DOI is available, a URL to the publisher's page is required; when neither exists, an archival URL must be provided. This makes DOI inclusion an unconditional submission requirement, not a best practice.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Misreading the word limit scope.** The 10,000-word cap excludes abstract, keywords, tables, figures, and references. Authors who count the whole file may under-cut a draft that is well within limits, or — more dangerously — assume tables and an extensive reference list do not count against the cap when they do not, and inadvertently bloat the body.

2. **Missing DOI links in references.** The DOI requirement is unconditional: every citable item with a DOI must carry a hyperlinked DOI, and items without a DOI require a publisher or archival URL. Reference lists assembled in reference managers that omit DOIs will fail the technical check.

3. **Inadequate anonymization.** The two-file system is strictly enforced. Self-citations, institutional affiliations embedded in the body, acknowledgements left in the manuscript file, or file metadata containing author names are common causes of desk rejection or return before review.

4. **Too few keywords.** The minimum is 4; 6 is the stated upper end of the target range. Fewer than 4 keywords violates the guideline; keywords that are too broad (e.g., "tourism", "behaviour") reduce discoverability but are not an editorial violation.
