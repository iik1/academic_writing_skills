# Journal Writing Skill: Development and Change

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell (on behalf of the International Institute of Social Studies, The Hague)
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary peer-reviewed journal devoted to critical analysis of development issues, receiving articles from all social sciences and intellectual persuasions; empirical, theoretical, and historical articles all welcome; explicitly invites unconventional and challenging work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.iss.nl/sites/corporate/files/information_for_contributors.pdf
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section order specified. Flexible across empirical, theoretical, and historical articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for qualitative and critical theoretical work.

### Length
**8,000–10,000 words total**, counting text, abstract, notes, and references. Editors reserve the right to reject manuscripts outside this range; exceptions require justification. Tables and figures are not excluded—their page extent is weighed against the word budget, so figure-heavy papers must have shorter prose. Authors must supply a word count with submission. ENFORCED.

### Abstract
Unstructured, **100–200 words**. No required fields or section labels. ENFORCED.

### Keywords
**2–8 keywords** required for all submissions. ENFORCED.

### Citations
Author/date (Harvard) system. Specific rules that differ from generic Harvard:

- **In-text format**: `(Brown, 1992: 63–4)`; `Lovell (1989, 1993)`. Colon (not comma) before page numbers.
- **Groups of citations**: ordered **alphabetically**, not chronologically: `(Brown, 1992; Gadgil and Guha, 1994; Lovell, 1989)`. ENFORCED.
- **'et al.'**: used for 3+ authors in-text; list all authors in the References unless 6 or more.
- **Reference list title formatting**: italics only for book titles and journal names. Single quotation marks `' '` for article titles, chapter titles, dissertations, working papers, and unpublished material. Title Case for all English titles. ENFORCED.
- **Non-English titles**: provide English translation in parentheses immediately after. ENFORCED.
- **Insufficient sources** (interviews, some web/media sources): cite in footnotes, not the reference list. ENFORCED.
- **Citation selectivity**: Guidelines explicitly caution against long citation lists, especially references to whole books or articles. Authors are instructed to be selective and to prefer specific page references. This is a RELAXATION of the FIELD_SKILL norm of high citation density in introductions and literature reviews. GUIDELINE ONLY (practice not verified from full-text access).

### Headings
Three-tier hierarchy, explicitly specified:
- **Level A**: ALL CAPS BOLD
- **Level B**: Bold
- **Level C**: *Italic*

**Numbered headings are prohibited.** Keep headings short and clear; avoid excessive sub-heading levels. ENFORCED.

### Tables and Figures
- Artwork (figures, graphs, maps) must be submitted **separately**, not embedded in the text; preferred formats: TIFF, EDS, or PDF. ENFORCED.
- Heading placed **above** each table/figure: *Table 1. Asset Ownership by Household Category* (italic, title case).
- Notes and sources placed **below** each table/figure.
- Table/figure extent counts toward overall word limit; figure-heavy manuscripts must compensate with shorter prose. ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Endnotes: prohibited. Use footnotes only (superscript numbers). ENFORCED.
- Numbered headings: prohibited. ENFORCED.
- Embedded artwork: figures must be submitted as separate files. ENFORCED.
- Simultaneous submission: manuscripts must be original and not under consideration elsewhere. ENFORCED.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Density
**FIELD_SKILL norm**: high citation density in introductions and literature reviews; dense citation clusters are common.

**This journal**: Guidelines explicitly warn that "long lists of citations in the text are not always helpful, especially if referring to whole books or articles." Authors are told to be selective and to use page-specific references where possible. This is a RELAXATION of the field norm. Full-text access was blocked, so observed practice could not be confirmed. Classification: RELAXATION / GUIDELINE ONLY.

### Reference Title Formatting
**FIELD_SKILL norm**: does not specify title formatting within the reference list.

**This journal**: Enforces a specific convention distinguishing book titles (italics) from article and chapter titles (single quotation marks). This is a concrete formatting requirement that authors from other social science journals may overlook. Classification: ENFORCED.

### In-text Citation Order
**FIELD_SKILL norm**: does not specify ordering within citation groups.

**This journal**: Citation groups must be alphabetical, not chronological. Classification: ENFORCED.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding the word budget with references and notes**: The 8,000–10,000 word cap includes abstract, footnotes, and references. Authors accustomed to ABS 4 journals with separate or uncapped reference lists routinely exceed this limit.

2. **Dense citation clusters in the introduction**: The guidelines explicitly flag this as a problem. Packing the introduction with long lists of whole-book citations is counter to the journal's stated style, even though it is standard practice at the ABS 4 tier of the field.

3. **Numbered headings**: Prohibited by the guidelines. Authors from journals that require or permit numbered sections must strip numbering before submission.

4. **Endnotes**: The journal requires footnotes. Manuscripts formatted with endnotes must be reformatted before submission.

5. **Embedded figures**: Artwork must be submitted as separate TIFF/EDS/PDF files, not inserted into the Word document.
