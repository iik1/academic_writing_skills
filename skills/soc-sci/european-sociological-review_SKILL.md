# Journal Writing Skill: European Sociological Review

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press
- **Scope:** Empirical and theoretical sociology with a European focus; quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-methods work published.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/esr/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (https://doi.org/10.1093/esr/jcaf007; https://doi.org/10.1093/esr/jcaf016)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Separate title page (affiliations, acknowledgements, funding) submitted alongside anonymous main manuscript. Author biography (50–100 words per author) required at submission. Supplementary materials submitted as a single PDF. ENFORCED.

### Length
Approximately 8,000 words including endnotes and references but excluding tables and figures. Longer submissions accepted only if authors justify the additional length in the cover letter. ENFORCED.

### Abstract
Unstructured; maximum 200 words. No required sub-fields. ENFORCED.

### Citations
Author-date parenthetical style with a comma between author and year: (Gans, 1962). Reference list alphabetically ordered. Standard OUP sociology format. ENFORCED.

### Headings
Maximum two levels of subheadings permitted. ENFORCED.

### Tables and Figures
Format-free at initial submission (inline acceptable). On acceptance: separate high-resolution files required (300 dpi colour/halftone; 1,200 dpi line art; TIF, EPS, PS, or JPG). All figures require alt text submitted directly beneath the figure legend. ENFORCED.

### Replication and Data
For statistical or computational work: replication package (code, data where feasible, README, codebooks) deposited upon conditional acceptance (effective January 2025). Data Availability Statement required for all submissions (effective September 2022). Qualitative researchers exempt from replication package requirement. ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No simultaneous submissions.
- Self-citations must appear in full (do not redact as "Author A"); however, self-citations should be used sparingly and citations to own unpublished work are discouraged. GUIDELINE ONLY.
- Blind review: all reviewer-visible documents must be fully anonymous, including supplementary files.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL treats Data and Methods as a single combined section ("Data and Methods or equivalent").  
**This journal:** Both articles split these into separate sections. Article 1 uses "Data" and "Analytical Strategy" as distinct sections; Article 2 uses "Graphical Model and Methods" and "Data" separately. Separating Data and Methods is the observed practice at ESR. PRACTICE ONLY.

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL labels the methods section generically as "Data and Methods."  
**This journal:** "Analytical Strategy" is a common label for the methods/design section in quantitative work, signalling identification logic rather than just procedural description. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Notes Convention
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify footnotes vs. endnotes.  
**This journal:** Notes must be formatted as endnotes (numbered continuously, printed at end of manuscript). Footnotes are not used. ENFORCED.

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL expects effect sizes, confidence intervals, and substantive magnitudes; no specific format prescribed.  
**This journal:** Confirmed practice leans toward bootstrap confidence intervals and avoidance of bare p-values; point estimates are paired with interval estimates in figures and text. Aligns with but explicitly enacts the stronger end of the FIELD_SKILL norm. PRACTICE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Exceeding 8,000 words without justification.** The word count includes endnotes and references but not tables and figures; authors who forget to include references in the count will exceed the limit on final check.
2. **More than two heading levels.** ESR explicitly caps subheadings at two levels; nested three-level structures common in longer manuscripts must be flattened.
3. **Missing replication package.** As of January 2025, conditional acceptance for quantitative/computational work triggers a mandatory replication deposit. Preparing code and documentation only after acceptance decision is too late.
4. **Redacting self-citations.** Unlike some blind-review journals, ESR requires full self-citations in the manuscript; use third-person framing ("Smith (2020) shows…") to preserve anonymity rather than replacing citations with "Author."
