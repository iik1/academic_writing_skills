# Journal Writing Skill: Review of Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press (on behalf of the European Finance Association)
- **Scope:** Empirical, theoretical, and structural research across all areas of financial economics; official journal of the European Finance Association.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/rof/pages/General_Instructions
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Ghosh & Otsu, "Subjective Beliefs Estimators and Their Properties," forthcoming RoF, working paper dated Oct 2025, via https://sticerd.lse.ac.uk/dps/em/em643.pdf)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No required section order stated in guidelines.

### Length
**60-page maximum, all-inclusive.** The 60-page cap covers the main text, appendices, bibliography, figures, and tables combined. Internet Appendix material is explicitly exempt from this count. Papers should be "as concise as possible" — the guidelines frame the 60-page limit as a ceiling, not a target. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph; **maximum 150 words.** [ENFORCED] This is more restrictive than the FIELD_SKILL baseline (150–250 words); stay at the tight end. JEL codes and keywords appear as separate fields on the published page, not inside the abstract body.

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (author-year parenthetical). No journal-specific citation style stated in guidelines.

### Headings
Not specified in guidelines. See Observed Deltas below.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific rules stated in guidelines.

### Submission
- PDF only. Minimum 12-point font, 1.5-line spacing, 1-inch side margins, 1.5-inch top/bottom margins, first page numbered as page 1. [ENFORCED]
- Submitted through Editorial Express (not ScholarOne). No cover letter required.
- A "Fast-Track" option guarantees an editorial decision within 14 days for a higher fee.
- Full anonymisation required: no author names, institutional affiliations, or identifying characteristics anywhere in the manuscript.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Identifying information of any kind in the manuscript (enforces double-blind review).
- Exceeding 60 pages (inclusive of all components except the Internet Appendix).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Section heading numbering — Roman numerals for top-level sections.**

FIELD_SKILL norm: Arabic numerals throughout (1, 2, 3, 2.1, 2.2), with Roman numerals described as rare.

RoF practice: Top-level sections use Roman numerals (I, II, III, IV, V, VI). Subsections use a Roman-Arabic decimal format (IV.1, IV.2, IV.2.1). The roadmap in the introduction references sections by Roman numeral ("Section II describes… Section III presents… Section VI concludes"). This pattern was consistent throughout the spot-checked article and is not an idiosyncratic author choice — the roadmap sentence and all cross-references use this convention.

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY (observed in article; not stated in guidelines). Note: evidence is from a pre-publication working paper; OUP typesetting may alter heading style in the final published version, but the numbering scheme used is consistent with expectations for this journal.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding 150 words in the abstract.** The 150-word cap is tighter than most ABS 4*/4 journals (which run to 200–250 words). Drafts written to field norms will routinely exceed it.

2. **Exceeding 60 pages.** The all-in page cap is strict and includes tables, figures, appendices, and the bibliography. Robustness material should be routed to an Internet Appendix (which is exempt) rather than a numbered appendix within the document.

3. **Using Arabic numerals for top-level section headings.** If the Roman-numeral convention holds in published form, submitting with Arabic-numbered sections (e.g., "1. Introduction") may be inconsistent with house style.

4. **Submitting through the wrong platform.** RoF uses Editorial Express, not ScholarOne or Manuscript Central. The submission is incomplete until a final confirmation step is completed post-payment.
