# Journal Writing Skill: Accounting, Organizations and Society

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary journal on the relationships among accounting, human behaviour, organizational and institutional structures, and the broader socio-political environment. Explicitly welcomes diverse methodologies drawn from across the social sciences — quantitative, experimental, qualitative, interpretive, critical, and historical.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/accounting-organizations-and-society/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (10.1016/j.aos.2025.101631 — "Being dishonest to feel better", quantitative experimental; 10.1016/j.aos.2025.101607 — "Accounting and post-colonial resistance", qualitative/interpretive)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm, with the following additions:
- A **Highlights** section is required (Elsevier submission requirement). [ENFORCED]
- A separate **title page** is required to support double-anonymous review. [ENFORCED]
- A **Generative AI use declaration** is required, placed immediately before the References section, whenever AI tools were used in manuscript preparation. If not used, no statement is needed. [ENFORCED]
- A **Data availability statement** is required. [ENFORCED]

### Length
No explicit word or page limit identified in the guidelines. Length norms in practice appear consistent with the field baseline (typical archival/experimental articles run 10,000–15,000 words including references; interpretive AOS articles may run somewhat longer given the need for extended theoretical framing and fieldwork presentation).

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No word limit specified in accessible guidelines. Articles spot-checked show abstracts of approximately 130–280 words. [GUIDELINE ONLY — word limit not confirmed; unstructured format ENFORCED]

### Citations
Elsevier Harvard author-date style. In-text format uses a comma between author and year: "Smith, 2020" (parenthetical) or "Smith (2020)" (narrative). Multiple authors separated by semicolons in parenthetical. Examples observed: "Latour, 2005a"; "Barry, 2013a". Reference list uses author-date format ordered alphabetically. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-mandated numbering scheme observed. Section headings in title case without section numbers are the observed practice in interpretive papers; quantitative papers in this journal may number sections — practice varies by author preference rather than a strict house rule.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Standard Elsevier formatting rules apply.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. [ENFORCED]
- Concurrent submission to another journal is prohibited. [ENFORCED]
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance; any pre-acceptance change requires a formal Authorship Change Request form. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Highlights section:** FIELD_SKILL does not mention a Highlights requirement. AOS requires a Highlights section — a set of short bullet points (Elsevier standard: 3–5 bullets, maximum 85 characters each including spaces) that capture the novel results and conclusions of the study. Highlights appear in the published article above the abstract and are submitted as a separate element. Classification: **ENFORCED**

**Double-anonymous review:** Manuscripts must be prepared for double-blind review. The title page (containing author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, and funding) must be submitted as a separate file from the anonymized manuscript. Self-identifying citations in the manuscript body must be anonymized. Classification: **ENFORCED** — this is a structural preparation requirement not specified in FIELD_SKILL.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Methodological breadth:** While FIELD_SKILL notes the AOS qualitative/interpretive tradition, AOS's scope is materially broader than any other ABS 4* accounting journal. It routinely publishes experimental, archival, qualitative, interpretive, critical, and historical work in the same issues. This is not merely tolerance for heterodoxy — it is the journal's defining identity. Manuscripts should not be positioned primarily as contributions to "accounting research" narrowly; positioning toward organizational, sociological, or institutional theory literatures is common and expected for qualitative submissions. Classification: **PRACTICE ONLY** (scope is stated in aims and confirmed in articles but framing implications are not explicit in guidelines).

**Inclusive language:** The guidelines explicitly require inclusive language throughout — avoiding descriptors that imply superiority based on age, gender, race, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, or disability unless directly relevant to the research. Classification: **ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting the Highlights section.** This is a required Elsevier submission element. Submitting without Highlights will generate a desk rejection or revision request before peer review.

2. **Failing to anonymize properly for double-blind review.** Author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, and funding disclosures must be removed from the manuscript body and placed on a separate title page. Self-citations must be anonymized.

3. **Positioning an interpretive or qualitative paper narrowly within accounting.** AOS reviewers for qualitative work expect engagement with social theory, institutional theory, or critical perspectives. A paper that presents organizational fieldwork without connecting to broader social-science theory will be seen as underselling its contribution to this journal's agenda.

4. **Treating the journal as a quantitative-only outlet and underselling scope.** The reverse error: authors positioning a carefully executed archival study as if AOS is primarily a theory-heavy interpretive venue. AOS publishes high-quality quantitative and experimental work; these papers need no special theoretical justification beyond the standard accounting research contribution framing — but the contribution statement should acknowledge the interdisciplinary readership.
