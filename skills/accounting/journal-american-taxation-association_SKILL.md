# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of the American Taxation Association

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** American Accounting Association (AAA)
- **Scope:** Tax research using quantitative, analytical, experimental, and descriptive methods; includes archival, behavioral, and legal-analytical approaches to taxation.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://aaahq.org/Research/Journals/The-Journal-of-the-American-Taxation-Association/Guide-for-Authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Xia 2025 forthcoming [JATA conference version]; Francis, Hasan, Wu, and Yan 2014, JATA 36(2): 171–202)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section ordering. Standard observed sequence: Introduction → Background and Hypothesis Development → Research Design and Sample Selection → Results → Robustness Tests → [Exploratory Analysis / Discussion, if present] → Conclusion → References → Appendices → Tables → Figures.

### Length
**Maximum 55 pages total**, including all references, tables, figures, and appendices. 12-point Times New Roman, double-spaced, 1-inch margins. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose; **maximum 150 words**; placed on a separate page before the body. **Personal pronouns are prohibited** ("I" and "we" must not appear in the abstract). Required content: research question, methodology, and principal findings. If generative AI tools were used in manuscript preparation, an **AI disclosure statement** (tool names, extent and reason for use) must immediately follow the abstract. [ENFORCED — abstract pronoun prohibition confirmed in Xia 2025; AI disclosure is guidelines-only]

### Citations
Style authority is **The Chicago Manual of Style, 16th edition**. Author-date format throughout. Key specifics that go beyond the FIELD_SKILL description:
- Up to five co-authors: all surnames spelled out in every in-text citation (e.g., `Scholes, Wolfson, Erickson, Maydew, and Shevlin 2008`).
- Six or more authors: first author et al. (e.g., `Dikolli et al. 2013`).
- Reference list uses **initials**, not full first names (e.g., `Austin, C. R., and R. J. Wilson.`).
- Article titles in the reference list appear in **quotation marks** (not italics); journal names and book titles in **italics**.
- DOIs or URLs required when available.
- **Tax law sources follow specialist citation format:** Internal Revenue Code citations as `I.R.C. § [section]`; Treasury Regulations as `Treas. Reg. § [section]`; court cases with reporter, volume, and year. This format is unique to tax journals and absent from FIELD_SKILL.
- No ibid or op. cit.
[ENFORCED]

### Headings
Level 1: centered, bold, **ALL CAPS**, numbered with **Roman numerals** (e.g., `I. INTRODUCTION`, `II. BACKGROUND AND HYPOTHESIS DEVELOPMENT`). Level 2: flush left, bold, title case. Level 3: flush left, bold, italic, title case. [ENFORCED — confirmed in Xia 2025]

### Tables and Figures
Arabic numbering. Every table/figure must be referenced in the text. Resolution: 600 dpi for line art, 264 dpi grayscale for halftones. Alt-text required for all images. In text: spell out "percent"; in tables: use "%" symbol. Equations numbered in parentheses flush right; no mathematical notation in footnotes. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Personal pronouns in abstracts.
- AI tools listed as co-authors.
- Simultaneous submission elsewhere.
- No notation (mathematical or citation) in footnotes — footnotes are for textual asides only.
- No retracted article citations without exceptional justification noted in a footnote.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** coefficient + t-statistic or p-value in prose; economic significance increasingly expected.
**JATA practice:** Confirms this norm, and adds an observed convention of reporting the **upper bound of the 95% confidence interval** expressed as a percentage of the variable's mean and standard deviation when discussing a null or inconclusive main result. This is used to bound the economic magnitude of effects that are statistically insignificant rather than simply reporting insignificance — observed in Xia (2025) with explicit citation to a methods framework for this practice. [PRACTICE ONLY — RELAXATION relative to field norm on p-value sufficiency; reflects evolving statistical transparency norms]

Regression tables include a **predicted direction column** ("+", "−", "+/−") alongside coefficient estimates. [PRACTICE ONLY — not stated in guidelines but present in both spot-checked articles]

### Structural Deviations
An **Exploratory Analysis** subsection or standalone section (distinct from Robustness) is used for results that extend beyond the main hypothesis test — e.g., policy implications or secondary outcomes without full hypothesis development. This section is labeled explicitly and follows the Robustness section. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Hypothesis labeling:** Hypotheses are labeled `H:` or `Hypothesis:` (not `H1:`, `H2:`). Single-hypothesis papers state one labeled hypothesis; multi-hypothesis papers may use `H1:`, `H2:` for ordering. Null-form hypotheses are common in archival work when no clear directional prediction exists. [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Keywords and JEL codes:** Both are required and appear after the abstract, before the body text. FIELD_SKILL notes these exist but does not flag them as required. [ENFORCED]

**ORCID IDs:** Required for the submitting author; recommended for co-authors. [ENFORCED — guidelines only]

**One-page plain-language summary:** Required upon acceptance (separate Word document; author names included; no mathematical notation or new citations). [GUIDELINE ONLY — submission-stage requirement, not verifiable from articles]

**Submission fee:** USD 150 (AAA members) / USD 200 (non-members), non-refundable. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Personal pronouns in the abstract.** The prohibition on "I" and "we" in abstracts is explicitly stated and represents a departure from body-text norms where "we"/"I" are expected. Authors who draft the abstract last from body text often carry pronouns over.
2. **Tax law citations formatted as standard academic references.** IRC sections, Treasury Regulations, and court cases require specialist legal citation format (e.g., `I.R.C. § 482`), not author-date. Omitting this or citing tax law in author-date format is a marker of unfamiliarity with the journal.
3. **Exceeding 55 pages.** The limit covers the entire submission including all back matter. Papers that comply in the body but append large variable-definition tables or extensive robustness appendices frequently exceed the limit.
4. **Missing AI disclosure statement.** Any use of generative AI in drafting, editing, coding, or analysis requires a disclosure immediately after the abstract. Omitting it when AI was used violates current submission policy.
