## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Guidelines require the title and abstract to appear on the first page. No further mandatory section sequence is specified; authors organise sections according to paper type. FIELD_SKILL norms apply for section ordering by paper type.

### Length
**ENFORCED.** Maximum 32 pages in the submitted format (1.5 spacing, 11pt font, one-inch margins). This page cap is all-inclusive: it covers the abstract, all tables and figures, all appendices, and the reference list. E-companion materials may exceed this limit and are published online only if the paper is accepted; the e-companion must be submitted as a separate file clearly designated as such. Title maximum: 25 words.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED.** Unstructured, maximum 350 words. The guidelines describe the abstract as a "mini-paper" that summarises "important contents of the paper" — not a roadmap or section-by-section preview. Formulas, references, and abbreviations are prohibited in the abstract. One to six keywords must follow the abstract.

### Citations
**ENFORCED.** In-text citations use parenthetical author-year format. The reference list is ordered alphabetically by first author's surname and follows a POM-specific format that differs from APA in three respects:

1. Only the first author is name-inverted (Last, F. M.); subsequent authors are written in normal order (F. M. Last), with no ampersand.
2. The year of publication follows the author list as a bare numeral with a preceding period — not in parentheses.
3. A space separates the volume number from the issue number in parentheses: `5 (1)`, not `5(1)`.

Example:
> Hayes, R. H., G. P. Pisano. 1996. Manufacturing strategy: At the intersection of two paradigm shifts. *Production and Operations Management*, 5 (1), 25–41.

A LaTeX template conforming to this format is available from the journal.

### Headings
Not specified in guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Guidelines require authors to supply "quality artwork" for accepted figures. Submission format rules otherwise not specified beyond the all-inclusive 32-page limit. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on placement and discussion.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Simultaneous submission to any other journal during POM review is prohibited.
- Formulas, references, and abbreviations are prohibited in the abstract.
- Plagiarism and self-plagiarism are prohibited; authors must certify that results are "substantially new."
- **Resubmission of any previously rejected manuscript is prohibited across all POM departments** — a rejected paper may not be resubmitted to the same department or to a different department. This is explicitly enforced and must be disclosed in the cover letter.
- Data fabrication or falsification is prohibited; authors must certify data integrity.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Citation Format
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes in-text citation as author-date (APA-adjacent) format.

**POM practice:** The reference list format is not APA-adjacent. Only the first author is inverted; subsequent authors are listed in normal order without an ampersand. The year is a bare numeral (not parenthesised). Volume and issue numbers are separated by a space before the parenthesis. Authors must follow the journal's exact format or use the provided LaTeX template.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### E-Companion Policy
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that appendices and online supplementary material exist as a category but does not specify journal policy.

**POM practice:** The e-companion is a formally defined submission component with a distinct file, no page limit, and conditional online-only publication. All robustness tables, supplementary analyses, and extended proofs that do not fit in 32 pages should be directed here. The e-companion must be clearly labelled as such.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (guidelines only; full-text articles not confirmed)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **All-inclusive page count.** The 32-page cap includes references, tables, figures, and appendices in the main manuscript. Authors accustomed to limits that exclude these elements routinely exceed the cap. Move non-essential robustness tables and extended proofs to the e-companion before submission.

2. **Non-standard reference format.** POM's reference style is not APA and is not produced by default by most reference managers. Subsequent authors are not inverted, there is no ampersand, and the year is bare (no parentheses). Verify manually or use the journal's LaTeX template.

3. **Abstract scope creep.** The guideline specifies a "mini-paper" abstract, not a roadmap. Formulas, references, and abbreviations are explicitly prohibited. Structured abstracts (with labelled fields) are not used at POM.

4. **Resubmission prohibition.** POM prohibits resubmitting a rejected manuscript to any department. This must be disclosed in the cover letter. Authors who have had a previous version rejected anywhere in the journal — including a different department — cannot resubmit without violating policy.
