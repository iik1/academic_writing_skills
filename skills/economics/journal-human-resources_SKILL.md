# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Human Resources

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** University of Wisconsin Press
- **Scope:** Empirical microeconomics; covers labor economics, development economics, health economics, and economics of education. Explicitly excludes HR management, staff management, and personnel research.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://jhr.uwpress.org/content/authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (Cascio 2023, JHR 58(1):1–42; Aurino et al. 2023, JHR 58(1):74–111; Oka & Yamada 2023, JHR 58(1):335–362)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No required section sequence mandated in guidelines.

### Length
**40 pages maximum**, including references, tables, and figures, at 12-point font, 1.5-line spacing, 1-inch margins. Double-spaced submissions may reach 50 pages. Supplementary online appendices are permitted but must not be excessive. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured in practice; no labeled fields. No word limit stated in guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm in function: quantified main findings announced directly. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical format. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Roman numerals observed consistently across all spot-checked articles (I, II, III…). FIELD_SKILL permits either Roman or Arabic; Roman appears to be the strong practice norm here. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables numbered sequentially; figures integrated in text; captions include variable definitions, clustering, and sample details.

### Mandatory Disclosures
Two requirements not standard in FIELD_SKILL:

1. **Replication policy**: Authors must make data and programs available to other researchers, or request an exemption at initial submission with written justification. [ENFORCED]

2. **Author disclosure statement**: Each author must submit a statement covering funding sources, potential conflicts of interest, and IRB approval status. Required at initial submission. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
The journal explicitly states it is **not a business management journal** and does not consider HR, staff management, or personnel research. Empirical economic methodology is required. Submissions outside this scope are desk-rejected. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** First-person plural ("we") is near-universal, including sole-authored papers.

**JHR practice:** Sole-authored papers use first-person singular ("I find," "I address," "my analysis"). Multi-authored papers use "we" as expected. This is a relaxation of the field norm permitted at ABS 3 level.

**Classification:** RELAXATION / PRACTICE ONLY (observed in Cascio 2023; not addressed in guidelines).

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Out-of-scope submission**: Any paper framing itself as HR management, organizational behavior, or personnel management will be desk-rejected. The paper must be positioned within empirical microeconomics.
2. **Length**: A 40-page hard cap including references, tables, and figures is strict. Material exceeding this must be pushed to an online appendix, which itself must not be excessive.
3. **Missing disclosure or replication materials**: Failure to submit the author disclosure statement or to commit to the replication policy (or request an exemption) at initial submission is grounds for rejection before review.
4. **Using "we" in a sole-authored paper**: Practice at JHR favors "I" for single authors. Using "we" in a solo-authored submission is not prohibited but runs against observed journal practice.
