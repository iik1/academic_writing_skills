# Journal Writing Skill: Economic Theory

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Springer, on behalf of the Society for the Advancement of Economic Theory (SAET)
- **Scope:** Publishes research across all areas of economics grounded in rigorous theoretical reasoning and mathematical analysis, including equilibrium theory, game theory, macroeconomics, public economics, and financial economics. Does not publish empirical papers.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://link.springer.com/journal/199/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Konishi, Sahuguet & Crutzen, *Economic Theory* 78:69–100, 2024; Pastrian, *Economic Theory* 80:1233–1265, 2025)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for theory paper structure. No required sections are mandated beyond what the paper type demands. Appendix containing proofs is standard and expected.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured abstract of **150 to 250 words** required. FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract length; this range is an additional hard constraint. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical in-text (e.g., "Holmstrom 1982", "Crutzen et al. 2020"). Consistent with FIELD_SKILL. Reference list follows Springer Basic author-date format. [ENFORCED — consistent with FIELD_SKILL]

### Headings
**Decimal system required, maximum three levels** (e.g., 1, 2, 3, 3.1, 3.2, 3.2.1). FIELD_SKILL permits either Roman or Arabic numerals without specifying decimal subsection notation. This journal's requirement for decimal notation is more prescriptive. [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated beyond standard research integrity requirements.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations

**Theory-only scope.** The journal publishes exclusively theoretical work. The empirical paper structure codified in FIELD_SKILL (Data, Empirical Strategy, Identification, Robustness sections) is entirely absent from this outlet. All submissions follow the theory/model paper structure from FIELD_SKILL. The identification strategy requirement, summary statistics tables, and robustness checks convention do not apply. [PRACTICE ONLY — scope-driven, not a relaxation of field norms]

**Formal mathematical environments.** Papers routinely deploy Assumption, Lemma, Proposition, Theorem, Remark, and Example environments as distinct numbered blocks, with proofs either in-line or collected in an appendix. FIELD_SKILL does not address these environments (they are specific to mathematical theory papers). Their use is expected and pervasive. [PRACTICE ONLY]

**"Concluding Remarks" as section title.** Both articles spot-checked use "Concluding Remarks" (not "Conclusion"). FIELD_SKILL already notes no standard naming convention exists; this is a frequent but not mandated variant. [PRACTICE ONLY]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Wrong heading format.** Using Roman numerals (I, II, III) or unnumbered section titles violates the journal's explicit decimal-system requirement. Subsections must be numbered 3.1, 3.2, not titled without numbers or labelled with letters.
2. **Abstract outside 150–250 words.** The stated range is a hard constraint; abstracts common in working paper circulation (often 100–150 words) are likely too short.
3. **Submitting empirical work.** The journal's scope is exclusively theoretical. Papers with reduced-form regressions, difference-in-differences designs, or structural estimation are outside scope regardless of quality.
4. **Omitting proofs or delegating them without an appendix.** Mathematical theory papers in this journal are expected to include full proofs, either in-line or in a clearly labelled appendix. Referring to proofs as "available on request" is not standard practice here.
