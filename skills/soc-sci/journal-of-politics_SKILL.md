# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Politics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** University of Chicago Press (for the Southern Political Science Association)
- **Scope:** Leading general-interest journal covering all subfields of political science — American politics, comparative politics, formal theory, international relations, methodology, political theory, public administration, and public policy. Emphasises theoretically innovative and methodologically diverse research.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/instruct; https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/style
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Dunaiski & Tukiainen, "Does Income Transparency Affect Support for Redistribution? Evidence from Finland's Tax Day," JOP Vol 87 No 3, 2025 — accessed as 2023 EconStor working paper version)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section order specified in guidelines. FIELD_SKILL norm applies for quantitative and qualitative work. One journal-specific note: articles must include a title and abstract page with (a) full title, (b) short title of no more than 50 characters, and (c) name and full contact information for every author.

### Length
- Regular articles: maximum 35 pages including all text, footnotes, references, tables, and figures. [ENFORCED]
- Short articles (a distinct submission category): maximum 10 pages including all text, footnotes, references, tables, and figures. [ENFORCED]
- Online appendix: maximum 25 pages, submitted as a separate file; does not count against the 35-page limit. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Single unstructured paragraph, maximum 150 words. Must describe the research question addressed, the analytical approach, and the major findings. No formal citations permitted in the abstract. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Author-date ("scientific") style throughout. References placed within parentheses in the text. Follows the APSA Style Manual for Political Science (2001/2006 revised edition) as primary authority, supplemented by The Chicago Manual of Style. Bibliography at end, ordered alphabetically, with each author's full name as given in the cited publication (not initials only, unless the cited work uses initials). [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Each section must be clearly labeled. Each major section begins on a new page. Observed practice: sections are numbered with Arabic numerals (e.g., 1, 2, 3, 4.1, 4.2). Guidelines do not further specify heading hierarchy rules.

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures must appear in the text, not at the end of the manuscript. Each table and each figure begins on a new page. Tables and figures should not exceed one page in length; exceptions are permitted for complex multipaneled figures. [ENFORCED for placement; GUIDELINE ONLY for one-page preference]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- No self-identification in the submitted manuscript (double-blind review): remove all author names, acknowledgments, expressions of gratitude, funding statements, and references to personal interactions with other scholars.
- No citations in the abstract.
- Manuscript length (35 pages) is an absolute cap, not a guideline.
- AI tool use must be disclosed in the text or footnotes; disclosure text must also be included in the author questionnaire at submission. Covers copyediting, drafting, code, proofs, and any other AI-assisted task. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes the Discussion and Conclusion as commonly either merged or separate, with no single dominant convention.

**JOP practice:** In quantitative empirical articles, the final section is typically titled "Discussion" and serves the combined discussion-and-conclusion function. No separate "Conclusion" section heading was observed. Within the Introduction, a labeled "Related literature" subsection is common, rather than a fully separate Literature Review or Theory section. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Footnote Usage
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify footnote versus endnote preference.

**JOP guideline:** Footnotes are explicitly preferred over endnotes. Notes should be used sparingly and kept as brief as possible. [GUIDELINE ONLY — in practice, quantitative articles can carry 30+ footnotes, used for methodological elaboration, data sources, definitional clarifications, and robustness pointers. The stated preference for sparingness is not strongly enforced in published work.]

### Replication and Data Transparency
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes pre-registration is increasingly valued but does not specify data deposit requirements.

**JOP requirement:** Authors of quantitative papers must deposit all data and replication files (including those for the online appendix) to the JOP's Harvard Dataverse. This is a condition of acceptance: required at the "Accept with Revisions" stage before final publication. Acceptance is also conditional on successful replication of empirical analysis. [ENFORCED]

## Dimensions With No Delta
- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural "we" throughout multi-authored quantitative work)
- Register: FIELD DEFAULT (uniformly high formality in quantitative and policy-oriented work)
- Hedging patterns: FIELD DEFAULT (moderate, calibrated to evidence strength)
- Citation density by section: FIELD DEFAULT (very high in introduction/literature review; low in results)
- Statistical reporting: FIELD DEFAULT (effect sizes, confidence intervals, substantive magnitudes expected alongside significance)
- Methods tense: FIELD DEFAULT (past tense)
- Separation of results and interpretation: FIELD DEFAULT (results sections report; discussion interprets)
- Sentence and paragraph style: FIELD DEFAULT (medium-to-long sentences; dense paragraphs in quantitative work)
- Argumentation (CARS model): FIELD DEFAULT

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Exceeding the 35-page all-in limit.** The limit covers everything — including references, tables, and figures. Authors who treat this as a text-only limit will be returned for revision.
2. **Abstract over 150 words or containing citations.** JOP enforces both constraints strictly; citations in the abstract are prohibited regardless of practice at other journals.
3. **Tables or figures placed at the end.** JOP explicitly requires tables and figures to appear in-text; submitting them at the end is a formatting rejection trigger.
4. **Omitting AI disclosure.** JOP requires affirmative disclosure of any AI tool use (including copyediting and code writing) in both the manuscript text/footnotes and the submission questionnaire. Failure to disclose when AI was used violates the journal's policy.
5. **Forgetting the short-title field.** The title page must include a short title of 50 characters or fewer, in addition to the full title and complete author contact information.
