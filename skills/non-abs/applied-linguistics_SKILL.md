## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are journal-specific requirements that override general field conventions.

### Structure
**GUIDELINE ONLY:** No mandatory section order prescribed. Articles must "foreground the real-world relevance of research" with identifiable applications in practice contexts.

**PRACTICE OBSERVED:** Standard IMRD+ structure dominates:
- Introduction
- Literature Review (or domain-specific variant: "Article abstracts and rhetorical moves")
- Theoretical Framework (when applicable, especially for Bourdieu, Vygotsky, or other theoretical lenses)
- Methods/Methodology
- Results / Analysis and Findings / Experimental Results and Analysis
- Discussion (often integrated with Results)
- Conclusion

### Length
**ENFORCED:**
- **Main manuscript:** Maximum 8,500 words including all material for print publication
- **Abstract:** Maximum 175 words
- **Biodata:** Approximately 90 words
- **Forum pieces:** Maximum 2,000 words (typically)
- **Book reviews:** Maximum 1,500 words (typically)

### Abstract
**ENFORCED:** 175-word maximum, unstructured (no labeled subsections).

**PRACTICE OBSERVED:** Abstracts follow narrative arc: context/gap → study design → key findings → contribution. No structured headings (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions) used in sampled articles.

### Citations
**ENFORCED:** Author-date system with Oxford HUMSOC style.

**Critical formatting rules:**
- **No comma between author name and date:** "Clark and Clark 1977: 57-85" (NOT "Clark and Clark, 1977")
- Multiple works listed in chronological order, separated by semicolons
- Page ranges follow colon after year: "1977: 57-85"
- **Anonymization required:** Author names in references must be replaced with "author" during submission to maintain double-blind review

**PRACTICE OBSERVED:** Very high citation density in introductions (6-8 citations per paragraph). Citations integrated syntactically rather than parenthetically clustered at sentence ends.

### Headings
**ENFORCED:** "No more than two levels of heading below the title. Headings should not be numbered."

**PRACTICE CONFIRMED:** All sampled articles use unnumbered, two-level maximum heading hierarchy.

### Tables and Figures
**ENFORCED:**
- Discourse data appears in text boxes and **counts toward total word limit**
- Figures/tables inserted at end or as separate files with clear numbering and captions
- **Alt text descriptions required** for all images, figures, illustrations, and photographs
- Alt text placed "directly under the figure legend"

**PRACTICE OBSERVED:** 
- Quantitative articles use extensive tables for statistical results (4+ tables common)
- Qualitative articles embed discourse excerpts as numbered examples within running text or formatted text boxes

### Language Requirements
**ENFORCED:**
- Articles must be written in English
- Authors must specify British or American English variant and maintain consistency
- Content must not include libelous or defamatory material

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED:** Papers will be rejected if they:
- Have "no clear connection with a real-world context"
- Offer "insights into a research methodology or theoretical construct, with minimal attention to real-world applications"
- Indicate applied implications "minimally, and only at the end"
- Are "not based on empirical research"

**Translation:** Every manuscript must demonstrate tangible relevance to language practices in education, workplace, healthcare, legal systems, or media. Purely theoretical or methodological papers are out of scope.

## Observed Writing Conventions

> NOTE: Without a generated NON-ABS field baseline, the following describes observed patterns rather than deviations from field norms.

### Voice and Register
**PRACTICE OBSERVED:**
- Formal academic register throughout
- Predominantly third-person voice
- Passive constructions common ("has been widely regarded," "are guided by," "were evaluated")
- First-person plural ("we") appears sparingly in methods and discussion; first-person singular absent
- Extensive hedging: "may be problematic," "is postulated," "it has been contended," "hardly considers"
- Citation-dense framing rather than personal positioning

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY (not explicitly mandated in guidelines)

### Statistical Reporting (Quantitative Studies)
**PRACTICE OBSERVED:**
- Full statistical notation with italicized test statistics: [*t*(68) = −0.26, *P* = .791 > .05]
- Explicit inequality symbols with alpha levels: *P* = .791 > .05
- Tables present multiple statistical measures (M, SD, t, df, P) in formatted columns
- Narrative interpretation accompanies each statistical test

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Qualitative Reporting
**PRACTICE OBSERVED:**
- Embedded vignettes as numbered discrete units (Vignette 1, Vignette 2)
- Thick description of classroom/contextual dynamics
- Translated data (Bangla → English) with explicit reflexive commentary about translation choices
- Discourse excerpts formatted as numbered examples or text boxes

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Citation Density
**PRACTICE OBSERVED:**
- Introduction sections: 6-8 citations per paragraph (very high density)
- Citations establish scholarly authority and position work within debates
- Theoretical framework sections: sustained engagement with single scholar's work (e.g., extended Bourdieu explication across multiple paragraphs)

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Real-World Application Framing
**ENFORCED + PRACTICE CONFIRMED:**
- All sampled articles foreground practical contexts in opening paragraphs
- Applications distributed throughout (not relegated to conclusion)
- Examples: L2 abstract writing pedagogy, ELT textbook adoption in Bangladesh classrooms, automated discourse analysis tools for applied linguists

**Label:** ENFORCED (guidelines explicitly require this; articles consistently demonstrate it)

### Theoretical Engagement
**PRACTICE OBSERVED:**
- Optional standalone "Theoretical Framework" section appears when using specific theoretical lens (Bourdieu, positive psychology models)
- Theory-first structure: sustained explication before empirical application
- Multiple theoretical models referenced (broaden-and-build theory, CVT, PERMA, EMPHATICS in psychology; Bourdieu's habitus/capital/field in sociology)

**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Relegating real-world applications to conclusion only:** Guidelines explicitly warn against indicating applied implications "minimally, and only at the end." Applications must be foregrounded and integrated throughout.

2. **Using numbered headings:** Guidelines state "Headings should not be numbered." Remove all section numbering (1., 1.1, etc.).

3. **Comma in author-date citations:** The Oxford HUMSOC style requires NO comma between author and date. Write "Smith 2020: 45" not "Smith, 2020: 45."

4. **Forgetting alt text for figures:** All images require alt text descriptions placed "directly under the figure legend." This is an accessibility requirement that will be checked.

5. **Submitting non-empirical papers:** The journal explicitly rejects papers "not based on empirical research." Purely theoretical or methodological pieces without data are out of scope.

6. **Exceeding word limits:** The 8,500-word limit includes discourse data in text boxes (which count toward total). Budget accordingly if including extensive qualitative excerpts.
