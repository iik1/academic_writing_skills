# Journal Writing Skill: Annals of Operations Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** Springer Nature
- **Scope:** Broad OR journal publishing theory, computation, and applications across all subfields of operations research; explicitly includes full-length research articles, short notes, surveys and expositions, computational studies, and case studies. A large share of content appears in thematic special issues.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://link.springer.com/journal/10479/submission-guidelines
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Zhu, "DEA as Data Enabled Analytics," AOR in press 2020; Fernández et al., arxiv:2107.03440, submitted to AOR 2021)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section list specified in guidelines.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured plain paragraph, **150–250 words**. Must not contain undefined abbreviations or unspecified references. [ENFORCED — confirmed in both articles as plain, non-sectioned paragraphs]

### Citations
**Author-year in parentheses, APA v7 format.** In-text: (Author Year) for single author, (Author and Author Year) for two, (Author et al. Year) for three or more; multiple references separated by semicolons. Reference list: Author, A. B. (Year). Title in sentence case. *Journal*, *volume*(issue), pages. [ENFORCED — confirmed in both articles]

### Headings
Numbered section headings throughout (e.g., "1. Introduction", "2.1. Sub-topic"). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm on substance; numbered format is standard Springer practice.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific rules stated beyond Springer standard.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Undefined abbreviations in the abstract
- Unspecified references in the abstract
- Footnotes that consist solely of a reference citation
- AI/LLM use for content generation without disclosure in the Methods section (copy-editing use exempted)

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Publication type breadth (RELAXATION)
**Field norm:** ABS 4*/4 outlets focus on full-length research contributions (theoretical, algorithmic, or empirical).
**This journal:** Explicitly accepts surveys and expositions, short notes, reports on computational studies, and case studies alongside regular research articles. Authors targeting AOR should not assume the same novelty threshold as ABS 4*/4 venues; surveys, replication studies, and applied case work are in-scope.
**Label:** RELAXATION (ABS 3)

### Special-issue dominance (PRACTICE ONLY)
**Field norm:** Articles submitted to a general editorial queue.
**This journal:** A substantial share of published content appears in named thematic special issues with separate guest editors and submission deadlines. Authors should check whether an open regular submission queue exists or whether entry via a relevant special issue is the more viable route.
**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Abstract word limit (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes the abstract as "brief statement" without specifying a count.
**This journal:** Hard cap of 150–250 words, stated in guidelines and consistent with observed articles.
**Label:** ENFORCED

### Citation format (ENFORCED)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify citation format.
**This journal:** APA v7 author-year throughout. This means full author lists in reference entries (not "et al." in the reference list), sentence-case article titles, and parenthetical in-text citation.
**Label:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Ignoring the special-issue model.** Many AOR issues are guest-edited thematic collections. Submitting a general manuscript without checking whether a relevant special issue is open can result in long waits or desk rejection on scope grounds.
2. **Citation format errors.** APA v7 requires sentence-case titles and full author lists in the reference list. Authors accustomed to abbreviated or numbered references (common in other OR venues) will need to reformat.
3. **Abstract out of range.** Authors used to shorter, punchy abstracts (common at ABS 4*/4 outlets) or longer structured abstracts (common in some management journals) must hit 150–250 words — no structured subheadings.
4. **Misjudging scope.** AOR's breadth is genuine: case studies and computational reports are in-scope, but this also means reviewers span a wide range of specialisations. A paper written for a narrow specialist audience may need broader framing than would be required at a dedicated sub-field outlet.
