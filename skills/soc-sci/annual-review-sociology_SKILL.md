# Journal Writing Skill: Annual Review of Sociology

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
This journal publishes **only invited critical synthesis reviews**, not empirical research articles. The IMRAD structure (Introduction → Data/Methods → Results → Discussion) does not apply. Articles consist of an introduction followed by thematically organized synthesis sections and a closing section. In addition to body text, the following optional elements may be added (in order after the main text):

- **Summary Points** — up to 8 complete sentences highlighting central points; placed before Acknowledgments/Literature Cited. [ENFORCED when used]
- **Future Issues** — up to 8 complete sentences or questions on research directions; placed before Acknowledgments/Literature Cited. [ENFORCED when used]
- **Terms and Definitions** — up to 20 key terms, each defined in ≤20 words; placed after Literature Cited. [ENFORCED when used]
- **Reference Annotations** — up to 10 brief (≤15 words) notes on citation importance; placed after Literature Cited. [ENFORCED when used]
- **Related Resources** — up to 10 references not in Literature Cited; placed after Literature Cited. [ENFORCED when used]
- **Sidebar** — 50–200 words, text only (no figures/tables), on a related adjacent topic; called out in text. [ENFORCED when used]

Submission is **by invitation only**; the editorial committee selects topics and authors.

### Length
No fixed word count. Length is **assigned per invitation letter** and includes figures and tables in the page allotment. Maximum reference count is set by the Production Editor and communicated per invitation. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; **150 words maximum**. No required fields beyond the abstract text itself. Up to 6 keywords required separately. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Harvard name-and-year style. **No comma between name and year** in parenthetical citations (e.g., `Smith 2009`, not `Smith, 2009`; `Smith et al. 2009`). Ampersand for two authors (`White & Gray 2004`); `et al.` for three or more authors in text. Multiple parenthetical citations separated by commas (semicolons when citations include multiple years or unlike items). Bibliography alphabetical by surname; last name first then initials, no periods after initials, no comma between surname and initials. Journal titles abbreviated per ISSN LTWA. Article titles included in bibliography (required for Sociology). [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Clearly formatted throughout; up to three heading levels permitted (unnumbered or numbered, author's choice). [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
- **Tables**: Placed all at end of article, following Literature Cited — **not inline in text**. Submitted as separate editable files. [ENFORCED]
- **Figures**: Submitted as separate files; **not integrated within the text**. Each figure requires its own caption file. Color figures included at no cost; use strongly encouraged. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Authors may **not post a postprint or the published version** (including edited text, tables, illustrations, bibliographies) anywhere on the open Internet.
- Preprints may be posted to institutional repositories only; after publication, the preprint must acknowledge the Annual Reviews journal and link to the published version.
- Do not use footnotes as references; do not use Microsoft Word's Footnotes/Endnotes functions for citations.
- Do not submit unsolicited manuscripts.
- Each manuscript must be **originally written** for Annual Reviews; recycling from other publications is prohibited.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** Research articles follow IMRAD or thematic qualitative structure with Data/Methods, Results, Discussion sections.

**This journal:** All articles are critical literature synthesis reviews, not empirical research reports. There are no Data & Methods, Results, or Robustness Check sections. Structure is: Introduction → thematically organized synthesis sections → (optional) closing summary/future directions → optional AR-specific elements (Summary Points, Future Issues, etc.). The genre expectation is encyclopedic synthesis and critical evaluation of an entire subfield, not a gap-filling empirical contribution.

**Classification:** ENFORCED (fundamental genre constraint)

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not include the following structural elements.

**This journal:** Annual Reviews format includes a suite of optional but standardized paratextual elements — Summary Points, Future Issues, Terms and Definitions, Reference Annotations, Related Resources, Sidebars — that are conventions of the Annual Reviews publishing platform and absent from standard SOC SCI research journals.

**Classification:** ENFORCED when used (optional but must follow exact format if included)

### Abstract
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract word limits.

**This journal:** 150-word hard maximum; unstructured.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Citations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes author-year citation is standard but does not specify punctuation details.

**This journal:** No comma between author name and year in parenthetical citations. Specific bibliography format: last name + initials (no periods, no comma between surname and initials), year after names followed by period, article titles in sentence case.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Tables and Figures
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify table/figure placement within the submission document.

**This journal:** Tables placed at end of article after Literature Cited (not inline); figures as separate files not integrated in text.

**Classification:** ENFORCED

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address footnote use.

**This journal:** Footnotes are explicitly discouraged as interrupting text flow; not to be used for references.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Writing an empirical research article.** ARS publishes only invited critical synthesis reviews. Submitting a paper structured around original data, methods, and findings is a category error. Reviewers expect coverage and evaluation of a subfield's literature, not a gap-filling empirical contribution.

2. **Placing tables inline or integrating figures in text.** Both must be submitted separately; tables go after Literature Cited.

3. **Comma in parenthetical citations.** The required format is `(Smith 2009)` not `(Smith, 2009)`. This is a hard house-style rule applied in copyediting.

4. **Exceeding the abstract word limit or ignoring the assigned page length.** Both are set as firm constraints per the invitation letter. The page allotment includes figures and tables.
