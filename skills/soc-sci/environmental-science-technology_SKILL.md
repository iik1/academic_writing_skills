# Journal Writing Skill: Environmental Science & Technology

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** American Chemical Society (ACS)
- **Scope:** Flagship interdisciplinary environmental science journal publishing chemistry, engineering, ecology, toxicology, and environmental policy research. Social-science-oriented work appears primarily in the Policy Analysis, Perspectives, and Features article types; natural-science conventions govern all submissions regardless of disciplinary origin.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://researcher-resources.acs.org/publish/author_guidelines?coden=esthag
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (PMC8969763 — "Systems Thinking for Effective Interventions in Global Environmental Health", Perspective, 2022; PMC10666266 — "ChatGPT and Environmental Research", Perspective, 2023)

---

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Research Articles follow a fixed sequence: Abstract → Keywords → Introduction → Materials and Methods → Results and Discussion → Acknowledgment → References → Supporting Information description → TOC Graphic. **No separate Conclusions section is permitted for Research Articles**; all conclusions must be incorporated into Results and Discussion. Perspectives, Features, and Policy Analysis allow more flexible section organisation. **ENFORCED**

### Length
- Research Articles: 7,000 word-equivalents (hard limit)
- Reviews: 10,000 words
- Policy Analysis: 7,000 words
- Perspectives: 4,000 words
- Features: 5,000 words
- Viewpoints: 1,000 words

Word count includes abstract + main text + figure/table titles and captions; excludes references, author affiliations, acknowledgments, and TOC graphic. Manuscripts exceeding limits are un-submitted or immediately rejected; authors who believe an exception is essential must include a compelling argument in the cover letter. **ENFORCED**

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, 150–200 words, for Research Articles, Reviews, Policy Analysis, and Perspectives. Must address purpose, methods, significant results, and implications. No citations, no reference numbers, no background-only material. After the abstract, authors must supply a **Synopsis**: a nontechnical statement of approximately 20 words stating the work's environmental relevance and significance, connecting findings to a specific environmental system (air, water, soil, human or other biota) or policy context. Features use a 3–5 sentence abstract written for a scientifically literate general audience. Viewpoints require no abstract. Keywords: 5–8 terms required for Research Articles, Policy Analysis, Reviews, Perspectives, and Features. **ENFORCED**

### Citations
ACS numbered superscript style. References are numbered in order of appearance; in-text markers are superscripted numerals placed outside punctuation. Any complete reference format is acceptable (titles encouraged); DOIs are helpful but not mandatory unless the DOI is the only available identifier. Excessive self-citation is prohibited. **ENFORCED — major delta from FIELD_SKILL author-year norm; confirmed in both spot-checked articles**

### Headings
No journal-specific heading style beyond the fixed section sequence for Research Articles. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for subsection labelling within permitted sections.

### Tables and Figures
Multi-panel figures must not exceed 4 panels; panels labelled by letter. All text in figures/tables must be legible at minimum 8-point font. Non-essential figures belong in Supporting Information. Any reproduced graphic from a prior publication requires a credit line and written permission. Chemical compounds must use IUPAC systematic nomenclature; trade names in parentheses. **ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
1. No separate Conclusions section in Research Articles.
2. No footnotes except author-information notes and table-detail footnotes.
3. No speculation unsupported by data presented.
4. No excessive self-citation.
5. AI tools may not be listed as authors.
6. No prior publication or simultaneous submission elsewhere (preprints on ChemRxiv, bioRxiv, or arXiv are permitted with DOI disclosed in the cover letter).
7. Posting on commercial author websites may endanger publication consideration.
8. Word limits are strictly enforced; excess length triggers rejection or un-submission.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline. Each delta includes the field norm, what this journal does instead, and its classification label.

### Citation Style
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Author-year citations (e.g., Smith, 2020) throughout; reference list alphabetical.

**This journal:** Numbered superscript citations in order of appearance; reference list numbered sequentially. Non-negotiable ACS house style. Confirmed in both spot-checked articles.

**ENFORCED**

### Section Structure
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Flexible section labelling; separate Discussion and/or Conclusion sections standard in quantitative and mixed-methods work.

**This journal (Research Articles):** Fixed labels mandated: Introduction → Materials and Methods → Results and Discussion. No separate Conclusions section allowed; conclusions must be embedded in Results and Discussion. Perspectives and Features may include a standalone Conclusion section (confirmed in spot-checked Perspective).

**ENFORCED**

### Abstract and Synopsis
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Unstructured abstract; no separate significance statement required by the journal.

**This journal:** Mandatory ~20-word nontechnical Synopsis immediately after the abstract. The Synopsis must explicitly connect findings to a specific environmental system or policy context. It is not a condensed abstract but a distinct lay-significance statement. No FIELD_SKILL equivalent exists.

**ENFORCED**

### Length Constraints
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Word limits not specified at the field level; length norms at ABS 4 tier are informal and vary by journal.

**This journal:** Hard 7,000-word cap for Research Articles (excluding references and ancillary matter). Strictly enforced at submission. RELAXATION relative to informal ABS 4 social-science expectations, which tolerate longer empirical papers.

**ENFORCED**

### TOC Graphic
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** No equivalent requirement.

**This journal:** A single-frame TOC graphic with a 50-word caption is required for Research Articles, Features, Reviews, Perspectives, and Policy Analysis. It must illustrate the work without being an exact duplicate of a figure in the paper.

**ENFORCED**

### Register and Intended Audience
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Technical density calibrated to a disciplinary specialist audience; assumes expert readers.

**This journal:** Guidelines explicitly instruct authors to "assume ES&T readers are professionals who are not necessarily experts in your particular field." The journal's interdisciplinary scope means articles must be accessible across chemistry, engineering, ecology, and social science sub-communities. Confirmed in spot-checked articles, which balance technical depth with broader accessibility.

**GUIDELINE ONLY** (guideline-stated; not independently falsifiable from two Perspective articles alone)

### Data and Materials Deposition
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Data availability increasingly standard but not universally mandatory at the field level.

**This journal:** Mandatory deposit of data, materials, and protocols in a named public repository (Dryad, figshare, Zenodo, or discipline-specific databases for genomics, proteomics, mass spectrometry, etc.). Accession numbers must be available by revision submission and reported in Materials and Methods.

**ENFORCED**

### Units and Nomenclature
**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Not specified at the field level.

**This journal:** SI units required throughout. IUPAC systematic nomenclature required for chemical substances. Not a social-science norm and adds a compliance requirement for authors working at the natural-social science interface.

**ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Using author-year citations.** The single most common formatting error from social-science authors submitting to this journal. ACS numbered superscripts are mandatory; author-year format will require full revision.
2. **Adding a standalone Conclusions section to a Research Article.** ES&T explicitly prohibits this; conclusions must be woven into the Results and Discussion section.
3. **Omitting the Synopsis.** The ~20-word nontechnical significance statement after the abstract is a required submission field, not optional. It must name a specific environmental system or policy context—generic statements about environmental relevance are insufficient.
4. **Exceeding the word limit without justification.** The 7,000-word ceiling is enforced at submission; a compelling case in the cover letter is required for any exception and is seldom accepted.
