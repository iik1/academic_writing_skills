# Journal Writing Skill: Long Range Planning

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** STRAT
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** International journal of strategic management covering corporate strategy, competitive dynamics, strategic leadership, international strategy, innovation, and governance. Explicitly publishes articles directed at both academic researchers and practising managers.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/long-range-planning/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** strat-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (abstracts only; full text inaccessible via ScienceDirect without session authentication — DOIs 10.1016/j.lrp.2025.102571 and 10.1016/j.lrp.2025.102581)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for research articles. No fixed section sequence mandated in accessible guidelines.

### Length
No explicit word limit stated for regular research articles in accessible guidelines. Perspective/Opinion articles: 2,000–4,000 words total (including abstract, text, and figure legends; references counted separately with a maximum of 35). Word limits exclude abstracts, references, and figure/table legends.

### Abstract
Unstructured prose; maximum 250 words. Briefly states purpose, principal results, and major conclusions. No labeled fields required.

Perspective/Opinion articles: maximum 50 words. — ENFORCED

### Citations
Author-year format (Elsevier Harvard style). In-text: (Smith, 2020); (Smith and Jones, 2020); (Atwood et al., 2002). Multiple citations separated by semicolons: (Screen and Simmonds, 2010; Brown, 2014). Reference list format: Author, Initials., Year. Title. Journal Volume, Pages–Pages. This differs from APA in comma and period placement but the in-text pattern is identical to FIELD_SKILL norm. — FIELD_DEFAULT for in-text format; reference list format is Elsevier Harvard, not APA.

### Keywords
3–8 keywords required. — ENFORCED

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Perspective/Opinion articles: maximum 1–2 figures or tables combined. Regular research articles: no explicit limit found in accessible guidelines. Figure legends required.

### Highlights
3–5 bullet points, each a maximum of 85 characters including spaces. Submitted as a separate editable file named with "highlights". Required for all submissions. — ENFORCED

This is a publisher-level Elsevier requirement absent from FIELD_SKILL. Authors must draft these in addition to the abstract; they are not a restatement of the abstract but capture the most novel results and any new methods.

### Graphical Abstract
Required at submission. A single image summarising the article's content. — ENFORCED

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- AI use in manuscript preparation must be declared in a dedicated statement placed before the references section.
- Peer review must not involve generative AI.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Practitioner Orientation (Scope-Level Delta)
**Field norm:** Managerial implications subsection expected but framed as one component of the discussion. **LRP addition:** The journal's explicit scope statement says articles "may be written for practicing managers and students in professional programs." This is stronger practitioner framing than the generic FIELD_SKILL expectation. In practice this means managerial implications sections are not optional courtesies — they are consistent with the journal's stated identity. — PRACTICE ONLY (scope statement confirmed in guidelines; section-level practice unverifiable from abstract-only article access)

### Publisher Submission Requirements (Writing-Adjacent)
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify highlights or graphical abstracts.
**LRP/Elsevier requirement:** Highlights (3–5 bullets, ≤85 chars each) and a graphical abstract are required at submission. These must be authored content, not auto-generated. — ENFORCED

### Perspective Article Type
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL covers empirical, theoretical, and review papers; it does not describe short opinion formats.
**LRP addition:** A dedicated Perspective/Opinion article type exists with tight constraints: 2,000–4,000 words, ≤50-word abstract, ≤35 references, 1–2 figures or tables. Purpose is to present a concise, novel viewpoint, propose a new hypothesis, or discuss implications. This is a journal-specific publication track not present in the FIELD_SKILL baseline. — ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing highlights or graphical abstract.** These are required by Elsevier at submission; omitting them will trigger a desk rejection or forced revision before review. They must be substantive content, not boilerplate.
2. **Perspective article abstract over 50 words.** The 50-word cap for Perspective pieces is strict; a 250-word abstract written for a research article will not fit this format.
3. **Treating managerial implications as optional.** Given the journal's explicit scope commitment to practitioner relevance, a research article with no implications for practice is out of scope regardless of theoretical contribution.
4. **Reference list formatted as APA.** LRP uses Elsevier Harvard style, which differs in punctuation (commas after author initials, no parentheses around year). Reference managers should be set to Elsevier Harvard, not APA 7th.
