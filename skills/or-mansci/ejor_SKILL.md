# Journal Writing Skill: European Journal of Operational Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** OR&MANSCI
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Covers the full breadth of operational research methodology and application, from continuous and discrete optimisation to stochastics, decision support, and computational intelligence. Spans theoretical, applied, and computational contributions.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/european-journal-of-operational-research/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** orandmansci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Cominetti, Dose & Scarsini, "Monotonicity of equilibria in nonatomic congestion games," EJOR 2024, DOI: 10.1016/j.ejor.2024.01.050; arXiv:2308.00434)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Four distinct submission types with different scope constraints:

- **Theory and Methodology Papers** — original research contributions. Upon submission, authors must classify their paper into one of seven subject areas: Continuous Optimization; Discrete Optimization; Production, Manufacturing and Logistics; Stochastics and Statistics; Decision Support; Analytics/Computational Intelligence/Information Management; Interfaces with Other Disciplines. [ENFORCED]
- **Innovative Applications of OR** — novel solutions to real-world problems demonstrating OR's practical value. [ENFORCED]
- **Invited Reviews** — creative syntheses of recent work including open problems; authors must obtain editorial invitation before submitting. Unsolicited review submissions are not accepted. EURO funds the open-access fee for one Invited Review per issue. [ENFORCED]
- **Short Communications** — strictly limited to corrections of important errors in EJOR papers published within the previous five years. These are NOT short research notes; novel findings must be submitted as Theory and Methodology Papers. [ENFORCED]

Required manuscript components (in order): Title page; Abstract; Keywords; Main body; Declaration of generative AI use (if applicable); Declaration of interest; Data availability statement; References.

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated for any article type. [GUIDELINE ONLY — no limit specified]

### Abstract
Unstructured (plain paragraph). No word limit stated. Abstract section must be present before peer review proceeds; absence causes desk rejection. [ENFORCED for presence; GUIDELINE ONLY for format details]

### Citations
**Harvard author-year style is required** — both in-text and in the reference list. In-text form: (Author, Year) or Author (Year). This diverges from the numbered-bracket style common in mathematical OR outlets. [ENFORCED]

Additional citation categories with specific formatting rules: web references, preprint references (e.g., arXiv), and special-issue papers.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
**Highlights are mandatory.** A Highlights section — a bulleted list of 3–5 key findings — must be submitted as a separate file and appears on the ScienceDirect article page. This is an Elsevier platform requirement, not a section within the manuscript body. [ENFORCED]

Figures and tables require captions. Electronic artwork must meet Elsevier technical standards.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Plagiarism of any kind — explicitly named as grounds for rejection
- Extended conference proceedings without substantial new content not solicited
- Simultaneous submission of similar manuscripts
- Ghost authorship
- AI tools may not be listed as authors
- Papers failing to meet "good standard of English" are desk-rejected before peer review

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Style
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL specifies citation density and engagement norms but does not mandate a citation format.
**This journal:** Harvard author-year format is explicitly required. In practice (Cominetti et al., 2024 arXiv preprint), in-text citations follow (Author, Year) form consistently. For authors accustomed to numbered-bracket styles (common in algorithmic/mathematical OR), this requires conscious adjustment.
**Label:** ENFORCED

### Structural Deviations
**Short Communications scope:** FIELD_SKILL does not address submission type definitions. EJOR's "Short Communications" category is narrowly scoped to error corrections in previously published EJOR papers — not brief research notes. Authors with short novel results must submit as Theory and Methodology Papers.
**Label:** ENFORCED

**Subject-area classification:** Theory and Methodology submissions require the author to assign one of seven designated subject-area labels at submission. This classification routes the manuscript to the appropriate handling editor.
**Label:** ENFORCED

**"Summary and open problems" as concluding section:** In the one theoretical article spot-checked (Cominetti et al.), the final section is titled "Summary and open problems" rather than "Conclusion." This naming signals open-problems framing common in mathematical OR but is not mentioned in the guidelines.
**Label:** PRACTICE ONLY

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Mandatory Highlights file:** A separate Highlights document (3–5 bullet points, each under ~85 characters) is required at submission. This is not a section within the paper — it is a standalone file for the ScienceDirect abstract page. It has no parallel in FIELD_SKILL conventions.
**Label:** ENFORCED

**Required administrative declarations:** All submissions require (a) a Declaration of Interest (or explicit statement "none"), (b) a Data Availability Statement, and (c) a CRediT author contribution statement. If AI tools were used in manuscript preparation, a declaration section must appear before the references. These are Elsevier-platform requirements absent from FIELD_SKILL.
**Label:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting without a Highlights file.** The Highlights document is a mandatory submission component; missing it will stall desk processing. Prepare 3–5 punchy bullet points (≤85 characters each) summarising the paper's key contributions before uploading.

2. **Using numbered citations instead of Harvard style.** Authors from theoretical OR backgrounds often default to [1]-style numbering. EJOR requires author-year throughout. Proofread the full manuscript, including appendices and captions, before submission.

3. **Submitting a short novel result as a Short Communication.** EJOR's Short Communications slot is reserved for error corrections in previously published EJOR papers. A novel finding — even if brief — must go through the Theory and Methodology or Innovative Applications route.

4. **Missing administrative declarations.** Declaration of Interest, Data Availability Statement, and CRediT author contributions are all required and must be present. Omitting any of them triggers desk-rejection or return without review.
