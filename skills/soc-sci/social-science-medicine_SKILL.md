# Journal Writing Skill: Social Science and Medicine

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary research on the social determinants of health, illness, and medical care. Spans medical sociology, health geography, health economics, medical anthropology, health psychology, social epidemiology, and health policy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/social-science-and-medicine/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (DOI: 10.1016/j.socscimed.2024.117434; PMC11585449)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Four article types, each with distinct word limits (see Length). Authors must select a disciplinary editorial office at submission (see Editorial Offices). Required submission elements: title page, abstract, keywords, highlights, main text, references. Supplementary material optional.

### Length
**9,000 words all-in** for original research articles and critical analytical reviews. The cap covers abstract, tables, figures, references, and appendices — not main text alone. Shorter papers explicitly preferred. ENFORCED.

**15,000 words all-in** for systematic and scoping reviews (including meta-analyses). Same inclusive counting method. ENFORCED.

Commentaries and responses are invited or submitted alongside selected articles; no word limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured (confirmed in articles: single paragraph, no labeled sections). No abstract-specific word limit stated; the abstract counts toward the overall word cap. PRACTICE ONLY.

### Citations
Author-year parenthetical style (e.g., Smith, 2020; Smith et al., 2020). Reference list alphabetically ordered; journal names abbreviated; DOIs included. Aligns with field norm. PRACTICE ONLY (confirmed in articles; no explicit guideline statement).

### Headings
Sections numbered with Arabic numerals (1., 2., 2.1., etc.). Main section headings bolded; subsection headings not bolded. PRACTICE ONLY (confirmed in articles; not specified in visible guidelines).

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures count toward the all-in word limit. No additional journal-specific formatting constraints identified beyond this. ENFORCED (implied by inclusive word count rule).

### Highlights
3–5 bullet points summarizing novel findings, each no more than 85 characters including spaces. Required as a separate submission element (Word document). Written for a general audience; jargon, acronyms, and abbreviations should be avoided. Not part of editorial consideration but required before final acceptance. ENFORCED.

### Editorial Offices
At submission, authors must select one of seven disciplinary offices: Medical Anthropology, Health Economics, Social Epidemiology, Health Psychology, Medical Sociology, Health Policy, or Health Geography. Selection routes the manuscript to editors with appropriate disciplinary expertise and affects reviewer matching. Selecting the wrong office can result in mismatch between the paper's contribution and the reviewing panel. ENFORCED.

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Concurrent submission to another journal
- Duplicate publication (preprints, abstracts, and theses are exempt)
- Authorship changes after acceptance
- Undisclosed competing interests

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Section numbering:** All sections use Arabic numeral prefixes (1., 2., 2.1., 3., etc.), not label-only headings. FIELD_SKILL does not specify section numbering conventions; this is a consistent journal practice. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Disciplinary office routing:** The journal operates as seven semi-independent disciplinary offices under one masthead. Authors must actively place their paper within one office at submission. A paper framed around place-based health processes belongs in Health Geography; one using welfare analysis belongs in Health Economics. This is not standard across FIELD_SKILL journals and affects both the framing of the contribution and the likely reviewer pool. [ENFORCED]

**All-in word count:** The 9,000-word cap includes everything — references, tables, figures, appendices. Many journals apply word limits to main text only. This journal's all-in counting convention materially constrains the length of the reference list and the number of tables. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Ignoring the all-in word count:** The 9,000-word cap covers tables, figures, and references. Authors accustomed to journals that count only main text can submit papers 20–30% over the limit without realising it.
2. **Missing or malformed Highlights:** Highlights (3–5 bullets, ≤85 characters each) are a required Elsevier submission element. Omitting them or exceeding the character limit is a common formatting error.
3. **Misrouting via editorial office:** Selecting the wrong disciplinary office — e.g., submitting a sociological framing to Health Psychology — risks reviewer mismatch and rejection on fit grounds unrelated to manuscript quality.
4. **Structured abstract:** Health and medical journals often require structured abstracts with labeled sections. SSM does not; submitting a structured abstract would be non-standard and signals unfamiliarity with the outlet.
