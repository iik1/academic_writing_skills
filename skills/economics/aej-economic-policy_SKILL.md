# Journal Writing Skill: American Economic Journal: Economic Policy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** American Economic Association
- **Scope:** Empirical and theoretical work on the effects of public policy, including taxation, regulation, environmental policy, health policy, labour policy, and political economy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/pol/submissions/guidelines
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (10.1257/pol.20220356 — Pless 2026, "Are Complementary Policies Substitutes? Evidence from R&D Subsidies in the United Kingdom"; author-hosted working paper version, pdfminer extraction)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for body structure. In-paper appendices must appear after the main text and before the references list. Supplemental appendices are permitted for non-essential material but are not edited or typeset; the main paper must be fully comprehensible without consulting the supplemental appendix — all essential data sources, methods, and proofs belong in the main text or in-paper appendix. [ENFORCED]

### Length
Recommended maximum of 40 pages at 11-point font, 1.5 spacing, 1-inch margins; or 45 pages at 12-point font, same spacing and margins. The average published article runs 33–34 typeset pages. Described as a recommendation, not a hard cap; shorter papers receive full consideration. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Abstract
Unstructured; 100 words or fewer. No required fields. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Chicago Manual of Style Author-Date format. In-text: all authors named for works with 1–4 authors; first author plus "et al." for 5 or more. Reference list: all authors named for works with 1–10 authors; first seven authors plus "et al." for 11 or more. Adherence required for accepted articles; optional at submission. [ENFORCED for published articles]

### Headings
Roman numerals for major sections (I., II., III., …); capital letters for subsections (A., B., C., …). Working papers submitted to the journal commonly use Arabic numerals because compliance with the AEA style guide is optional at submission and required only upon acceptance. [GUIDELINE ONLY at submission; ENFORCED at acceptance]

### Tables and Figures
- **Significance asterisks prohibited.** Do not use *, **, *** to denote statistical significance. Report standard errors in parentheses beneath coefficients; statistical significance is communicated by the coefficient and standard error together, not by symbols. [ENFORCED — explicit prohibition in AEA style guide]
- Maximum 9 columns including row headings.
- Horizontal rules only; no vertical lines, no shading.
- Arabic numerals for table numbers.
- Lead zeros required on decimals (0.357, not .357).
- Table-specific notes use lowercase letter superscripts (a, b, c); source notes and general notes accompany every table.
- Figures: vector formats (PDF, EPS, AI, WMF, or PPT); photographs at 300 dpi minimum. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI software may not be listed as an author; any AI assistance in manuscript preparation must be disclosed.
- Papers simultaneously under review elsewhere are ineligible.
- Papers previously rejected by AEJ: Economic Policy may not be resubmitted.
- Field experiments must be registered in the AEA RCT Registry prior to submission.
- Replication package (data and code) required before final acceptance.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Voice and Register
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** First-person plural ("we") is near-universal, including in sole-authored papers.

**This journal:** First-person singular ("I") is used and published in sole-authored papers. Confirmed in Pless (2026): "I implement," "I use," "I find," "I argue" throughout the abstract and body. Multi-authored papers are expected to follow the field norm of "we." [PRACTICE ONLY — RELAXATION relative to FIELD_SKILL at ABS 3]

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Does not specify a prohibition on significance asterisks; standard errors in parentheses and asterisk notation both appear in the field.

**This journal:** Asterisks for statistical significance are explicitly prohibited by the AEA style guide that governs all AEA journals including this one. Standard errors must appear in parentheses beneath coefficients. This is routinely violated in working paper versions (compliance is required only upon acceptance), so authors should remove asterisks and ensure standard errors are present before submission to avoid revision requests at acceptance. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Asterisks in tables.** Authors accustomed to any other economics outlet will have asterisks in their working paper. Remove them before final acceptance and replace with standard errors in parentheses. Do not wait until post-acceptance revision requests — the prohibition is stated in the style guide and reviewers familiar with AEA norms will notice.
2. **Abstract over 100 words.** The limit is firm and shorter than many authors expect. FIELD_SKILL specifies no abstract word limit, so authors arriving from other ABS 4 outlets may not trim aggressively enough.
3. **Paper not self-contained.** The journal explicitly requires the main text to stand alone without the supplemental appendix. Moving identification assumptions, key variable definitions, or data source descriptions to the online appendix will trigger revision requests.
4. **Sole-authored papers written in "we."** The field baseline favours plural even for sole-authored work, but AEJ: Economic Policy publishes "I" in sole-authored papers. Using "we" is not an error, but "I" is the observed practice and avoids false plurality.
