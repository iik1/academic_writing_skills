# Journal Writing Skill: Critical Perspectives on Accounting

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Critical, emancipatory, and heterodox perspectives on accounting and its role in social, political, economic, and ecological life. The journal explicitly rejects conventional accounting theory as its reference point; all submissions are expected to engage critically with accounting practice and its societal consequences.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/critical-perspectives-on-accounting/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (10.1016/j.cpa.2024.102784 — Alazzeh & Uddin, 2025, repository version)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Title page → Abstract → Keywords → Highlights → Main article → References. No required IMRaD template; internal organization is author-determined and typically thematic. **ENFORCED**

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. Observed length approximately 150–200 words. Required fields: none (no structured headings). **GUIDELINE ONLY** (word limit not published; length inferred from practice)

### Highlights
Elsevier requires 3–5 bullet points summarizing the article's core findings or contributions, submitted as a separate field. Not part of the abstract. Not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. **ENFORCED**

### Citations
APA author-date format. In-text: (Author, Year) with a comma between name and year — e.g., (Buanaputra & Uddin, 2023), (Scobie et al., 2024), (Neu, 2000a, 2000b). Multiple sources separated by semicolons: (IMF, 2022; UNCTAD, 2016). Reference list follows APA 7th-edition conventions: Author, A. (Year). Title. *Journal*, Volume(Issue), pages. https://doi.org/ **ENFORCED**

### Headings
Arabic numeral sections and subsections (1., 2., 2.1., etc.). Title-case headings. No ALL-CAPS. Follows observed Elsevier/APA norm. **ENFORCED**

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables present empirical or documentary data rather than regression output.

### Explicit Prohibitions
1. **AI in research:** "We do not want Critical Perspectives on Accounting to receive submissions in which AI played a significant role in any step of the research process, except for language translation and copyediting purposes." This goes beyond standard disclosure requirements — the journal's editorial board has stated a substantive objection to AI-assisted research, not merely a transparency requirement. **ENFORCED**
2. **AI as author:** AI tools must not be listed or cited as an author. **ENFORCED**
3. **Authorship changes after acceptance:** Not permitted. **ENFORCED**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Orientation: exclusively critical/interpretive

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** The field baseline describes a dominant quantitative/archival/experimental strand, with AOS qualitative/interpretive papers as a noted minority strand.

**This journal:** CPA publishes *only* in the critical and interpretive tradition. Quantitative, archival, and experimental papers are not published here. Every submission is expected to engage with critical theory, broadly construed — including postcolonial theory, feminist theory, Marxist political economy, settler colonial studies, Foucauldian analysis, Habermasian critical theory, or comparable frameworks. The theoretical frame is not optional scaffolding around an empirical design; it is the organizing logic of the paper. **PRACTICE ONLY** (not stated in guidelines, but confirmed across all articles observed and consistent with the journal's stated scope)

**RELAXATION relative to FIELD_SKILL:** The identification-strategy concerns and hypothesis-testing conventions central to FIELD_SKILL do not apply. Authors are never expected to claim causal identification or enumerate formal hypotheses. This is a structural relaxation relative to the quantitative strand.

### Social theory citation density

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Social theory cited in AOS interpretive papers; not prominent in the quantitative mainstream.

**This journal:** Social theory citations are as dense in CPA as empirical literature citations. Major theoretical anchors (Veracini, Foucault, Bourdieu, Marx, etc.) are cited and unpacked in extended theoretical sections that frequently occupy a third or more of article length. **PRACTICE ONLY**

### Direct quotation of theoretical sources

**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Paraphrase is universal in the accounting mainstream; direct quotation of prior scholarly literature is "essentially absent." Fieldwork quotation is acceptable in AOS interpretive papers.

**This journal:** Direct quotation of theoretical scholars (with page numbers) is observed alongside fieldwork quotation. Authors quote canonical theorists verbatim to ground interpretive claims — e.g., quoting Wolfe (2012, p. 137) verbatim in the methods section to anchor the study's framing. This practice extends beyond what FIELD_SKILL notes for the AOS interpretive tradition. **PRACTICE ONLY**

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first person plural "we" throughout)
- Sentence and paragraph style: FIELD DEFAULT (dense, discursive paragraphs consistent with AOS interpretive norm)
- Hedging level: FIELD DEFAULT (mechanistic claims hedged; evidence-based claims stated directly)
- Limitations acknowledgment: FIELD DEFAULT (expected and present)
- Double-anonymous peer review: FIELD DEFAULT

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting quantitative or archival work.** CPA does not publish regression-based studies, experimental designs, or archival hypothesis-testing papers. Submissions of this type will be desk-rejected.
2. **Omitting a theoretical frame.** Unlike some ABS 3 journals that accept empirical work with light theory, CPA expects the critical theoretical framework to be substantive and integral — not a brief literature review followed by an empirical analysis.
3. **Using AI for anything beyond translation or copyediting.** The editorial board has issued an unusually strong position against AI in the research process. Even analytic or writing assistance beyond copyediting may be grounds for rejection or retraction.
4. **Forgetting Highlights.** CPA uses the Elsevier Highlights field (3–5 bullet points). Authors accustomed to journals without this requirement may omit it; missing Highlights will generate a revision request before review.
