# Journal Writing Skill: International Journal of Operations and Production Management (IJOPM)

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** OPS&TECH
- **Publisher:** Emerald Group Publishing
- **Scope:** Operations and supply chain management (OSCM); covers strategies, systems, processes and practices in operations management, with emphasis on both theoretical contribution and practical relevance. Official journal of the European Operations Management Association (EurOMA).
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.emeraldgrouppublishing.com/journal/ijopm (author guidelines accessible via ScholarOne portal)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section sequence for standard empirical manuscripts beyond field norms. IJOPM formally names six article types: (1) Empirical manuscripts, (2) Conceptual manuscripts, (3) Impact Pathways articles, (4) Africa Initiative submissions, (5) Special Issues, and (6) Invited contributions. The Impact Pathways type has a distinct structure requirement (see Length below). Otherwise follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Length
- **Standard manuscripts:** No explicit word cap found in accessible public guidelines.
- **Impact Pathways articles:** ≤ 4,000 words. This is a journal-specific article type (see Other Journal-Specific Patterns below). [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
**Format:** Structured abstract with **4–7 sub-headings**, maximum **250 words in total** (inclusive of keywords and article classification). [ENFORCED]

**Standard headings observed across spot-checked articles:**
1. Purpose
2. Design/methodology/approach
3. Findings
4. Practical implications *(optional fifth heading; included when practical relevance warrants a dedicated entry)*
5. Originality/value

This heading set is an Emerald platform standard enforced for IJOPM. FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract format; this is a hard constraint that authors from fields using unstructured abstracts must observe.

### Citations
**Harvard author-date style required.** In-text format: (Author, Year) or Author (Year); multiple sources separated by semicolons. "et al." used from three or more authors. [ENFORCED]

FIELD_SKILL characterises the field as using "APA-adjacent" author-date format; IJOPM specifies Harvard explicitly. The practical difference is minor but authors should follow the Emerald Harvard reference list format, not APA.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Numbered section headings are used in practice (1., 2., 2.1, 2.2, etc.).

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Statistical tables include footnotes specifying significance thresholds (see Statistical Reporting below).

### Explicit Prohibitions
Two categories of submission are explicitly rejected at desk, constituting a journal-specific scope restriction not stated in FIELD_SKILL:

1. **Purely mathematical modelling papers** — papers without empirical grounding or substantial managerial insight are out of scope. [ENFORCED]
2. **Literature reviews lacking strong theoretical advancement or conceptual insight** — systematic or bibliometric reviews without a substantial theoretical contribution face desk rejection. [ENFORCED]

Additionally, the journal has adopted an explicit **AI/LLM disclosure policy** aligned with other leading OSCM journals; use of large language models in manuscript preparation must be disclosed. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Statistical Reporting
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL states that whether asterisks or exact p-values are required is journal-specific and does not specify a standard.

**IJOPM practice:** Significance asterisks are used universally across observed empirical articles, with explicit p-value thresholds listed in table footnotes (e.g., *** p < 0.01, ** p < 0.05, * p < 0.10). Effect sizes and confidence intervals are reported alongside beta coefficients and standard deviations. Bootstrapping iteration counts appear in table notes when applicable. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Structural Deviations
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that Discussion and Conclusion may be separate or merged; no consensus norm exists.

**IJOPM practice:** In spot-checked articles the merged "Discussion and Conclusions" section consistently contains three named subsections: (1) Academic/Theoretical Implications, (2) Managerial Implications, (3) Limitations and Future Research. This internal sub-sectioning of the final section is more explicit than field norms suggest and appears to be a stable IJOPM structural convention. [PRACTICE ONLY]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Impact Pathways article type:** IJOPM operates a named short-format section called *Impact Pathways* (≤ 4,000 words) for practitioner-oriented contributions that bridge theory and practice. These are fast-tracked (target: ~8 weeks submission to decision). Impact Pathways pieces are phenomenon-driven, grounded in current practice or policy, and advance OSCM by suggesting future research directions. They are not shortened versions of standard empirical papers. [ENFORCED]

**Cover letter requirement:** Authors are expected to include a focused cover letter that explains: (a) the paper's core contribution, (b) its theoretical positioning, and (c) how it engages with current IJOPM debates. This is operationally enforced at desk review. [ENFORCED — stated in editorial guidance]

**Scope boundary:** The journal explicitly expects that, even when theory is borrowed from adjacent disciplines (e.g., strategy, organisational behaviour, economics), the primary contribution must reside within OSCM. Submissions that merely apply established OSCM tools to non-OSCM phenomena face scope-based desk rejection. [ENFORCED]

**British spelling:** At least one spot-checked article uses British English conventions (e.g., "stylised," "organised"). As a UK-based journal (Emerald, Lancaster University editorial office), British spelling is consistent with the journal's house style, though guidelines do not explicitly mandate it. [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Keywords:** Maximum **6 keywords** required. [ENFORCED]

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Submitting a mathematical modelling paper.** Purely analytical/modelling work without empirical grounding or strong managerial insight is desk-rejected. If your paper relies on formal proofs, it must demonstrate practical significance to be in scope.
2. **Unstructured abstract.** IJOPM requires a structured abstract with 4–7 labelled sub-headings (max 250 words). An unstructured paragraph-form abstract does not meet submission requirements.
3. **Generic cover letter.** The editorial team expects the cover letter to demonstrate engagement with ongoing IJOPM debates and explicitly articulate theoretical positioning. A boilerplate "we submit our paper for consideration" letter is a missed signal.
4. **Literature review without theoretical advancement.** Systematic or bibliometric reviews that synthesise existing knowledge without a substantial new theoretical argument face desk rejection; frame the contribution as theory-building, not just synthesis.
