## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
**ENFORCED** — Principal divisions must appear in this exact order:
1. Body of the article
2. References
3. Footnotes
4. Tables
5. Figures
6. Appendices

This differs slightly from FIELD_SKILL in explicitly requiring footnotes (if any) to appear after references but before tables. FIELD_SKILL does not specify footnote placement.

### Length
**ENFORCED** — 50-page maximum, inclusive of all components (notes, references, tables, figures, appendices).

FIELD_SKILL notes typical lengths of 10,000–15,000 words for quantitative deductive work; JOM's 50-page limit (double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman) roughly translates to ~12,500–15,000 words depending on table/figure count. This is a hard ceiling rather than a guideline.

**Note:** Authors are evaluated on page-to-contribution ratio. Manuscripts that exceed 50 pages will be immediately returned without review.

### Abstract
**ENFORCED** — 250-word maximum.

FIELD_SKILL indicates 150–250 words as typical; JOM specifies 250 as the hard upper limit. Structure (structured vs. unstructured) not mandated—both are acceptable per FIELD_SKILL baseline.

### Citations
**ENFORCED** — APA style, with one JOM-specific modification:

For sources with **more than six authors**, use "et al." form even for the first citation. However, the reference list must include the complete author list.

FIELD_SKILL indicates "et al. for three or more authors from first citation" as the APA norm. JOM requires full authorship display in-text until the seventh author, which is a stricter standard than general APA practice.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Concise headings should organize the manuscript; when a section is divided, use at least two subsections.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Must be placed after footnotes and before appendices. Must be double-spaced like all other manuscript components.

### Formatting Requirements
**ENFORCED:**
- 12-point Times New Roman font
- Double-spaced throughout (including references, appendices, tables, figures)
- 1-inch (2.54 cm) margins on all sides

### Hypothesis Presentation
**ENFORCED** — In manuscripts reporting tests of formal hypotheses, each hypothesis must be:
1. Explicitly identified as a hypothesis
2. Stated separately
3. Given a distinct number (H1, H2, H3...)

This aligns with FIELD_SKILL but is more explicitly mandated in JOM guidelines.

### Explicit Prohibitions
**ENFORCED:**
1. **No footnotes for citations** — Use conventional in-text referencing format. Manuscripts using footnotes instead of proper APA citations will be immediately returned without review.
2. **No single-spacing** — Manuscripts that are single-spaced will be immediately returned.
3. **Concurrent submission** — Papers under concurrent consideration at another journal are prohibited.

### Contemporary Requirements
**ENFORCED** — **AI disclosure**: If AI was used in writing the manuscript, producing images/graphics, or collecting/analyzing data, authors must disclose:
- Which tool was used
- How it was used
- Disclosure must appear in the Methods section of all versions

**PRACTICE ONLY** — ORCID linking is "strongly encouraged" but not required. All co-authors should link ORCID iDs during submission.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding 50 pages** — This is the most common desk-reject trigger. Count every page: title, abstract, body, references, footnotes, tables, figures, appendices. No exceptions. If you're at 48 pages, cut—don't hope for leniency.

2. **Using footnotes for citations** — JOM explicitly prohibits footnote-based citation systems. Use APA in-text citations exclusively. Footnotes may be used for clarifications or tangential points, but not for referencing sources.

3. **Citing et al. too early** — JOM requires full author names in-text for sources with six or fewer authors, even on first citation. This is stricter than standard APA (which allows et al. from second citation for 3+ authors). Review your citations: if the source has 4 authors and you wrote "Smith et al. (2020)," change it to "Smith, Jones, Lee, and Brown (2020)."

4. **Generic contribution claims** — JOM evaluates papers on page-to-contribution ratio. Vague claims like "We contribute to the literature" without specifying which literature and how will be penalized. Be precise: name the theoretical conversation, identify the gap, state the advance.

5. **Forgetting AI disclosure** — If you used ChatGPT to polish sentences, Grammarly for editing, or any AI tool for coding, analysis, or visualization, disclose it in Methods. Omission risks editorial queries or revisions.
