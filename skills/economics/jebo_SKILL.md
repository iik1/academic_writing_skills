# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Behavioral and organizational economics, broadly construed. Publishes empirical, experimental, and theoretical papers on how economic behavior is shaped by organizations, institutions, and incentives. Frequently publishes special issues with guest editors.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-economic-behavior-and-organization/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Aytun, Hinz & Özgüzel, JEBO 2025, vol. 231, DOI 10.1016/j.jebo.2025.106821; Moreno & Petrakis, JEBO 2026, vol. 246, article 107531)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Appendices are lettered (A, B, C...).

### Length
No explicit word or page limit stated in guidelines.

### Submission Fee
**ENFORCED.** A non-refundable submission fee of US$125 is required for all new unsolicited manuscripts at the point of submission. This is mandatory and precedes peer review.

### Abstract
Unstructured plain prose. No word limit stated in the guidelines; observed abstracts run approximately 150–200 words. JEL classification codes and Keywords are listed as separate fields below the abstract. **ENFORCED** (JEL codes and keywords confirmed in all articles examined).

### Highlights
**ENFORCED.** Elsevier requires a "Highlights" section: 3–5 bullet points, each no longer than 85 characters (including spaces), summarising the paper's key novel findings. These appear in the submission system and in the published record. This is in addition to the abstract and is not a substitute for it.

### Citations
Author-year (Elsevier Harvard style) throughout. In-text: (Author, Year) or Author (Year). Reference list: authors in **bold**, article title in plain quotation marks, journal name and volume in *italics*, followed by year, volume, and page or article number. Alphabetical ordering. **ENFORCED** (confirmed by Zotero CSL file and practice in articles).

### Headings
Arabic numerals; decimal subsections (1, 1.1, 1.2, 2, 2.1...). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Table and figure notes explain variable definitions, clustering, and sample restrictions (confirmed in practice).

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Generative AI tools may not be listed as authors.
- Authors must declare any use of generative AI in manuscript preparation.
- Inclusive language required: avoid bias, stereotypes, and culturally specific assumptions.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Reference List Format
**PRACTICE ONLY.** The Elsevier Harvard style gives author names in **bold** in the reference list, with journal name and volume in *italics*. FIELD_SKILL specifies author-year citation but does not prescribe reference list typography. Authors submitting from other style environments (Chicago, APA, numbered) must reformat to this style.

### Submission Barrier
**ENFORCED.** The $125 non-refundable submission fee is a pre-review hard requirement with no parallel in FIELD_SKILL. Payment is required before the manuscript enters the editorial queue.

### Highlights
**ENFORCED.** A stand-alone Highlights field (3–5 bullets, ≤85 characters each) is mandatory in the Elsevier submission system. FIELD_SKILL does not address this element. It is distinct from the abstract and typically distils the paper's empirical or theoretical contribution into plain-language claims.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Forgetting the submission fee.** The $125 must be paid before the manuscript is processed. Authors unaware of this may be surprised at the submission portal and should budget for it.

2. **Omitting Highlights.** Many non-Elsevier authors first encounter the Highlights field at the point of upload. Preparing 3–5 punchy ≤85-character bullets in advance prevents last-minute drafting of inadequate summaries.

3. **Wrong reference format.** Authors converting from numbered or Chicago-style manuscripts frequently omit bold author names and italicisation of journal name and volume. The Elsevier Harvard CSL file (available via Zotero) automates this.

4. **Missing JEL codes.** JEL codes are required and are a separate mandatory submission field; they are not embedded in the abstract. Omitting them will cause a submission error.
