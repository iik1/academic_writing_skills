# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Banking and Finance

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Broad empirical and theoretical research on banking, financial markets, corporate finance, risk, and financial regulation; spans academic and policy-oriented work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-banking-and-finance/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 ("Fifty shades of QE: Robust evidence", doi:10.1016/j.jbankfin.2023.x; "Risk-taking incentives and risk-talking outcomes", doi:10.1016/j.jbankfin.2023.x)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Required manuscript components (Elsevier standard): Title page, Abstract, Keywords, Highlights, main body, References, and — where applicable — a Data availability statement and supplementary material. The title page must be separate from the manuscript for double-blind review.

**ENFORCED**

### Length
No explicit word or page limit is stated in the author guidelines. **GUIDELINE ONLY** (absence of limit, not a constraint).

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. Word limit not specified in the guidelines; spot-checked articles run approximately 200 words. No structured sub-headings within the abstract. **ENFORCED (format); GUIDELINE ONLY (length).**

### Citations
Author-year (Harvard) format throughout, consistent with field norm. Confirmed in article spot-check: "(Author, Year)" in-text. **FIELD DEFAULT**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Alt-text descriptions required for figures and images. Otherwise follows FIELD_SKILL norm. **ENFORCED (alt text); otherwise FIELD DEFAULT.**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors.
- Simultaneous submission to other journals is prohibited.
- Previously published work (excluding preprints) may not be resubmitted.
- Authorship changes after acceptance are prohibited without formal approval via an Authorship Change Request form with all co-authors' written consent.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Highlights — Mandatory Elsevier Submission Element

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not mention highlights; they are not a feature of ABS 4*/4 journals outside the Elsevier ecosystem.

**This journal:** A "Highlights" element is a mandatory separate submission component for all Elsevier journals including JBF. Format: 3–5 bullet points; each bullet should be a self-contained finding or contribution statement, maximum approximately 85 characters including spaces. These are displayed on the ScienceDirect article page and indexed separately. Spot-checked articles showed approximately 5 bullets per article.

**Classification: ENFORCED**

### Submission Fee

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address submission fees; leading ABS 4*/4 journals (JF, JFE, RFS) do not charge submission fees.

**This journal:** USD 350 non-refundable fee per unsolicited new submission. Papers rejected by the editors that are resubmitted are treated as new submissions and incur a second fee. Discount codes may be available on request. This is a hard submission barrier with no equivalent at 4*/4 level.

**Classification: ENFORCED**

### Double-Blind Review

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify review format; leading ABS 4*/4 journals in finance typically use single-blind review.

**This journal:** Double anonymized (double-blind) peer review is required. Authors must remove identifying information (name, affiliation, acknowledgments, identifying citations to own work) from the manuscript file before submission.

**Classification: ENFORCED**

### Inclusive Language

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not include a language equity requirement.

**This journal:** The guidelines explicitly require inclusive language use and recommend reporting of sex- and gender-based analyses where relevant. Authors are expected to avoid language that implies assumptions about gender, disability, ethnicity, or other identity characteristics.

**Classification: ENFORCED**

### Data Availability Statement

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that data disclosure and replication packages are "routinely required or expected" at ABS 4*/4 level as a general trend.

**This journal:** A formal Data Availability Statement linking to datasets is a required manuscript component, not merely expected. Must be included in the submitted manuscript file.

**Classification: ENFORCED** (more explicit and mandatory than the FIELD_SKILL baseline implies)

### AI Use Declaration

**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that pre-registration and ethics statements are growing in experimental papers but does not address generative AI.

**This journal:** If generative AI tools are used in manuscript preparation, authors must include a declaration statement in the manuscript body with the exact form: "During preparation of this work the author(s) used [TOOL] in order to [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the author(s) reviewed and edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of the publication."

**Classification: ENFORCED**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Omitting the Highlights file.** Unlike ABS 4*/4 journals, JBF requires a separate Highlights submission element. Authors coming from JF/JFE/RFS have no habit of drafting these. Each bullet must be under ~85 characters and stand alone as a meaningful finding.

2. **Submitting without removing author identity.** Double-blind review is mandatory. JF and JFE use single-blind review; authors familiar with those outlets may overlook anonymization, including acknowledgments and self-citations phrased as "In our prior work (Smith, 2023), we showed…."

3. **Forgetting the USD 350 fee.** The submission fee applies to every new submission attempt, including resubmissions of previously rejected papers. Rejected papers resubmitted are not treated as revisions.

4. **Skipping the Data Availability Statement.** At ABS 4*/4 journals this is often added in production; at JBF it is a required manuscript element from submission. Omitting it will likely trigger a technical check rejection.
