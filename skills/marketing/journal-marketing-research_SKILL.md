# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Marketing Research

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** MKT
- **Publisher:** SAGE Publishing (American Marketing Association)
- **Scope:** Publishes articles representing the entire spectrum of marketing topics, welcomes diverse theoretical perspectives and methodological approaches, emphasizes methodological rigor and novel analytical techniques
- **Guidelines URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/mrj (defers to AMA Submission Guidelines)
- **Field baseline:** mkt-field-writing-style

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure

**File organization:** Minimum 2 files required at submission
- File 1: Title page (separate, with author information)
- File 2: Main document with structure: Title → Abstract → Keywords → Main Text → References → Appendices
- File 3 (optional): Web Appendix as single PDF

**Opening section:** Do not label opening commentary as "Introduction." Opening commentary must not carry a section heading. **[ENFORCED - explicit prohibition in guidelines]**

**Section organization:** Otherwise follows FIELD_SKILL norm (study-by-study for behavioral work, Data → Model → Results for quantitative work).

### Length

**Maximum:** 50 pages, properly formatted and **inclusive** of title, abstract, keywords, text, footnotes, references, tables, figures, and print appendices. Web appendices excluded from this count. **[ENFORCED]**

**Title:** Must not exceed 25 words. **[ENFORCED]**

**Footnotes:** Each footnote must not exceed 40 words; maximum 10 footnotes total. **[ENFORCED]**

### Abstract

**Format:** Unstructured (consistent with FIELD_SKILL)

**Word Limit:** 200 words maximum (FIELD_SKILL says 150–250; JMR fixes at 200) **[ENFORCED]**

**Voice:** Must be written in third person (e.g., "The authors find that…" rather than "We find that…"). This differs from the main text, which uses first-person plural. **[ENFORCED - delta from main text convention]**

**Keywords:** Up to 8 primary keywords required **[ENFORCED]**

### Citations

**Style:** APA format, consistent with FIELD_SKILL baseline

**In-text format:** (Author Year) or Author (Year); specific pages as (Author Year, p. 112)

**Multiple authors in-text:** Up to three authors, list all names; four or more authors, use first author + "et al." from first citation (FIELD_SKILL says behavioral work uses "et al." for three or more; JMR threshold is four or more) **[ENFORCED - minor delta]**

**Reference list:** Alphabetical by first author's last name. Use full first names, not initials. For works with up to 20 authors, include all names in reference list; use "et al." for 21+ authors. **[ENFORCED]**

**No limit:** No restriction on number of references (consistent with FIELD_SKILL)

### Headings

**Hierarchy:** Maximum three levels permitted. **[ENFORCED]**

- **PRIMARY:** Centered, title-style capitalization, bold, with extra return before and after
- **SECONDARY:** Flush left, title-style capitalization, boldface and italics. Must have at least two sections with secondary headings per primary section (cannot have a single secondary heading standing alone)
- **TERTIARY:** Left justified, sentence-style capitalization, italics

**Section numbering:** Sections must not be numbered. Do not refer to sections as "Section No." in the text. **[ENFORCED - differs from some quantitative outlets that use numbered sections]**

### Tables and Figures

**Table placement:** Tables must be placed within the text at appropriate locations, not grouped at the end. **[ENFORCED - differs from some submission systems]**

**Numbering:** Tables and figures numbered consecutively in order of first mention (consistent with FIELD_SKILL)

**Titles:** Must reflect the substantive take-away, not generic labels. Example: Use "Effect of Framing on Purchase Intentions" not "Study 1 Results" **[ENFORCED]**

**Table-specific requirements:**
- Designate units (%, $, n) in column headings
- Notes cued by lowercase superscript letters at bottom
- Must be editable in Word (not inserted as pictures) **[ENFORCED]**
- **Include actual p-values, not asterisks** **[ENFORCED - major delta from FIELD_SKILL quantitative norm]**
- Include standard errors of parameter estimates **[ENFORCED]**

**Figure-specific requirements:**
- Use Arial font whenever possible
- Label both axes with Headline-Style Capitalization
- **All bar graphs must include error bars** with specification in notes of what they represent (SE or SD) **[ENFORCED]**
- Place calibration tick marks outside axis lines
- Avoid red-green and blue-purple color combinations (colorblind accessibility)
- Upon acceptance: submit original Excel/PowerPoint files, or PDF/EPS if exported from original application
- Resolution: photographs/screenshots minimum 300 ppi/dpi; line drawings minimum 800 ppi/dpi

### Statistical Reporting

**Mandatory requirements** (major delta from some FIELD_SKILL conventions):

- Report **actual p-values** (e.g., p = .031) rather than thresholds (p < .05) or asterisks (*, **, ***) throughout manuscript and in all tables and figures **[ENFORCED - overrides FIELD_SKILL quantitative convention of using asterisks]**
- Report standard errors (SE) of all parameter estimates in tables
- Report effect sizes for all analyses
- Include error bars on all graphs and specify what they represent (SE or SD) in figure notes

**Format specifics:**
- Standard deviation abbreviated as SD, standard error as SE
- Do not place zero before decimal points: .97 not 0.97
- Leave one space on either side of operational signs: β = .45 not β=.45
- Do not use more than three decimal places
- Every in-text citation must match reference list (identical spelling and year)

### Explicit Prohibitions

- Do not submit via mail or email (ScholarOne only) **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not add headers/footers on pages **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not number manuscript pages upon submission (ScholarOne adds these) **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not use more than three heading levels **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not use gendered or biased language **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not list AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) as authors **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not insert tables as pictures **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not use light colors (e.g., yellow) against light backgrounds in figures **[ENFORCED]**
- Do not use single secondary heading within a primary section (must have at least two) **[ENFORCED]**
- Avoid Word's Equation Editor, MathType, or LaTeX formatting for simple in-line mathematical symbols **[ENFORCED]**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Analysis limited to guidelines due to paywall preventing article access.
> Deltas classified as ENFORCED (stated in guidelines) or PRACTICE ONLY (observed in articles).

### Statistical Reporting

**FIELD_SKILL quantitative norm:** Regression tables present significance with asterisks (*** p < 0.01, ** p < 0.05, * p < 0.1)

**JMR requirement:** All tables and figures must report actual p-values (e.g., p = .023, p = .147) rather than asterisks or thresholds. This applies to both quantitative and behavioral work. **[ENFORCED - major delta]**

**Why:** Exact p-values provide more information for meta-analyses and allow readers to apply their own significance thresholds.

**How to apply:** In all regression tables, ANOVA tables, and figure notes, report the specific p-value. Replace asterisk systems with p-value columns or inline notation.

### Voice and Person (Abstract Only)

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** First-person plural ("we") throughout paper including abstract (though FIELD_SKILL notes abstracts have "no citations" but doesn't specify person)

**JMR requirement:** Abstract must be written in third person ("The authors find…", "The research demonstrates…", "This study shows…") while main text uses first-person plural ("we"). **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Use third-person constructions in the 200-word abstract, then switch to "we" starting with the unlabeled opening section.

### Opening Section Label

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify whether "Introduction" label is used

**JMR requirement:** Opening commentary must not be labeled "Introduction" or carry any section heading. The paper begins with title and abstract, followed immediately by unlabeled text. **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Delete any "Introduction" or "1. Introduction" heading. First primary heading appears when transitioning to Theoretical Background, Literature Review, or conceptual development section.

### Page Limit Specificity

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** States typical length as 8,000–15,000 words for empirical papers

**JMR requirement:** Hard cap of 50 pages including title, abstract, keywords, text, footnotes, references, tables, figures, and print appendices. Web appendices excluded. **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Format manuscript per AMA guidelines (12-point Times New Roman, 1-inch margins, double-spaced text) and count total pages. If over 50, move supplementary material to web appendix or trim content.

### Section Numbering

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Notes that Marketing Science uses numbered sections (economics style) but doesn't specify for other outlets

**JMR requirement:** Sections must not be numbered. Do not use "Section 1," "Section 2," etc., or refer to sections by number in text. **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Use descriptive section names only. Refer to sections by name ("As discussed in the Theoretical Background section…") not by number.

### Heading Hierarchy Limit

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify maximum heading levels

**JMR requirement:** Maximum three heading levels (primary, secondary, tertiary). **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** If manuscript uses four or more heading levels, consolidate or restructure. Most common structure: Primary = major sections (Theoretical Background, General Discussion); Secondary = subsections (H1, H2, Study 1, Study 2); Tertiary = sub-subsections (Methods, Results, Robustness Checks).

### Table Placement

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Does not specify table placement

**JMR requirement:** Tables must be placed within text at the point of first reference, not grouped at end of document. **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Insert each table immediately after the paragraph that first references it, or at the top of the next page if mid-paragraph.

### Error Bars Requirement

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** States "figures illustrating the interaction are common" but doesn't mandate error bars

**JMR requirement:** All bar graphs must include error bars. Figure notes must specify what error bars represent (SE or SD). **[ENFORCED]**

**How to apply:** Add error bars to all bar charts. In figure note, state "Error bars represent ±1 SE" or "Error bars represent ±1 SD."

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Using asterisks for significance:** JMR requires actual p-values throughout. Replace all *, **, *** systems with exact p-values (p = .003, p = .042, etc.). This is the most common rejection-triggering formatting error.

2. **Labeling the opening section "Introduction":** The guidelines explicitly prohibit this. Start the paper with title/abstract/keywords, then begin unlabeled text. First heading appears at Theoretical Background or equivalent.

3. **First-person in abstract:** Abstract must use third person ("The authors…", "This research…") even though main text uses "we." Many authors mistakenly apply the main-text voice convention to the abstract.

4. **Exceeding 50 pages:** The page limit is hard and includes everything except web appendices. Authors often underestimate formatted page count. Format properly before final word count.

5. **Missing error bars on bar graphs:** All bar graphs require error bars with specification in notes. Reviewers will flag this immediately.

6. **Tables as images:** Tables must be editable Word tables. Screenshots or pasted graphics will be rejected by production staff.
