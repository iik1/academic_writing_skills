# Journal Writing Skill: Annual Review of Financial Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Annual Reviews
- **Scope:** Invited review articles synthesizing recent developments in financial economics, covering capital markets, corporate finance, financial institutions, market microstructure, and behavioral finance. Does not publish original empirical research.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.annualreviews.org/page/authors/general-information (requires authentication)
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Greenwood, Ialenti & Scharfstein 2025, Annu. Rev. Financ. Econ. 17:189–206, DOI 10.1146/annurev-financial-082123-105625; Dew-Becker & Giglio 2024, Annu. Rev. Financ. Econ. 16, NBER WP 31878 preprint)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
All articles are invited reviews. Standard back-matter order (confirmed in published article): main sections → DISCLOSURE STATEMENT → ACKNOWLEDGMENTS → LITERATURE CITED. Optional elements (Summary Points, Future Issues, Sidebars, Related Resources) may appear between main text and DISCLOSURE STATEMENT. **ENFORCED**

### Length
Set by editorial invitation letter on a per-article basis. No public word or page limit. **GUIDELINE ONLY**

### Abstract
Single unstructured paragraph. JEL codes and Keywords appear as separate metadata fields on the published page, not inside the abstract body — consistent with FIELD_SKILL. Word limit not publicly stated; observed range ~150–200 words. **ENFORCED**

### Citations
Author-year in-text, but format differs from FIELD_SKILL in two ways:
1. Uses "&" not "and" between co-authors: "(Greenwood & Scharfstein 2013)", "(Andonov, Bauer & Cremers 2017)"
2. No comma before year: "(Philippon 2015)" not "(Philippon, 2015)"
Multiple citations within parentheses separated by semicolons. **ENFORCED**

### Reference List
Section heading is "LITERATURE CITED" (not "References"). Format: LastName Initials. Year. Title. Abbrev.J. Volume:Pages — with no comma separating last name from initials. Journal names abbreviated throughout (e.g., J. Finance, Am. Econ. Rev., Rev. Financ. Stud., Q. J. Econ., J. Financ. Econ., J. Econ. Perspect., Annu. Rev. Econ.). Volume and pages joined by colon: "88:559–86". **ENFORCED**

### Headings
Level 1 headings in ALL CAPS with arabic numeral: "2. REVIEW OF RECENT LITERATURE". Level 2 in Title Case with dotted sub-numbering: "2.1. Cost of Intermediation". Unnumbered back-matter headings (DISCLOSURE STATEMENT, ACKNOWLEDGMENTS, LITERATURE CITED) also in ALL CAPS. **ENFORCED**

### Tables and Figures
Submitted as separate files. Tables placed after LITERATURE CITED or in a separate file. Figures submitted individually with captions; a combined-figures PDF also required. **GUIDELINE ONLY** (no table-design norms observed that differ from field practice)

### Explicit Prohibitions
None stated publicly beyond standard editorial quality thresholds.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Genre — FUNDAMENTAL DEVIATION

**ENFORCED.** All articles are invited literature surveys. This is the most consequential deviation: the entire FIELD_SKILL framework assumes original empirical research, and nearly all its norms are genre-dependent.

Specific consequences (all RELAXATION relative to FIELD_SKILL):
- **Introduction structure:** No preview of an identification strategy, no preview of quantitative findings with specific numbers, no numbered contribution list. Review introductions instead: motivate the topic's importance, define the scope of the survey, and close with a roadmap of the review's structure. Contribution claims ("we survey," "we organize," "we document patterns across") are modest and scope-defining rather than priority-asserting.
- **Results and reporting:** Regression tables and coefficient narration don't apply. Primary vehicle is narrative synthesis of prior literature. Statistical results from cited papers are described in prose; authors do not report their own estimates. FIELD_SKILL norms on economic significance, standard-error clustering, and significance notation are irrelevant.
- **Robustness sections:** Absent. Not applicable to review articles.
- **Mechanism sections:** Replaced by thematic organization of the surveyed literature.

### Disclosure Statement

**ENFORCED.** A mandatory section titled "DISCLOSURE STATEMENT" appears after the final main-text section and before ACKNOWLEDGMENTS. Authors disclose conflicts of interest, funding sources, board memberships, advisory roles, and consulting relationships. This section is required even when there are no conflicts to report (the statement then reads that the authors are unaware of relevant affiliations). FIELD_SKILL has no equivalent requirement.

### Citation Format

**ENFORCED.** Deviates from FIELD_SKILL in two respects: (1) "&" replaces "and" between co-authors in all citation contexts; (2) no comma between author name and year. These are publisher-level requirements applied across all Annual Reviews journals.

### Reference List Format

**ENFORCED.** The abbreviated journal-title system and "LastName Initials. Year." format (without commas) deviate from the author-year reference style typical in finance journals. Finance journals generally use full journal names and comma-separated author-year references; Annual Reviews uses abbreviated names and a compact no-comma format.

### Heading Capitalization

**ENFORCED.** FIELD_SKILL does not specify heading capitalization. Annual Reviews requires Level 1 headings in ALL CAPS. This is a hard publisher-level constraint visible in all published articles.

### Optional Structural Insertions

**GUIDELINE ONLY.** Annual Reviews author guidelines describe several optional elements not found in FIELD_SKILL: Summary Points (up to 8 complete sentences summarizing key take-aways), Future Issues (up to 8 complete sentences indicating open questions), Sidebars (50–200 words on an adjacent topic, inserted below LITERATURE CITED with an in-text callout), Terms and Definitions (margin glossary entries), and Related Resources (up to 10 references to non-academic materials such as datasets or websites). These elements were not used in the observed published article but appear in the author handbook as standard options. Authors should consider whether Summary Points and Future Issues add value for their survey.

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural "we" for multi-authored reviews; active voice)
- Formality and register: FIELD DEFAULT (high; confident declarative prose)
- Hedging vocabulary: FIELD DEFAULT ("suggest," "consistent with," "we interpret" for mechanisms)
- JEL codes and keywords: FIELD DEFAULT (separate metadata fields, not embedded in abstract)
- Roadmap sentence: FIELD DEFAULT (introduction closes with a roadmap of sections)
- Section numbering system: FIELD DEFAULT (arabic numerals)
- Figure citation in text: FIELD DEFAULT (bold inline reference to figures and tables)
- Paragraph density and sentence length: FIELD DEFAULT

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Treating this as an empirical research outlet.** Submitting or drafting a paper structured around new data, identification strategies, and robustness tables is a categorical mismatch. All articles are invited surveys; the request for a review comes before writing begins.

2. **Using "and" in citations or commas before years.** FIELD_SKILL's "(Fama and French, 1993)" is wrong here; the correct form is "(Fama & French 1993)". Easy to miss on revision.

3. **Omitting the Disclosure Statement.** Authors accustomed to standard finance journals may forget this required section. It must appear even when conflicts are absent.

4. **Framing introductions as empirical-paper introductions.** A FIELD_SKILL-style introduction — previewing identification strategy, listing numbered contributions with specific coefficients — is out of register for a review article. The introduction should scope the literature and announce the survey's organizing structure.
