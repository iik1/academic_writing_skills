# Journal Writing Skill: Transportation Research, Part A: Policy and Practice

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SECTOR (Tourism, Transport and Hospitality)
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Interdisciplinary transport policy and practice journal. Papers may use any disciplinary lens (economics, engineering, psychology, sociology, urbanism) but must have a clear policy concern or be of direct interest to practitioners. All methodological cultures are welcome; the policy relevance requirement is non-negotiable.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/transportation-research-part-a-policy-and-practice/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** tourism-transport-hospitality-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Liao, Luo & Spiller, "A tale of two investments: Charging stations and purchase subsidies for EV adoption", TRA vol. 202, 2025; preprint: RFF Working Paper WP 24-12, July 2024)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for full research articles. No mandatory section order beyond abstract and references is specified in accessible guidelines. See Observed Deltas for practice-level structural patterns in policy-economics papers.

### Length
No word or page limit specified in accessible guidelines.

### Abstract
Unstructured plain paragraph. Maximum 250 words. Must stand alone; avoid references. If references are essential, cite author(s) and year only. No structured field labels. **Label: ENFORCED**

### Citations
Author-year (Elsevier Harvard). In-text format: (Smith, 2020), (Smith and Jones, 2020), (Smith et al., 2020). Multiple citations separated by semicolons. Reference list: sentence-case article titles; full journal names; year in parentheses after author list. Consistent with FIELD_SKILL author-year norm but reference list formatting follows Elsevier Harvard rather than APA conventions. **Label: ENFORCED**

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables and figures numbered sequentially; notes placed below each table.

### Highlights
Required at submission. Standard Elsevier format: 3–5 bullet points, each a maximum of 85 characters including spaces. Capture novel results and new methods. **Label: ENFORCED**

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors.
- Authorship may not be changed after acceptance.
- Simultaneous submission to another journal is not permitted.
- Authors must use inclusive language free from bias regarding age, gender, race, ethnicity, or other personal attributes.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Full research articles have separate Introduction, Literature Review, Method, Results, Discussion, and Conclusion sections. Discussion and Conclusion are typically separate. Limitations and Future Research are expected as explicit components, with 2–3 concrete limitations.

**This journal (RELAXATION — ABS 3):** Policy-oriented economics papers observed in the journal frequently omit a standalone Discussion section; results are discussed inline within the Results section or integrated into the final section. The concluding section is commonly labelled "Conclusion and Policy Implications" (or "Concluding Remarks and Policy Implications"), merging what FIELD_SKILL treats as separate sections. Formal Limitations and Future Research paragraphs are absent or minimal in published economics-style papers: limitations may be noted in passing in the results discussion rather than collected into a distinct subsection.

Additionally, policy-economics papers commonly include a standalone **Policy Background** or **Institutional Background** section positioned after the introduction and before the data and methods. This section contextualises the policy environment, relevant legislation, or programme design that motivates the study — a structural move not present in the FIELD_SKILL canonical sequence. **Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

### Policy Implications

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** Both theoretical and practical implications are expected; weight varies by subfield.

**This journal:** Because all accepted papers must have a clear policy concern, policy implications are not optional — they are load-bearing. In practice, policy implications are woven into the conclusion rather than relegated to a brief closing paragraph. Papers that bury implications at the end risk desk rejection on scope grounds. **Label: PRACTICE ONLY**

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing policy connection.** The journal's scope requirement is firm: papers must demonstrate a clear policy concern or practical relevance. A methodologically strong paper with no link to policy or practitioner decision-making is likely to fail desk review. Policy implications must appear in the substance of the paper, not as a token closing sentence.

2. **Submitting without Highlights.** Highlights are required at submission, not optional. Omitting them will delay processing. Each bullet must be ≤85 characters including spaces.

3. **Abstract exceeds 250 words or uses structured fields.** The abstract must be a single unstructured paragraph within the 250-word limit. Structured labels (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions) are not used in this journal.

4. **Applying ABS 4 structural expectations rigidly.** At ABS 3, the formal separation of Discussion, Conclusion, Limitations, and Future Research sections is not enforced across all methodological cultures. Economics-style papers in this journal routinely merge these into a combined concluding section without penalty. Imposing FIELD_SKILL's ABS 4 structural norms may produce a stilted structure that diverges from published practice.
