# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Environmental Economics and Management

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Environmental and resource economics; publishes empirical, theoretical, and methodological work of broad interest to the field, not limited to a single region or method.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-environmental-economics-and-management/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (De Cian et al. 2025, 10.1016/j.jeem.2025.103122; Isaksen & Johansen 2025, 10.1016/j.jeem.2025.103154 — abstracts and metadata accessed via ScienceDirect and IDEAS/RepEC; full text inaccessible due to paywall)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandated section order for regular articles. Required manuscript components: title page, abstract, keywords, highlights, main body, references. Graphical abstract is optional. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for body section architecture.

### Length
Regular articles: no explicit word limit stated in guidelines.
Notes and Short Papers: maximum 6,000 words excluding tables, figures, and appendices; maximum 5 combined tables and figures, each limited to one page; appendices published online only and not subject to a length cap. **[ENFORCED]**

### Abstract
No stated word limit or structured-field format in the guidelines. Abstracts in observed articles are unstructured prose. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for content (results announced, quantitative where possible).

### Citations
Not explicitly specified in the guidelines beyond a note that references in short papers "should be kept concise." Observed articles use standard author-year parenthetical format consistent with the FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Regular articles: no stated limit. Notes and Short Papers: maximum 5 combined, each limited to a single page. **[ENFORCED for Notes]**

### Explicit Prohibitions
The journal will not consider: book reviews; literature reviews; policy briefs; papers that merely replicate previously identified empirical relationships; papers that apply established methods to new case studies without novel contribution; theoretical analyses that only extend results from well-known models. Work must be both novel and of broad interest to environmental and resource economists. **[ENFORCED]** AI tools may not be listed as authors or co-authors. **[ENFORCED]**

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Mandatory Highlights Section
**Field norm:** No such requirement; not part of the FIELD_SKILL baseline.
**JEEM practice:** Elsevier requires a Highlights section as a discrete manuscript component, submitted alongside the abstract. Highlights consist of short bullet-point statements summarising the article's core novel findings, intended for display on ScienceDirect. This is an Elsevier platform requirement, not an editorial style choice, but authors must prepare it for submission.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Notes and Short Papers Format
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL describes a single article format; no sub-format for brief papers is part of the baseline.
**JEEM practice:** A second submission category exists with binding constraints: ≤6,000 words (body only), ≤5 total exhibits (tables + figures), each exhibit ≤1 page, appendices relegated to online supplementary material. Short papers are peer-reviewed to the same standard as regular articles but with expedited handling. Must demonstrate "significant contribution comparable to regular-length papers."
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Scope-Based Rejection Criteria
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address editorial scope policy.
**JEEM practice:** The guidelines explicitly name categories of work that will be desk-rejected: pure replications, methods-to-new-cases applications, and incremental theoretical extensions. A paper must offer a genuinely novel empirical finding, an insightful theoretical analysis, or a creative methodology to pass editorial screening. This is a higher stated novelty bar than the implicit norms of many ABS 3 journals and should shape how contribution paragraphs are framed — the contribution must be identifiable as one of the three approved categories.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing Highlights.** Elsevier submission portals require Highlights as a separate field. Omitting them blocks submission. Prepare 3–5 bullet points (typically ≤85 characters each) summarising key novel findings before uploading.

2. **Desk rejection for contribution framing.** JEEM's explicit prohibition on "established methods applied to new cases" means that contribution paragraphs framed as "we apply X method to Y context" without an additional novel element will fail editorial screening regardless of execution quality. Frame contributions as novel findings, new identification strategies, or methodological innovations.

3. **Notes word count misapplication.** The 6,000-word cap for Notes excludes tables, figures, and appendices but includes all body text including footnotes. Appendices must be designated as online-only supplementary material, not included in the main PDF at submission.

4. **No JEL code requirement stated, but codes appear universally.** JEL classification codes are not mentioned in the author guidelines but are present in all published articles (confirmed via IDEAS/RepEC metadata for multiple 2025 articles). Omitting them is likely to require revision at the editorial stage; include them with the abstract.
