# Journal Writing Skill: American Journal of Sociology

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4*
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** University of Chicago Press
- **Scope:** Presents pathbreaking work from all areas of sociology, with emphasis on theory building and innovative methods. Publishes six issues per year; covers the full methodological spectrum of the discipline.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/instruct

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section order is mandated. Manuscript assembly order for submission: abstract → text → references → notes (if using endnotes rather than footnotes) → tables → figures. Tables are collected at the back, not placed where cited in text. [ENFORCED]

### Length
No formal word-count limit. Papers over 18,000 words may prompt referees to request extended review time. Authors are encouraged to be as concise as possible. [GUIDELINE ONLY — soft threshold, not a hard cap]

### Abstract
Unstructured; target length ~150 words (hard cap: excess will be trimmed by the editorial office before production). Must include: (1) the research question or puzzle, (2) identification of the data used, and (3) some indication of findings. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
ASA (American Sociological Association) author-date style: parenthetical in-text citations in the form (LastName Year) or (LastName Year:PageNumber); full reference list titled "References" at end of text. Article and book-chapter titles enclosed in quotation marks; journal titles italicised. Headline-style capitalisation for all titles (all words capped except articles, coordinating conjunctions, and prepositions). [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Subheads must be kept concise. Two-part subheads separated by a colon are explicitly prohibited. If a section is subdivided, it must contain at least two sub-entries (e.g., a section with sub-parts A and B, not a lone sub-part A). [ENFORCED]

### Tables and Figures
Tables collected at the back of the manuscript (after references and any endnotes), not inserted at point of citation. Alt text must be provided with every figure caption at submission; this is a required accessibility field and will appear in page proofs. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Two-part subheads separated by a colon are prohibited.
- A section may not be subdivided into fewer than two sub-entries.
- Abstracts exceeding ~150 words will be trimmed without author consultation before production.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where AJS deviates from or tightens the field baseline.

### Abstract Content Requirements
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that abstracts are standard but does not specify required content elements.
**AJS practice:** Abstract must explicitly cover three elements—research question or puzzle, data used, indication of findings. Omitting any element is a submission deficiency.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Notes Style
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not address footnote vs. endnote preference.
**AJS practice:** Both footnotes and endnotes are acceptable at submission. The acknowledgment note is placed on the cover page, marked with an asterisk, and excluded from numbered note sequence; substantive notes begin at "1". If endnotes are used, they appear after the reference list and before tables in the manuscript assembly.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Heading Conventions
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify constraints on subhead formatting.
**AJS practice:** Colon-separated two-part subheads are explicitly forbidden. Any subdivided section must carry at least two sub-entries (no lone A-level sub under a single section).
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Tables Placement
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify where tables sit in the manuscript file.
**AJS practice:** All tables collected at the back of the manuscript file (after references and endnotes), not embedded at the point of citation.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Data and Code Sharing
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes that data availability statements are "increasingly standard but not yet universal."
**AJS practice:** For all papers involving numerical data analysis, sharing of analytic code is required at acceptance or upon reviewer request. Authors must include a statement specifying whether data are shareable in whole or in part. Both requirements are mandatory, not optional.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Accessibility (Alt Text)
**Field norm:** Not addressed in FIELD_SKILL.
**AJS practice:** Alt text is a required submission element for every figure, supplied alongside the caption. It appears in page proofs for author review.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Over-length abstract.** AJS trims abstracts exceeding ~150 words without author input before production. Submit within the limit and ensure all three required elements (question, data, findings) are present in the trimmed version.
2. **Colon subheads.** Two-part subheads such as "Inequality and Firms: A Swedish Case Study" are explicitly prohibited. Revise to a single concise phrase before submission.
3. **Missing data/code statement.** Quantitative papers that omit the data-shareability statement and code-sharing commitment are non-compliant at submission. This applies to all numerical analysis, including secondary data work.
4. **Tables embedded in text.** AJS requires tables at the back of the file. Embedding tables at point of citation in the text body is a formatting error that will require correction before review.
