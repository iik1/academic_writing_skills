# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of International Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier
- **Scope:** Open-economy economics in the broadest sense: international trade, international macroeconomics, foreign direct investment, exchange rates, and related empirical and theoretical work.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Amiti, Duprez, Konings & Van Reenen, JIE 2024, 10.1016/j.jinteco.2024.103972; Redding & Weinstein, JIE 2024, 10.1016/j.jinteco.2023.103910)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section order is prescribed for standard articles. A distinct **Short Paper** category exists (see Length).

### Length
Two manuscript types:

**Regular articles:** No explicit word limit stated in the guidelines.

**Short Papers:** At most 6,000 words and at most 5 exhibits (tables or figures combined). Word count excludes title, abstract, acknowledgments, references, and exhibits. Authors must submit a separate word-count PDF generated using the AEA word-count tool (https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/word-count). [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured; must not exceed **150 words**. Should be standalone (no references unless essential; if used, cite author and year only). Avoid non-standard abbreviations. [ENFORCED]

### Keywords
1–7 keywords required. English only. [ENFORCED]

### JEL Codes
JEL classification codes appear in every published article. Not explicitly discussed in the accessible guideline text but consistently required via the submission system. [PRACTICE ONLY — treat as de facto required]

### Highlights
Authors are **encouraged** (not required) to provide 3–5 bullet points, each at most 85 characters including spaces, capturing novel results and any new methods. Submitted as a separate element. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

### Citations
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (author-year parenthetical). Confirmed in spot-checked articles.

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm.

### Tables and Figures
Short Papers: combined exhibits capped at 5. Regular articles: no explicit cap stated.

### Replication and Data
All results must be replicable. Authors are required to deposit all materials (data, code) in **JIE's own secure repository** — not as generic supplementary files. [ENFORCED]

### AI Disclosure
If generative AI tools were used in manuscript preparation, a declaration is mandatory and must appear **before the reference list** in the published article. AI tools may not be listed as authors. [ENFORCED]

### Funding Disclosure
Required: list funding sources or explicitly state that no specific grant was received. [ENFORCED]

### Simultaneous Submission
Prohibited. No authorship changes permitted after acceptance. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools listed as authors
- Submission to other journals while under review
- Authorship changes post-acceptance

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations
**Short Paper category:** JIE operates a named Short Paper track with a hard 6,000-word / 5-exhibit ceiling and a mandatory word-count PDF submission. FIELD_SKILL has no analogue for a formally defined short-paper type with these mechanics. Authors targeting this track must use the AEA word-count tool and submit the count as a separate PDF. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL does not specify abstract word limits.
**JIE:** Hard cap of 150 words. [ENFORCED]

### Replication Infrastructure
**Field norm:** FIELD_SKILL notes replication/robustness as an expected component but specifies no repository.
**JIE:** Code and data must go to JIE's own secure repository—not author websites, Dataverse, or ICPSR—unless the editorial office specifies otherwise. [ENFORCED]

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns
**Highlights:** Elsevier-standard bullet-point highlights (3–5 items, ≤85 characters each) are encouraged at submission. Not a field-wide norm. Omitting them is permissible but including them is good practice for discoverability. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

**JEL codes:** Required in practice (present in every observed article); FIELD_SKILL explicitly excludes JEL requirements from its scope. Treat as mandatory. [PRACTICE ONLY]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding 150-word abstract.** The cap is tighter than many economists expect; ruthless editing is required. Unlike AER (which does not state a limit), JIE enforces this explicitly.

2. **Missing word-count PDF for Short Papers.** The submission system requires a separate PDF generated by the AEA word-count tool. Submitting without it delays desk review.

3. **Depositing replication materials in the wrong repository.** JIE uses its own secure repository. Uploading to Dataverse, GitHub, or a personal website does not satisfy the requirement.

4. **Omitting JEL codes.** The submission portal requires them even though they are not prominently described in the publicly visible author guidelines.
