# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Money, Credit and Banking

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** FINANCE
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell (on behalf of Ohio State University)
- **Scope:** Monetary economics, banking, financial institutions, and related macroeconomic topics. Primarily empirical and structural macro-finance; less equity/asset-pricing oriented than JF/JFE.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://economics.osu.edu/about-us/journal-money-credit-banking/submission-instructions
- **Field baseline:** finance-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Ben Zeev, Gunn & Khan, "Monetary News Shocks," JMCB 52(7), 2020, pp. 1793–1820)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No required section order specified. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for general structure.

### Length
Maximum 40 pages, inclusive of references, figures, and tables (but excluding any online appendix). The guideline states submissions that are "excessively long may be summarily rejected." This is a firm soft cap, not a suggestion. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No word limit specified in guidelines. Observed length in practice: approximately 100 words, notably shorter than the 150–250-word field norm. JEL codes and keywords appear as separate fields below the abstract, not within it. [PRACTICE ONLY for short length; no guideline specifies a word limit]

### Citations
**In-text format deviates from field norm.** FIELD_SKILL baseline uses a comma between author and year: "(Fama and French, 1993)." JMCB requires no comma: "Barro (1981)" or "(Barro 1981)." When citing within parentheses: "(see, for example, Gurkaynak et al. (2005) and Nakamura and Steinsson (2015))." [ENFORCED — stated in guidelines and confirmed in articles]

**Reference list format deviates substantially from field norm.** JMCB uses a distinctive colon-delimited format:

> Baker, S. R., Bloom, N. and Davis, S. J.: 2016, Measuring economic policy uncertainty, *The Quarterly Journal of Economics* **131**(4), 1593–1636.

Structure: `Last, F. M. and Last, F. M.: Year, Title, *Journal* **Vol**(Issue), pages.` The year follows a colon immediately after the author block. Journal name in italics; volume in bold; no parentheses around year. [ENFORCED]

**Explicit prohibition:** Footnotes must NOT be used for citation purposes. All citations must appear in text. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm (Arabic numerals, hierarchical subsections).

### Tables and Figures
- Tables placed on separate sheets at the end of the manuscript (after references).
- Tables must include descriptive titles and column headings.
- General footnotes use "Note:" or "Notes:"; specific footnotes use lowercase letters (a, b, c).
- **Significance notation: Guidelines prescribe only two asterisk levels: \* = 5%, \*\* = 1%.** No triple-asterisk level is specified. [GUIDELINE ONLY — practice contradicts this; see Observed Deltas below]
- Figures must be professional quality, black ink on white paper, with no typewritten lettering. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Footnotes may not be used for citations.
- Typewritten lettering in illustrations is prohibited.
- Excessively long manuscripts may be desk-rejected without review.

### Submission logistics (not stylistic, for reference)
- Submit PDF via http://editorialexpress.com/jmcb
- Submission fee: $150 (subscribers) / $200 (non-subscribers), paid before processing.
- Data sharing required; if data cannot be shared publicly, a data exemption must be requested at submission.
- 1.5 line spacing required.

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

### Citation Format
**Field norm:** Parenthetical citations use a comma between author and year: "(Fama and French, 1993)."
**JMCB:** No comma. Author name immediately followed by year in parentheses: "Barro (1981)" or "(Barro 1981)." When the author name appears in running text, the year alone is parenthesized: "Beaudry and Portier (2006) show that…"
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Reference List Format
**Field norm:** Author (Year). Title. *Journal* vol(issue), pages. [or similar APA-adjacent style]
**JMCB:** Uses a distinctive format with a colon separating authors from year, bold volume numbers, and no parentheses around the year. See Hard Constraints → Citations for the full template.
**Classification:** ENFORCED

### Statistical Reporting — Significance Notation
**Field norm (and JMCB guidelines):** JMCB guidelines specify only two asterisk levels (* = 5%, ** = 1%).
**Observed practice:** Published articles use three levels: \*\*\* = 1%, \*\* = 5%, \* = 10% — the inverse ordering from what the guidelines describe and using an additional level. Authors appear to follow the three-level convention despite the two-level guideline.
**Practical implication:** Submit with three-level notation (*** 1%, ** 5%, * 10%) as this matches published practice. The two-level guideline appears outdated.
**Classification:** Guidelines prescribe two levels (GUIDELINE ONLY); three-level practice is PRACTICE ONLY.

### Structural Deviation — Standalone "Related Literature" Section
**Field norm:** Related literature is either a labeled subsection within the introduction or integrated as narrative paragraphs; a standalone literature review section is essentially never used.
**JMCB practice:** At least some published articles place the literature review as a full top-level section (e.g., Section 2 "Related literature"), separate from and following the introduction. The introduction's closing roadmap then directs readers to this section explicitly.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY — not mentioned in guidelines; observed in articles.

### Abstract Length
**Field norm:** 150–250 words.
**JMCB observed:** Approximately 100 words in the spot-checked article. Guidelines do not specify a limit. Whether this is a systematic norm or paper-specific cannot be confirmed from one article; treat as a caution rather than a rule.
**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY (tentative; single observation)

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Comma in citations.** Authors accustomed to "(Fama and French, 1993)" must remove the comma: JMCB requires "(Fama and French 1993)" or "Fama and French (1993)." This is a hard guideline requirement.
2. **Wrong reference format.** The colon-delimited reference style with bold volume numbers is non-standard; pasting in references formatted for other journals will require reformatting.
3. **Length.** The 40-page cap including tables and references is strict. Papers that routinely run 50+ pages at JFE/JF need to be trimmed or rely heavily on an online appendix before submission.
4. **Footnote citations.** Any in-text citation placed in a footnote violates explicit guidelines. Footnotes are reserved for substantive comments only.
