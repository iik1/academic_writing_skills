# Journal Writing Skill: New Political Economy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Routledge (Taylor & Francis)
- **Scope:** Critical and international political economy; normative and analytical work on capitalism, state theory, global governance, and political economy of contemporary crises. Methodologically skewed toward theoretical argument, qualitative analysis, and critical essays; quantitative work rare.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/journals/cnpe20 (direct access to instructions page blocked; guidelines inferred from articles and secondary sources)
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 3 (Ban & Hasselbalch 2025, 10.1080/13563467.2024.2434469; Sparke & Williams 2024, 10.1080/13563467.2024.2304180; Lotze 2025, 10.1080/13563467.2025.2553690)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No mandated section sequence. Typical published structure: Introduction → thematic/analytical sections → Conclusion or research agenda. Section headings are unnumbered and title-case. A "Methodology" section appears in qualitative empirical work (Lotze 2025) but not in theoretical essays (Ban & Hasselbalch 2025; Sparke & Williams 2024).

### Length
Approximately 8,000 words (sourced from poliscijournals.com database; not directly verified against official guidelines). Observed articles range from 13–18 journal pages, consistent with this figure. **GUIDELINE ONLY** (unverified through official page).

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, approximately 150 words. A **KEYWORDS** field (typically 4–5 terms, semicolon-separated) is required on the title page alongside an **ARTICLE HISTORY** box (Received/Accepted dates). Confirmed across all three articles. **ENFORCED.**

### Citations
Author-year in parentheses throughout, consistent across all articles. Reference list format: Author, Initial(s), Year. Title. *Journal*, Volume(Issue), pages. doi:xxx. DOIs included as standard. No footnote-based citation system. **ENFORCED** (from practice; consistent with Taylor & Francis house style).

### Headings
Unnumbered, title-case. No observed use of decimal numbering (1.1, 1.2, etc.). **ENFORCED** (from practice).

### Tables and Figures
Tables used in qualitative empirical work where data summaries are relevant (e.g., think-tank corpus overview). Not used in theoretical essays. No journal-specific formatting rules identified beyond standard Taylor & Francis practice.

### Explicit Prohibitions
None identified (official guidelines page inaccessible).

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Voice and Register — Single-Author Practice
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** First-person singular "I" is fully acceptable for single-authored work and is used without apology.

**NPE practice:** The single-authored article in the sample (Lotze 2025) consistently uses "this paper" as the grammatical subject in place of "I" ("This paper seizes the pandemic...", "This paper studies the reactions...", "This paper asks how..."). First-person "I" is not visible. Co-authored articles use "we" throughout, consistent with FIELD_SKILL.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY. This pattern may reflect author preference rather than journal policy, but three articles across two authorial configurations show no first-person singular "I" anywhere.

**Implication for writers:** For sole-authored submissions, treat "this paper argues/shows/finds" as the safer default over "I argue/show/find."

### Methodological Orientation — Quantitative Norms Largely Inapplicable
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** Extensive norms for quantitative causal inference work (identification strategies, robustness checks, effect sizes, pre-registration, etc.) are specified for ABS 4 journals.

**NPE practice:** All three articles are theoretical or qualitative. Quantitative causal inference work does not appear in this journal's recent output. FIELD_SKILL's quantitative conventions (dedicated robustness section, statistical narration precision, confidence intervals) are not applicable to typical NPE submissions.

**Classification:** RELAXATION (ABS 3). NPE does not publish the type of work to which those norms apply; submitting a quantitative causal inference paper in the style prescribed by FIELD_SKILL would be atypical for this outlet.

### Footnotes
**Field norm (FIELD_SKILL):** No explicit norm stated for footnotes.

**NPE practice:** Footnotes observed in qualitative empirical work (Lotze 2025 uses numbered superscript footnotes for methodological caveats and sample details). Not observed in theoretical essays (articles 1 and 2). Footnotes are used sparingly for substantive asides, not for citations.

**Classification:** PRACTICE ONLY.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Submitting quantitative work:** NPE's published output is overwhelmingly qualitative and theoretical. A regression-based paper with a standard identification strategy is unlikely to match the journal's scope regardless of its technical quality.

2. **Using "I" in sole-authored work:** Observed practice avoids first-person singular; use "this paper" or restructure to "we" (if co-authored) to match journal convention.

3. **Over-structuring with numbered headings:** NPE uses plain title-case headings only; numbered section labels (1., 1.1, etc.) are not used and would look out of place.

4. **Abstract length creep:** The abstract is approximately 150 words. Exceeding this—common when writers try to summarise a complex argument—risks immediate desk rejection or revision requests. Include KEYWORDS as a separate field.
