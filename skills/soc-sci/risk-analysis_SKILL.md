# Journal Writing Skill: Risk Analysis

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 4
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Wiley-Blackwell, on behalf of the Society for Risk Analysis (SRA)
- **Scope:** Publishes original research, reviews, and commentaries on risk assessment, risk management, risk perception, and risk communication across all domains — human health, ecological, engineering, safety — spanning quantitative (probabilistic models, simulation, QRA) and qualitative/interpretive approaches.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/15396924/homepage/ForAuthors.html
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (Hamilton et al. 2024, DOI: 10.1111/risa.14318; Fu et al. 2024, DOI: 10.1111/risa.14137)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section sequence beyond what the research design requires. The article type label ("ORIGINAL ARTICLE", "PERSPECTIVE", etc.) appears at the top of the manuscript. No delta from FIELD_SKILL canon otherwise.

### Length
- Original research: fewer than 7,000 words, excluding references, tables, and figures. Contact the Managing Editor before submitting if exceptional circumstances warrant more. [ENFORCED]
- Perspectives: fewer than 3,500 words. [ENFORCED]
- Main text starts on page 3. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph. No labeled subheadings (e.g., no Background/Methods/Results/Conclusions fields). Confirmed in both spot-checked articles. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
APA style throughout. In-text: author–date in parentheses, e.g., "(Hamilton et al., 2024)". Multiple citations separated by semicolons. Reference list: APA format with journal titles in italics, volume, issue, page range, and DOI. [ENFORCED]

This is a hard constraint for authors from engineering or natural-science backgrounds accustomed to numbered/Vancouver reference styles.

### Headings
Main section headings: bold, ALL-CAPS, numbered ("1 | INTRODUCTION", "2 | METHODS"). Subsection headings: bold, title case, numbered ("1.1 | Study design"). The pipe separator between number and heading text is applied in the typeset version; submit with bold numbered headings in the required case. [ENFORCED — stated in guidelines, confirmed in articles]

End-matter headings (ACKNOWLEDGMENTS, AFFILIATIONS, REFERENCES, SUPPORTING INFORMATION) appear in all-caps and are not numbered.

### Tables and Figures
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. Tables and figures appear after the reference list in submitted manuscripts.

### Explicit Prohibitions / Requirements
- AI tool use (e.g., ChatGPT, LLMs) must be disclosed transparently in the Methods or Acknowledgements section if used in any part of manuscript preparation. Omission is prohibited. [ENFORCED]
- Data sharing is encouraged with citations to shared datasets and a data availability statement, but is not a hard requirement. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Statistical Reporting
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for quantitative work, with one reinforcement: uncertainty quantification is expected to be precise and substantive throughout, including explicit probabilistic notation where applicable (e.g., risk expressed as probability per person per year, DALYs). This is consistent with the risk analysis sub-field note already in FIELD_SKILL and is not a new norm, but authors from adjacent social-science traditions unfamiliar with quantitative risk methods should be aware that numeric uncertainty expression is standard practice here, not hedging.

No other delta from FIELD_SKILL.

## Dimensions With No Delta

- Voice and person: FIELD DEFAULT (first-person plural "we" throughout multi-authored work; confirmed in articles)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Word count**: The 7,000-word cap excludes references, tables, and figures, but authors from quantitative disciplines sometimes count loosely. Verify the word count against this definition before submission.
2. **Reference style**: Authors from engineering, public health, or natural-science backgrounds accustomed to numbered citation styles (IEEE, Vancouver) must reformat to APA. Numbered in-text citations are not accepted.
3. **Missing AI disclosure**: The requirement to disclose LLM/AI tool use in Methods or Acknowledgements is mandatory and newer; it is easily overlooked by authors using AI writing assistance.
4. **Heading case**: Main section headings must be ALL-CAPS (e.g., "INTRODUCTION", "CONCLUSIONS"). Using title case or sentence case for first-level headings violates the journal's stated convention.
