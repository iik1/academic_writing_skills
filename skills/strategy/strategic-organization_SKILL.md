## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Direct article spot-check was not possible (SAGE blocked all PDF/HTML access). Deltas below are drawn from documented journal practice and comparison with FIELD_SKILL baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** At ABS 4*/4, a dedicated Managerial Implications subsection is expected in most outlets; Discussion and Conclusions are typically merged.

**This journal:** As an ABS 3 journal with an organizational theory orientation, a stand-alone managerial implications subsection is not a standard expectation and is not consistently present in published work. Authors writing applied or practice-oriented pieces may include one, but it is not required by editorial practice. — RELAXATION

### Methodological and Scope Norms

**FIELD_SKILL norm:** The baseline acknowledges qualitative and mixed methods but situates the primary expectation around quantitative causal inference with explicit identification strategies.

**This journal:** Interpretive, critical, discourse-analytic, ethnographic, and process-based approaches are explicitly within scope and are not treated as methodological exceptions requiring extra justification. The journal's founding purpose was to carve out space for organizational theory perspectives that do not fit narrowly positivist strategy outlets. Qualitative process studies are a mainstream paper type, not a secondary category. — PRACTICE ONLY (documented in editorial statements and scope description, but cannot confirm no formal guideline statement was seen)

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**EXPRESS articles:** The journal operates an EXPRESS track, resulting in accelerated online-first publication ahead of full volume/issue assignment. EXPRESS status is visible in article metadata and titles prior to final publication. This is a submission pathway not present in FIELD_SKILL. — ENFORCED (confirmed from CrossRef metadata showing "EXPRESS:" prefix on recent articles)

**Large editorial collective:** As of 2026, the journal operates with 11 co-editors (confirmed from editorial announcement DOI 10.1177/14761270251411657). This is atypical for a STRAT journal and reflects the journal's deliberate pluralism. Manuscripts may be handled by editors with diverse methodological expertise; authors should not assume the handling editor has a positivist background.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Importing ABS 4*/4 positivist expectations:** Authors accustomed to SMJ or ASQ may over-design for causal identification at the expense of theoretical contribution. This journal values theoretical depth and process insight; elaborate quasi-experimental machinery is not a substitute for conceptual clarity.
2. **Adding a managerial implications section unnecessarily:** Inserting a Managerial Implications subsection to signal relevance is not expected and can read as padding. If practical relevance is genuine, integrate it into the conclusion rather than forcing a separate subsection.
3. **Treating method plurality as marginal:** Framing a qualitative or interpretive design as a limitation or as preliminary to future quantitative work misreads this journal's editorial purpose. Qualitative designs stand on their own terms here.
4. **Missing the EXPRESS track:** Authors prioritizing time-to-publication should be aware of the EXPRESS pathway, which provides faster online-first availability without waiting for issue assignment.
