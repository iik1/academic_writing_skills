# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Social Policy

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** SOC SCI
- **Publisher:** Cambridge University Press (on behalf of the Social Policy Association)
- **Scope:** Social policy and administration across all aspects; interdisciplinary; covers welfare states, social benefits, housing, health, disability, poverty, and family policy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://assets.cambridge.org/JSP/JSP_ifc.pdf (last updated November 2014)
- **Field baseline:** soc-sci-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 2 (metadata and abstracts only; full text inaccessible) — Cheetham et al. 2024 (DOI: 10.1017/s0047279424000333); Dahan & Tarshish 2024 (DOI: 10.1017/s0047279424000308)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
No prescribed section order or required headings. Guidelines direct authors to verify format against a recent issue. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm for section logic; the specific sequence is left to author judgment and methodology.

### Length
**6,000–8,000 words all-inclusive** — the word count explicitly covers the abstract, endnotes, and bibliography. Shorter articles may be accepted by prior arrangement with the editors. This ceiling is notably tight relative to many ABS 3 social science journals. [ENFORCED]

### Abstract
Unstructured prose, **100–200 words**, plus **up to six keywords**. The guideline explicitly requires that abstracts disclose the substantive argument or finding, not merely the article's subject matter or formal structure. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Harvard author-date style implied by bibliography format: references arranged alphabetically by author surname, then chronologically for multiple works by the same author. Full journal article titles required (not truncated); first and last page numbers required. Book entries include place of publication and publisher. In-text format confirmed as (Author, Year) by journal practice. [ENFORCED]

### Headings
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No journal-specific heading hierarchy rules stated; authors are directed to check a recent issue.

### Tables and Figures
- **Monochrome required for print**; colour figures may appear in the online version only (free), but colour in print incurs charges. Authors must state at submission whether colour is needed for meaning or aesthetic only. [ENFORCED]
- Tables must fit within **234 mm × 155 mm**.
- **No vertical lines between columns.** Horizontal lines limited to top of table, bottom of table, and below column headings only.
- Totals and percentages must be labelled; units must be identified.
- Tables and figures submitted as **separate attached files**, not embedded in the manuscript. [ENFORCED]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Discriminatory language (sexist, racist, disablist) is explicitly prohibited. Authors are referred to the **British Sociological Association (BSA) style guide** on language. The editorial team offers advice on terminology not covered by the BSA guide (e.g., age-related language). [ENFORCED]
- Simultaneous submission or prior publication: submission implies the paper is not under review elsewhere and has not been previously published. Related or prior working-paper versions must be explicitly acknowledged and detailed at submission. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Endnote Placement
FIELD_SKILL does not specify note placement or note type. JSP explicitly requires **numbered endnotes** (superscript in-text), placed in a block after the body text and before the references. Footnotes are not used. [ENFORCED — guideline; confirmed by journal practice]

### Post-Acceptance Engagement Requirements
After acceptance, contributors are asked for a **tweet of ≤140 characters** summarising the article and invited (not required) to write a **blog post of 300–1,000 words** for the journal. The blog is read by both co-editors and should not simply repeat the abstract. This is an unusual post-acceptance obligation not mentioned in FIELD_SKILL. [GUIDELINE ONLY — blog is invited, not mandatory; tweet is expected]

### Intra-Journal Dialogue
Guidelines explicitly encourage submissions that engage with, extend, or critique debates previously published in JSP. FIELD_SKILL describes constructive engagement with prior literature generally; JSP adds a specific editorial preference for articles in dialogue with its own published record. [GUIDELINE ONLY]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Exceeding the word budget.** At 6,000–8,000 words all-inclusive (abstract + notes + bibliography), a typical quantitative paper with 45–50 references can easily consume 800–1,000 words in references alone, leaving roughly 5,000–7,000 words for the article body. Plan the word budget before drafting, not after.

2. **Abstract describes structure, not findings.** The guideline explicitly warns against abstracts that state "this article examines X using Y method." The abstract must state the actual substantive argument or finding.

3. **Colour in tables or figures without flagging it.** Manuscripts submitted with colour tables or figures must declare at submission whether colour is required for meaning; undeclared colour in print-formatted submissions may require rework.

4. **Placing notes as footnotes.** JSP uses numbered endnotes placed after the body text and before the references; footnote formatting is incorrect for this journal.
