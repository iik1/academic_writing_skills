# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Public Economics

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ECON
- **Publisher:** Elsevier (ScienceDirect)
- **Scope:** Empirical and theoretical research in public economics, including taxation, public expenditure, fiscal federalism, public goods, and redistributive policy.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-public-economics/publish/guide-for-authors
- **Field baseline:** econ-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Bilicka, Dubinina, and Janský, "Fiscal Consequences of Corporate Tax Avoidance," accepted JPubE May 2026; accessed as CESifo WP 10415)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. No mandatory section order is imposed beyond standard scholarly requirements. A standalone appendix is expected but must supplement, not substitute for, the main text; in the Short Paper track, the main text and all exhibits must be fully self-contained without recourse to the appendix.

### Length
**Two submission tracks:**

- **Short Paper track:** Maximum **6,000 words** (approximately 15 pages double-spaced, 11pt font, 1-inch margins) with a hard cap of **five exhibits** (figures and tables combined). Appendix permitted but body must be self-contained. Select "Short Paper" article type at submission. [ENFORCED]
- **Regular track:** No explicit word limit is stated in the guidelines.

Authors targeting the Short Paper track should count words before submission; the 6,000-word ceiling and five-exhibit cap are strictly enforced.

### Abstract
Unstructured; no word limit specified in the guidelines. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Citations
Harvard author-year (parenthetical). Reference list arranged alphabetically. Reference format: Surname, I. [and Surname, I.] (Year). Title, *Journal* Volume(Issue): Pages. Journal titles may appear in full in submitted manuscripts; Elsevier applies the house reference template at proof stage. Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Headings
Arabic-numbered without trailing period ("1 Introduction", "4.2 Data", etc.). Follows FIELD_SKILL norm. [FIELD DEFAULT]

### Tables and Figures
Maximum five exhibits (figures and tables combined) for the Short Paper track. No stated maximum for regular submissions. Standard Elsevier figure-quality requirements apply. [ENFORCED for Short Paper; FIELD DEFAULT otherwise]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- AI tools must not be listed as an author or co-author. [ENFORCED]
- Authorship changes are not permitted after acceptance. [ENFORCED]
- Submission fee of **USD 250 (non-refundable)** is required per new submission; desk-rejected manuscripts are not refunded. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Short Paper Track — review process and revision norms** [ENFORCED]

Field norm (FIELD_SKILL): standard peer review with revise-and-resubmit rounds assumed.

This journal instead: The Short Paper track replaces the R&R stage with conditional acceptance. Resubmissions after conditional acceptance are not returned to peer reviewers; authors have four weeks to return revisions. Expected outcomes at initial review are desk rejection, outright rejection, or conditional acceptance — not R&R. This compresses the argument structure of a short paper: every claim must be defensible in the submitted draft, since there is no revision round to address reviewer concerns.

Academic standards are held identical to regular submissions despite the abbreviated review process.

### Other Journal-Specific Patterns

**Single-anonymized (single-blind) review** [GUIDELINE ONLY]

The guidelines state that author identities are visible to reviewers. This differs from the double-blind norm at some economics journals (e.g., AER, JPE) and means authors are not required to anonymize their manuscripts or suppress self-citations. No writing-style consequence, but relevant for submission preparation.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Short Paper word count.** The 6,000-word ceiling is firm. Authors writing a "short" paper should verify the count before submission; submitting an over-length manuscript will require a rewrite, and the $250 fee is non-refundable.

2. **Exhibit cap applies to figures and tables combined.** Five exhibits total, not five of each type. Papers with separate robustness and mechanism figures can exhaust the cap quickly; relegate supplementary exhibits to the appendix.

3. **Main text must be self-contained in the Short Paper track.** The body and exhibits cannot defer key results or identification arguments to the appendix. Readers should be able to evaluate the paper's claims without consulting appendix material.

4. **Non-refundable submission fee.** The $250 fee is charged per new submission and is not refunded for desk rejections. Authors should verify fit with the journal's scope before submitting; desk rejections are a stated expected outcome under the Short Paper track.
