# Journal Writing Skill: Journal of Business Finance and Accounting

## Journal Profile
- **ABS 2024 Rating:** 3
- **Field:** ACCOUNT
- **Publisher:** Wiley (John Wiley & Sons Ltd.)
- **Scope:** Broad empirical and theoretical research spanning financial accounting, management accounting, corporate finance, financial markets, and banking. Does not restrict to any single strand of the accounting or finance literature.
- **Guidelines URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14685957/homepage/forauthors.html
- **Field baseline:** account-field-writing-style
- **Articles spot-checked:** 1 (Chronopoulos, Rempoutsika & Wilson, DOI: 10.1111/jbfa.12738)

## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These override FIELD_SKILL where they conflict.

### Structure
Required submission order: (i) title, abstract, and keywords; (ii) main text; (iii) appendices; (iv) references; (v) figures and tables. A Data Availability Statement is required as a separate named section at the end of the main text, before references. [ENFORCED]

### Length
No stated word or page limit. Free Format applies.

### Abstract
Unstructured single paragraph, maximum 250 words, must contain the major keywords. Keywords and JEL classification codes are required as separate fields after the abstract. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Free Format submission: any consistent citation style is accepted at submission. Wiley reformats to journal style upon acceptance. At submission, references must include author names, journal/book title, article title, year, volume/issue or chapter, pagination, and DOI where available. [RELAXATION — no style enforced at submission; contrast with FIELD_SKILL's author-date specification]

### Headings
Publisher (Wiley) imposes the following format in published articles: Level-1 headings use Arabic numerals + vertical pipe + ALL CAPS (e.g., "1 | INTRODUCTION", "7 | CONCLUSION"). Subsection headings use decimal Arabic numerals + pipe + sentence case (e.g., "2.1 | Financial reporting quality in the banking industry"). Back matter (ACKNOWLEDGMENTS, DATA AVAILABILITY STATEMENT, REFERENCES) appears in ALL CAPS with no numbering. [PRACTICE ONLY — publisher-imposed, not stated in submission guidelines]

### Tables and Figures
Tables and figures submitted separately after references. Table notes carry variable definitions and standard error/significance level conventions. [Follows FIELD_SKILL norm otherwise.]

### Explicit Prohibitions
- Identifying author information must not appear in the main text file (double-blind review). [ENFORCED]
- Submission fee of USD 200 required at submission via ScholarOne; resubmission fee USD 200 for major revisions; no fee for minor revisions. [ENFORCED — unusual for accounting journals]
- ORCID iD required from all authors. [ENFORCED]

## Observed Deltas from FIELD_SKILL

> Only dimensions where this journal deviates from the field baseline.

### Structural Deviations

**Standalone Literature Review section.** FIELD_SKILL describes hypothesis development and literature review as integrated within a single "Hypothesis Development / Theory / Background" section. In JBFA practice, a dedicated numbered section labeled "LITERATURE REVIEW" (or equivalent) appears as a separate section between the Introduction and the Research Design/Methods section. Literature review content is not embedded in the Introduction or merged with hypothesis development. [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Conclusion without Discussion.** FIELD_SKILL states that Discussion and Conclusion are typically merged into one final section. In JBFA practice, the final section is titled "CONCLUSION" only, with no "Discussion" label. Mechanistic interpretation is handled within the Results section (as a subsection), not in a separate Discussion section. [PRACTICE ONLY]

**Data Availability Statement.** Required as a formally labeled section between Acknowledgments and References. FIELD_SKILL notes data statements are "increasingly required" but does not treat them as a fixed structural element. At JBFA this is non-negotiable. [ENFORCED]

### Citations
Free Format at submission relaxes the author-date requirement specified in FIELD_SKILL. Any internally consistent reference style is accepted. [RELAXATION — ABS 3 journal]

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Missing submission fee.** JBFA charges USD 200 at initial submission via ScholarOne — unusual among accounting journals. Failure to pay halts processing.
2. **Merging Literature Review into the Introduction or Hypothesis section.** JBFA practice treats Literature Review as a standalone numbered section. Submitting without it, or embedding all prior literature in the Introduction, departs from observed convention.
3. **Abstract over 250 words.** The 250-word limit is a hard constraint. Standard ABS 4* accounting abstracts often run longer; trim aggressively.
4. **Formatting references before acceptance.** Free Format means referencing need not conform to a specific style at submission. Spending time reformatting to APA or Chicago before acceptance is unnecessary.
