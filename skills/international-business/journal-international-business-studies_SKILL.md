## Hard Constraints (Author Guidelines)

> These are ENFORCED requirements from official JIBS submission guidelines.

### Structure

**ENFORCED**

Required end-matter order (each component begins on new page):
1. Endnotes
2. References
3. Figures
4. Tables
5. Appendices

Typical manuscript structure:
- Introduction (state objective, motivation, context)
- Literature review (limited to items with direct bearing on topic)
- Theoretical framework (for theoretical papers)
- Methodology/Empirical section
- Results
- Discussion/Conclusion

Long methodological details should be placed in separate appendix.

### Length

**ENFORCED**

- **Major contributions:** ~10,000 words (≈30 pages double-spaced)
- **Narrower contributions:** ~7,000 words

Word count **includes:** abstract, text, endnotes, references  
Word count **excludes:** tables, figures, online supplemental appendices

Manuscripts considerably longer than these guidelines will be returned immediately without review.

### Abstract

**ENFORCED**

- **Word limit:** 200 words maximum
- **Format:** Unstructured, self-contained
- **Content:** Must summarize entire article, not just conclusions
- **Prohibition:** No reference citations in abstract

### Citations

**ENFORCED** - APA author-date style

**In-text citations:**
- Single source: (Offringa, 2006)
- Multiple sources: alphabetical order, separated by semicolons: (Buckley & Casson, 1976; Dunning, 1993a, 1993b; Rugman & Verbeke, 2003)
- 2 authors: both names every time (Rugman & Verbeke, 1998)
- 3-6 authors: all names first citation, then first author + "et al." for subsequent
- 7+ authors: first author + "et al." for ALL citations, including first
- Direct quotations: page number after year, colon + space: (Boddewyn, 1988: 538)

**Reference list:**
- Alphabetical order by author surname
- Format: Author, Initial. Year. Title. *Journal Name*, volume(issue): page range.
- Journal and book titles italicized

### Keywords

**ENFORCED**

3-6 keywords required. First 3 must be selected from official JIBS Keywords list.

### Headings

**GUIDELINE ONLY** (inferred from sister journal JIBP)

Three levels maximum:
- **Level 1:** Left-aligned, bold, 14-point (major sections)
- **Level 2:** Left-aligned, bold, 11-point, title case
- **Level 3:** Indented, italicized, sentence case, followed by period then text

### Formatting

**ENFORCED**

- **Font:** 11-point Times New Roman throughout
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced all body text
- **Margins:** 1 inch (2.5 cm) on all sides
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned
- **Sentence spacing:** One space between sentences, NOT two

### Tables and Figures

**ENFORCED**

- Word count excludes tables, figures, and online supplemental appendices
- Place at end of manuscript after references
- Each table/figure begins on new page
- Tables and figures cited consecutively in text
- Vertical rules should NOT be used in tables
- Every table must have descriptive title
- Units must be included in column headings for numerical measurements

### Hypotheses

**ENFORCED**

- Separately state each hypothesis
- Number each hypothesis
- Indent the hypothesis paragraph

Example format:
> Hypothesis 1. Title length is inversely related to the probability of publication.

### Statistical Reporting

**ENFORCED**

- Report **actual p-values**, not just threshold levels (not "p < .05" but the actual value)
- **Explicitly discuss effect sizes** of relevant estimated coefficients
- Provide appropriate citations for statistical methodology
- Full statistical details must be in paper itself (not relegated to appendices unless methodological details)

### Explicit Prohibitions

**ENFORCED**

1. **No "news column" style** (multi-column layout)
2. **No reference citations in abstract**
3. **No spurious citations** to JIBS articles (viewed unfavorably, interpreted as poor scholarship)
4. **No teaching materials/methods** manuscripts
5. **No pure literature reviews**
6. **No practitioner-only** manuscripts
7. **No manuscripts without theoretical contribution** to IB
8. **No manuscripts without specific relevance** to international business domain
9. **No two spaces between sentences** (use one space only)

### Writing Style

**GUIDELINE ONLY**

From official guidelines: "Write in an interesting, readable manner. Vary your sentence structure. Keep sentences short so the reader does not get lost before the end of a sentence. Do not write long, run-on paragraphs."

## Common Pitfalls

Based on explicit guidelines and prohibitions:

1. **Spurious self-citation:** Gratuitous citations to JIBS articles are viewed unfavorably and interpreted as signal of poor scholarship. Engage with extant IB knowledge authentically, not performatively.

2. **Abstract over-runs:** 200-word limit is strict. Abstracts must summarize entire article, not just conclusions. No citations allowed.

3. **Length violations:** Manuscripts considerably longer than 10,000 words (major contributions) or 7,000 words (narrower contributions) are returned immediately without review.

4. **Statistical reporting:** Failure to report actual p-values (instead reporting thresholds like "p < .05") or failure to discuss effect sizes will likely trigger revision requests.

5. **Scope mismatch:** Manuscripts must make theoretical contribution to international business AND have specific relevance to IB domain. Teaching materials, literature reviews, or practitioner-only pieces will be desk-rejected.

6. **Two spaces between sentences:** Guidelines explicitly prohibit this. Use one space only.
